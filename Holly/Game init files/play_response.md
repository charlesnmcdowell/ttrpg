The stool comes apart under Holly's hands the way a sentence comes apart under a translator's. *Sudden Insight* — twenty seconds, eyes on, the whole little machine of joinery laid bare: someone wedged a folded leather scrap under the third dowel six months ago to stop the wobble, and the leather has compressed into a useless ribbon, and the dowel itself is loose because the wood took a knot the carpenter ignored. The mithril needle from the satchel slides a fresh shim of folded oak veneer (pulled from the pouch — half a strip, chopped to size with the paring knife Mama Po set down) into the joint. Two presses with a thumb. The leg seats. The wobble is gone. And while Holly is at it: glance at the coin-counter scale on the corner of the stall — *Sudden Insight* on the brass — and the rigged piece is so obvious it feels like cheating to call it a reading. A copper shim, half the thickness of a fingernail, wedged under the western pivot pin. Tilts the fulcrum maybe four percent toward the merchant's side. Six months of fish.

"...Hng." Mama Po takes the stool back. Sits on it. Rocks. Doesn't rock. Stands up. Sits again. Stands again. Looks at the stool. Looks at Holly. Looks at the stool. Picks up the knife and the half-mango and just *hands them over* — knife first, mango second, like she's forgotten there's a transaction in this. "Six months. *Six months,* love. I had three apprentices try this stool. The last one charged me eight coppers and made it *worse.* You did it with — what was that, a sliver? — and you ain't even sat down." Her eyes track to the scale. "And what'd you see on the scale? Don't tell me 'nothing,' because I been watching your face. You saw something."

A longshoreman has stopped twenty feet off and is unsubtly listening, pretending to retie a boot. The other one's already moving — not running, but moving — toward the cluster of stalls upmarket from Mama Po's. The smell of frying garlic is thicker now. The sun has cleared the second warehouse. Mama Po is still holding the mango out, and she has not yet asked Holly's name.
