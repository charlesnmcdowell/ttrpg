## Synopsis

Kenji wakes face down in the mud on the edge of a forest that shouldn't exist, with no memory of who he is or how he arrived. No gear. No coin. No name anyone recognizes. The only thing he carries is an ember — a quiet fire burning at the base of his skull that responds not to study or incantation, but to sheer force of will.

Armed with a borrowed hand axe and instincts he can't explain, Kenji walks into the village of Duskfen and into a world unraveling at the seams. Something has been stirring in the deep forest to the northeast. Livestock vanish. Hunting dogs don't come back. Farmhands return from the northern fields hollowed out, whispering that the ground is *breathing*. And six-limbed predators called Hexcrawlers now patrol the woods in coordinated packs, driven by an intelligence no animal should possess.

After killing his first Hexcrawler with nothing but a pit trap and stubborn creativity, Kenji earns a sword, a set of dead man's armor, and the attention of three people who matter: **Sera**, a razor-sharp fighter who was already scouting the threat and waiting for someone worth following into it; **Edwyn**, a village priest and healer who has been quietly documenting an affliction he can't cure; and **Maren Ashby**, the village elder who holds the one piece of the puzzle no one else connected — a farmer named Harlan Brennick, who opened a stone door beneath his cellar six weeks ago and never truly came back.

What Kenji's party discovers beneath the Brennick farm is not a monster. It is something far older — a dying god of entropy, once worshipped as the gentle ending of things, now starving and desperate after centuries of being forgotten. Its hunger bleeds through the boundary between worlds, spawning Hexcrawlers, draining the life from the land, and calling to anyone who can hear it. And the ember inside Kenji — a force of raw creation dropped into this world six weeks after the god began to wake — may be the only thing that can restore the balance between endings and beginnings before the unraveling consumes everything.

*The Sorcerer-Sword* is a first-person LitRPG that follows a man with no past, a magic system that has opinions, and a mouth that runs faster than his judgment — as he fights, talks, and occasionally charms his way through a world that something sent him to save. Progression is earned in blood, clever tactics, and the willingness to touch a dying god bare-handed and see what happens.

## Features

- **First-person progression fantasy** with stat blocks, leveling, and a magic system built on willpower rather than study
- **Tactical combat** — pit traps, infused throwing stones, and fights won by preparation over raw power
- **A party that earns its trust** — a scout who doesn't suffer fools, a healer wrestling with his faith, and a locksmith who just wants to survive
- **Moral complexity** — not every threat is a villain, and not every god deserves to die
- **Isekai with teeth** — no menus, no tutorials, no kindly guide; just mud, monsters, and an ember that won't go out

## Chapters

1. **Day One — A World Without Maps** — Kenji arrives with nothing, kills a Hexcrawler, earns his first blade, and meets the people of Duskfen.

3. **Day Three — What Walks Toward Us** — The road to the Brennick farm. The stone door. A dying god speaks. And Kenji learns what the ember inside him was sent here to do.
