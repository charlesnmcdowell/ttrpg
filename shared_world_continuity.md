# Shared world continuity — Amaris + Kenji

**Universe policy:** All **future PCs** use this same realm. Amaris and Kenji remain **fixed lore**; each new character adds their own region and story (see `universe_campaign_framework.md` and `realm_lore_registry.json`). Copy `templates/new_character_campaign.template.json` when you start a new hero.

All campaigns take place in **the same realm**. The current era (25 years post–Book 4) is defined by the **Kingdom of Ankunyx** — a unified continental authority forged by the Dragon Emperor **AnkuNyx** (born Kenji). The old Crown realm, Academy, and Mage Council still exist as institutions within this larger kingdom, but ultimate authority flows upward to Ankunyx.

### Era: The Kingdom of Ankunyx (25 years post–Book 4)

**How it formed:** AnkuNyx did not conquer territory — he **united pre-existing territories** that were already allied or subordinate after the Iron Crown War and the Five-Threat Campaign. Each region keeps its own leaders, laws, and internal governance. All ultimately answer to Ankunyx. The kingdom is held together not by love but by **fear of regime change** — AnkuNyx will personally crush any leadership that attempts to fracture the union.

**Governance model:** Decentralized. Each faction retains its own power structure (stewards, tribal councils, elven courts, dwarven thanes, vampire covens). Local conflicts still occur — border disputes, trade disagreements, cultural friction. But all factions recognize Ankunyx as final authority. The Dragon Emperor disappears for long stretches of time (months, sometimes years), leaving stewards and regional leaders to manage day-to-day affairs. When he returns, disputes settle quickly.

**Kingdom-wide laws (enforced everywhere):**

- **Hunting dragons is illegal.** Dragons are protected citizens under Ankunyx decree. Violation is a capital offense.
- **Vampires are citizens.** The Red Court was dismantled; vampires who follow kingdom law have full legal standing.
- **Orcs are law-abiding mercenaries.** The Sunderplains tribes integrated as the kingdom's primary military labor force. Orc mercenary companies are licensed and regulated.
- **Dwarves are cave-dwelling engineers.** Kharn-Dural opened trade relations; dwarven engineering is the backbone of kingdom infrastructure. They remain mostly underground but are full participants in kingdom commerce.
- **Necromancy remains broadly illegal** except under licensed conditions (legacy of the Ashenveil resolution).

**What "same world" means now:** The old Crown realm's legal and magical order (Academy-trained mages, Mage Council oversight, ley line scholarship) persists as the **institutional core** of the kingdom. But the kingdom is larger than the old Crown ever was — it spans from the Dragonspine Mountains to the Ashenveil, from the Sunderplains to the eastern coast. New PCs awaken in this world where all of this is established history.

### Description map (words)

*Primary reference: no drawn map required. This is how travelers, priests, and merchants **talk** about place — belief and habit, not a survey grid.*

#### 1. The “middle of the world” (what people think it is)

Almost no one means the **geometric center of the planet**. They mean one of two things, depending on who is speaking:

- **The girdle of the world** — sailors and old stories describe a **temperate belt** of sea and cropland between **cold north** and **hot south** as *the middle lands*: where most mortals live, farm, and trade. The **Crown heartland** (the river country that holds **Varenholm** and the **Academy**) sits **in** that belt, **inland** from the western trade routes.
- **The heart of the realm** — day-to-day, “**middle**” means **toward the Crown** and **toward Varenholm**: where **law**, **guild charters**, and **licensed mages** are normal. “**Out** toward the frontier” means **away** from that center — **east** into wilder country, or **toward** the **Bleakmoor** border.

Use whichever phrasing fits the NPC. Scholars may nod at the girdle; a farmer says “toward the Academy” or “back toward the Crown roads.”

#### 2. The biggest kingdom in the area (anchor for everything else)

In the current era (25 years post–Book 4), the **largest** **unified** **power** is the **Kingdom of Ankunyx** — a continental authority forged by the Dragon Emperor **AnkuNyx** from the former Crown realm, his coalition territories, and the resolved threat regions. The old **Crown realm** still exists as the **institutional core** (Academy, Mage Council, guild charters) but is now one province within the larger kingdom.

**Varenholm** remains a **major city** in the **heartland** — the scholarly and administrative center. But the kingdom now extends far beyond the old Crown borders, encompassing everything from the Dragonspine to the Ashenveil.

**For NPC dialogue:** Common folk say "the kingdom" or "Ankunyx’s domain." Older people still say "the Crown" when they mean the heartland. Scholars and officials use "Kingdom of Ankunyx." Those close to the emperor (very few) still call him Kenji; everyone else knows him as AnkuNyx the Dragon Emperor — a figure more legend than man after 25 years of intermittent rule and long absences.

From the kingdom’s **core** (heartland / Varenholm cluster), describe other places like this:

## Campaign supersedes character

**The campaign is bigger than any single player.** A player’s death or absence does NOT end a campaign — it ends that player’s participation. The world continues. The threats continue. The clocks continue. Other players can enter the same world and attempt to finish what was started. A failed campaign has severe world ramifications (territories fall, populations die, the political map redraws). A successful campaign rewards the completing player with power, fame, and permanent world-state changes.

**Canonical example:** In Book 4, Kenji is operating in Millhaven — Amaris’s campaign territory. The seal, the dead road, the undead activity — that’s her threat chain, her content. She’s absent. Kenji walked in and started solving her problems. This is a player doing another player’s campaign in their absence. The world doesn’t wait for the “right” player to show up. Whoever is there, plays.

**Every campaign must have defined success and failure states.** Success = resolving the campaign’s central threats. Failure = any single threat overwhelming the world. These states persist regardless of which player is active.

## What “same world” means at the table

- **Both Amaris and Kenji campaigns are now completed lore.** New PCs inherit a world where the Kingdom of Ankunyx is established fact — 25 years of history. The Dragon Emperor is a legend. The five threats are resolved. The coalition became a kingdom.
- **Amaris’s outcome** (Greenveil corruption ended, Briarstone secure) is ancient history — happened before Kenji’s campaign even started. Eastern frontier is peaceful, remote, and largely forgotten by kingdom politics.
- **Kenji’s outcome** (five threats resolved, kingdom unified, Level 40 Apotheosis, 25 bonded lovers across the Soul Nexus) defines the current political map. AnkuNyx is the most powerful entity on the continent but is rarely seen — he disappears for months or years at a time.
- **Crossover remains real**: Any new PC operating within kingdom borders is operating in AnkuNyx’s domain. They may encounter his stewards, his laws, his constructs patrolling roads, or even an Ankuspawn (one of his many children). Direct encounter with AnkuNyx himself should be exceedingly rare — reserved for world-shaking moments.
- **Rumors vs. facts:** New PCs know the kingdom exists, that the Dragon Emperor united the territories, that dragons are protected, that vampires are citizens. Deeper knowledge (Soul Nexus, Ember, specific companions) depends on proximity, social class, and background. Common folk know legends; scholars know more; almost no one knows the full truth.

## Notable figures in the Kingdom era (25 years later)

*These are former campaign NPCs who have become part of the world's living texture. New PCs may encounter them, hear about them, or be affected by their presence. Add entries here as post-timeskip lore develops.*

### The Old Guard — Aging Companions (50s, retiring or retired)

Twenty-five years ages humans. Pip, Amaris, Sera, and the rest of Kenji's mortal companions are in their 50s now — graying, slowing down, settling into the lives they built after the campaigns ended. Some have families. Some are alone. A few are simply missing. But they all left marks on the map, and future players can find them — or their children.

**Companion locations (encounter-ready for new PCs):**

- **Pip** — Runs an **inn** in the heartland with her **husband**, a respectable man who helps manage the business. No children with him — the Mother's Curse. Her Ankuspawn children are 24-25 now, the oldest generation. Still sharp-tongued, still dangerous if provoked, but the adventuring days are behind her. **FROZEN GOAL: One of Pip's children has not returned home or sent word in over a year.** She doesn't know why. She's worried but hasn't acted yet — she's old, the roads are long, and she doesn't want to believe the worst. **Unknown to Pip, her missing child is one of the Ankuspawn captured and imprisoned by Nyx at the Ashenveil Academy.** Any new PC who visits Pip's inn and earns her trust will hear about the missing child. This is a direct on-ramp to the Cult of Anku investigation.
- **Amaris** — Lives on her **farm** near Thornfield / Briarstone, eastern frontier. The Greenveil hero, now a middle-aged farmer with a **husband** — a local man, steady and kind. No children with him — the Mother's Curse. Her Ankuspawn children are in their 20s, among the first generation. She moved on from Kenji long ago. Quiet life, no regrets.
- **Sera** — Operates out of **Stormhaven port**. The Darkblades evolved from a mercenary fleet into a legitimate maritime security force under kingdom law. Sera is semi-retired but still commands respect on the docks. Her Ankuspawn children grew up on ships. Whether she married is her business — ask at your own risk.
- **Other companions** — Scattered across the kingdom. The pattern holds: those who bore Ankuspawn found husbands but could not conceive again (the Mother's Curse). Some run shops, some train soldiers, some tend gardens. A few are MIA — gone without explanation, possibly dead, possibly just done with the world. Their exact locations can be defined when a new PC stumbles into them.

**The "Find Kenji" women:** Every woman who had it on her goal sheet to find Kenji — they found him. Bane of Eve made sure of that eventually. It led to intimacy, it led to Ankuspawn, and then Kenji disappeared again as he always does. Eventually every one of them stopped chasing. They settled down with successful human men, or they raised their children alone, or they moved on entirely. The longing faded. The children remained. Those children are now 18-25 and scattered across the kingdom — the bulk of the Ankuspawn population.

**All prior character goals (as of campaign end) are WIPED.** Twenty-five years resolved, abandoned, or rendered irrelevant every active goal from Book 4. The only surviving goal threads are:

- **Nyx** — Head of the Anku conspiracy (Cult of Anku, Ashenveil Academy, Ankuspawn harvesting, elixir production). This is the only active antagonist thread from the old cast.
- **The three elf sisters + the Eldest** — Raising the Ousaki. This is their entire focus. The sisters are devoted mothers; the Eldest observes and guides.
- **Pip** — Frozen goal: missing child (see above). Activates when a new PC visits her inn.

Everyone else: retired, settled, MIA, or dead. The old guard's story is over. Their children's stories are beginning.

### Vess & Mirenne — The Coalition Heartland

Vess is technically close to 80 but looks almost 40 — Vigor's lasting gift. She rose from Kenji's spymaster and portal administrator to **Head of the Council** for the coalition territories, the human-governed core of the kingdom. She and **Mirenne** (the vampire alchemist, now a legal citizen) have maintained law and order over a society that would have been unthinkable 30 years ago: **humans, undead, orcs, and dwarves** living under one administrative umbrella. It works because Vess is ruthless about fairness and Mirenne is ruthless about everything else.

**Dwarven artisans** have cornered the **blacksmith and enchantment markets** across the coalition. Their obsessive skill and dedication to those crafts made it inevitable once trade opened with Kharn-Dural — no human smith can compete with a dwarf who's been hammering for 200 years. Dwarven-forged weapons and enchanted gear are the prestige standard. Human smiths either specialize in volume work or apprentice under dwarves.

**Korrim "The Stone"** — the dwarven thane and Kenji's old ally — has entered an intimate relationship with Vess. The rumor mill is in overdrive: word is Korrim intends to **take Vess as one of his wives**, and a **wedding is expected this year**. A human-dwarf political marriage between the coalition's council head and Kharn-Dural's representative would be the diplomatic event of the decade.

**The fertility question:** Vess bore Ankuspawn with Kenji years ago. Like every woman who carried his children, she is now subject to the **Mother's Curse** — unable to conceive with any other man. Age isn't the issue; Vigor keeps her body decades younger. The curse is the issue. She wants to carry Korrim's lineage as his wife, and her body won't allow it. Rumor says she's been quietly looking into **Nyx's mysterious elixirs** — specifically one reported to dramatically increase fertility. She doesn't know what those elixirs are made from (harvested Ankuspawn — children who exist because of the same curse that sterilizes their mothers). If Vess pulls that thread, she's the most dangerous person who could possibly discover the truth — the coalition's top administrator, with the political power and personal fury to crack Nyx's entire operation open.

**What new PCs know:** Vess is a public figure — the coalition's top administrator, known for competence and an iron will. Mirenne is her shadow, less visible but widely feared. The Korrim wedding rumors are tavern gossip across the heartland. The fertility elixir angle is whisper-tier — only those connected to court politics or dark arts circles would hear it.

**DM hooks:** The wedding is a set piece waiting to happen. Vess investigating Nyx's elixirs is a slow-burn connection to the Cult of Anku plotline — if she discovers what the elixirs are made from (Ankuspawn), the political fallout could crack the kingdom. A new PC could be hired as wedding security, get caught up in court intrigue, or stumble into the elixir supply chain.

### The Eldest & Deepwood / Silvandris — Unchanged

The Eldest endures. The Deepwood endures. Twenty-five years is nothing to elves — a single breath in a life measured by centuries. The forest grows healthier, the wards grow stronger, the Heart Grove deepens its roots. If you visited Silvandris the day after Book 4 ended and again 25 years later, you would barely notice a difference. The same ancient trees. The same patient silence. The same permission required to enter.

The elven community grows slowly, as it always has. The 1/10,000 fertility rate means new elven children are events celebrated across the entire forest. The three sisters — Faelindra, Sylindra, and Vaelith — look almost exactly as they did when Kenji first met them. A few laugh lines around Faelindra's eyes, perhaps. Nothing more.

**Where Kenji goes when he disappears:** This is where AnkuNyx spends most of his vanishing stretches. He returns to the Deepwood and spends his time almost exclusively with the three daughters of the Eldest. It is the closest thing the Dragon Emperor has to peace. The elves tolerate his presence because the Eldest permits it — and because his children with her daughters have become something remarkable.

### The Ousaki — "Guardians of the Forest" (3 half-elf children)

Each of the three sisters bore **one child** with Kenji — three half-elves known in the Deepwood as the **Ousaki**, meaning "Guardians of the Forest." They are Ankuspawn, but the elves don't use that word. In Silvandris, they are simply the Ousaki.

**Aging:** Like all Ankuspawn, they age at human speed — fast by elven standards. They are young adults now (18-25), already fully grown while their mothers haven't aged a day. They carry **half-elf physical features** and **heavily favor their mothers** in appearance — anyone looking at them sees the sisters first, Kenji second.

**The Three Ousaki:**

- **The Mage** (Faelindra's child) — A fast caster of extraordinary talent. Raw magical throughput that outpaces elven mages who've studied for centuries. Her Ember spark manifests as pure arcane acceleration — she casts faster, adapts faster, innovates faster. The Academy elves find her unsettling.
- **The Warrior** (Sylindra's child) — A fighter of immense skill. Where most half-elves inherit elven grace, this one inherited elven precision paired with something heavier — a physicality that elves don't produce. Blade work that senior wardens can't match. Her Ember spark manifests as combat instinct — she reads fights before they happen.
- **The Druid** (Vaelith's child) — Attuned to the forest at a level that approaches the Eldest herself. The trees respond to her. The wards bend toward her. Animals follow her without being called. Her Ember spark manifests as deep natural communion — she doesn't cast druid magic so much as *ask the forest* and it answers. Some whisper she may be a true successor to the Eldest. The Eldest has not commented.

**What new PCs know:** Almost nothing. The Deepwood is closed to outsiders. The Ousaki are known only within Silvandris. A new PC with elven ties or deep forest connections might hear whispers — "the Eldest's grandchildren," "the fast-growing ones," "the guardians" — but details don't leave the tree line. If the Ousaki ever left the forest, they would be recognized as Ankuspawn by anyone who knew the signs.

**The Ousaki's secret:** Unknown to their mothers, unknown to the Eldest, unknown to Kenji — the three Ousaki secretly feel the Deepwood is **too small for them**. They are children of the Dragon Emperor. They aged fast, they grew powerful fast, and the patient eternal rhythm of elven life chafes against everything they inherited from their father. The forest is beautiful. It is also a cage. Quietly, in conversations they have only with each other, a darker ambition is forming: why stop at ruling the woods? Why not challenge their father? Why not take what he built?

They don't know what waits outside the tree line. **The moment any Ousaki steps out of the Deepwood, Nyx's Cult of Anku will detect them** — three Ankuspawn of extraordinary power, half-elven, carrying potent Ember sparks. They would be the highest-value targets the cult has ever identified. The Ousaki's ambition and Nyx's hunger are on a collision course, and neither side knows it yet.

**DM note:** The Ousaki are NOT available as starting PCs without explicit DM permission. They are named, established characters with defined abilities and a specific home. They exist as lore, as potential NPCs, and as a reason why AnkuNyx keeps returning to the Deepwood. The druid is a succession-level plot hook for the elven nation. The secret ambition is a ticking clock — when it detonates, it hits the Deepwood, the kingdom, AND the Nyx plotline simultaneously.

### Vorathiel — Ancient Black Dragon / Dragonspine Matriarch

Vorathiel hasn't changed. The Dragonspine Mountains haven't changed. She remains an ancient dragon ruling the peaks the same way she did before the kingdom existed. The treaty with AnkuNyx means humans don't hunt dragons and dragons don't raze cities — beyond that, dragon culture is what it always was. The strong rule. The weak die or leave. The flights keep their territories, their hierarchies, their thousand-year grudges.

Most dragons stay in the mountains or their claimed territories. They do not integrate. They do not attend festivals. They do not open shops in Varenholm. A few — very few — have chosen to travel the lowlands like Ignis, but they do so knowing Vorathiel and the traditional flights look down on it. A dragon playing music for humans is, to Vorathiel, roughly equivalent to a wolf performing tricks for sheep. She tolerates it. She does not respect it.

**What new PCs know:** The Dragonspine Mountains are off-limits. Dragons are protected by law — you cannot hunt them — but that law exists to protect *you*, not them. If you wander into dragon territory uninvited, the law won't save you and the kingdom won't retrieve your body. Vorathiel's name is known to scholars and military commanders as the dominant power in the north. Common folk just know "don't go to the mountains."

**DM note:** Dragons don't change. Don't modernize them, don't soften them, don't give them character arcs about learning to live among humans. They are ancient, territorial, and fundamentally alien in their values. Ignis is the exception that proves the rule.

### Ignis — "The Firebird" (Elder Fire Dragon / Bard)

Ignis, the Elder Fire Dragon bonded to AnkuNyx during Book 4, took an unexpected path after the kingdom's founding. She enrolled in a **bard school**, graduated with distinction, and **mastered multiple instruments**. She now travels the kingdom as a **performing artist** — a fantasy pop star whose songs and voice are known far and wide. Her concerts feature a spectacular **magical fireworks show** powered by her own dragon fire, blending bardic artistry with draconic power. She performs in human-shifted form but makes no secret of what she is.

**What new PCs know:** Almost everyone in the kingdom has heard of Ignis or "The Firebird." Her music is everywhere — taverns, festivals, noble courts. Seeing one of her shows is a life event. She's one of the most publicly visible dragons in the kingdom, and her fame does more for dragon-humanoid relations than any law AnkuNyx ever passed. Few know she's bonded to the Dragon Emperor. Most just know her as the greatest entertainer alive.

**DM hooks:** A new PC could attend one of her shows, be hired as a roadie or guard for her traveling troupe, encounter her in a tavern between performances, or hear one of her songs carrying a hidden message. She's a natural quest-giver, information source, or dramatic set piece.

### Nyx — The Lich Queen / Wife of AnkuNyx (HIDDEN ANTAGONIST)

Nyx, the ancient Lich and wife from whom the kingdom takes half its name ("Anku-Nyx"), grew tired of Kenji's endless womanizing — the Bane of Eve drawing women to him constantly, the God Emperor drawing dragons, children scattered across the continent. Rather than kill every woman who shared his bed (which she considered), she devised something far more cunning.

**The Ashenveil Academy:** Nyx opened her lands — the former dead marshlands of the Ashenveil — to the living for the first time. She built a **new magic academy** rivaling Varenholm's, specializing in **soul magic, necromancy, and the dark arts**. Students and masters from across the kingdom and beyond travel to study there. It is a legitimate institution on the surface — prestigious, exclusive, and feared in the way powerful schools always are.

**The Vampirism Cure:** Over the decades, Nyx discovered a **cure for vampirism** and used it to restore herself to a state of being **mostly alive**. The cure is not widely known or available — it is Nyx's personal achievement, and she controls who benefits from it.

**The Cult of Anku:** Nyx's true masterwork. She founded a secret cult of **women who practice the dark arts and swear personal loyalty to Nyx**. The cult's mission: infiltrate Kenji's life by presenting members as natural Bane of Eve encounters. To Kenji, they're new lovers — beautiful, willing women drawn to him as always. He has no idea they serve Nyx. After mating, the cult members **harvest his sperm and deliver it to Nyx in vials**. The cult operates across the kingdom, recruiting from dark arts practitioners and ambitious women who want access to Nyx's knowledge.

**Nyx's Research Program:**

- **Replication (FAILED):** Nyx attempted to replicate Kenji's essence — to synthesize whatever makes him what he is. She failed completely. His nature cannot be bottled or copied.
- **Vampirism cure (SUCCESS):** Using the harvested resource in quantity, Nyx incorporated it into her cure for undeath. The essence of a living god-tier being, applied in sufficient volume, can restore life to the undead — including herself. She is now "mostly alive," no longer fully a Lich but something between life and undeath.
- **Ankuspawn research (SUCCESS — DISTURBING):** Nyx expanded her research to Kenji's children who reached maturity (18+). She collected samples — unethically, without consent. Her findings: Ankuspawn are **stronger than normal mortals but far weaker than Kenji**. Each carries their **own individual spark of Ember** that manifests differently per person — potent powers spanning many domains of magic and abilities. **Female Ankuspawn** are particularly notable: high aptitude for gaining special abilities, otherworldly beauty, the capacity for powerful magic of all kinds, and a **strong scent/aura they exude that drives men wild** — an inherited echo of their father's Bane of Eve magnetism.
- **Ember nullification (SUCCESS — SECRET):** Through years of studying captured Ankuspawn, Nyx has **mastered the ability to nullify Ember-based abilities**. This works on Ankuspawn AND on Kenji himself. Her cult assassins and captors carry wards, devices, or enchantments that suppress Ember on contact or proximity. **AnkuNyx does not know she can do this.**
  - **Against Ankuspawn:** They lose their Ember spark. They're still tougher than normal mortals but their signature powers go dark. They must rely on non-Ember skills to survive. The difference: Ankuspawn are young. They may learn new tricks, develop non-Ember abilities, adapt. Their story isn't over.
  - **Against Kenji (THE NUCLEAR OPTION):** Nyx can nullify Kenji's Ember. She **prefers not to** — the current arrangement is profitable. She's collecting plenty of semen the way things are, and she's already verified that each child's Ember is unique while only Kenji's produces Vigor. He's more valuable operational than neutralized. **But if a player character tries to warn Kenji, or if Kenji somehow realizes the conspiracy and confronts Nyx, she pulls the trigger.** Without Ember, the Dragon Emperor ceases to exist. What remains is a **Level 40 charming rogue** with barely trained lockpicking, mediocre swordsmanship, an Intelligence of 9, and all stats below 20 because **every perk he has is Ember-based**. No Soul Nexus. No regeneration. No dragon form. No Vigor. No Ember flame. Nothing. Twenty-five years of coasting on god-tier power, and he never trained anything else. Nyx puts him in a cell. And if she has sex with him for even a few minutes without his Ember protecting him, he becomes her **thrall**. The Dragon Emperor — a puppet. The kingdom named after them both, now ruled by one.

**Nyx's contingency is the answer to "just tell Kenji."** Any player who thinks the solution is running to the most powerful being on the continent and saying "your wife is evil" is handing Nyx exactly the scenario she prepared for. Kenji confronts her. She nullifies him. He's in a cell by sundown. The player just made things infinitely worse. This is why the campaign requires a *different* solution — one that doesn't rely on Kenji's power, because his power has an off switch and Nyx is holding it.
- **Ankuspawn harvesting (ACTIVE — RECENT):** In the last few years, Nyx escalated. She directed the Cult of Anku to **capture mature Ankuspawn and secretly imprison them within the Academy**. There, she harvests them for their valuable biological resources — blood, essence, Ember fragments — which she uses to brew **extremely potent elixirs** of all kinds. The imprisoned Ankuspawn are a renewable resource for her alchemy.

**Nyx's Elixir Catalog (what's hitting the market):**

These elixirs are brewed from harvested Ankuspawn resources. They are reaching the wider market through the Academy's trade networks. People don't know what they're made from — they just know they work, and they're *impossibly* powerful:

- **Elixir of Titan's Might** — Increases the drinker's physical strength by **x100** for a duration. A common laborer could flip a wagon. A soldier could punch through a castle wall.
- **Elixir of Infinite Wells** — Grants **unlimited spell slots** for a set duration. A mage who normally casts three fireballs a day could cast three hundred.
- **Elixir of the Adamantine Skin** — Makes the drinker **immune to physical damage** for a set duration. Swords bounce. Arrows shatter. Only magic can touch you.
- **Elixir of Restoration** — Dramatically **increases fertility**, reverses aging effects, cures chronic ailments. This is the one Vess is seeking.
- **Others** — Nyx's catalog is expanding. If it can be imagined, she's probably working on it. The raw material (Ankuspawn Ember essence) is versatile enough to fuel almost any alchemical effect at absurd potency levels.

**CAMPAIGN RULE — INVISIBLE TO KENJI:** The Anku Conspiracy is specifically designed to be undetectable by Kenji/AnkuNyx. If the active player character IS Kenji, the following applies:

- **Cult members are indistinguishable from Bane of Eve / God Emperor encounters.** Kenji cannot tell the difference. The cult was designed from the ground up to exploit these perks. Beautiful women showing up wanting him is *literally his daily life*. There is no roll, no insight check, no magic detection that separates cult from coincidence — because from Kenji's perspective, there is no difference. They ARE beautiful women who want him.
- **Nyx's operations look normal to her husband.** Kenji is AnkuNyx. Nyx is his wife. When he visits her (between marathon sessions of sex that last days before he leaves again), he sees her Academy, her students, her creations — powerful dark magic stuff. He assumes Nyx being Nyx. She's always been a Lich who does Lich things. He'd have to stop and actually pay attention to notice something wrong, and Nyx makes sure he never gets that chance. She keeps him occupied. By the time he leaves, he's seen nothing.
- **The missing Ankuspawn don't register.** Kenji doesn't track his children. He never has. Kids he fathered years ago going quiet isn't a signal — it's the default. He doesn't get letters from them. He doesn't check on them. They're scattered across the kingdom living their own lives as far as he knows.
- **This campaign cannot be solved by playing as Kenji.** If someone plays Kenji in this era, the conspiracy is background noise he will never detect. It takes a different character — someone outside the blind spot, someone who isn't sleeping with the villain — to see what Kenji can't.

**These elixirs are the player-facing hook.** Tavern talk, merchant whispers, adventurer rumors — "Have you heard about that elixir that increases your strength a hundredfold?" "Have you heard about the one that gives you unlimited spell slots?" "I heard there's a potion that makes you immune to swords." The elixirs are too powerful to ignore, and anyone who asks where they come from starts walking toward Nyx.

**Cult of Anku — Member Profile:**

Every cult member is a **graduate of Nyx's Ashenveil Academy**, specifically trained and selected for the cult's mission. They are not amateurs. They are not thugs. They are the most dangerous women in the kingdom.

- **Level:** 30+ minimum. These are endgame-tier opponents. A new adventurer encountering a cult member is encountering their death.
- **Classes:** Assassins, necromancers, poison specialists, charm wielders, curse wielders, witches, and every other dark magic specialization the Academy produces. Most are multiclass — a charm specialist who is also a poison expert, an assassin who wields curses.
- **Alignment:** Chaotic evil. No exceptions. Nyx does not recruit the hesitant.
- **Appearance:** All female. All races — human, elf, half-orc, dwarf, halfling, dragonborn, vampire, dragon (shifted form), and any other humanoid race living in the Kingdom of Ankunyx. All extremely beautiful and seductive. This is not cosmetic — it is operational. Their primary mission is to seduce Kenji via Bane of Eve encounters. They need to be the kind of woman a god-emperor doesn't question. They are trained in every form of social manipulation, seduction, and infiltration. Nyx recruits from every race because Kenji sleeps with every race — the cult needs to match whatever the Bane of Eve draws next.
- **Vigor buff:** All cult members carry Kenji's Vigor buff — acquired through successful seduction missions. This makes them physically enhanced on top of their already high-level abilities.
- **Operational discipline:** Cult members give **one warning**. If they see you sniffing around, asking questions, following leads — a beautiful stranger will find you. She'll be friendly. She'll buy you a drink. And she'll tell you, very clearly, to **stop looking**. That's the player's get-out-of-jail-free card. Walk away and you live. Keep digging and the next visit isn't a conversation — it's a body disposal. The cult prefers silence over bloodshed (bodies attract attention), but they do not give second warnings.
- **Ember nullification gear:** Cult members on Ankuspawn capture missions carry Nyx's Ember suppression wards. This neutralizes the target's strongest abilities on contact or proximity.
- **Semen harvesting protocol:** After seducing Kenji, cult members covertly collect as much of his semen as possible in vials while he's distracted or sleeping. The vials are transported to Nyx at the Academy. This is the cult's primary ongoing mission — maintaining Nyx's supply of raw material.

**Why this matters for gameplay:** A player who stumbles into the cult first gets **the warning** — a beautiful stranger buys them a drink and tells them to stop looking. That's the narrative fork. Walk away and you live, but the conspiracy stays hidden and the missing kid stays missing. Keep digging and you're now facing Level 30+ chaotic evil specialists who are beautiful, charming, magically overpowered, carry Vigor buffs, and will not warn you again. Surviving the **second** encounter — the lethal one — is the true trigger for the Anku Conspiracy campaign. It proves the player is strong enough and committed enough to engage with a kingdom-spanning threat.

**Current Status (rumors spreading):** Word has recently begun to leak. Whispers about disappearing Ankuspawn, about the Academy's true purpose, about experiments in the lower levels. But **no one dares investigate**. Nyx is the Lich Queen, wife of the Dragon Emperor, the woman the kingdom is half-named after. Those who looked too closely into her affairs have not been heard from again. AnkuNyx himself is absent for long stretches — and even if he were present, would he move against his own wife? The kingdom holds its breath.

**What new PCs know:** The Ashenveil Academy exists and is prestigious. Nyx is the Dragon Emperor's wife — respected, feared, untouchable. Beyond that, knowledge depends on background. An Ankuspawn PC might have heard rumors of others going missing. A dark arts practitioner might have been approached by the Cult. A common villager knows nothing except that the marshlands have a school now and you don't ask questions about the Lich Queen.

**DM hooks:** This is a **major campaign-tier threat** hiding in plain sight. The Cult of Anku can serve as recurring antagonists. Missing Ankuspawn can drive investigation quests. The Academy itself is a dungeon, a social environment, and a political powder keg. The elixirs are the breadcrumb trail — too powerful to ignore, too mysterious to explain, and everyone's talking about them.

### Campaign Scenarios (three outcomes, not binary)

**Scenario 1 — The plot remains hidden.** The player never discovers the conspiracy, or discovers pieces but doesn't act on them. Nyx continues unchallenged. Ankuspawn remain hunted. The elixirs spread further — from the Academy to black markets, to merchants, to courts. Rumors about the elixirs reach more ears, more places. The potions become a fixture of the world economy. The cult grows bolder. The imprisoned Ankuspawn stay imprisoned. The world doesn't end — it just gets darker, and nobody knows why Ankuspawn keep disappearing. This is the **status quo outcome** — things continue as they are, getting slowly worse.

**Scenario 2 — The plot is discovered but Nyx is not stopped.** The player knows the truth. They know about the cult, the Academy prison, the elixir supply chain, maybe even the Ember nullification. But they can't or won't take Nyx down — she's too powerful, too politically protected, too entrenched. The elixirs keep selling. The cult keeps hunting. If the player is an Ankuspawn, this is something they have to **live with** unless they find a way to stop her. Critical detail: **Nyx's Ember nullification means Ankuspawn PCs cannot power their way out.** Her assassins and captors suppress Ember abilities on contact. Escaping or fighting the cult requires skills, cunning, alliances, and abilities **outside** of Ember — the one thing that makes Ankuspawn special is the one thing that doesn't work against Nyx's people. This forces Ankuspawn players to develop as complete characters, not just Ember batteries.

**Scenario 3 — The dark side option (non-Ankuspawn PCs).** A non-Ankuspawn character who discovers the conspiracy gains the option to **work for the Cult of Anku and Nyx directly**. The pay is extraordinary: **massive gold payouts and free elixirs** for every Ankuspawn they capture and deliver to Nyx. This is a genuine player choice — not a trap, not a gotcha. A mercenary, a bounty hunter, or a morally flexible adventurer might look at this offer and take it. They become part of the machine. The DM plays this straight: the gold is real, the elixirs are real, and the Ankuspawn screaming in the Academy basement are real. The player decides what they can live with.

**The player finds their own solution.** These three scenarios are frameworks, not rails. A player might expose Nyx to Kenji. They might blackmail her. They might destroy the Academy. They might free the Ankuspawn and start a war. They might broker a deal where Nyx switches to willing donors. They might burn the elixir supply and collapse the market. The campaign doesn't prescribe an ending — it presents a situation and lets the player decide what kind of person they are.
