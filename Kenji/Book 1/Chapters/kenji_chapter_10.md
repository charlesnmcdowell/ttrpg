# KENJI

## *Day Eight (continued) — What the Ember Sees*

---

I close the token in my fist. The brass is warm.

"Master Aldwin. Thank you."

The grey eyes soften. One degree. Half a second.

"Don't thank me. Thank whatever dropped you in the mud with that fire in your skull." He picks up his staff. "I gave you a test. You gave me a reason to keep teaching."

He pauses at the field's edge.

"Same time tomorrow. Dawn. I'll have something different." The ghost of something that was a smile forty years ago. "Bring the knife trick. I want to see it again."

He shuffles into the colonnade shadow and disappears the way he appeared — like architecture deciding to relocate.

---

The furnace demands fuel. The gauntlet burned through breakfast and then some. I find a food cart near the training grounds — a heavyset woman selling meat pies and roasted apples. Two pies and three apples vanish before she can count her payment.

Zero gold. Zero silver. Zero copper. I am a certified Combat Mage with a brass token and absolutely no money.

The woman's expression shifts toward unpleasant.

"I'll cover it."

Sera. Leaning against the colonnade pillar. Arms folded. Two copper in her fingers. She flips them to the cart woman without looking. "He's good for it."

She falls into step beside me.

"Your schedule," she says. Professional voice. But her eyes keep drifting to the brass token. "Midday — Elara's theory lesson. Private session. Room twelve. She said to bring the ember and an open mind. Also to eat first."

Second finger. "Afternoon — free. Library's open. Training grounds available. Garrett sold the surplus weapons for eighteen gold. Wants to discuss guild business at dinner."

Third finger. "Tomorrow dawn — Aldwin again. Then the vault tour in the afternoon. And Edwyn's presentation to the theological faculty. He'd like us there."

She's leading me somewhere through the colonnade. Toward the east wing.

"Kenji."

"Yeah."

"That thing with the Stalker. The knife. When did you learn Radiant Edge could infuse a thrown weapon that returns to your hand?"

"About three seconds before I threw it."

Silence. Four steps. Five.

"You improvised a new combat technique in the middle of a fight with a paralytic ambush predator in supernatural darkness."

"It worked."

"That's not the point." She stops. Turns. "The point is that you keep doing things that shouldn't be possible and treating them like they're obvious."

She looks at the token in my hand.

"Aldwin gave you the certification. He's given eleven. I'm one of them. It took me three years and a field examination that nearly killed me." A pause. "You got yours on your first morning."

Something on her face. Not jealousy. The vertigo of watching someone climb a mountain by a route that isn't on any map.

"You earned it," she says quietly. "I watched."

A beat.

"Don't tell Elara I was there. She told me not to come."

"But she was there too."

Sera's expression flickers. "She told both of us not to come. We came anyway. Separately. Ended up behind the same pillar." A pause. "It was awkward."

---

Room twelve. The paper avalanche has grown. New stacks on the windowsill. A cleared space on the desk already being reclaimed. A chalkboard propped against the bookshelf — diagrams, circles, connecting lines, symbols I don't recognise.

Elara is at the chalkboard. Chalk dust joining the ink stains. She doesn't turn.

"Close the door."

The latch clicks. The corridor sounds go muffled. The predator's eyes catch the reason — silver lines running along the doorframe, window edges, floor perimeter. Wards. Not containment. Surveillance countermeasures. She's soundproofed her own study against eavesdropping.

She turns. The dark eyes do the sweep-that-stalls. But today the theoretical curiosity is gone. She's seen the gauntlet. Something sharper has replaced it.

"Sit."

I find the chair. The only clear surface in the room.

She pulls a second chair close. Four feet between us. The chalkboard behind her. Ledger on her knee. Fresh page.

"I'm going to be direct. Yesterday I asked what you are. You made a joke and winked at my contractor." The ink-stained fingers tap the ledger. "Today I need real answers. Not because I'm curious — because what I saw this morning changes things."

She pauses.

"I told Sera and myself not to go. We both went. I saw everything."

No pretense.

"Three academy-grade specimens. You cleared all three without a meaningful hit. You improvised a radiant-infused returning knife throw through supernatural darkness into an eye socket. You dismantled a construct's engineering with precision our fourth-years can't match."

She leans forward.

"But that's not what frightened me. The ember. I could see it with mage-sight. The energy signature was visible from the colonnade. And what I saw was not sorcery. Not wizardry. Not divine casting. Not any classification in three hundred years of Academy taxonomy."

She picks up the chalk. Draws a circle.

"This is how magic works. For everyone."

Lines radiating outward. The triangle — source, will, effect.

"Wizards have weak sources but powerful will — precision through study. Sorcerers have powerful sources but less control — instinct. Priests have external sources — divine energy through faith."

She taps the triangle.

"Every practitioner in history fits inside this triangle."

She turns.

"You don't fit."

A second circle. Larger. Lines radiating *inward*. Converging on a center point.

"Your source isn't generating energy. It *is* energy. The ember isn't a talent or a connection. It's a *force*. Raw creation. When you cast, you're not shaping energy into an effect. You're allowing the energy to express itself through your will. The relationship is collaborative, not hierarchical."

She sets the chalk down.

"That's why you develop new techniques mid-combat. The ember responds to necessity in real time because it's not following a formula. It's *creating* one. Every spell you cast is being invented at the moment of casting."

She sits. Opens the ledger.

"I need you to reach for the ember. Not to cast. Not to infuse. Just feel what it is. And describe it to me."

---

I close my eyes.

The room disappears. The paper. The chalkboard. Elara. All replaced by the interior landscape I've visited in fragments since the mud.

The ember.

It's always there. But reaching for it without purpose is different. Like looking at the sun after only ever using it to see by.

"It's warm. Always warm. But not temperature. The warmth of something that's *there*. Completely. Without doubt."

Chalk scratches on the ledger.

"It has depth. Not size — *depth*. When I first woke up it felt like a candle. Now it feels like a well. Every time I push it, the bottom drops further. I haven't found the floor."

"Describe the texture."

"It's not separate from me. When I reach for it I'm reaching for myself. A part that was always there but wasn't turned on." A pause. "No. It was always on. I didn't know how to listen."

The ember pulses. Agreeing.

"It has opinions. It doesn't talk. It has preferences. Weapons — yes, always. Protection — yes. The Blade Ward came naturally. Like the ember already knew how."

"Things it refuses?"

"Utility. I asked for night vision. Flat no. Cold. Disinterested. Like asking a sword to stir soup. But then I framed it as Eyes of the Hunter — predator vision, target acquisition — and it couldn't infuse fast enough. The intent matters. The framing matters. If I approach it as a weapon, it responds. As a tool, silence."

Elara's breathing quickens.

"And creation. The altar."

The memory. Gold and black. The cycle.

"That was different. That wasn't the ember being a weapon. It was the ember being *itself*. The truest expression. Not force, not light. *Creation*. The fundamental act of making something exist. I poured it into the god and it didn't fight the entropy. It *completed* it. Two halves of a sentence finding each other."

Silence. The chalk has stopped.

"Open your eyes."

I do.

She's pale. White. The ledger covered in frantic shorthand. The chalk broken in her fingers. Her dark eyes fixed on a point two inches in front of my chest.

"Don't move. The ember is still open. I can see it with mage-sight. The energy signature is—"

She swallows.

"It's the most beautiful thing I've ever seen."

The words escape before the scientist catches them. She blinks. Resets. But the crack is there.

"The signature is generative. Not emitting energy — emitting *potential*. The difference between a fire and a seed. Your ember is an infinite seed that keeps becoming without ever finishing."

She stands. Paces.

"Creation and entropy aren't opposites — they're phases. The same energy in different directions. And you're carrying one half of that engine inside your chest."

She sits back down. Slower.

"Kenji. I need to tell you something."

The ink-stained fingers grip the ledger.

"For three years I've been tracking a pattern. Graduates who received their Assignment Seals have been declining. Slowly. Their magical abilities diminish over months and years. They become less sharp. Less powerful. Most chalk it up to stress."

She looks at the warded door.

"It's not stress. Something is draining them. The seal has been corrupted. Modified. It's acting as a siphon — drawing magical essence and funneling it somewhere."

She pushes her sleeve back. Her left inner forearm — a mark. Stylized seal. Academy insignia. The size of a coin.

"I received mine eight years ago. I'm being drained too. I thought it was age. It's not."

The fear underneath everything since yesterday. Named at last.

"I don't know who corrupted the seals. I don't know where the energy goes. I don't know how deep it reaches." She grips the ledger. "But whatever is receiving it — whatever is feeding on twelve years of graduates — is hungry. And it's getting closer to full."

The nighthollow. Voss. The highway operation.

"I think it's connected. Someone inside this Academy is funneling resources outward — sedatives, money, influence — to maintain a network that protects whatever is happening underground." She looks at the floor. "Sub-basement level two manufactures the nighthollow. Level three is restricted. No student has ever accessed it."

She looks at me.

"You walked into a cellar and touched a dying god. You carry creation energy — the one force that could theoretically detect corrupted entropy in the seals."

She holds out her left wrist. The seal facing me.

"I'm asking you to touch my seal. Reach for the ember. Not to cast — to feel. If the seal is corrupted with entropy, the creation energy should react."

Her eyes are steady. Her hand is shaking.

I look her deep in the eyes. Pause for a moment. Awkwardly.

"You want me to touch your *what*?"

---

The silence stretches.

The fear in her eyes transforms. Confusion. Processing. The exact moment the double meaning lands.

Color rises from her collar. The ink-stained fingers retract. The wrist vanishes into the sleeve.

"My *seal*, Kenji. The Academy seal. On my *wrist*." Each word enunciated with the precision of a woman who has taught for fourteen years and never once had a student say *that* in a warded room. "The magical mark that I just spent five minutes explaining is potentially draining my life force."

She stands. Sits. Stands again.

"I am trying to show you evidence of a conspiracy that may be killing hundreds of graduates and you're — you —"

She stops. Turns.

"Sera warned me. She said *he does this*. I thought she was exaggerating."

A beat.

"She was not exaggerating."

She sits down. Aggressively. Pushes a strand of hair behind her ear with controlled fury.

"Are you going to help me or are you going to make jokes while a potentially existential threat gathers power beneath the building we're sitting in?"

The wrist again. The seal. The hand no longer shaking.

Rigid.

"*Touch. The seal.*"

I grin. "Yes ma'am."

---

I take her wrist. Gently. The seal under my thumb — raised, warm, magic-warm. I reach for the ember. The open reach. The listening reach.

The ember touches the seal.

The world drops away.

The surface first — legitimate enchantment. Classification matrix. Elara's specialty. Her assignment. Her graduation year. Normal. Clean.

Beneath that.

The ember recoils. Not in pain. In *recognition*.

Entropy.

I know this feeling. The cellar. The altar. The same flavor — cold, pull, patient hunger. But not the same god.

The Duskfen entity was vast and desperate and lonely. What I feel in Elara's seal is different. Smaller. Precise. Not gasping — *sipping*. A thin thread of entropy embedded in the seal's enchantment like a parasite in healthy tissue. Drawing from Elara — not much, not fast. A trickle so subtle the body adjusts the way it adjusts to a slow bleed.

The thread leads down. Through the seal, through the ley web, through stone and ward and darkness. Into the bones of the Academy. It doesn't end at a god gasping through a crack. It ends at something *deliberate*. Something placed with precision and patience and the confidence of a craftsman who knows his work won't be examined.

And at the far end — distant, muffled by layers of stone and ancient warding — a pulse. Faint. Rhythmic. Like a heartbeat. Something receiving the trickle. Collecting. Growing fuller.

The ember flares. Hot. Angry. Not at the entropy — at the *design*. The Duskfen god was starving and took what it could reach. This is architecture. Someone *built* this. Someone wove entropy into a seal of achievement and turned every graduate into an unwitting battery.

The creation energy pushes against the thread. Instinctive. Protective. The thread *flinches*. For one instant the trickle stops.

Elara gasps. Her fingers clench around mine.

Then the thread reasserts. The parasitic draw resumes. The ember could burn it out — I feel the capacity — but the thread is woven into the seal itself. Burning it means burning the seal. And I don't know what that would do to her.

I let go.

---

The room. The paper. The chalkboard. Elara's face — white. Eyes wide. Fingers still gripping mine.

"You felt that," she whispers. "The drain stopped. For one second. The first time in eight years that I've felt..." She searches. "...*whole*."

My hand is trembling. Not weakness. Anger. The ember is furious — burning hot with the particular rage of creation encountering something that corrupts creation itself. Turning achievement into theft.

"It's there. Exactly what you suspected. Entropy woven into the seal underneath the legitimate enchantment. Siphoning your essence in a constant trickle. So slow you'd never notice."

"Where does it go?"

"Down. Through the ley web. Into the deep structure of the Academy. Something at the other end is receiving. Collecting. It has a pulse — like a heartbeat."

"How many years."

"You said twelve years. If every seal is corrupted—"

"Three hundred and forty-seven graduates." The number sits in the room like a weight. "Three hundred and forty-seven slow-burn siphons feeding something beneath this building for over a decade."

"Can you remove it? The thread. You stopped it for a moment."

"I could. The ember has the force. But the thread is woven into the seal. Removing it might damage you. I'd need the original seal design — the uncorrupted version. Compare the clean design to what's actually there, map where the entropy was inserted, find how to remove it safely."

She nods. The scholar understanding.

"The original designs are in the Academy archives. Restricted section. Accessible only to senior faculty and—"

She stops.

"—the Chancellor."

The name.

"Chancellor Marius Vael controls the archives. He controls enrollment. He controls the sealing ceremony. He's controlled all of it for the last twelve years."

The calculation she didn't want to make.

"I don't have proof. Correlation isn't causation. But—"

"But you don't believe in coincidences."

"No. I don't."

She looks at the chalkboard. The circles. The triangle.

"We need the original seal design. We need the restricted archives. And we need to get into sub-basement level three." She looks at me. "None of that is possible without the Chancellor's authorization or breaking into the most heavily warded sections of the Academy."

A pause.

"You said Ryn taught you to pick locks."

---

END OF CHAPTER TEN — NO LEVEL UP (No Long Rest)

The conspiracy is open. The threads are visible. Three hundred and forty-seven graduates draining into something that pulses like a heartbeat beneath the Academy. A Chancellor who controls everything. A warded archive with the answers. And a sub-basement that no student has ever accessed.

The ember is angry. Creation has found something that corrupts creation itself.

The afternoon is young. The questions are old. And somewhere beneath my feet, something is getting fuller.
