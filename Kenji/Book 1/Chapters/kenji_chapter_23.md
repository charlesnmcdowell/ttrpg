# KENJI

## *Day Twelve — The Gauntlet, Revisited*

---

Dawn. The Silver Draft. Pip cross-legged at the foot of the bed. The dimensional folding book across her knees. The Whisperstone Ring catching first light. Mouthing words as she reads.

"Chapter nine. The author finally admits he was wrong about membrane theory in chapter eight. Took him two hundred pages."

She looks at me.

"The old man with the staff. Aldwin. He wants to see me and Ryn this morning?"

Level eight. The body different again. Six more HP. An L4 slot sitting empty. The cross-guard integrated overnight.

I eat. Sausage, bread, cheese from the satchel. Pip steals cheese without asking.

---

"If you keep this up, you may be able to join Sera and me on our adventures. I'd recommend focusing on something to help us stay alive — like abjuration magic. Ryn would make a great spell thief if he applied himself."

Pip looks at me over the stolen cheese. "Abjuration. You want me to learn protection magic."

She holds up the book.

"I'm nine chapters into a text about folding space. The same principle that makes your portals work. And you want me to learn how to make a shield."

"Woah woah woah. It was only a suggestion. A void mage would be much cooler. Think about the implications of literally transforming space to cut people in half. Fast movement. Teleporting products around for your inn. You'd put my portals to shame."

Her eyes widen. The book comes back up.

"Void mage. Transforming space. Cutting things by folding the distance between two points to zero." She's already running the theory. "Chapter six — spatial compression — if you compress a three-dimensional volume to a two-dimensional plane, anything occupying that volume gets—"

She makes a slicing gesture.

"And teleportation — that's just folding the distance between two points. Your portals are permanent folds. I'd be making temporary ones. On demand. Anywhere."

She looks at the book like it just became a weapon.

"In a fight I could fold the space between an enemy's head and their body."

She looks at me.

"You're right. That's much cooler than shields."

---

Garrett's room. Down the hall. Ryn opens — already awake. Three open books in a semicircle on the floor. Satchel packed by the door.

"The foundational theory texts cross-reference badly," he says.

"Ryn, Kenji thinks you'd make a great spell thief," Pip says. Grabbing his arm.

Ryn lets himself be pulled into the corridor. "What's a spell thief?"

"Someone who studies other people's magic and learns to copy it, redirect it, shut it down. Counterspell. Dispel. Absorption. The kind of mage who watches an enemy cast and turns their own magic against them."

Ryn is quiet. Then: "That's just paying attention."

"Yep. Plus you may be able to magically pick locks. And copy magical tomes and scrolls while maintaining the magical use."

Ryn stops walking. The quiet eyes sharpening.

"Copy scrolls. And they still work."

"If you're good enough."

He walks faster. Pip adjusts her pace. The young man who said "I don't have magic" three times is now leading the group.

---

The eastern training grounds. Dawn. The portal humming gold. Aldwin on the bench. Staff across his knees. Grey eyes finding me, then moving to the two people beside me.

Sera against the colonnade. Clean leathers. Arms folded. Assessing Pip and Ryn the way she'd assess terrain.

Aldwin stands. Staff plants.

"These are them. Names."

I walk past. To the colonnade. Lean against the pillar beside Sera. Arms folded. Mirroring her stance.

Eyes of the Hunter. Arcane Stride. The ley alcove refills my slots.

Sera glances at me. "You're not introducing them?"

"Aldwin asked for names. They have names."

---

Pip steps forward. Token on her belt. Book under her arm.

"Pip. I run the Broken Antler inn in Duskfen. I've been reading about dimensional folding for eighteen hours and the author of this book is wrong about planar membrane theory."

Aldwin's eyebrows rise. One millimeter.

"The author is Professor Emeritus Callum Drake. Dead for thirty years. His membrane theory is the foundation of our spatial curriculum. How is he wrong?"

Pip opens to a dog-eared page. "He assumes planar membranes are uniform in thickness. They're not. The portal in your training ground creates a fold that's thinner at the edges. If Drake's model were correct, the edges would shimmer the same as the center. They don't. The edges are brighter because the membrane is thinner and more energy bleeds through."

Silence. Aldwin looks at the portal. At the edges. At the center. The shimmer is brighter at the edges.

He looks at me. Accusation and admiration.

"You brought me a woman from a marsh inn who just corrected a dead professor's spatial theory by looking at your portal."

He turns to Pip. "What do you want to learn?"

"Void magic. Spatial folding. Compression. Teleportation. Dimensional combat." She ticks them off like a supply order. "And abjuration. Because someone has to keep the idiots alive."

She glances at me when she says idiots.

"Sit on the bench. Don't talk."

Pip sits. The book opens. Chapter nine.

---

Ryn steps forward. No token. No book. Satchel on his shoulder.

"Ryn."

That's it.

Aldwin studies him. Ten seconds. The deep read.

"You're the one who read four books in six hours."

"The cross-referencing was poor."

"What did you learn?"

"That foundational magical theory is taught in contradictory layers. The primer simplifies to inaccuracy. The advanced text assumes knowledge the primer doesn't provide. The gap is where students fail."

"You identified a curricular flaw in six hours that the faculty has argued about for twenty years. What else?"

"The entropy classification system is wrong. It categorizes entropy as destructive. The priest's lecture described entropy as a phase of a cycle. The textbooks don't account for that."

Aldwin looks at me. "Where did you find these two?"

"A marsh. They were making porridge and sweeping ash."

"Pick up a training sword," Aldwin tells Ryn.

Ryn walks to the rack. Selects — doesn't grab. A wooden sword weighted for balance. The grip is wrong but the stance is instinctive. Feet shoulder-width. Weight centered. The body position of someone who watched Kael train mercenaries from a smithy corner.

"Who taught you to stand like that?"

"Nobody. Kael does it. At the guild."

"You watched Kael."

"I watch everyone."

Aldwin looks at Sera. "Throw something at him. Slow."

Sera picks up a stone. Tosses it. Underhand. Gentle arc toward Ryn's chest.

Ryn doesn't block. Doesn't dodge. He tracks the stone — calculating trajectory — and shifts his weight one inch left. The stone passes his right shoulder.

"Again. Faster."

Sera throws hard. A real throw. Ryn tracks it. Shifts. The stone clips his sleeve.

"He's reading the trajectory," Sera says. "Not reacting to the stone. Reading the arm. Watching where the throw starts, not where it goes."

She looks at me.

"That's what a spell thief does."

Aldwin taps the staff. "Both enrolled. Provisional. Individual curricula."

He looks at me and Sera. "Terrible recruiter. Excellent results. Get out of my training ground."

---

I turn to Sera. The thought arriving mid-morning.

"Now that I think about it — I haven't read a book. A sign. Wait a sec." I look at my hands. "Can I even read?"

A moment. The man who woke in mud twelve days ago with no memory. Who has navigated an entire world through conversation, combat, and instinct. Never once picked up a book, read a sign, or checked a map with his own eyes.

I shrug. Continue watching Aldwin work.

Sera looks at me. The dark eyes recalibrating. The combat lead standing beside a man who outsmarted a Chancellor, negotiated with gods, and restructured an institution — who may not be able to read the menu at the Silver Draft.

She doesn't say anything. The arms stay folded.

---

"Sera. You have access to the gauntlet?"

The competitive flicker. Instant.

"Aldwin controls the summons. But the ward infrastructure is passive. A senior combat mage can activate independently." She looks at me. "I'm a senior combat mage."

She pushes off the colonnade.

"What's the bet?"

"If I win — you give me reading lessons. Two hours. In your room."

Five seconds of calculation. Two meanings. She walks into it anyway.

"Fine. If I win — you stand on the podium in the lecture hall and tell every faculty member Sera is the better combat mage. Using the god-sword as a megaphone. Public. Loud."

"Full loadout?"

"Full loadout."

---

The containment circle. Emberfang right. Merchant longsword left. Dual-wield. Stride humming. Eyes sharp. Ward Mastery cast between waves.

Sera at the control anchor. The combat lead becoming the audience.

"I'll be watching. Try not to embarrass yourself."

"Send it."

Sera leans against the colonnade. Arms folded. The dark eyes locked on me. Running predictions. *If I were him — Duality Aspect. Fly above the Basilisk. Let the aura work while throwing crescents from safety. The efficient play.*

---

### WAVE 1: Stonehide Basilisk

I throw the returning knife across the floor. Not to hit — to sound. The blade skittering off stone. Mimicking footsteps.

The Basilisk's head snaps toward the sound. Petrifying gaze locking on the wrong target. The armored body turning. Exposing the left flank.

I close from the blind side. Stride speed. Silent on Windrunner Boots. Ten feet. The neck-shoulder seam.

The longsword comes down on the seam. Thunderous Strike — L3. The ember takes the slot and makes it more. The thunder doesn't just detonate. It erupts. A concussive shockwave from the point of contact. The Basilisk — eight hundred pounds of stone plates — launches twenty feet across the arena. Hits the containment barrier. Stone plates shattering on impact. Landing on its back. Legs up. Underbelly exposed. Stunned.

Twin Fang trailing — Emberfang at the underbelly. Advantage. The blade punches through soft tissue. Amber fire flooding the interior.

Stride extra action — Emberfang again. Natural twenty. Critical hit. The blade to the crossguard. Creation fire detonating inside the body cavity. Plates cracking from within.

62 damage. One round. Dead before its legs stop twitching.

Sera from the colonnade: "You launched it. The thunder didn't do that two days ago."

"It didn't have an L3 slot two days ago."

She predicted Duality and crescents from safety. She got a thrown knife, a flanked Basilisk, and a thunder launch into a wall. The combat lead recalculating.

---

### WAVE 2: Twin Duskmantle Stalkers

Drop Stride. Cast Duality Aspect — creation mode. The golden aura blooms. Flight. Drop Eyes. Cast Radiant Edge L3 on Emberfang. The amber glow shifts to golden-white radiance. Thirty feet of holy light.

The Stalkers' darkness auras recoil. Supernatural shadow hitting radiant light and dissolving. Two creatures that hunt in manufactured darkness suddenly exposed.

Sheathe the longsword. Single weapon. Emberfang blazing — fire and radiant.

I fly. Thirty feet from Stalker 1. Flame Crescent. The golden-white crescent rips across the distance. Fire and radiant combined. The Stalker's shadow-flesh burns on two frequencies. Fire eats the body. Radiant eats the darkness itself.

I fly to max distance. 120 feet. A floating sun. The Stalkers can't reach me. Their ceiling crawl speed is forty feet. They chase a light they'll never catch.

Four rounds. Flame Crescents. Fire and radiant. The darkness auras destroyed, not suppressed. Each Stalker burning and exposed in golden light that consumes their fundamental nature.

Stalker 1 — purified. Shadow-flesh dissolving. Scorch mark and wisps.

Stalker 2 — purified. The darkness detonating outward and the radiant eating it in half a second.

Zero damage taken.

Sera: "That wasn't a fight. That was an execution."

Sera recalculates again. Adjusts her model. *The Sentinel — he'll go for the knees. Same as certification. Lead with longsword, trail Emberfang for fire. Speed-based joint destruction.* She's sure this time.

---

### WAVE 3: Iron Sentinel

Drop Duality. Drop Radiant. Recast Eyes, Stride L3, Ward Mastery. Refill at the ley line.

The construct materializes. Iron plates. Shield. Ward-light eyes.

I sheathe both swords. Sprint unarmed at the Sentinel. 120 feet of Stride speed. Wide arc — behind it. The mechanical tracking too slow. I jump. Windrunner Boots on iron plating. Both hands on the neck piece. Riding it like a horse made of iron.

The Sentinel spins. Trying to shake me. I hold. The neck piece under my fingers — ward-light pulsing. The creation energy reading the instructions. Simple. Mechanical. Identify threat. Close distance. Destroy.

I push. The ember through my palms. Raw creation into the ward-architecture. Not destroying instructions. Rewriting them.

Old: Identify threat. Close distance. Destroy.

New: Identify Kenji. Protect. Obey.

The ward-light shifts. Blue to gold. The iron body goes still. The shield lowers. Eight feet of construct standing at attention.

Wave 3 — CLEARED. The gauntlet accepting conversion as victory.

Aldwin from across the training ground: "He does this. Get used to it."

She predicted the knees. He climbed it and rewrote its brain. Three waves. Three wrong predictions. The combat lead stops trying to predict the general. Some things can't be modeled.

---

### WAVE 4: ???

Ley alcove. Full refill. Stride L3, Eyes L3, Ward Mastery — force resistance.

The anchors pulse brighter than any previous wave. More energy. More mass. The summoning takes longer. Fifteen seconds.

Sera straightens at the colonnade.

"That's more than a standard Sentinel. The gauntlet is compensating. You converted Wave 3 without taking damage — the system is escalating harder than normal."

The containment circle flares.

---

END OF CHAPTER TWENTY-THREE — NO LEVEL UP (No Long Rest)

The arena. The containment wards blazing. Something building in the circle that's bigger than anything the gauntlet has produced. Sera watching from the colonnade. Aldwin watching from the far side. Pip and Ryn watching from the bench — the void mage and the spell thief seeing their first real combat.

The converted Sentinel standing beside the arena with gold eyes. My creation. My soldier. About to meet whatever the gauntlet thinks is the answer to a man who doesn't fight the way the system expects.

Three waves cleared. A Basilisk launched into a wall. Two Stalkers purified in radiant light. A Sentinel rewritten with bare hands.

Wave 4 builds.

The bet is still on. The reading lessons or the megaphone. Sera is keeping count.

The circle flares brighter.
