# KENJI

## *Day Eight (continued) — What's Underneath*

---

I find Garrett in the market district. Outdoor table near a weapons stall. A tankard and a ledger he didn't have yesterday. The liaison building infrastructure.

"How was the old man?"

"I killed three monsters, got a brass token, and made two women regret spying on me."

"So a normal morning." He pushes a chair out. "Sit. I've got numbers."

The surplus weapons sold — longbows, crossbows, short swords, bolts and arrows. Eighteen gold total. Already deposited in a holding account at a moneylender on Merchant Row. The guild needs a treasury that isn't a lockbox.

My income — ten percent of guild earnings. The guild hasn't earned anything yet beyond the weapon sale. The highway contract won't be formalized for three or four days. Once active, forty gold per month flows in. My cut — four gold monthly.

"Short version — you're broke right now. The guild has eighteen gold in holding but that's operating capital. Brindle needs weapons, rations, and at least two more recruits before the contract activates. A garrison that can't feed itself doesn't last a month."

He looks at my belt.

"What have you got to sell?"

I lay it out. Voss's keys — unknown locks, hold them. Flint stones — ammunition, keep them. Pip's honey cakes — he looks at me, looks at the cakes, doesn't ask. The entropy anchor ring — might be worth something at the Gilt Lens. The Combat Mage token — not for sale. Ever.

"Honest assessment — you've got nothing to sell except maybe the ring."

He pushes five gold across the table.

"Advance on your founder's share. Meals, flint, incidentals for the week." He looks at me. "Don't spend it on enchanted knives."

---

The Silver Draft. Edwyn's table. The same table, the same ledger, the same tea. He's turned a corner of the common room into a field office. Notes spread in organized stacks — the presentation taking shape.

"You're not injured. That's new for this time of day."

"Give it time." I set the returning knife on the table. "I need more poison for this. Sleep or paralysis. Something non-lethal."

He examines the blade. Studies the surface.

"Somnus — what Maren sold you — I can't replicate. The refinement requires equipment I don't have." He holds up a finger. "But."

Three items from his satchel. A cloth pouch. A glass jar. A dark bundle of leaves.

Three options. Slumber paste — brightcap mushrooms concentrated into a topical sedative. Disadvantage on everything for a minute, second hit knocks unconscious. Four doses, two hours prep. Lockjoint resin — staghorn cap spores disrupting muscle control. Half speed, no reaction, one action only, three rounds. Three doses, four hours prep.

And bittershade.

"Rendered differently — cold extraction instead of heated — it produces a nerve toxin. Convulsions. Full incapacitation. Five minutes. They're conscious but can't move, can't speak. They feel everything."

He sets the leaves down.

"This one is a *poison*, not a sedative. The distinction matters to me even if it doesn't matter to the blade."

"I'll take the bittershade. I'll pick it up at your presentation."

He nods. The professional accepting. The priest filing the weight.

"When you use it — remember the target is conscious. They feel everything."

"Noted."

I set the brass token on the table. His eyebrows climb slowly.

"Combat Mage certification. On your first day." He sets it down gently. The priest's fingers handle it the way they handle relics — with the weight of what it represents rather than what it's made of. "There are faculty who've taught here twenty years and never earned one."

"Aldwin said I'm number twelve."

"In *forty years*." He shakes his head. "You know, when I met you in that clearing, I thought the Sovereign had sent me a project. Turns out He sent me a front-row seat." "Tomorrow. Second bell, afternoon. Main lecture hall, west wing. I'd like you there — not as support. As evidence. Having the man who touched the altar in the room reduces skepticism."

"I'll be there."

"And eat before you come."

---

I pull a honey cake from my chest pocket. Pip's handwriting on the cloth. *Come back. — P*

Three days old. Not preserved — the cakes were in my pocket, not the satchel. Slightly stale. The lavender faded. A hairline crack down the middle where the road put its weight on it.

I eat it anyway. It tastes like a girl standing in a doorway. Like someone who believes you'll keep a promise you haven't figured out how to make yet.

Two cakes remaining. I transfer them into the Satchel of Holding. Preserved now.

---

The Gilt Lens. The bell chimes. Maren Holt behind the counter. Same spectacles. Same magnifying glass.

"Back already. Spent all your gold and you want more things."

"Appraisal today."

"One gold standard. Two if it's cursed."

I set the entropy anchor ring on the counter. Still wrapped in cloth.

"Don't touch it directly."

Her eyebrow rises. Heavy leather gloves — rune-stitched, for dangerous objects. She unwraps the cloth. The ring on the counter. Small. Iron. A dull black stone that catches the ward-light and holds it. Light enters and doesn't come back.

Magnifying glass first. Then a crystal lens — pale blue, faceted. The predator's eyes catch what happens: the lens reveals micro-enchantments inside the stone. Dozens of threadlike structures converging on the center.

"Where did you get this."

Not a question. A demand wearing a question's clothes. The magnifying glass is steady but the spectacles have slid down her nose and she hasn't pushed them back. Maren Holt always pushes them back.

"A cellar beneath a farmhouse in Duskfen."

She's quiet for a long time.

"This isn't ancient. The iron is old — a century, maybe two. But the enchantment is older. Significantly. The stone predates the setting by a margin I'm not comfortable estimating."

She looks at me.

"This is an entropy siphon. The stone absorbs ambient magical energy and channels it along a predetermined path. The micro-enchantments are routing threads directing absorbed energy to a specific destination."

She taps the counter beside the ring.

"The structure is sophisticated. This isn't hedge magic. This is institutional-grade enchantment work."

*Institutional-grade*. In a city with one institution.

"To a collector — fifteen to twenty gold. To a researcher — fifty or more." She looks at me. "To you — it's evidence. And evidence is worth more than gold."

One gold for the appraisal. Four gold remaining.

---

Room twelve. I knock. One dark eye in the gap.

"I told you to—" She sees my face. The door opens.

I set the ring on her desk. Tell her everything Maren said. The iron. The stone. The routing threads. Institutional-grade.

Elara sits. Pulls her own crystal lens. Holds it over the ring.

Two minutes of silence. Her free hand writing without looking.

"The routing threads. The encoding pattern. I've seen this before."

A worn leather folder. Pages of hand-drawn diagrams. Three years of work.

"I've been trying to reverse-engineer the entropy threads in the seals. Too sophisticated. Too old. Didn't match any modern methodology."

She sets one of her diagrams next to the ring. Crystal lens over both.

"Because it's not modern."

She looks up.

"The encoding pattern in this ring is the same pattern as the entropy threads in the seals. Same methodology. Same architect."

She stops.

"Or the architect is still alive," I say.

The wards hum. The ley web pulses.

"The Chancellor is old. Visibly old. He's been Chancellor for twelve years. Before that, senior faculty for fifteen. Already old when he arrived."

She looks at the ring and the diagram.

"What if he was already old because he was *already old*. This methodology is centuries old. If the same person designed this ring and corrupted the seals..."

She looks at me.

"Then Marius Vael is not an elderly Chancellor. He's something much older wearing the face of one."

"Keep the ring," I tell her.

She wraps it back in cloth. Holds it out. "You keep it. When you're in his office — the ember might tell you more."

I take the ring back. Evidence that connects a farmhouse cellar to an Academy sub-basement. Same threads. Same architect. Centuries apart.

---

I find Sera at the stone owl fountain. Cleaning her sword. Afternoon light on the blade.

I drop two copper into her lap.

She looks at the coins. "I was going to let that debt build. See how long before your pride caught up." She pockets them. "Six hours. Not bad."

I sit on the fountain edge beside her. Students thinning in the courtyard. Afternoon classes ending.

"I need to talk to you about something."

The sword goes back in the scabbard.

"How hard would it be to sneak into sub-basement level three. Tonight."

"Sub-basement three. The restricted level that no student has ever accessed. In an Academy with enchanted guards and ward systems refined for three hundred years." She looks at me. "Why."

I don't answer why. Not yet. Instead I ask a different question.

"Are we close enough for me to touch your seal?"

One second. Two. Her hand on the sword pommel goes still.

The CHA of seventeen knows exactly what it's doing. The INT of nine is just happy to be here.

"Your seal. The Combat Mage certification. Aldwin gave you one."

"My *what*."

"I saw the mark on your forearm during training at Duskfen. Same system. Same Academy."

Her eyes narrow. The color hasn't arrived yet — she's holding it back through sheer force of will.

"Elara warned me. She sent a note. Three words. *He does this.*"

A beat.

"You asked Elara the same question."

"Technically she offered."

"She *offered* to let you—"

She stops. Closes her eyes. The color arrives with reinforcements.

"Kenji. I just met you eight days ago. You've fought a god, founded a guild, made a killer swear loyalty, and earned a token that took me three years of nearly dying. And twice now you've asked a woman to let you touch her seal with a face that belongs in a brothel."

She stares at me. Color in her ears.

"What exactly are you checking for."

---

I don't answer that question either. Instead I say something I've been thinking since the training grounds.

"Sera. It just occurs to me — you have a rare token signifying you're an elite battle mage. Yet I've never seen you cast a spell."

The words land like a stone in still water.

Her expression doesn't change. That's how I know it hit.

Five seconds.

"I'm a fighter. The certification recognizes integrated combat — magic and steel."

"But you fight with steel. Only steel. Every fight I've seen. Not once have you cast."

"Combat magic requires focus. Field conditions aren't—"

"Sera."

She stops.

"When's the last time you cast a spell."

The silence changes. Not defensive. The silence of someone hearing the ground crack beneath something they've been avoiding.

"Two years." Quiet. The quietest I've ever heard her voice. Her hand drops from the sword pommel to her lap — the first time I've seen her voluntarily release the weapon. "I haven't been able to cast in two years. I told myself it was field rust. That if I trained, it would come back."

She closes her fists. Opens them. The fingers that should crackle with force magic flex against nothing.

"It hasn't come back. I've been here two days and I've tried. In my room. Alone. Force bolts, shield projections, blade enchantments — gone. Not weak. Gone. Like reaching for a shelf that used to have books on it and finding empty wood."

She looks at me. The mask cracked. Not the hairline fracture from the bridge. A real crack.

"I took the Duskfen contract because I thought if I got back into real danger, the magic would have to come back. Survival instinct." Her voice steady. The steadiness of a rehearsed confession never spoken aloud. "It didn't. Every fight since Duskfen — the Hexcrawlers, the bandits, the bridge — I fought like a soldier. Because it's all I have left."

She looks at the brass token in my hand.

"I earned that certification with magic I can't do anymore."

She looks at me.

"You know why. Don't you."

---

"Yes, Sera. I understand you're guarded." I stare into her eyes. "Maybe all you need for the magic to come back is for something magical to happen. I know we don't have the privacy of your room and all, but I'll be quick. With your permission, I just need to rub your seal for a moment."

The journey her face takes starts at raw vulnerability, passes through disbelief, accelerates through fury, and arrives at a destination without a name. The color doesn't rise. It *detonates*.

"You — I — that's not —"

A student glances over. Sera's death-glare. The student walks faster.

"I am going to kill you. Not today. Today I need you alive. But it's on the calendar."

She stares at her palm. Then extends her left forearm. Pushes the bracer back. The Combat Mage seal — smaller than Elara's, different insignia, same Academy mark.

"Do it. And if you make one more comment about rubbing anything, I will break your hand."

---

I take her forearm. Fingers find the seal. Magic-warm. Wrong-warm.

The ember touches it.

The same surface. Certification matrix. Aldwin's signature in arcane shorthand. Clean on top.

Underneath — the thread. The same entropy parasite. The same encoding. But *worse*. The certification seal has more surface area than a graduation seal. More integration points. The parasite has a wider pipe. And it's been draining for three years.

The shelf with empty books. The magic that won't come back. The thread didn't diminish it. It *emptied* it. The slow bleed became a hemorrhage because the wider seal gave the parasite more to hold onto.

Sera's magical essence isn't atrophied. It's been *stolen*.

The ember flares. Hotter than Elara's reading. Because this isn't an academic researcher. It's the woman who taught me the cage. Who planned the boulder ambush. Who carried me out of a cellar. Who watched from a colonnade she wasn't supposed to be on and said *you earned it*.

The creation energy pushes against the thread. Hard. The thread flinches. The drain stutters. Stops.

Sera gasps.

Her free hand grabs my wrist. Eyes wide. Not pain — the opposite. The absence of something she'd carried so long she forgot it was there.

"Kenji — what — *what is that* —"

Her right hand comes up. Palm open. A flicker. Faint. Blue-white. The ghost of a force bolt that hasn't existed in two years. It sparks once. Twice. Gutters. Dies.

But it was *there*.

The thread reasserts. The drain resumes. The flicker goes dark.

Sera stares at her empty palm.

"It was there," she whispers. "For a second. It was there."

She looks at me. The mask gone. Completely. Someone younger underneath. Someone who went to an Academy and learned to throw force bolts and believed in what she was becoming.

"What's happening to me, Kenji?"

---

"Nothing. I just have that effect on women. Now that I got you off, I need a plan to get into sub-basement three this evening. Your plans are way better than mine, so... get to planning."

The words land like a grenade in a library.

"You — did you just — *got me off*?!"

A student turns. Sera's death-glare. The student accelerates.

"The calendar is getting crowded, Kenji." Her voice is flat. The flatness of a woman updating a list she started ten minutes ago.

She stares at her empty palm. The two seconds she was whole. Given and taken away by the man grinning at her on a fountain.

The fist closes. The jaw sets. The machine clicks into place.

"Sub-basement three. Tonight. You're insane." She looks at the Academy. "But you're not wrong."

She's already working. Eyes moving across architecture.

"Give me what you know."

I give her what I have. Sub-basement two manufactures nighthollow. Level three below that — restricted, never accessed. Elara's tried and failed.

"I've been mapping internal security for two days. Guards work shifts — four main gate, two ground floor, two sub-basement entrance on the west side. Shift change at midnight. Six-minute window — outgoing pair walks up, incoming walks down. Nobody watching anything."

She draws on the wet stone.

"That's your window into level one. Research resident with a Combat Mage token and a confident walk bluffs past questions."

Second line. "Level two. Ward locks. Keyed to faculty. You can't pick those." She looks at me. "But Elara can."

Third line. "Level three. Unknown. No guard rotation — either they don't know it exists or access is controlled by something other than guards."

Two approaches. The bluff through the front using the shift change. Or the crawl — ventilation shafts, drainage channels, ley conduit maintenance passages. Three hundred years of infrastructure running through the building's bones.

"Both need Elara for level two. Both need me because you're a combat mage, not a thief."

One condition.

"When we find what's down there — whatever is feeding on three hundred and forty-seven people including *me* — you tell me everything. Whatever Elara told you in that warded room. Whatever the ember showed you. Everything."

Dark eyes. Steady. The crack visible.

"I've earned that. On a bridge and in a cellar and on a colonnade I wasn't supposed to be on."

---

"Alright. Option two — the crawl. We all meet down there. Honestly I was barely paying attention to Elara. She asked me to touch her seal and she started moaning and gasping... just like you a few seconds ago. I'll have Elara give you a recap during the crawl."

I hop off the fountain. Walk away before Sera can kill me.

"She was NOT — I did NOT — that is not what *gasping* —"

I'm already walking. The Windrunner Boots carrying me at a pace that's technically not running.

"KENJI."

I don't turn around. The predator's eyes catch her reflection in a window — fists clenched, color visible from thirty feet, mouth forming words the courtyard should never absorb.

A student near the fountain looks at Sera. Looks at me. Quietly closes his textbook and relocates.

I make it around the corner before the laugh comes. Real. Full. The first from pure joy since the mud.

The ember pulses. Warm. Amused. Even creation has a sense of humor.

---

Evening. The Silver Draft. Shepherd's pie. A second shepherd's pie. The boy has stopped making eye contact.

Garrett's out — a note says *Following a lead on guild contracts. Back by morning. Don't do anything stupid without me.* He knows me well enough already.

Edwyn's in his room. The chemical tang of bittershade through his door.

The evening darkens. Eyes of the Hunter fades. The world goes normal. Arcane Stride fades. Normal speed. Normal weight. Just a man in a room.

Not for long.

11:45 PM.

Gear up. Light configuration — no chest piece, no shoulder guards. Underlayer and bracers only. Windrunner Boots. Satchel of Holding cross-body. Returning Somnus Knife on belt. Lockpick set. Flint stones. Hand axe — compact, concealable. Longsword left behind — too conspicuous underground.

Two spells in the dark.

Eyes of the Hunter. The world sharpens. Twelve hours.

Arcane Stride. Silver hum in the legs. Twelve hours.

Back door. Alley. The Academy district at midnight — empty streets, closed shops, the pale tower catching moonlight. The ley web pulsing beneath the cobblestones.

---

The Academy's west side. A service door half-hidden behind an ivy-covered buttress.

Two shapes in the shadows.

Sera. Dark leathers. Sword on hip. Hair pulled back. The face of a woman on a mission.

Elara. Almost unrecognizable. Dark trousers, fitted jacket, soft-soled boots. Hair hidden under dark cloth. She looks like a woman who planned to never do this and prepared for it anyway.

"You're late," Sera says.

"I'm two minutes early."

"I've been here twenty." She looks at Elara. "She's been here thirty."

Elara's ink-stained fingers grip a small satchel. Tools clinking.

"The maintenance channel access is through sub-basement two. I open the ward lock. The ley conduit hatch is in the northeast corner — behind the distillation array. The conduit runs vertically through the building's core. It connects every level including—"

She looks down.

"—whatever is below."

Sera looks at me. Then at Elara.

"Did you brief her?"

"I told her to ask you for the details on the way down."

"You told me to ask Elara for a recap."

"I told you Elara was gasping."

Elara's head turns. Slowly. The dark eyes find me.

"He said *what*."

"We should get moving."

END OF CHAPTER ELEVEN — NO LEVEL UP (No Long Rest)

Three people at a door at midnight. Two of them want to kill me. All three of us are about to descend into something that's been feeding on this Academy for twelve years.

The ember burns steady. The creation energy that completed a dying god, tamed a creature of entropy, and gave a woman back two seconds of stolen magic.

Whatever's underneath — it's built from the opposite of what I carry. And unlike the god in the cellar, it didn't get there by accident.

Someone put it there. And someone has been feeding it.

Time to find out who.
