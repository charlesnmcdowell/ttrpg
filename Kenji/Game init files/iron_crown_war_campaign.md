# THE IRON CROWN WAR — Campaign Bible
## Books 3-4: The Sundered Gate → The Iron Crown War
### Level 20-40 | Extended Territory | Kingdom Warfare

---

## CAMPAIGN OVERVIEW

The Sundered Gate is sealed. The western territories are at peace. Then Dren's whisper stone goes dead for three days. When it reconnects, one word: **"Ironholt is under siege."**

The Dominion of Vyranthos — a militaristic empire from the eastern continent — has crossed the Ashenmere Strait and landed an invasion force in the eastern territories. Their Emperor-Mage, **Malcus Vyr**, detected the ley shockwave from the Confluence Lens activation. A permanent ley seal visible across the entire network. To Vyranthos, that's not a repair — it's a weapon demonstration. The western territories just announced they have the power to reshape ley networks at continental scale. Vyranthos intends to claim that power or destroy it.

**Campaign objective:** Defend the allied territories. Push the Dominion back. Conquer or force surrender of the Vyranthos invasion force — and eventually, the Dominion itself.

**Campaign does not end until:** The Dominion of Vyranthos surrenders unconditionally, or Kenji conquers their capital city of Vyranth.

## WORLD MAP — EXTENDED TERRITORIES

### WESTERN TERRITORIES (Kenji's Domain — known)
- **Varenholm** — Academy city. Kenji's base. Portal hub.
- **Duskfen** — Marshland settlement. Portal.
- **Broken Antler** — Guild headquarters. Brindle/Kael. Portal.
- **Ashward Mines** — Mining operation. Portal.
- **Thornwall** — Fortress city. Iron Coliseum. Portal.
- **Crestfall** — Trade city. Garrett's base. Portal.
- **Bleakmoor Crater** — Former gate site. Sealed. Wardbreaker Camp. Portal.
- **Mordecai Ridge** — Confluence Lens site (destroyed). Portal.

### EASTERN TERRITORIES (New — Dren's region)
- **Ironholt** — Dren's Place of Power. Fortified city. Population 1,200. Ley Core. Under siege at campaign start.
- **Ashenmere** — Port city on the eastern coast. The Strait crossing. First city to fall to Vyranthos. Trade hub, shipyards, naval power.
- **Thornkeep** — Border fortress between western and eastern territories. Choke point. Neutral garrison — will side with whoever holds it.
- **Grimhollow** — Deep forest settlement. Deepwood Confederacy border. Elven trade relations. Logging, herbalism, beast-taming.
- **Stormhaven** — Coastal fortress. Southern coast. Naval academy. The Dominion's secondary landing site.

### THE DOMINION OF KAELTHORNE (Enemy territory — eastern continent)
- **Vyranth** — Capital. The Iron Throne. Emperor-Mage's seat. Ley Forge powering the Dominion's war machine.
- **Kharst** — Military staging ground. Where the invasion force launched.
- **The Ashenmere Strait** — 40 miles of open water between continents. Vyranthos controls the crossing.
- **Veilwatch** — Dominion intelligence headquarters. Spy network.
- **The Crucible** — Dominion training facility. Where their transported fighters are tested, broken, and rebuilt.

### THE FREEHOLD MARCHES (Contested border region)
- **Brackenmoor** — Swamp fortress. Independent. Holds mercenary contracts from both sides.
- **Cinderpeak** — Volcanic highlands. Rich in rare minerals. Contested mining territory. Home to Zarek Ashborne.

### THE DEEPWOOD CONFEDERACY (Elven nation — potential ally)
- **Silvandris** — Capital. Ancient. The Eldest's seat. Neutral in human wars for centuries. Has their own transported champion.
- **The Rootways** — Underground network beneath the Deepwood. Living tunnels. Elven military highway.

## THE TRANSPORTED — LITRPG PROTAGONIST ANALOGUES

Every transported individual arrived in this world through the same mechanism — the ley network pulling souls from other realities during moments of extreme magical flux. Kenji arrived during the Duskfen anomaly. Dren arrived years earlier during a ley convergence at what became Ironholt. Others arrived at different times, in different places, with different gifts.

They are all Level 20+ at campaign start. They are all dangerous. Not all are allies.

### 2. JACE CORWIN — THE OUTWORLD TRICKSTER
**Analogue:** Jason Asano (He Who Fights With Monsters)
**Level:** 22 | **CR:** 15

**Origin:** Australian. Arrived 3 years ago in the Freehold Marches. No combat experience. No magic affinity. Found an ancient affliction-class grimoire in a ruin that nobody else could read because it was written in English.

**Build:** Affliction specialist. Damage-over-time. Debuffs. Conditions that stack and compound. His magic doesn't hit hard — it makes everything worse, slowly, inevitably. Enemies rot. Buffs corrode. Healing reverses. He's the anti-healer. 

**Fighting style:** Never fights fair. Never fights directly. Talks constantly during combat — not taunts, *narration*. Describes what his afflictions are doing to his enemy's body in clinical detail while dodging. Infuriating.

**Stats:**
- HP: 95 | AC: 16 (enchanted longcoat, mobility-focused)
- STR: 8 (-1) | DEX: 18 (+4) | CON: 14 (+2)
- INT: 20 (+5) | WIS: 14 (+2) | CHA: 20 (+5)
- Attack: +11 (spell attack) | Save DC: 19
- Speed: 40ft (enhanced by gear)

**Key abilities:**
- **Creeping Doom** — Touch range. Target makes CON save DC 19 or takes 2d8 necrotic per round, escalating +1d8 each round. Lasts until cured or target drops. No concentration.
- **Essence Drain** — Ranged 60ft. Target's healing is reversed for 1 minute — any HP restoration deals damage instead. WIS save DC 19.
- **Miasma** — 30ft radius. All enemies: disadvantage on CON saves, -2 to all saves, attacks against Jace have disadvantage. 1 minute. Concentration.
- **Parasitic Link** — Bonus action. 60ft. Links two enemies. Damage dealt to one is split 50/50 to both. Duration 1 minute.
- **Condition Cascade** — Reaction. When an enemy fails a save against any condition, Jace can inflict a second condition of his choice (from: poisoned, blinded, deafened, frightened, slowed). No additional save.
- **Silver tongue.** CHA 20. Talks his way into and out of everything. Makes enemies question their life choices mid-fight.

**Personality:**
- Funny. Relentlessly, inappropriately funny. Uses humor as armor. The worse the situation, the more jokes. 
- Deeply moral underneath. Won't let innocents die for strategy. This makes him both reliable and inconvenient.
- Hates authority. Distrusts kings, lords, and ArchMaguses on principle. Kenji will need to earn his respect, not command it.
- Australian idioms nobody in this world understands. "She'll be right." "No worries." "That's cooked."

**Location at campaign start:** Brackenmoor. Running a clinic for plague victims. The plague is Vyranthos bioweapon. Jace recognized the magical signature because his affliction magic uses the same principles — and he's *furious*.

**Relationship to Kenji:** Hasn't met him. Has heard of the "Wizard King." Thinks it sounds "cooked as hell."

### 3. CARRICK DUNNE — THE CRAWLER
**Analogue:** Carl (Dungeon Crawler Carl)
**Level:** 23 | **CR:** 16

**Origin:** American. IT worker. Arrived 5 years ago. Dropped into a dungeon — literally. Spent the first two years underground, clearing floors, gaining levels, surviving by improvisation and sheer stubbornness. Emerged from the Ashenmere Deep Dungeon at Level 18 with a talking cat and a reputation.

**Build:** No class. No specialization. Adaptive combatant. Uses everything — traps, environment, enemy abilities turned against them, improvised weapons, social manipulation of dungeon mobs. His "class" is *Survivor*.

**Fighting style:** Dirty. Creative. Never the same trick twice. Will collapse a building on an enemy rather than fight them. Will negotiate with a dungeon boss mid-fight. Will use his own blood as bait for a trap. The kind of fighter who makes experienced warriors uncomfortable because he doesn't follow any rules they recognize.

**Companion:** **Duchess** — A silver-furred cat with telepathic speech, a devastating superiority complex, and the ability to generate sonic blasts from her yowls. CR 8 on her own. Carrick and Duchess function as a unit — she's his scout, his alarm, his conscience, and occasionally his artillery.

**Stats:**
- HP: 140 | AC: 18 (dungeon-forged adaptive armor — changes properties based on threats faced)
- STR: 16 (+3) | DEX: 18 (+4) | CON: 20 (+5)
- INT: 16 (+3) | WIS: 12 (+1) | CHA: 18 (+4)
- Attack: +12 (versatile — melee or ranged depending on current loadout)
- Speed: 35ft

**Key abilities:**
- **Adaptive Armor** — After taking damage from a type, gains resistance to that type for 1 hour. Stacks up to 3 resistances.
- **Dungeon Sense** — Passive. Detects traps, hidden passages, structural weaknesses within 60ft. Cannot be surprised underground.
- **Improvised Lethality** — Any object Carrick wields as a weapon deals 2d8+STR. Chairs, rocks, severed limbs, a cat (with her permission).
- **Collapse Point** — Action. Identifies structural weakness in any building/cave/structure. One strike triggers controlled collapse in 30ft radius. 8d10 bludgeoning. DEX DC 18. 1/long rest.
- **Mob Boss** — Can attempt to negotiate with any intelligent creature mid-combat. CHA check DC = 10 + creature's WIS. On success, creature pauses for one round to hear him out.
- **Duchess Sonic Blast** — Duchess action. 40ft cone. 4d8 thunder. CON DC 16 or deafened + stunned 1 round.

**Personality:**
- Exhausted. Five years of dungeons. He's tired in a way that goes past physical. But he keeps going because stopping means thinking about what he's lost.
- Sarcastic. Dark humor. The humor of a man who has seen too much and processes it through jokes.
- Protective of anyone weaker than him. The dungeon taught him that the strong eat the weak — and he decided to be the exception.
- Duchess does most of his emotional processing for him. She's brutally honest about his feelings in ways he'd never admit.
- Deeply distrustful of systems. Dungeons have rules that seem fair and are designed to kill you. He sees governments and armies the same way.

**Location at campaign start:** Stormhaven. The Dominion just landed their secondary force here. Carrick was eating breakfast at the docks when warships appeared on the horizon. He's evacuating civilians.

**Duchess:** "The fish was excellent. The invasion is inconvenient. Priorities, Charles." (She calls him Charles. His name is Carrick. She doesn't care.)

### 4. ZAREK ASHBORNE — THE EDGEWALKER
**Analogue:** Zac Atwood (Defiance of the Fall)
**Level:** 26 | **CR:** 18

**Origin:** Unknown. Doesn't talk about it. Arrived 4 years ago in Cinderpeak during a volcanic eruption. Walked out of the lava. Something changed him during the crossing — he exists at the boundary between life and death. Dual-natured. Two fighting forms. Two energy systems.

**Build:** Dual-class berserker. **Life Form** — massive HP regeneration, nature-aspected attacks, growth and vitality. **Death Form** — necrotic aura, life drain, withering strikes, undead resilience. He can switch between forms once per combat, or maintain both simultaneously at great physical cost (Hybrid Form — 10 rounds max before system shock).

**Fighting style:** Overwhelming force. A greataxe that's too large for physics and a body that doesn't obey biology. He doesn't outthink enemies — he outsurvives them. Every wound heals. Every death strike empowers him. He fights like a natural disaster with an axe.

**Stats:**
- HP: 280 (Life Form) / 220 (Death Form) / 250 (Hybrid) | AC: 17 (natural + enchanted hide)
- STR: 24 (+7) | DEX: 12 (+1) | CON: 24 (+7)
- INT: 10 (+0) | WIS: 14 (+2) | CHA: 8 (-1)
- Attack: +15 (greataxe, Worldsplitter) | Damage: 2d12+7 base
- Speed: 40ft (Life), 30ft (Death), 50ft (Hybrid)

**Key abilities:**
- **Life Form:** Regen 20 HP/round. Nature damage added to attacks (2d6). Entangling roots on crit (restrained, STR DC 20). Resistant to poison, disease, necrotic.
- **Death Form:** Necrotic aura 15ft (2d8/round to all enemies). Life drain on hit (heal 25% damage dealt). Immune to fear, charm, poison. Vulnerable to radiant.
- **Hybrid Form:** Both active simultaneously. 10 round limit. After: exhaustion 3, cannot fight for 1 hour.
- **Worldsplitter** — Greataxe. 2d12+7. On crit: cleaves through target to next enemy within 5ft (second attack roll). Can split stone, metal, and magical barriers.
- **Evolutionary Cascade** — Passive. Each enemy killed in combat grants +1 to all attack rolls for the remainder of the encounter. Caps at +5.
- **Undying** — 1/day. When HP hits 0, instead drops to 1 HP and enters Death Form if not already in it. All enemies within 30ft take 6d10 necrotic.

**Personality:**
- Quiet. Not mysterious-quiet — empty-quiet. The silence of a man who burned away everything unnecessary.
- Not cruel. Not kind. Practical. He'll save a village because it's in his path. He'll kill a bandit army because they're in his way. The moral calculation is simple: does this serve his goal?
- His goal: become strong enough that nothing can threaten what he cares about. The problem is he's not sure what he cares about anymore.
- Respects strength. Will follow a stronger leader without ego. Will leave a weaker one without apology.
- The volcano changed him. He sometimes speaks to something that isn't there — the boundary between life and death isn't metaphorical for Zarek. He can hear it.

**Location at campaign start:** Cinderpeak. The Dominion wants the volcanic minerals. Zarek has been killing their mining expeditions for two weeks. He doesn't care about the war — he cares about his mountain. But the Dominion keeps sending more.

### 5. JAREK THORNVEIL — THE APEX HUNTER
**Analogue:** Jake Thayne (Primal Hunter)
**Level:** 24 | **CR:** 17

**Origin:** Danish. Competitive archer. Arrived 2 years ago in the Deepwood. The forest recognized something in him — a predator's instinct amplified by ley energy into supernatural perception. The elves found him three days after arrival, already hunting creatures twice his level by reading micro-expressions in the way wind moved through leaves.

**Build:** Perception-god archer. His senses operate on a spectrum most mages can't detect. He sees mana flows, predicts movement, reads intent. His arrows don't miss because he fires where the target *will be*, not where it is. Solo hunter. Does not fight in formation because formations are for people who can't see the whole battlefield.

**Fighting style:** Long range. Maximum distance. He prefers kills where the enemy never knows he existed. If forced to melee, he fights with a hunting knife and instinct — dirty, animal, nothing trained. The bow is the weapon. The knife is survival.

**Stats:**
- HP: 105 | AC: 19 (enchanted ranger leathers + preternatural dodge)
- STR: 14 (+2) | DEX: 24 (+7) | CON: 16 (+3)
- INT: 12 (+1) | WIS: 22 (+6) | CHA: 8 (-1)
- Attack: +16 (Apex Bow, ranged) | Damage: 2d8+7 base | Range: 600/1200ft
- Speed: 45ft (enhanced by Deepwood blessing)

**Key abilities:**
- **Apex Perception** — Passive. Cannot be surprised. Sees invisible creatures. Reads intent (hostile/neutral/friendly) at 300ft. Detects lies automatically (WIS-based, contested by CHA).
- **Predator's Mark** — Bonus action. Marks one target. All attacks against marked target: +4 to hit, +2d8 damage, advantage on tracking. Lasts until target dies or Jarek marks another. 
- **Mana Arrow** — Channels ley energy into arrow. +4d10 force damage. Ignores resistance. Ignores cover. 3/long rest.
- **Instinct Dodge** — Reaction. When targeted by an attack Jarek can see, he can add +6 to AC against that attack. Unlimited uses but only against attacks he's aware of.
- **Killzone** — Action. Fires 4 arrows in one round at up to 4 different targets within range. Each at full attack bonus.
- **The Stillness** — Stealth ability. When Jarek stops moving, he becomes effectively invisible to non-magical detection. Perception DC 30 to spot. Breaks on movement or attack.

**Personality:**
- Lone wolf. Not antisocial — asocial. He doesn't dislike people. He just doesn't need them. The forest is enough.
- Extremely competitive. If someone claims to be the best at anything, Jarek will quietly prove them wrong without announcing it.
- Respects Dren because Dren built something from nothing. Respects Kenji's reputation but hasn't met him.
- Uncomfortable in cities. Indoor spaces make him twitchy. Crowds make him hostile.
- The elves respect him because he hunts the way they do — with patience and precision. He's the first human the Deepwood Confederacy has granted passage rights to in a century.

**Location at campaign start:** The Deepwood border. Dominion scouts have been mapping the Rootways. Jarek has been killing them one by one. The elves haven't asked him to. He's doing it because they're in his forest.

### 6. MIRA ASHVEIL — THE UNKILLABLE
**Analogue:** Ilea (Azarinth Healer)
**Level:** 27 | **CR:** 19

**Origin:** German. Paramedic. Arrived 6 years ago — the earliest of the transported after Dren. Appeared in a battlefield between two mercenary companies. Healed everyone on both sides without asking which side was which. Both companies tried to recruit her. She beat the recruiters unconscious and walked into the wilderness.

**Build:** Healer-tank hybrid. She heals by hitting things. She tanks by healing herself faster than anything can damage her. Her magic is restoration-aspected but delivered through fists — every punch repairs her body and damages the target. She's the paradox: the healer who fights on the front line, and the front liner who never dies.

**Fighting style:** In your face. Always. She closes distance, gets hit, heals through it, and punches until something stops moving. No range. No subtlety. No strategy beyond "be tougher than whatever is trying to kill me." It works because she IS tougher than whatever is trying to kill her.

**Stats:**
- HP: 200 | AC: 20 (ash-forged gauntlets + body enhancement magic)
- STR: 20 (+5) | DEX: 16 (+3) | CON: 22 (+6)
- INT: 10 (+0) | WIS: 18 (+4) | CHA: 12 (+1)
- Attack: +14 (unarmed, ash-infused strikes) | Damage: 2d10+5 per hit
- Speed: 50ft (body enhancement)

**Key abilities:**
- **Sentinel Reconstruction** — Passive. Regenerates 30 HP/round. Cannot be reduced below 1 HP by any single hit (overkill protection).
- **Ash Infusion** — All unarmed strikes deal additional 2d6 fire + heal Jessica for 50% of total damage dealt. Stacks with Reconstruction.
- **Reversal** — Reaction. When struck in melee, Jessica can immediately strike back (1 unarmed attack) before damage resolves. The counter-hit heals her for the amount she's about to take. If the heal exceeds the damage, she takes zero.
- **Destruction Sphere** — Action. 20ft radius centered on self. 6d10 fire+force. DEX DC 20. Jessica takes no damage. 1/short rest.
- **Absolute Restoration** — Action. Touch. Heals 100 HP. Removes all conditions, diseases, curses. Regrows lost limbs. 1/long rest.
- **Pain Threshold** — Passive. Immune to stun, unconscious, and incapacitation effects. She fights until her HP hits 0. Nothing short of death stops her.

**Personality:**
- Direct. Blunt. No filter. Says exactly what she thinks at the volume she thinks it.
- Doesn't understand social hierarchy. Lords, kings, ArchMaguses — she treats everyone the same. Which means she'll punch a king in the face if he says something stupid.
- Fiercely protective of healers and medical personnel. Anyone who targets a healer in her presence gets her *undivided attention*.
- She's been alone for six years because nobody can keep up with her. She fights alone because partners slow her down.
- Secretly lonely. Won't admit it. The paramedic who heals everyone else and never asks for help.

**Location at campaign start:** Ironholt. She arrived two days before the siege began. She was passing through — heading west, actually, chasing rumors of the "Wizard King" who heals people by touching them. She wanted to meet the only other healer-fighter in the world. Instead she got a siege. She's on Dren's wall punching Dominion soldiers off ladders.

### 7. AUREN GREYHOLM — THE TWICE-BORN KING
**Analogue:** Arthur Leywin (The Beginning After the End)
**Level:** 28 | **CR:** 20

**Origin:** This one is different. Auren didn't arrive from another world — he was *reborn* into this one. In his previous life, he was a king of a now-destroyed kingdom on the eastern continent. He died defending his throne against the Dominion's grandfather-emperor two hundred years ago. The ley network preserved his soul. He was reborn as a child in Silvandris, the Deepwood capital, thirty years ago. He remembers everything.

**Build:** Sword-mage supreme. Two centuries of martial arts mastery combined with elven aether-magic training. He fights with a blade in one hand and spells in the other, seamlessly, like breathing. Every swing is enhanced. Every spell is delivered through steel. He is what Kenji could become if Kenji had two hundred years of practice.

**Fighting style:** Perfect. Technical perfection earned over two lifetimes. No wasted movement. No wasted energy. Every action is the optimal action. This makes him devastating and deeply boring to watch — there's no drama in a fight where one side has already calculated every outcome.

**Stats:**
- HP: 170 | AC: 23 (aether-forged blade-dancer armor + enhanced reflexes)
- STR: 18 (+4) | DEX: 20 (+5) | CON: 16 (+3)
- INT: 22 (+6) | WIS: 20 (+5) | CHA: 16 (+3)
- Attack: +17 (Dawn Edge, longsword + spell channeling) | Damage: 2d8+4 + 2d6 radiant base
- Spell Save DC: 22 | Spell Attack: +14
- Speed: 60ft (aether step)

**Key abilities:**
- **Dawn Edge** — Longsword. 2d8+4 slashing + 2d6 radiant on every hit. Can channel any spell through the blade (spell delivered on hit, no separate attack roll).
- **Aether Cloak** — Passive. All spells cost 1 slot level lower (minimum 1). Cantrips enhanced to 1st level power.
- **King's Domain** — Action. 60ft radius. All allies: +2 AC, +2 saves, advantage vs fear. All enemies: -2 attack, -2 saves. Concentration. 1 minute.
- **Reincarnation Memory** — Passive. Cannot be deceived by any illusion, enchantment, or disguise. He's seen it all before. Literally.
- **Absolute Counter** — Reaction. Counterspell at will, no slot cost. Can counter up to 6th level automatically. 7+ requires INT check DC 10 + spell level.
- **Two Lifetimes** — 1/day. Auren draws on his past life's combat experience. For 1 minute: advantage on all attacks, all saves, and all ability checks. Extra attack (3 total per round). This is what two centuries of skill looks like compressed into sixty seconds.

**Personality:**
- Old soul. Literally. He speaks like a man who has lived too long and remembers too much.
- Kind but distant. The distance of someone who has lost an entire kingdom and everyone he loved and then had to grow up again knowing what loss feels like.
- The Deepwood Confederacy doesn't know what he is. The Eldest suspects. The elves raised him as one of their own. He loves them for it and carries the guilt of a secret he'll have to reveal.
- He views the Dominion as personal. Their grandfather-emperor killed him. Their current emperor is finishing the conquest his ancestor started. This war is Auren's unfinished business from two centuries ago.
- He is the most dangerous individual combatant on the continent. And he's spent thirty years hiding it.

**Location at campaign start:** Silvandris. The Eldest has called a war council. For the first time in centuries, the Deepwood Confederacy is considering entering a human war. Auren is the reason. He just told the Eldest who he really is.

## NATIVE NEW CHARACTERS (13)

### DOMINION ANTAGONISTS (5)

**8. EMPEROR VAREK SOL — THE IRON CROWN**
Ruler of the Dominion of Vyranthos. Level 30. The most powerful war-mage alive. Wears the Iron Crown — a ley-forged artifact that lets him channel the Dominion's entire ley network through himself. On his throne in Vyranth, he is functionally a god. In the field, he's merely the most dangerous mage on the continent.

Not evil. Expansionist. He genuinely believes unifying the continent under Vyranthos's system will save more lives than the chaos of independent territories. He's wrong, but he's wrong with conviction.

**9. GENERAL KATYA VOSS — THE IRON FIST**
Dominion invasion force commander. Level 22. Non-magical. Pure military. She commands 12,000 soldiers through discipline, logistics, and an understanding of terrain that borders on supernatural. She broke three kingdoms before this one. Cold. Professional. Respects worthy enemies. Executes unworthy ones.

**10. ARCHON THESSIK — THE MIND**
Dominion intelligence chief. Level 20. Telepath. Runs the spy network from Veilwatch. Never seen in person. Communicates through agents, puppets, and infiltrators. Every secret the western territories have, Velnis already knows — except the things protected by clarity pendants and ley-wards.

**11. WARDEN KORR — THE WALL**
Dominion siege specialist. Level 21. Built the war machines. Enchanted the siege towers. Designed the formation that broke Ashenmere's walls in four hours. A dwarf. The only non-human in Dominion high command. Brilliant, bitter, and loyal to Malcus Vyr because the Emperor is the first human ruler who judged him by his work.

**12. BLADE PRINCE SAELEN — THE HEIR**
Malcus Vyr's son. Level 19. Young. Ambitious. Sent to lead the Stormhaven landing to prove himself. He's talented — genuinely talented — but untested against anyone at Kenji's level. His father sent him to the secondary front because failure at Stormhaven is survivable. Failure at Ironholt is not.

### ALLIED / NEUTRAL CHARACTERS (8)

**13. THE ELDEST — DEEPWOOD SOVEREIGN**
Ruler of the Deepwood Confederacy. Ageless. Elven. Level unknown — the Eldest doesn't fight. She *decides*. Her word moves ten thousand elven warriors. Her neutrality has lasted centuries. Breaking it requires something unprecedented. Auren Greyholm just provided it.

**14. COMMANDER RENNA THORNE — THORNKEEP GARRISON**
Commander of the border fortress. Level 16. Professional soldier. Neutral — she serves whoever holds Thornkeep, and right now nobody does. Both sides are courting her. She'll side with whoever proves they can hold the border. Pragmatic. Scarred. Forty years old and tired of politics.

**15. TORVALD IRONHAND — BOUNTY GUILD MASTER**
Kenji's bounty runner from Book 1 — promoted. Now managing the Vanguard Hall operations across three cities. Level 12. Not a fighter — an administrator. But he knows everyone, hears everything, and can mobilize fifty bounty hunters within a day.

**16. THESSALY VORN — FIELD ARCHIVIST (PROMOTED)**
Thessaly. New role — Chief Field Archivist for the war effort. Her ley-reading capability with the Veylan staff makes her the only person who can track Dominion troop movements through their ley-signature. She's not a combatant. She's the most important intelligence asset in the allied force.

**17. CAPTAIN HARSK — THE BRIDGE**
From Book 1 — Brindle's bridge lieutenant. Now running the Broken Antler waypoint as a legitimate trade crossing and military checkpoint. Level 8. Not a main combatant but controls the critical supply line between western and eastern territories.

**18. MORDECAI — THE ARCHITECT**
Former enemy, current neutral ally. His Confluence Lens methodology is the only known way to manipulate ley networks at continental scale. The Dominion wants him. Badly. If Vyranthos captures Mordecai, they can weaponize the ley network. He's the war's most valuable non-combatant.

**19. KEX — DREN'S DRAKE (EVOLVED)**
Dren's drake companion. Evolved during the siege — the Ironholt Core fed him. Now CR 14 on his own. Telepathic link with Dren. Psi-blasts. Flight. The size of a horse. The intelligence of a tactician. Kex doesn't just fight — he coordinates Ironholt's aerial defense.

**20. QUEEN LIRIEN — THE DEEPWOOD WAR-SINGER**
Auren Greyholm's elven mother (adoptive). Level 18. Aether-voice — she casts through song. Healing, buffs, battlefield control. Her war-songs can rally a broken army or shatter a siege tower's enchantments. She doesn't know Auren is a reincarnated king. She just knows her son is volunteering for a human war and she's not letting him go alone.

## CAMPAIGN STRUCTURE

### ACT 1 — THE SIEGE (Levels 20-24)
- Break the Ironholt siege
- Secure the Thornkeep border fortress
- Recruit the first transported allies (Jessica at Ironholt, Jace at Brackenmoor)
- Establish eastern portal network
- First encounter with General Katya Voss

### ACT 2 — THE COALITION (Levels 24-28)
- Recruit the Deepwood Confederacy (Auren Greyholm's revelation)
- Liberate Stormhaven (Carrick and Blade Prince Drazen)
- Secure Cinderpeak (Zarek Ashborne)
- Find Jarek Windmere in the Deepwood
- Build the allied army: constructs + mercs + elves + transported
- Naval battle for the Ashenmere Strait

### ACT 3 — THE CROSSING (Levels 28-32)
- Cross the Ashenmere Strait
- Establish beachhead on eastern continent
- First encounter with Emperor Malcus Vyr (he fights in person — Level 30)
- Siege of Kharst (Dominion staging ground)
- Archon Velnis's spy network must be dismantled

### ACT 4 — THE IRON THRONE (Levels 32-40)
- March on Vyranth
- The Iron Crown's true power revealed — Malcus Vyr channels the continent's ley network
- Final battle: allied force vs Dominion at full strength
- Kenji vs Malcus Vyr — Level 40 vs Level 30+Iron Crown
- Conquest or surrender

Kenji is in the Silver Draft. Day 30. Morning. The Confluence Lens just fired. The world is at peace.

Dren's whisper stone goes silent.

Three days later it reconnects. Static. Interference. Kex's telepathic signal bleeds through — panicked, broken, a drake screaming across a psychic link that's being jammed.

One word from Dren. Flat. The Lord of Ironholt doesn't waste words on fear.

**"Come."**

The Ironholt siege clock starts ticking.
