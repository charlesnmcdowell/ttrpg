## Campaign Bible: Five Threats, One Kingdom, No King
### Level 35-40 | Continental Scale | Kingdom Survival

## CAMPAIGN OVERVIEW

Six months ago, the man who held the kingdom together walked away. The portals still hum. The constructs still patrol. The inns still profit. But the center is missing. Vess runs governance. Katya runs the army. Pip runs the economy. Garrett runs the paperwork. They've been holding the machine together with competence and duct tape — but the world doesn't stay still because the Wizard King retired.

Five threats have been building while Kenji picked berries and trained bears. Each one targets the coalition he built. Each one has a ticking clock. Each one will destroy a piece of the kingdom if left unchecked. And the man who could stop them is sitting in a dying village under a fake name, pretending to be nobody.

**Campaign triggers when:** Kenji's identity or location is exposed. The mechanism doesn't matter — name recognition, power display, ember use, someone puts the pieces together. The moment the world knows where Kenji is, everything converges. Bane of Eve activates. The baby mamas come looking. The kingdom's stewards come begging. And five existential threats come knocking because the one thing keeping them cautious — the legend of the Wizard King — just got a confirmed address.

**Campaign ends when:** All five threats are resolved (destroyed, negotiated, contained) OR the kingdom falls. Partial victories are possible — save three territories, lose two. The coalition fractures but survives. The world is different either way.

**Kenji hits Level 40 — Apotheosis — during this campaign.** ~230,000 EXP remaining at Ch18 close (sync `kenji_state.json` **exp**). Five threats provide the content. Bane of Eve provides daily supplemental EXP. The cap is the cap. What happens after Level 40 is the epilogue.

Each arc states **core problem**, **regional stakes if unresolved**, and **plot-driving NPC agendas** (including actors who **profit from a bad outcome**). Not every arc ties back to the Hollowing; when a bible threat applies, link it **once** instead of duplicating encyclopedic lore.

## THE FIVE THREATS

Each threat operates independently. Each has its own ticking clock. They are NOT coordinated (at first). They are five separate problems that happen to converge because the world smells weakness when the king disappears.

The DM runs all five clocks simultaneously. Kenji chooses which to address. Neglected threats escalate. Resolved threats free resources for others. The player cannot do all five sequentially — some will reach critical while he's handling others. Triage is the game.

### THREAT 1: THE HOLLOWING
**Location:** Kharn-Dural — Dwarven Undermountain (NEW TERRITORY — northwest)
**Race:** Dwarves, deep gnomes, duergar, and what they dug into
**Clock:** 60 days to breach | +2%/day | Current: 15%

The dwarves of Kharn-Dural have been mining for eight centuries. Their undermountain is the largest subterranean city on the continent — 40,000 dwarves, two miles deep, built around a vein of mythril so pure it sings when you strike it. Three months ago, the deep miners broke through the floor of the lowest shaft and found something underneath.

Not a cave. Not a void. A **prison**.

Something was sealed beneath Kharn-Dural before the dwarves arrived. Before the mountain existed. A pre-civilizational entity called **The Fathom** — a living absence. Not undead. Not demon. A thing that exists by *unmaking*. Where it touches stone, the stone forgets it was stone. Where it touches flesh, the flesh forgets it was alive. It doesn't kill. It *erases*.

The dwarves sealed the breach with everything they had — mythril gates, runic wards, the combined stonecraft of eight centuries. It's holding. Barely. The Fathom is patient. It presses against the seal the way water presses against a dam — constant, mindless, inevitable. Every day the wards weaken. The mythril sings a different note. The deep miners report hearing *nothing* from below — not silence, but the sound of sound being eaten.

**King Thorgrim Ironvault** — Level 22 Runesmith. Ruler of Kharn-Dural. Eight hundred years old. He's the one who ordered the dig. He's the one who broke the floor. He carries that weight like a crown made of guilt. He sent emissaries to every surface nation requesting aid. Most ignored him — dwarven problems stay underground. The coalition received his letter. Vess filed it. Garrett flagged it. Nobody acted because nobody was authorized to commit resources without the ArchMagus.

**The threat:** If the seal breaks, The Fathom rises. It doesn't conquer — it unmakes. The undermountain collapses. 40,000 dwarves die or flee to the surface. The Fathom spreads through the tunnel network — and Kharn-Dural's tunnels connect to the Ashward Mine system. The corruption reaches coalition territory within weeks.

**Book 4 table layer (Kharn — player-facing):** On the surface of the mountain, the crisis presents as a **volatile master forge** in the **deep works**: accidents escalating, **quarantine**, **economic shock** (metalware prices surge), **rumor suppression** with harsh penalties, and **Iron Mule / factor** muscle called north—while **banditry** still torches trains (**western cut**). Kenji enters via **Mursha** (buyout, bond) and **survivor beats**, then gains **classified** forge picture through **paid intel + leverage** (e.g. **Haldra tube**, **Tamsin** escrow—see `arcs/north_relay_two_chapter_plan.md` § **Kharn forge crisis — authoritative table spine**). **Mursha’s motive** includes **forcing the quarantine truth**, not only escort duty. **Oathbreaker** and Kenji’s deeper inventory tie into the **same death–creation artifact web** as southern undead arcs—often **before Kenji understands what he carries**. Revelation should land **at** the forge or **after** loss/confiscation; either path advances **Threat 1**.

**What Kenji can do:** The Fathom is a ley-entity — it exists in the space between ley lines. Ember magic (creation energy) is its antithesis. Kenji can reinforce the seal, but only if he uses the ember. Which means breaking his cover. Alternatively, Amaris's Bloom Purge might stabilize the ley lines feeding the prison — buying decades instead of months. But she'd need to go two miles underground into a dwarven mountain.

**Key NPCs:**
- **King Thorgrim Ironvault** — Runesmith, Level 22. Proud. Guilty. Will kneel to anyone who can save his people. Has never knelt.
- **Brunhild Deepdelve** — Head Miner, Level 18. She was on the drill team that broke through. She heard The Fathom's non-sound first. She hasn't slept in three months. She knows the tunnels better than anyone alive.
- **Grimjaw** — Duergar exile, Level 20. Lives in the upper tunnels. Trades with the surface. He's been feeling The Fathom's pressure through the stone for weeks. He knows something the dwarves don't — there's a second breach forming in the eastern shaft. He'll trade the information for citizenship.

### THREAT 2: THE PALLID MARCH
**Location:** The Ashenveil — Dead Marshlands (NEW TERRITORY — southeast)
**Race:** Undead — skeletons, revenants, wraiths, death knights, a lich
**Clock:** 45 days to the March | +3%/day | Current: 20%

The Ashenveil has always been haunted. A swamp the size of a small country, perpetually grey, where the dead don't stay buried. For centuries it was contained — the undead were mindless, the swamp was impassable, and nobody lived close enough to care.

That changed. Something woke up in the center of the Ashenveil — a **Living Lich** known to her enemies as **the Lych** and to her followers as **their Lady**. She signs border-proclamations as simply **"— the Lady."** (Her true name — Lady Nyx — is a reveal beat, gated to Kenji alone and only at neutral-or-higher disposition. See `character_tracker.md` for full alias rules.) She was a Warden-elect who chose the Living Lich path in 1042 AR — alive with full dominion over death. Not undead. Real heartbeat, real warmth. She holds a lich's power (undead army, territorial control, phylactery-equivalent bronze ring with 4 seams) without crossing the threshold into undeath. She's been ley-suspended in the weeping elm grove (4 miles east of Millhaven) at 1% metabolism, gathering power and anchoring 612 bodies along a 32-mile perimeter.

Now she's awake. And she's organized.

The mindless undead aren't mindless anymore. Skeleton formations march in ranks. Wraiths scout the borders. Death knights — the Lych's personal guard, each one a fallen hero she raised and bound — lead assault columns. The swamp is no longer a graveyard. It's a staging ground.

**The Lych's goal:** She doesn't want the kingdom. She wants the ley network. The coalition's 13 portals are ley-anchored. She can feel them from the Ashenveil — thirteen points of concentrated life energy that she can siphon to fuel mass resurrection. She wants to raise an army of ten thousand and march it north into the coalition's southern territories. She calls it **The Pallid March**.

**The threat:** The March launches in 45 days. Her advance columns are already testing the southern borders — Stormhaven and Thornkeep report "unusual undead activity" that Renna Hale is logging with increasing alarm. When the full March launches, it hits Thornkeep first. Then Stormhaven. Then the southern farmland. The undead don't eat. Don't sleep. Don't stop. Every coalition soldier who falls rises on her side.

**What Kenji can do:** Destroy the phylactery-equivalent — the bronze ring with 4 seams. Without it, her power structure collapses and the undead army loses coordination. But the ring's seams are tied to named anchors (one of which, Corban, is unknown in status), and the 4th seam is being parasitized by the death-binder (a second-order antagonist Kenji does not yet know about). Creation energy (the ember) is devastating against undead — but using it means revealing himself. Alternatively, the coalition army under Katya could fight the March conventionally — but every casualty feeds the enemy. **WARNING:** Intimacy with her (human form) triggers Lover's Vigor, which gives her +50% stats for 5 days and accelerates her conquest to 10% of the kingdom per cycle. The romance path IS the threat path.

**Key NPCs:**
- **Lady Nyx / "the Lych" / "their Lady" / "— the Lady" (border signature)** — Living Lich, Warden-Queen of the Pallid March, Level 32. Chaotic Evil. Over 200 years of patience since her 1042 binding. She speaks in complete sentences from a time before contractions existed. She is not evil in the way villagers use the word — she is *finished* with mortality and believes her path is the natural evolution of all things. She'll negotiate. Her terms are unacceptable. See tracker for Vigor consequence mechanics (romance = kingdom conquest accelerator + capture-Kenji loop) and Close to Death contagion (1%/sec intercourse damage; partners rise as thralls — Sir Corwyn is the canonical exception).
- **Sir Corwyn the Fallen** — Death Knight, Level 26. **Lawful Good.** Was a paladin of the Oath of Protection. Seduced by Lady Nyx in her Lady form; his paladin CON resisted Close to Death for 72 continuous hours before he finally died. When he died, his Oath warped rather than broke — the *good* survived intact; only the *law* he served was redirected to the Lady. He retains full paladin class features, his personality, his memories, and the silver-inlay sword Oathbreaker that screams in two signatures (holy + necrotic). He hates what he's become but cannot stop loving the thing that did it to him — that tension is the binding. He protects Lady Nyx the way a paladin protects anyone he has sworn to protect: with every tool he has and no hesitation. **Why he serves:** she is the object his Oath now points at; breaking service means breaking Oath, and the Oath is the only thing keeping him sentient. The chest Kenji stole in Ch5 was his. Contents unknown. If Kenji can break the binding, Corwyn becomes an ally. If not, he's the most dangerous single combatant in her army.
- **Mags** — Halfling grave-robber, Level 8. Lives on the edge of the Ashenveil. Tiny. Foul-mouthed. Has been looting the swamp for twenty years. She knows every path, every sinking pit, every safe camp. She is the guide. She charges ten gold a day and she's worth fifty.
- **Corporal Jostin** — Thornkeep garrison, Level 10. Renna Hale's best scout. He's been watching the southern swamp border and his reports are getting shorter and more frightened. The last one said: "They're practicing formations."

### THREAT 3: THE RED COURT
**Location:** Ashenmere — Port City (EXISTING TERRITORY — eastern coast)
**Race:** Vampires, thralls, doppelgangers, corrupted nobility
**Clock:** 90 days to takeover | +1%/day | Current: 30%

Ashenmere was the first city to fall during the Vyranth invasion. It was liberated. It was rebuilt. But something moved into the power vacuum during reconstruction.

The **Red Court** is a vampire coven that has existed for centuries — hidden, patient, embedded in the aristocracies of a dozen cities. They don't conquer. They *replace*. Doppelgangers take the faces of key officials. Vampire lords charm their way into noble houses. Thralls fill the servant class. By the time anyone notices, the city belongs to the Court and every authority figure is either turned, replaced, or blackmailed.

Ashenmere is 30% converted. The Harbor Master is a thrall. Two of the five city council members are doppelgangers. The garrison commander was turned three weeks ago. The trade guild is compromised. The temple has been quietly abandoned — the priest left in the night and nobody asked why.

**Lady Mirenne** — Elder Vampire, Level 28. She's been alive for six hundred years. She looks thirty. She's charming, sophisticated, cultured, and she views mortals the way a farmer views livestock — with a certain professional affection for things that serve a purpose. She doesn't want to destroy Ashenmere. She wants to *own* it. A city that feeds itself to her coven willingly because every person in a position to object has been replaced.

**The threat:** Once Ashenmere falls completely, the Red Court uses the port and the portal to infiltrate the rest of the coalition. Doppelgangers in Varenholm. Thralls in Crestfall. A vampire in the Council chambers. The conquest is silent. No army. No march. Just a slow replacement of every person who matters until the kingdom belongs to the Court and nobody remembers when it changed.

**What Kenji can do:** Root out the infiltration. But the Red Court doesn't fight fair — they fight with faces. The Harbor Master greets you warmly. The council member offers wine. The garrison commander pledges loyalty. Everyone is friendly because the friendly ones are already turned. Kenji's Irresistible Presence is uniquely dangerous here — Lady Mirenne is immune (vampire charm immunity) but every female thrall and doppelganger in the city will be drawn to the ronin, creating chaos in the Court's carefully maintained cover. His presence is a disruption bomb just by walking through the streets.

**Key NPCs:**
- **Lady Mirenne** — Elder Vampire, Level 28. Immune to charm effects. Irresistible Presence does not affect her (undead). She's the most dangerous political opponent Kenji has ever faced — because she'll invite him to dinner and mean it.
- **Sevren** — Doppelganger spymaster, Level 20. Runs the replacement network. Has seventeen faces memorized. Paranoid. Brilliant. The only person who knows the full roster of who's been replaced. If captured alive, he's an intelligence goldmine. He'll try to become Kenji's face if cornered.
- **Captain Halvard** — (no relation to Katya Voss) — Ashenmere garrison, Level 14. Recently turned. He doesn't want to be a vampire. He was bitten three weeks ago and he's fighting the hunger. If Kenji can cure him (creation energy? Bloom Purge? Alchemical?), Halvard becomes an inside man. If not, the hunger wins within a month.
- **Old Tam** — Goblin fence, Level 6. Runs a pawn shop in the Ashenmere docks. Not Court. Not coalition. Just a goblin who buys and sells and notices things. He knows which officials stopped eating garlic. He knows which nobles stopped attending day-court. He knows the temple priest ran because "the altar bled." He'll sell this information for the right price.
- **Brother Morvak** — Orc priest of the Sunfather, Level 12. Runs a mission in the dock district for orc dockworkers and mixed-race poor. His wards have been keeping the vampires out of his block. He's noticed the pattern. He's been documenting it. He's terrified and he's right.

### THREAT 4: THE IRON HORDE
**Location:** The Sunderplains — Open Steppe (NEW TERRITORY — west/northwest)
**Race:** Orcs, goblins, trolls, ogres, half-orcs, worgs, war mammoths
**Clock:** 30 days to invasion | +4%/day | Current: 35%

The Sunderplains are a vast open steppe west of the coalition territories. For centuries, the orc clans fought each other — a hundred tribes, a hundred feuds, a hundred warlords who'd sooner kill their neighbor than march in the same direction. The western border was safe because the orcs were their own worst enemy.

**Warchief Gorath Skullsplitter** changed that. A half-orc with a human mother and an orc father, raised between two worlds that both rejected him. He's smart — strategist smart, Katya-level smart — and he's furious. He unified the clans not through combat but through **shame**. He walked into every tribal council and said the same thing: "The humans built an empire in a month. They have portals. They have glass soldiers. They have a king who conquered a continent. And you — you fight over goat pastures."

It worked. Thirty tribes. 50,000 warriors. War mammoths. Troll shock troops. Goblin sappers. Worg cavalry. The largest orc horde assembled in three centuries, and it's pointed east at the coalition border.

**Gorath's goal:** Not destruction. Recognition. He wants the Sunderplains recognized as a sovereign nation with trade rights, portal access, and a seat at the coalition table. He'll burn the western territories to the ground to get that seat because he's learned from human history that nobody negotiates with orcs until the orcs make them.

**The threat:** The Horde hits the western border in 30 days. Thornwall is first — Katya's military HQ. If Thornwall falls, the army loses its staging ground and the western territories collapse. Katya has 12,000 troops. Gorath has 50,000. The math doesn't work.

**What Kenji can do:** Fight. Or talk. Gorath is the rarest thing in orcish politics — a warlord who'd prefer a treaty to a battle. He unified through shame, not bloodlust. If Kenji can offer recognition, trade, and a portal (the three things Gorath wants), the Horde becomes an ally. 50,000 orc warriors joining the coalition changes the continental power balance overnight. But the coalition Council will not accept orcs at the table easily. Vess will support it (pragmatic). The Council will resist. Katya will have opinions about integrating a force four times the size of her army.

If Kenji fights — the Horde is massive but undersupplied. Gorath's logistics can sustain 30 days of campaign. After that, the tribes fracture. Delay tactics win the war but lose any chance of alliance. And 50,000 orcs who were offered nothing go back to the steppe with a grudge that lasts generations.

**Key NPCs:**
- **Warchief Gorath Skullsplitter** — Half-Orc Warlord/Strategist, Level 27. Seven feet tall. Tusks filed flat — a human affectation that infuriates purist orcs. Speaks four languages. Reads. The most dangerous thing about him is that he's *right* — the orcs have been excluded from civilization and he's done asking nicely. He respects strength but he respects intelligence more. He'll test both.
- **Magra Bloodtusk** — Orc shaman, Level 24. Gorath's spiritual adviser. She doesn't trust humans. She didn't trust the unification. She came because the spirits told her the half-blood would either save or destroy the clans and she intends to ensure it's the former. She sees spirits of the dead. She speaks in prophecy and profanity equally.
- **Snikkit** — Goblin sapper-engineer, Level 14. Designed the Horde's siege equipment. Brilliant. Twitchy. Talks too fast. Built a catapult that fires goblin-riders in clay pots. He's not proud of it but he is a little proud of it. If the orcs join the coalition, Snikkit and Garrett would either become best friends or destroy each other.
- **Vrokka** — Female troll berserker, Level 22. Gorath's champion. Eight feet tall. Regenerates. Dumb as a bag of rocks and twice as mean. She's the Horde's equivalent of a nuclear deterrent — you don't want to fight her and she doesn't want to think. The perfect arrangement. Irresistible Presence triggers on trolls (humanoid). The DM should roll and have fun with whatever happens.

### THREAT 5: THE TWIN WYRMS
**Location:** The Dragonspine Mountains (NEW TERRITORY — far north)
**Race:** Dragons, dragonborn, kobolds, dragon cultists, and two ancients
**Clock:** 120 days to the Reckoning | +1%/day | Current: 5%

The Dragonspine Mountains have been off-limits for a thousand years. An ancient treaty between the first human kingdoms and the dragonflights established the range as sovereign dragon territory. Humans don't go up. Dragons don't come down. The treaty has held because both sides had reason to respect it.

Two ancient dragons enforce the balance. They are siblings. They disagree about everything. Their disagreement has kept the world safe for a millennium.

**Vorathiel the Conqueror** — Ancient Red Dragon, Level 38. She believes dragons are the supreme race and the treaty is a cage. For a thousand years she's argued that the flights should descend, burn the kingdoms, and rule as they were meant to. She's patient — a thousand years of patience — but the coalition's rise has accelerated her timeline. A human built a portal network in a month. A human conquered a continent. A human commands an army of glass soldiers. To Vorathiel, this is proof that humanity is becoming too powerful to contain. The treaty must end. The flights must descend. Now.

**Kaeltharion the Keeper** — Ancient Silver Dragon, Level 38. He believes the treaty is sacred and balance is the only law that matters. He has spent a thousand years countering his sister's arguments, maintaining alliances with the dragonborn settlements in the lower peaks, and ensuring that no flight descends. He is tired. A thousand years of holding the line against his own blood. He's looking for a reason to believe the balance can hold.

**The crisis:** Vorathiel has convinced four of the seven dragonflights to support descent. Kaeltharion holds three. The vote is in 120 days at the Reckoning — the millennial assembly of all flights. If Vorathiel wins, seven dragonflights descend on the kingdoms. Ancient reds, blues, greens, blacks — each one a CR 30+ apocalypse. The coalition, the Deepwood, the Freehold Marches, the Dominion remnants — everything burns.

**The threat:** This is the slow clock. 120 days. But it's the biggest threat on the board. The other four might destroy territories. The Twin Wyrms can destroy the world. And the only way to stop it is politics — not combat. Kenji cannot fight two Level 38 ancient dragons. Nobody can. The solution is convincing enough flights to vote for balance — which means understanding what each flight wants, negotiating with beings who view human lifespans as a rounding error, and somehow proving that humanity deserves to exist as equals.

**What Kenji can do:** Attend the Reckoning. Speak for humanity. But to get an invitation, he needs Kaeltharion's endorsement — and Kaeltharion doesn't endorse anyone he hasn't tested. The Dragonspine Mountains are a gauntlet. Dragonborn settlements that distrust humans. Kobold warrens that worship the flights. Dragon cultists who want the descent because they believe they'll be spared. And at the summit — two ancient siblings who've been arguing for a thousand years and are about to settle it.

Irresistible Presence has no effect on dragons (non-humanoid). Dragonborn — roll normally. The politics are gender-neutral because the stakes are existential.

**Key NPCs:**
- **Vorathiel the Conqueror** — Ancient Red Dragon, Level 38. She is not a villain. She is a mother protecting her children from a species that keeps getting more dangerous. She watched the Sundered Gate. She watched the Iron Crown War. She watched a single human reshape the ley network and conquer an empire in sixteen days. She's afraid — and an afraid ancient dragon is the most dangerous thing alive.
- **Kaeltharion the Keeper** — Ancient Silver Dragon, Level 38. Tired. Kind. Certain that the treaty matters. Less certain every decade that it will hold. He's been looking for a champion — a human who can stand before the flights and make the case for coexistence. He doesn't know Kenji yet. When he meets him, the first question won't be about power. It'll be about the **hidden children** (**Sera**, **Pip**, **Bracken** — and any future confirmations like **Mursha**) and what that says about how he treats the bonds he makes.
- **Drazhara** — Dragonborn elder, Level 20. Leads the largest dragonborn settlement in the lower peaks. Caught between flights — she respects Kaeltharion but her people are tired of being second-class citizens in dragon territory. She wants a seat at the Reckoning that dragonborn have never had. Kenji offering that could flip the vote.
- **Ignis** — Young red dragon, Level 18. Vorathiel's youngest child. Curious about humans. Has secretly flown to the lowlands three times to watch them. She thinks they're fascinating and terrifying and she's not sure her mother is wrong but she's not sure she's right either. She's the swing vote in Vorathiel's own family. Irresistible Presence works on dragonborn but NOT on true dragons. Ignis in humanoid form, however...
- **Skratch** — Kobold prophet, Level 4. Tiny. Manic. Claims to have seen the future in a puddle. The future showed "a man made of fire kneeling before two mountains." He's been waiting at the base of the Dragonspine for eleven years. He's wrong about almost everything. He might be right about this.

## CLOCK MANAGEMENT — THE DM'S GAME

All five clocks run simultaneously from the moment Bane of Eve triggers (identity exposure).

| Threat | Start | Rate | Critical | Deadline |
|--------|-------|------|----------|----------|
| The Hollowing (Dwarves) | 15% | +2%/day | Seal breach | ~42 days |
| The Pallid March (Undead) | 20% | +3%/day | March launches | ~27 days |
| The Red Court (Vampires) | 30% | +1%/day | City falls | ~70 days |
| The Iron Horde (Orcs) | 35% | +4%/day | Invasion | ~16 days |
| The Twin Wyrms (Dragons) | 5% | +1%/day | Reckoning vote | ~95 days |

**Reading the table:** The Iron Horde hits first (~16 days). The Pallid March hits second (~27 days). The Hollowing hits third (~42 days). The Red Court falls fourth (~70 days). The Reckoning is last (~95 days).

**Kenji cannot do all five sequentially.** The Horde and the March overlap. The Hollowing fires while he's handling either. The Red Court is slow but if ignored for two months, Ashenmere is gone and the infiltration has spread to Varenholm.

**Delegation is the mechanic.** Kenji can send allies, army units, and partners to handle threats he can't attend personally. Katya can hold the border against the Horde (buying time, not winning). Dren can reinforce Thornkeep against the March. Sera can investigate the Red Court in Ashenmere. But each delegation is a gamble — the ally might fail, and Kenji isn't there to save them.

**Player choices matter:**
- **Address the Horde first** → fastest clock. Diplomacy could turn them into allies. But the March launches while you're negotiating.
- **Address the March first** → existential undead threat. But the Horde hits Thornwall while you're in the swamp.
- **Address the Red Court early** → slow clock, early intervention prevents deep infiltration. But the fast clocks fire while you're playing detective.
- **Address the Hollowing** → dwarven alliance is powerful. But everything else escalates.
- **Rush the Reckoning** → if Kenji can get to the Dragonspine early, he has months to build the case. But everything at home burns.

- **Sera** doesn't come. She waits. If he wants to see his child, he knows where Stormhaven is. The door is hers and she doesn't chase.
- **Senna** — DM decides based on her pregnancy choice. If she kept it, she might not tell him. If she shows up, it's about the War College, not the baby.
- **Elara** — DM decides based on her pregnancy choice. If she kept it, she's buried the paternity. She comes as the Chancellor with Academy business. The child is not mentioned.

### Vess

She's been governing for six months without authorization. She's exhausted. She's angry. She's the most competent person in the coalition and she was never given the title to match. When Kenji resurfaces, her first words will not be warm. She needs him to legitimize what she's built or step aside and let her rule officially. Either way, Tuesdays are over until this conversation happens.

### The Eldest

The Deepwood alliance was personal — Kenji earned it. Without him present, the alliance is a piece of paper. If the Twin Wyrms descend, the Deepwood needs to know the coalition will defend them. The Eldest will send an emissary. Then Faelindra. Then she'll come herself. An ageless being walking into a human city for the first time in centuries means something is very wrong.

### Garrett

The courier found an empty Hearthstead. Garrett panicked quietly (Garrett does everything quietly). He's been trying to find Kenji through the civil service network — postmasters, innkeepers, trade route reports. He has nothing. When Kenji resurfaces, Garrett will be relieved for ten seconds and then hand him a stack of paperwork two feet tall.

### Mordecai

Gone. Building something. The gentleman's understanding holds. But Mordecai watches the ley network. If Kenji uses the ember at scale — Mordecai notices. Whether he helps, hinders, or simply observes depends on what Kenji's actions mean for the network Mordecai considers his life's work.

## RACE & CULTURE GUIDE — NEW PEOPLES

### Dwarves (Kharn-Dural)
- Clan-based society. Eight major clans, each controlling a level of the undermountain.
- Runesmiths are their mages — magic through carved stone and metal, not spoken words.
- Honor debt culture. A dwarf who owes you will die before defaulting. A dwarf you owe will remind you daily.
- Deep gnomes live in the upper levels — tinkers, alchemists, the weird neighbors the dwarves tolerate because they're useful.
- Duergar (grey dwarves) are exiles in the tunnel margins — distrusted, pragmatic, survive by trading information.

### Orcs & Goblins (Sunderplains)
- Thirty tribes, recently unified. Old feuds still simmer under Gorath's banner.
- Orcs respect strength but Gorath taught them to respect strategy. A cultural revolution in progress.
- Female orcs fight. Female orcs lead. Gender parity is not progressive for orcs — it's ancient. They find human gender roles confusing.
- Goblins are the engineer caste. Every siege weapon, every trap, every piece of infrastructure is goblin-designed. They're treated as lesser by orcish tradition — Gorath is changing this, slowly.
- Trolls are the heavy weapons. Not smart. Terrifyingly strong. Regenerate. Used as shock troops and left alone otherwise.
- Half-orcs like Gorath exist between worlds. Distrusted by both. The Horde's unification under a half-orc is revolutionary and fragile.

### Undead (Ashenveil)
- Mindless undead are tools — animated by ambient necromantic energy.
- Sentient undead (death knights, wraiths, revenants) retain personality and memory. They serve the Lych because the binding compels it — not because they choose it.
- The Lych's court has culture — art, music, conversation. It's a parody of life performed by beings who remember living and miss it in ways they can't articulate.
- Not all undead are hostile. Revenants in the swamp margins sometimes help travelers — the last echo of who they were before the binding took hold.

### Vampires (Red Court)
- Not monsters — parasites with a hierarchy. Elder vampires at the top. Spawn at the bottom.
- Doppelgangers are not vampires — they're a separate species the Court employs. Shapeshifters who sell their services. Mercenary identity thieves.
- Thralls are living humans under vampire influence. Not turned. Not killed. Controlled. They function normally — eat, sleep, work — but they serve. The horror is that nobody can tell.
- The Red Court has rules. No feeding on children. No turning without elder approval. No exposure to daylight. They view themselves as civilized. The civilization is built on blood.

### Dragons & Kin (Dragonspine)
- True dragons are ancient, intelligent, and view humans the way humans view ants — with occasional interest and frequent indifference.
- Dragonborn are a separate race — dragon-adjacent, mortal, living in the mountain settlements. They serve the flights but they're not dragons and the distinction matters to them.
- Kobolds worship dragons as gods. They're small, fragile, and fanatically loyal. A kobold warren is a temple with teeth.
- Dragon cultists are humans who worship the flights. They exist in lowland cities — hidden, waiting for the descent. When Vorathiel calls, they answer.
- Chromatic vs metallic is not good vs evil here. It's philosophy. Chromatic flights favor dominion. Metallic flights favor balance. Both have valid arguments. Both have atrocities in their history.

## DM RULES FOR BOOK 4

1. **All five clocks run from Day 1.** The DM advances every clock at the start of each in-game day. Post the status if the player asks. Show consequences of neglect.

2. **Delegation is a real mechanic.** The player can assign allies and armies to threats. Roll outcomes based on the ally's capability vs the threat's difficulty. Failure is possible. Death is possible. Sending Dren to fight the Lych alone gets Dren killed.

3. **The personal threads don't pause for the war.** Pip shows up with a baby and a ledger during a war council. Sera's absence is louder than anyone's presence. Vess demands legitimacy while the Horde is at the gates. The pregnancies, the relationships, the kingdom politics — they run alongside the threats, not after them.

4. **Race diversity is mandatory.** Every territory has multiple races. Kharn-Dural has dwarves, gnomes, and duergar. The Horde has orcs, goblins, trolls, and half-orcs. Ashenmere has vampires, doppelgangers, humans, goblins, and an orc priest. The Dragonspine has dragons, dragonborn, kobolds, and cultists. NPCs reflect this.

5. **Not every threat is solved by combat.** The Horde wants diplomacy. The Red Court needs investigation. The Reckoning needs politics. The Hollowing needs magic. Only the Pallid March is a straight fight — and even that has a shortcut (phylactery).

6. **Kenji's cover erodes.** Each threat he personally handles reveals more of his power. By the third threat, the ronin identity is tissue paper. By the fifth, the world knows the Wizard King is back. Plan the Bane of Eve activation around this escalation.

7. **The kingdom can fall.** If the player ignores too many clocks, territories are lost. NPCs die. The coalition fractures. Book 4 can end in tragedy. That's not a failure state — that's a consequence state. What Kenji rebuilds from the ashes is Book 5.

8. **Apotheosis is the finale.** Level 40 should coincide with the resolution of the fifth threat — the Reckoning. Kenji stands before ancient dragons and speaks for humanity at the peak of his power. What he says matters more than what he can do.
