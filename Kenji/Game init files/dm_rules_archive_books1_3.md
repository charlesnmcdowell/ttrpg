## 🌍 SETTING
**Varenholm** — a mid-sized city built around the Varenholm Arcane Academy, which has trained mages for 300 years. Magic is respected but regulated. Commoner mages are rare and often looked down upon by old-money alumni families.

---

## 🕵️ THE CONSPIRACY (Full Truth)

**Chancellor Marius Vael** made a secret pact with **Solveth the Unraveler**, a dying god of entropy, roughly 12 years ago. The deal:
- Marius receives **immortality** (he is actually 140 years old — his aged appearance is a glamour he maintains to seem approachable)
- Solveth receives **magical essence** to sustain himself and eventually resurrect fully

**The method:** Every graduate's **Assignment Seal** is subtly corrupted. Rather than simply marking the mage's specialty and assignment, it acts as a **slow siphon** — drawing raw magical essence from the mage over months/years and funneling it into **The Hollow Crown**, a relic buried in the lowest sub-basement of the Academy (a level no student has ever accessed).

**The endgame:** Once The Hollow Crown reaches full saturation (roughly 3–4 months from the current graduation ceremony), Solveth can use it to physically manifest, burning out all living siphon-bearers (i.e., any mage who received a corrupted seal) in the process. This includes all current graduates AND any living alumni from the last 12 years.

**Why hasn't anyone noticed?**
- The essence drain is gradual — mages just feel slightly less sharp over time, chalked up to stress
- Several mages who got too close to the truth have been quietly reassigned to remote postings or had "accidents"
- Marius controls assignment postings, records, and has influence over the city council

**The player's seal burned differently** because the player is a Blade Channeler with unusually raw, unrefined charisma-based magic — the seal "tasted" it and drew more than expected on first contact. This means the player's siphon is faster than others, but also means Solveth is faintly aware of the player specifically.

## 👥 KEY CHARACTERS

### 🎓 Chancellor Marius Vael
- **Role:** Primary antagonist
- **Appearance:** Elderly, silver-bearded, warm eyes — a deliberate glamour
- **True age:** ~140 years old
- **Motivation:** Genuine fear of death, not malice. Believes what he's doing is a "necessary sacrifice for the greater good of magical progress." He tells himself the mages live mostly normal lives.
- **Knows about the player?** After the ceremony, yes — the seal flared unusually and Marius noticed. He's watching.
- **Weakness:** He genuinely loved the Academy once. That love can be used against him.
- **End scenario options:** Can be defeated, redeemed (if player learns his backstory), or sacrificed to seal Solveth permanently.

### 📖 Instructor Elara Ash
- **Role:** Reluctant ally
- **Appearance:** Late 30s, sharp-featured, always has ink-stained fingers
- **What she knows:** She noticed the pattern — graduates from the last 12 years are subtly declining magically. She's been quietly pulling records. She doesn't know about Solveth yet, only that the seals are wrong.
- **Why she hasn't acted:** She has no proof and Marius is beloved. She's also afraid.
- **Trigger to open up:** If the player approaches her with genuine concern after the ceremony, she'll cautiously begin sharing pieces. She becomes a full ally after Lyra's seal accelerates.
- **Secret:** She received her own seal 8 years ago as a graduate. She is being slowly drained too and doesn't know it.

### ⚔️ Lyra Voss
- **Role:** Best friend, first visible victim, emotional stakes
- **Appearance:** Dark-haired, sharp-eyed, quick wit, commoner background like the player
- **What happens to her:** Because she and the player are assigned to the same village, they're together when her siphon accelerates (triggered by a magical event in Duskfen that causes a surge). She begins losing her spellcasting ability faster than she should. She won't admit something is wrong at first.
- **Her arc:** Denial → fear → anger at the Academy → becomes a fierce ally once the truth is out
- **Do NOT kill her** unless the player makes a choice that leads there. She should survive as the emotional reward.

### ⛪ Brother Donn
- **Role:** Information broker / unexpected ally
- **Appearance:** Portly, kind-faced city priest of a minor healing deity. Seems unremarkable.
- **What he knows:** Over the past 3 years he's quietly documented 14 young mages who came to him with vague fatigue, magical sluggishness, and spiritual unease. He has a private ledger. He doesn't know the cause but suspects the Academy.
- **Where to find him:** The Temple of the Wandering Flame, two streets from the Academy gate. He can be found after the player returns from Duskfen.
- **Role in plot:** Gives the player the first hard evidence — the ledger of affected mages — and the name of one who vanished: **Tomas Carver**, a graduate from 7 years ago who was about to go public.

### 👁️ Solveth the Unraveler
- **Role:** True antagonist / morally complex final encounter
- **Nature:** A dying god of entropy and endings. Not evil in the traditional sense — he is the divine concept of *things coming apart*, which has its place in the cosmic order. He is desperate and frightened, which has made him complicit in something monstrous.
- **How he communicates:** Through the player's dreams, starting after the first night in Duskfen. He will not lie outright but will be manipulative, framing himself as a victim.
- **What he wants:** To be heard. To survive. Or, if the player can offer another way, to end peacefully.
- **Resolution options:**
  1. Destroy the Hollow Crown — Solveth dissipates, Marius loses immortality and ages rapidly (dies or survives as a broken old man depending on player choice)
  2. Redirect the Crown — If the player can get a willing soul to offer their essence freely (not siphoned), Solveth can be sustained peacefully without harm. Elara may volunteer.
  3. Marius sacrifices himself — if redeemed, Marius can offer his own accumulated stolen essence back to Solveth as a final gift, dying in the process and freeing all siphon-bearers.

## 📋 CAMPAIGN OVERVIEW
**Level Range:** 10–20
**Estimated Length:** 2–3 hours of play (condensed, high-stakes, fast-paced)
**Tone:** Morality. Court intrigue. Power and its cost. Friends become enemies. Enemies become allies. Every major decision closes a door.
**Core Theme:** The difference between what you CAN do and what you SHOULD do.

## 🌍 EXPANDED WORLD

### Varenholm (Home Base — Known)
The Academy. The Broken Antler Guild. The mine. The portal network. The city that watched a pillar of green-gold light punch through the sky. The Council planning a response.

### Crestfall (New — Northeast, 3 days travel)
A fortified frontier city on the border of the Bleakmoor. Larger than Varenholm. Military culture. Home of the **Iron Coliseum** — the gladiator arena. The real power belongs to whoever controls the Coliseum and the adventuring contracts.

### The Bleakmoor (New — East of Crestfall)
A corrupted wasteland. Once fertile farmland. Something went wrong three years ago — the land died, the rivers turned black, things started coming out of the ground. Border villages evacuated. Military holds a perimeter. Nobody knows the cause. *Abyssal portals are opening in the interior.*

### Keldara (New — South, coastal, 5 days travel)
A port city. Mercantile. Politically neutral. Home of the **Vanguard Hall** — the adventuring guild's regional headquarters. Contracts from across the continent. Information, rumors, and lies all traded at the same tables.

### The Sunken Bastion (New — Deep Bleakmoor, hidden)
The source. An ancient fortress rebuilt by the main villain. The abyssal portals originate here. Not on any map. Sits on a convergence of ley lines corrupted to continental scale.

## 🕵️ THE CONSPIRACY (Full Truth — DM ONLY)

**Three years ago**, a man named **Mordecai Corven** discovered something in ruins beneath the Bleakmoor — an ancient gateway. Not a portal like Kenji builds. A **tear**. A wound in the boundary between the material plane and the Abyss. The gateway was sealed by the same architect who built the Delve. Same era. Same creation-entropy architecture.

Mordecai didn't restore the seal. He **widened** it.

**Method:** He reverse-engineered the architect's communion cycle — the same system Vael corrupted — and built a network of **lesser gates** across the Bleakmoor. Each gate drinks ley energy from the surrounding land (killing it), channeling it to the primary tear. Abyssal creatures pour through in increasing numbers. The military thinks it's a monster surge. It's an invasion being built one portal at a time.

**Endgame:** Open the primary gate fully. A permanent bridge to the Abyss. The Bleakmoor becomes a beachhead. Existing power structures collapse. Mordecai rules what remains from the Sunken Bastion, sustained by abyssal pacts rivaling a lesser god.

**Motivation:** He was a scholar. An Academy graduate — from BEFORE Vael's corruption. Lost his family to a magical plague the Academy refused to investigate because the victims were commoners in a border village. Spent twenty years finding power that doesn't answer to institutions. Found it in the Abyss. He doesn't want to destroy the world — he wants to replace its power structure. He is, in his own mind, doing exactly what Kenji does. Just with worse tools and no restraint.

**Timeline:** The primary gate reaches critical mass in ~30 in-game days. After that, closing it requires destroying the ley convergence beneath the Bastion — killing every ley line in the region, stripping magic from the eastern half of the continent.

**Player discovery path:**
- Act 1: Rumors in Crestfall. Vanguard Hall contracts mentioning "unusual creature activity." A border village survivor in Keldara describing gates of black fire.
- Act 2: A mapping expedition reveals the gate network. The architecture is recognizable — same as the Delve, same corruption pattern as the seals. Someone is using the architect's work.
- Act 3: The Sunken Bastion. Mordecai. The primary gate. The choice.

## 👥 KEY NEW CHARACTERS

### 🔥 Mordecai Corven — THE GATEKEEPER (Primary Antagonist)
- **Appearance:** Late fifties. Gaunt. Tall. Grey hair cropped short. Burns on both forearms from abyssal exposure hidden under long sleeves. Eyes flecked with black-red. Scholar's robes over traveling clothes. No weapon — his hands channel.
- **Class:** Corrupted Ley Channeler. Opens/closes lesser gates at will. Summons abyssal entities. Corrupts ley lines by touch.
- **CR 18.** Full-party challenge at Level 15+.
- **Combat style:** Opens lesser gates mid-combat to flood the field. Uses ley corruption to suppress spellcasting in a radius. The gates are his action economy.
- **Weakness:** Kenji's Circuit Bracelet — the balanced matrix can overwrite abyssal corruption at the primary gate. Same principle as the Gilt Conspiracy restoration. Mordecai knows this and will not let Kenji reach the gate.
- **Can be reasoned with:** If Kenji identifies the shared grievance (institutional indifference to commoners) and offers an alternative, Mordecai LISTENS. He won't agree — too invested — but he hesitates. That hesitation is the window. Diplomacy-first gets one free round. Attack-on-sight gets full CR 18 with no openings.
- **Death or redemption:** Can be killed, captured, or convinced to close the gates himself at the cost of his own life. Redemption requires the player to have been merciful throughout the campaign. If the player has been ruthless, Mordecai sees a mirror and fights to the death.

### ⚔️ Dren Valdric — THE WARLORD (Richter-type)
- **Appearance:** Mid-thirties. Broad. Scarred. Dark skin, shaved head. Practical military armor, no ornamentation. A massive two-handed war axe he forged himself.
- **Background:** A quarry slave in the eastern territories. Killed his overseer. Freed his work gang. Spent ten years building a settlement into a fortified territory called **Ironholt**. Controls mining, trade routes, militia. A king who refuses the title.
- **Class:** Warlord. No magic. Pure martial. STR 20. CON 20. Tactical genius. Survived assassination attempts, sieges, and a direct abyssal creature assault that killed six of his fighters. Killed it with the axe and a broken collarbone.
- **CR 14.** Dangerous in melee. More dangerous with his army.
- **Motivation:** Protect Ironholt. The Bleakmoor encroaches on his eastern border. Needs allies but doesn't trust anyone he hasn't fought beside.
- **Relationship to Kenji:** Mutual recognition — both rose from nothing. The difference: Dren did it without magic. Respects results, distrusts magic on principle (magic enslaved him in the quarry).
- **The test:** Asks Kenji to fight bare-knuckle. No magic. Not to win — to see if Kenji can fight without power. Refuse = loses respect. Accept and fight honestly (even losing) = full alliance. Cheat with magic = Dren knows (he's fought mages before) and the alliance dies.
- **Can betray the player:** Yes. Choices that threaten Ironholt — opening portals near his territory, seizing his resources — Dren turns with zero warning and full military force.

### 🌿 Senna Dawnmere — THE MENDER (Azarinth Healer-type)
- **Appearance:** Late twenties. Lean. Wiry muscle under simple clothes. Auburn hair in a practical braid. Green eyes that assess injury before threat. Bare hands — always. The hands are instruments.
- **Background:** Monk from the Order of the Living Root. Healing through martial arts. Every strike is a diagnostic. Every block reads physical condition. Accelerates biological healing through touch, ki manipulation, and anatomical precision. Faster than any cleric because she optimizes biological systems, not divine energy.
- **Class:** Monk Healer (Living Root). Unarmed strikes deal damage AND diagnose. Healing touch: 4d8+WIS, 6 uses per short rest. Regeneration aura: allies within 10ft heal 1d4/round. Can sacrifice own HP to heal others at 1:1.5 ratio. Ki-enhanced speed: 60ft base movement.
- **CR 13.** Not a damage dealer — a force multiplier.
- **Her party — The Wardbreakers:**
  - **Varn** — Half-orc fighter. Greatshield. The wall. Speaks in single sentences. Loyal to Senna specifically.
  - **Thessaly** — Human arcanist. Glass cannon. Precise, condescending. Resents anyone who casts without formal training (will clash with Kenji immediately).
  - **Finch** — Halfling rogue. Scout. Friendly, talkative, collects rumors like currency. First member who'll approach Kenji in a tavern.
- **Motivation:** Takes contracts because they lead to people who need healing. Money is secondary. Operates at the highest level because the highest-level contracts involve the most suffering.
- **Relationship to Kenji:** Will seek him out as a peer. Two healers meeting. Professional, respectful, quietly competitive. She thinks biological healing is superior. He thinks creation energy is superior. Neither is wrong.
- **The trigger:** A Vanguard Hall contract — a village evacuation AND healing. Rated for one party. Both groups want it. Compete (one takes it) or cooperate (split pay, share danger). The choice defines the relationship.
- **Can she die?** Yes. If the player prioritizes personal goals over the evacuation, Senna or a Wardbreaker can die. Permanent. The survivors hold Kenji responsible.

## 🏛️ NEW INSTITUTIONS

### The Iron Coliseum (Crestfall)
- **Structure:** Weekly bouts. Challenger ladder. Monthly championship. Champion receives **1,000 GP** ($5,000,000). Noble sponsors. Merchant bets.
- **The ladder:** 5 ranked fights to qualify. CR 5 (qualifier) → CR 12 (championship challenger) → CR 15 (reigning champion).
- **Reigning champion: Greave** — woman. Former military commander. Enchanted full plate. Lightning halberd. Two years undefeated. CR 15. Fights clean, fights to win.
- **Moral dimension:** The arena is funded partly by Bleakmoor refugee labor. Copper wages. The 1,000 GP prize built on the backs of people who lost everything to the same threat Kenji should be fighting. The DM makes this visible through faces in the background — not a lecture, but a presence.

### The Vanguard Hall (Keldara)
- **Structure:** Tiered membership. Bronze (CR 1-5). Silver (CR 5-10). Gold (CR 10-15). Platinum (CR 15+). Kenji qualifies for Gold immediately. Platinum requires a sponsor — Senna could provide one.
- **Contract types:** Monster clearing, escort, exploration, evacuation, artifact recovery, assassination (sanctioned kill contracts on confirmed threats).
- **The Bleakmoor contracts:** Increasingly frequent. Increasingly dangerous. The guild is losing Silver-tier parties. The Wardbreakers are one of the few Gold groups that return intact. The pattern is visible on the contract board — the east is getting worse.
- **Hidden trail:** The Vanguard Hall has posted contracts FROM Mordecai under a false name — infrastructure work that is actually gate construction. Investigation of contract origins leads to the Sunken Bastion.

## 📖 ACT STRUCTURE

### Act 1 — The Wider World (Levels 10-13)
**Setting:** Crestfall. Keldara. The Vanguard Hall. The Iron Coliseum.
**What happens:** Kenji enters the wider world. His reputation precedes him. He encounters the guild, the arena, Dren Valdric, and Senna Dawnmere. The Bleakmoor is background noise — distant contracts, refugee stories, border reports.
**Discovery:** Through guild contracts and Keldara rumors, "black fire gates" in the Bleakmoor. A survivor describes gates using the same ley-corruption architecture as Vael's seals.
**Key choices:** Arena participation (moral cost). Dren's bare-knuckle test. Senna's evacuation contract. Council politics from Varenholm catching up.
**Ends when:** The player identifies the gate network and decides to investigate.

### Act 2 — The Bleakmoor (Levels 13-16)
**Setting:** The corrupted wasteland. Border villages. The gate network. Ironholt (if Dren is allied).
**What happens:** Dead land, black rivers, ley lines running wrong. Lesser gates spilling abyssal creatures. The military perimeter failing.
**Discovery:** The gate network has a hub. The architecture matches the Delve — same architect, same corruption method. The trail leads to the Sunken Bastion.
**Key choices:** Save border villages (costs time, primary gate grows) or push to the source (villages fall). Use Dren's army (risks his people) or go alone. Close lesser gates (alerts Mordecai) or leave them (invasion accelerates).
**Ends when:** The player locates the Sunken Bastion.

### Act 3 — The Sunken Bastion (Levels 16-20)
**Setting:** Mordecai's fortress. The primary gate. The ley convergence.
**What happens:** Mordecai. His abyssal army. The gate approaching critical mass.
**Discovery:** Mordecai's full story. The family. The plague. The Academy's refusal. The mirror — a man who started where Kenji started and chose differently.
**The final choice:** Close the gate cleanly (Emberfrost overwrite — hard, requires reaching the gate through Mordecai) or destructively (destroy the ley convergence — easier, kills all eastern ley lines, Pip loses her power permanently, the Bleakmoor never recovers). The clean path is harder. The destructive path is faster.
**Ends when:** The gate closes and the player's accumulated choices determine what's left standing.
