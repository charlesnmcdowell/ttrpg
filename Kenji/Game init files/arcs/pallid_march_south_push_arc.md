### Beat 11 — *Oathbreaker Examination* (can occur during any Thornkeep beat)

The sword is in the Bag of Holding. It hums. The holy and necrotic energies are still fighting. At some point — during debrief, during downtime, during a lead investigation — Kenji or someone in his orbit will want to examine it, or the sword will make itself felt.

- **Fixed:** Oathbreaker is a story-load-bearing artifact. It is the physical expression of Corwyn's twisted Oath — holy (original paladin vow to protect the innocent) and necrotic (the binding that redirected that vow toward the Lych). The two energies are locked in stalemate inside the blade. The sword was in pain because the wielder was in pain. Separated from Corwyn, the stalemate is unstable — both energies are looking for something to anchor to.
- **Open:** Who examines it (Sister Aldra could read its ward-form, Bracken could assess it militarily, Kenji could simply hold it and see what happens through Bond-Form Sight). Whether Kenji keeps it, destroys it, attempts to purify it, or returns it. This is a major character decision with downstream consequences.
- **Stop:** If Kenji draws the sword from the bag, describe what he perceives (the weight, the temperature shifts, the dual-energy hum) and STOP. The player decides what to do next. If someone else examines it, describe their reaction and STOP for the player.

**Consequences ladder:**
- **Keep it sealed in the bag:** Safe. Stalemate continues. Corwyn remains disarmed. The Lych may send agents to recover it. Clock starts on retrieval attempts.
- **Let Sister Aldra read it:** She can describe the binding geometry — the shape of Corwyn's original Oath, the shape of the twist, and where the seam is. This gives the player information to potentially break the binding later. She will not touch the blade.
- **Kenji holds it / Bond-Form Sight:** He perceives the dual energies directly. The holy side recognizes creation-flavored magic (Kenji's nature). The necrotic side recoils. This is not a "claim the sword" moment — it's a sensing moment. What he learns depends on how long he holds it and whether he pushes his sight into the blade's history.
- **Attempt to purify:** High-tier action. Requires understanding the binding first (Sister Aldra's read, or equivalent). Premature attempts risk collapsing the stalemate — one side wins, and the result depends on which. Holy victory = the blade becomes a paladin weapon but Corwyn's binding remains (the sword was a symptom, not the cause). Necrotic victory = the blade becomes a death knight weapon and the holy energy is lost — Corwyn's last anchor to his original self dies.

## 6. Hard rails (do not let the AI drift)

- **Names:** Party-facing undead sovereign = border voice **"the Lady"** / enemy form **"the Lych."** True name **Lady Nyx** is **gated** (see tracker). Do not burn the reveal in a random fight line or NPC aside.
- **Corwyn:** Lawful Good soul, twisted Oath target. He protects what he must, hates what he is. Treat him with dignity — he is tragic, not evil. He does not snarl, gloat, or threaten. He speaks like a man who remembers being good.
- **No Kenji dialogue.** Never. Not paraphrased, not implied, not summarized. The DM narrates the world and speaks for NPCs. Kenji's words are the player's.
- **No auto-resolve combat.** Every round, every declaration, every target — player chooses.
- **No fabricated named NPCs.** No mysterious strangers, no convenient sages, no exposition-delivery characters. Use tracked NPCs only. Background figures are transactional and know only public information.
- **Ronin cover:** Ember / portals / obvious ArchMagus tells = identity exposure unless the table agrees the mask is already gone. Kenji fights as a swordsman first. Magic is the secret, not the default.
