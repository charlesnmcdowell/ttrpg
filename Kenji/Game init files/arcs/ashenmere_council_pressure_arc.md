## 1. Arc compass

**What this arc is:** The Coalition's political machinery processing the intel Kenji has generated — relay blackout, border shift, Lady contact, Oathbreaker custody, death-knight duel, identity exposure risk. The paper trail that turns a ronin's fieldwork into council debate, troop commitments, and the question of who Kenji actually is.

**What this arc is NOT:** Combat. The south push handles combat. This arc handles the consequences of combat — the dispatches, the hearings, the questions that captains ask when a stranger walks into their war and breaks things.

**Kenji posture:** The ronin mask is under pressure. Name on file with the Lady. Oathbreaker in his bag. Coalition paper trail building. Each council-facing interaction risks connecting "Kenji the ronin" to "the ArchMagus who vanished six months ago." Low INT means the exposure risk is real.

---

## 2. Key NPCs (political layer)

**Commander Renna Bracken (Thornkeep):** Garrison commander. First point of contact. Already holds: 8-hour debrief on Seravane/Corban/bronze-ring network, relay blackout field reports, Oathbreaker custody knowledge (Ch17 — grease on steel in her office). Bracken is the filter between Kenji's fieldwork and the Coalition chain.

**Jostin:** Filed his field report. The paper spine. Whatever Jostin wrote goes into Bracken's dispatch to Ashenmere. Jostin's professional framing vs Kenji's actual actions — the gap between the report and the reality.

**Coalition Council (Ashenmere City):** Vess sits on this council. If Kenji's identity reaches council-level discussion, Vess recognizes him. If Taryn's letter reaches them, it opens: who is the ronin working Bracken's contracts? Who killed the relay network? Who has Oathbreaker?

**Taryn:** Letter of commission is the trigger document. 475g upfront + 5,000g pending council review. The letter names Taryn as agent but the intel behind it came from Kenji's recon. Paper trail.

**Vess (distant but relevant):** Coalition Council Chief. If the ronin's identity surfaces at council level, Vess is the person most likely to recognize it — and the person most motivated to either protect it or exploit it depending on her read of the situation.

## 3. Beat ladder (expected path)

### Beat 0 — Thornkeep debrief (PARTIALLY PLAYED Ch17 — Kenji left before completion)
Bracken processed the Ch14-16 intel in her office beat (Ch17). Jostin's report filed. Brynn back in squad rotation. Kenji left Thornkeep Day 256 / Ashmere 34 **before completing the debrief** — Stealth egress, north gate, no formal goodbye. The question is what Bracken does with the incomplete information — she is LG and a commander, so dispatch is **probable** (estimated Ashmere 35–37), not optional.

**Open fields:** How much of Kenji's identity leaks into the dispatch. Bracken knows him as "Kenji" — the ronin. She does not know he is the ArchMagus. But the dispatch describes a masked swordsman who solo'd a death knight, walked into the Pallid March's command presence, and left with a holy weapon in his bag. That description narrows the candidate list.
**Stop line:** This beat is **partially played** — it migrates to Beat 1 (Dispatch sent) when the DM decides Bracken has processed enough to escalate.

### Beat 1 — Dispatch sent
Bracken's report reaches Ashenmere. The relay blackout, the border shift, the death-knight duel, the custody of a holy weapon — any one of these is council business. All of them together is an emergency session.

**Fixed:** The dispatch goes. Bracken is Lawful Good and a garrison commander. She does not sit on intelligence that affects the coalition's southern flank.
**Open:** How much of Kenji's identity leaks into the dispatch. Bracken knows him as "Kenji" — the ronin. She does not know he is the ArchMagus. But the dispatch describes a Level 35 swordsman who solo'd a death knight and walked into the Pallid March's command presence. That description narrows the candidate list.

### Beat 2 — Council receives
The council reads Bracken's dispatch. Taryn's letter of commission is either already in their stack or arrives around the same time. Two documents from Thornkeep, both describing unusual ronin activity.

**Fixed:** The council notices. This is their job.
**Open:** Does Vess connect the dots? Does someone else? Does the council send an investigator south, or do they summon Bracken (and by extension, Kenji) north to Ashenmere?

### Beat 3 — Identity pressure
The net tightens. Someone — Vess, an investigator, a council clerk who reads old academy records — asks the question: who is this ronin?

**Fixed:** The question gets asked. The paper trail is too hot.
**Open:** Kenji's response. Does he go to Ashenmere voluntarily? Does he run? Does he reveal? Does he send a smoke clone? Low INT means the answer is probably messy.

### Beat 4 — Council hearing or confrontation
Either Kenji faces the council (voluntarily or summoned) or the council sends someone to face him. The identity question lands on the table.

**Fixed:** The confrontation happens. The arc does not allow indefinite avoidance.
**Open:** Everything about how it plays out. Vess's role. Kenji's mask. The political consequences of the ArchMagus being alive and operating under cover in a war zone.

### Beat 5 — Fallout
The political consequences of whatever Beat 4 produced. Troop commitments to the south. Funding. Authority. The question of Oathbreaker's custody — does a ronin get to keep a holy weapon, or does the coalition claim it? The question of the Lady — does the council treat her as a military target or a diplomatic opportunity?

**Open:** Everything. This is where the arc generates its own momentum based on player choices.

## 5. Hard rails (DM-enforced)

- **No Kenji dialogue.** NPC voices only. Stop at decision points.
- **Vess does not magically know.** She recognizes Kenji only if the paper trail reaches her desk with enough detail. She is smart, not omniscient.
- **Bracken is Lawful Good.** She escalates because duty requires it, not because she wants to expose Kenji. The conflict between duty and personal trust is the character beat.
- **The council is bureaucracy.** They move at council speed — dispatches, sessions, votes. Not instantaneously.
- **Identity exposure is graduated, not binary.** "Ronin named Kenji who solo'd a death knight" is suspicious. "The ArchMagus of Varenholm operating under cover" is a different category. The gap between the two is where the drama lives.

---

## 6. Exit conditions

- **A) Identity revealed and accepted:** Kenji is outed as the ArchMagus. The council processes it. Political consequences play out. Arc complete — new arc handles post-reveal state.
- **B) Identity protected:** Kenji navigates the pressure without being outed. The paper trail is managed. Arc complete — but the clock resets, not stops.
- **C) Kenji leaves:** He walks away from Coalition territory before the pressure lands. Arc pauses — resumes whenever he returns to council-adjacent space.
