# WORLD CALENDAR — THE RECKONING OF SEASONS
## Shared Universe Lore Document

---

## OVERVIEW

The common calendar used across human kingdoms, trade routes, and coalition territories. Elves, dwarves, and orcs maintain their own systems but trade in the common calendar.

---

## YEAR STRUCTURE

- **360 days.** 12 months of 30 days. 4 seasons of 3 months.
- **Weeks are 6 days.** 5 work days + 1 rest day (Stillday). Each month has exactly 5 weeks.
- **Current year:** 1,247 Age of Roots (AR)

**Age of Roots** — the elven name for the current era. Counts from the planting of the Eldest Tree in Silvandris. Humans adopted it because the elves were counting first and nobody wanted to argue with immortals about whose calendar was right.

## DAYS OF THE WEEK (6-day cycle)

| Day | Name | Character |
|-----|------|-----------|
| 1 | **Rootday** | First work day. Named for the Eldest Tree. Elves consider it sacred. Humans consider it Monday. |
| 2 | **Forgeday** | Dwarven influence. Trade and craft day. Smiths and merchants do their best business. |
| 3 | **Windday** | Travel day. Caravans depart. Letters sent. The roads are busiest. |
| 4 | **Fieldday** | Agricultural day. Market day in villages. Farmers bring goods to town. The village square is full. |
| 5 | **Crownday** | Governance day. Courts sit. Official business conducted. Laws proclaimed. Taxes collected. |
| 6 | **Stillday** | Rest day. Temples open for services. No trade required (shops may open but aren't expected to). Families gather. People sleep in. |

## MAJOR HOLIDAYS & OBSERVANCES

### Leyrise — Bright Turn 15 (Spring Equinox)
Ley lines surge to annual peak. Mages feel it physically — a hum in the bones, spells come easier, enchantments flare brighter. Academies hold demonstrations and open lectures. Villages light bonfires at crossroads to "feed the ley." The Deepwood blooms overnight — the entire forest erupts in color in a single evening. The Eldest sometimes speaks to gathered elves at the Heart Grove. 

**Culturally:** A celebration of magic and renewal. Gifts of seeds and handmade tools. Children plant their first garden. New apprentices are taken on.

### The Long Day — Highsun 15 (Summer Solstice)
Longest day of the year. Military parades in garrison towns. Tournament season officially opens — the Iron Coliseum at Thornwall holds its annual championship, drawing fighters from across the territories. Athletic competitions, weapon demonstrations, mock battles. Villages hold archery contests and wrestling matches. Ale flows freely. Brawls are expected and generally forgiven.

**Culturally:** A celebration of strength, skill, and competition. Soldiers and fighters are honored. War dead are remembered at dawn. The rest of the day is loud.

### Harvest Week — Goldtide 1-7
The biggest event of the year for common people. First harvest brought in. Week-long market festival in every village and city. Trade caravans arrive from every direction. Merchants set up stalls. Traveling performers, musicians, puppet shows. Debts are called. Contracts renewed. Marriages are most common during Harvest Week — the logic being that you start a family when the food is in.

**Culturally:** Commerce, community, and coupling. Pip would run three simultaneous inn events across the portfolio. Garrett would process six months of contracts in seven days. Villages that barely survived the year celebrate that they survived.

### Leyfall — Dim Light 15 (Autumn Equinox)
Ley lines drop to baseline. Magic feels thinner — spells cost more effort, enchantments dim slightly, sensitive mages report headaches. Elves hold vigil at ancient groves, communing with root networks. Druids feel it most — the green world pulling inward, conserving, preparing for the dark months. The Deepwood's leaves turn in a single night (matching the Leyrise bloom — the forest operates on ley time, not weather).

**Culturally:** Quiet. Reflective. The opposite of Harvest Week. People take stock. Wynn would spend this day in her garden with her hands in the soil. Amaris would feel it through every root network she's connected to.

### The Still Night — Hollowmere 15 (Winter Solstice)
Shortest day. Longest night. The most significant holiday across all cultures.

**Humans:** Fires in every window. Families gather for the largest meal of the year. Stories are told — specifically, each person tells one story from the year that mattered to them. Temples hold all-night services. The Still Night is when you say the things you've been carrying. Some families make it solemn. Some make it rowdy. All make it warm.

**Elves:** The Grove Silence. Entire Deepwood goes quiet. No one speaks from sundown to dawn. The Eldest meditates. The forest listens. What it hears, the elves don't share with outsiders.

**Dwarves:** The Deep Seal. All tunnels below the residential levels are sealed for the night. Dwarves drink, feast, and tell stories about the things they've built. The tradition began because of what lives in the deep — the Still Night is when deep things press closest. The sealing is practical, not ceremonial.

**Orcs:** The Bone Fire. Each tribe builds a fire from the bones of animals hunted that year. The fire burns through the night. Young orcs who came of age that year jump the fire. If you land clean, the year will be strong. If you stumble, the shaman gives you extra training.

**Undead:** The Lych's army would be at peak strength. The Ashenveil is most dangerous on this night. Death knights patrol. Wraiths are solid enough to touch. The ley lines at their lowest means the barrier between living and dead is thinnest.

**Culturally:** Family. Memory. Warmth against the dark. The holiday that matters most and the one Kenji has never experienced.

### The Turning — Lastmere 30 (New Year's Eve)
Old year ends. New year begins at midnight. Bonfires at crossroads — the tradition says the fire guides the new year in and burns the old year out. Debts are settled or formally forgiven. Grudges are supposed to be released (they mostly aren't). Stories of the year told at every hearth.

The Eldest marks a ring on the tree. 1,247 rings. Each one visible. The tree is the calendar.

**Culturally:** Closure and beginning. Garrett would have the books balanced by noon. Pip would host the largest event of the year at every inn simultaneously. Sera would drink alone and watch the fire from her window.

## RACIAL CALENDARS (Reference)

**Elven (Deepwood):** Count in Great Cycles of 500 years. Currently in the 3rd Cycle of the Roots. They consider the human calendar "charmingly granular." The Eldest doesn't track years. She tracks rings.

**Dwarven (Kharn-Dural):** Count from the First Delve — when the founders broke ground on the undermountain. Year 847 FD. Their months are named for minerals. Their weeks have 8 days (6 work, 2 rest — they rest more because the work is harder).

**Orcish (Sunderplains):** Count winters. This is Winter 1,247 to the orcs. (The fact that it matches the AR count is a coincidence that deeply annoys elven scholars.) Orcs don't track months. They track moons. Twelve moons. Each named for a predator. The current moon: the Spider Moon. Gorath would appreciate the irony.

**Draconic:** Dragons do not count years. Years count themselves. The Reckoning at the Dragonspine happens "when it happens." That it happens to fall on a human millennial boundary is because Kaeltharion is polite enough to align with mortal calendars. Vorathiel considers this weakness.

## CULTURAL GAP — KENJI

Kenji has been in this world for eight months and has never:
- Celebrated a holiday
- Observed a Stillday (he doesn't know rest days exist)
- Attended a Harvest Week market
- Seen a Leyrise bonfire
- Experienced the Still Night
- Known what month it is (he counts days, not dates)

He arrived in spring. Fought through spring. Conquered in spring. Vanished in spring. Hid through summer and early autumn. The entire cultural calendar happened around him while he was either at war or alone.

The Season of Dark is coming. The Still Night — the holiday about family, warmth, and saying what you've been carrying — is seventeen days away. Four women are carrying or raising his children. Twenty women have his ring on their finger (metaphorically). One man in Crestfall is managing his empire. And the ronin just destroyed an entire undead relay network, slept with the shield infantry assigned to protect him, and is walking back to Thornkeep with a woman whose eyes turned gold overnight and a scout pretending he didn't hear five hours of it. He doesn't know what day of the week it is.

This is a narrative thread. The first time Kenji encounters a holiday — hears villagers preparing, sees decorations, smells festival food — he has to learn what the world celebrates and why. And he has to decide if he has anyone to celebrate with.
