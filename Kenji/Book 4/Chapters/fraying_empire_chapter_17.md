# Fraying Empire — Chapter 17: *Paper and Smoke*

**Session span:** Ashmere 31 morning (Day 253), opens immediately after Chapter 16’s **Thornkeep Waystation** long rest.

### Provisions like a siege you can eat

**Ashmere 31**, mid-morning: he bought **luxury** the way border towns sell it — **iced jars**, **honey-cakes**, **spice-cured meats**, **salt-fish** — enough **preserved** weight to mock hunger for roughly **thirty days**, then stowed the lot in the **Bag of Holding** where it would not **perfume** every checkpoint.

The coin hurt cleanly (**240 GP**), which was also a way to pretend he was still the kind of man who solved problems with **ledger** instead of **blade**.

### Garrison approach

He walked **inn → smoke → stone** until tabards looked **real** again: runners, chalk slates, the **second floor** smell of **ink and iron**.

He meant to **meet** the commander.

What he did first was **smoke** — a **clone** left with the **desk sergeant** in a waiting posture polite enough to buy minutes — while the real body went **invisible** up the seam where **timber pretends it is not watching**.

### Ceiling politics

**Bracken’s office** was not empty.

**Jostin** read numbers off the world into a journal margin. **Brynn** stood like a shield line remembered muscle even without the shield on her arm. Both were **reporting**: split order, nodes dead, relay **blackout**, **Kenji** south solo into the cordon, **Corwyn** disarmed, **Oathbreaker** taken — facts spoken the way soldiers speak them when they are trying not to make a disaster **larger**.

Bracken’s questions were **boring in the right way** — sightlines, stagger, where the **silence** started — until she **stopped** mid-sentence and scanned the room the way a captain scans a gate line.

**“If there’s a listener, speak like a soldier — or leave like one.”**

### Chair

He dropped **invisibility** like smoke deciding it had never been solid.

He took the **spare chair** — garrison offices always have one extra for bad news — and sat with his hands **laced behind his head**, elbows out, the posture of a man who wanted the room to **file him** correctly.

**Jostin’s** pen stopped.

**Brynn’s** body did the trained **half-step** before discipline locked her still.

**Bracken** did not grab her sword first. She grabbed **policy** first — and looked at Kenji the way a receipt looks at ink.

Downstairs, the **sergeant** was about to learn he had been babysitting **smoke**.

### Civilian

Kenji spoke like a man daring the room to laugh so it would not shoot.

**“Don’t mind me,”** he said. **“I’m just a civilian listening to top-secret military intel.”** A beat — too cheerful for innocence. **“I think your undead problem is resolved, though.”**

**Jostin** closes his eyes for one second — the way a man counts backward from ten without moving his lips.

**Brynn** makes a sound that could become a **laugh** if discipline did not strangle it at birth.

**Bracken** does not smile. Her jaw works once, like she is chewing **paper** and **steel** at the same time.

**“Civilian,”** she repeats, tasting the lie. **“Intel.”** Another taste. **“Resolved.”**

She leans forward, palms flat on the desk — not fast, not theatrical; the motion a commander uses when she wants a man’s **spine** to remember **rank**.

**“The road had quiet,”** she says, **“quiet is not dead. Blackout is not peace. And you —”** her eyes flick to the ceiling he came from, **“— are not a farmer lost in a hallway.”**

She straightens — not relaxing, **choosing** the next shape of the moment.

**“You will stop performing,”** she says, **“and start reporting — or you will tell me which cell you prefer while I send someone downstairs to explain to my sergeant why he was babysitting smoke.”**

### Turkey

**“Ah,”** Kenji says, like she handed him a gift. **“The cell kink again.”**

The **Bag of Holding** coughs up **roast turkey** wrapped like honest sin — honey-skin, still warm enough to lie about how physics works. He tears a piece with his fingers and eats like this is a tavern and not a **command office** with two witnesses who are trained to remember **everything**.

**Jostin** writes one word in the margin anyway — not a joke, a **category**.

**Brynn** stares at the turkey like it personally insulted the **Coalition**.

**Bracken** goes very still. Stillness, in her, is not peace. It is **where orders come from**.

**“Put,”** she says, **“the bird down.”** Each syllable has a weight class. **“Or I will have you put down with it.”**

He finishes the **turkey** anyway — not fast, not slow; the way a man finishes a meal because hunger is older than rank.

Then the room learns what **extradimensional silence** sounds like when it **breaks**.

The **greatsword** comes out of the **Bag of Holding** like a secret forced into daylight: broad, brutal, **wrong** in a garrison office the way a live grenade is wrong on a dinner table. Runes and old oaths cling to the steel — **dual-bound**, holy and necrotic braided until the eye refuses a single moral color.

He wipes **grease** off his fingers along the blade — a casual filth — and the metal answers with something too soft to be a **hum** and too clean to be a **voice**.

Not words. **Pressure**. Like something inside the steel remembered it used to be **spoken to** with respect.

He slides it back into the **bag** as if the moment never happened.

He **stares** at **Bracken**.

**“I’m sorry,”** he says, pleasant as poisoned wine. **“What was that about a bird?”**

**Jostin** stops breathing on purpose — scout trick — so he does not miss a detail.

**Brynn’s** hand finds empty air where a shield should be, then finds her **belt** anyway, because the body does not care about missing kit when the world becomes **simple**.

**Bracken** does not shout.

Her eyes do the shouting — **Bond-Form Sight** doing what it was built to do when a man brings **proof** into a room: not the proof of his joke, the proof of his **war**.

She sees the shape of what was on that steel the way a surgeon sees **infection** under skin.

**“Out,”** she says — not to Kenji first. To **Jostin** and **Brynn**, voice flat as a drawn blade. **“Now. Shut the door. Post outside. No runners. No ears.”**

When the latch clicks, the office becomes **small**.

She looks at Kenji like she is deciding whether the next minute is **debrief** or **detainment**.

**“That,”** she says, **“was not a bird.”**
