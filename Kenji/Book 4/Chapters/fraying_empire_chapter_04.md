# Fraying Empire — Chapter Four: *Millhaven*

*Book 4 · The Ronin arc · Kenji*

---

## Chapter Four: Millhaven

### Ashmere 19-22, 1247 AR — South Road & Millhaven

---

### The Road

---

Otten Dray. Dry goods. Flour, salt, lamp oil. Eleven years on the Millhaven circuit. Two mules, a cart, a crossbow, and a dog that sleeps through everything.

Three silver a day for a guard. I take the bench. The dog sniffs my boot. Decides I'm acceptable.

Two days south. The road is good — packed earth, moderate traffic. Autumn farmland turning gold and copper. The first frost came and went. The puddles have thin ice at dawn.

A boar in the road. Hour one. Big. Three hundred pounds. Diagnostic Touch reads it healthy from a hundred yards. It looks at the cart and decides we're not worth the trouble.

A stone marker off the road. Hour two. Old. Covered in moss. Living Ground reads something underneath — a foundation. Ancient. Pre-civilizational. The kind of stone nobody builds with anymore. Filed.

Wolves at the treeline. Hour five. Three of them calculating whether mules are worth the fight. They see the silhouette on the cart bench and leave. Whatever they read said *not worth it*.

Otten talks. Traders talk the way rivers flow — constant, low, following the path of least resistance. He tells me about Millhaven. About the soldiers who went to Thornfield and didn't come back. About the Ashenveil marshes. About the ArchMagus who vanished and the world falling apart.

"Strange times. The big hero disappears and the world starts falling apart. Almost like he was the only thing holding it together."

The fire pops. I don't answer.

Night camp behind a stone wall where traders have stopped for decades. Old fire rings. Watch rotation — I take first, Otten and the dog take second. Both watches clean. The road is quiet. The dog doesn't bark because there's nothing to bark at.

Women on the road. Ten over two days. Every one of them looks too long when we pass. Every one of them blinks and goes back to their conversation and forgets the masked swordsman by dinner. One farmer's wife — nat 20 — doesn't look twice. The world's highest compliment: being unremarkable to someone who's seen enough.

---

### The Gate

---

Millhaven. Stone walls on a hill. Four guards at a checkpoint. A queue. Otten pulls his trade license.

"Weapons check. All bladed weapons shown at the gate."

I hand the scabbarded nodachi to the female guard. She draws it for inspection. Stops halfway.

"What is this. The color is *in* the metal. This grain — crystalline. Like it was grown, not forged."

She finishes the draw. Examines it. Sheathes it. Hands it back. Her gauntlet brushes my hand.

"Peacebond it inside the walls."

She ties the cord. Close. Professional. Mostly.

I tell her drinks are on me after shift. Find me at the bar.

---

### Taryn

---

The Broken Wheel. Garrison tavern. Rough. Loud. I wait at the bar.

She walks in at seventh bell. Out of armor. Dark hair loose. Late twenties. Lean. The build of a woman who's drilled with a halberd since she was sixteen. A burn scar on her wrist. A scar across her eyebrow from something she doesn't talk about.

She sits down. Doesn't look at me yet. Orders ale. Drinks.

"I don't do this. I don't meet strangers at bars after shift. My name's Taryn."

Private room. Roast duck. Honey mead. She eats with her hands. Tears the wings off. Talks with her mouth full.

"Your blade's balanced wrong. Tip-heavy like that, you're committing everything to the first cut. If you miss, you're dead. Which means you don't miss."

She sucks the bone clean.

"Arm wrestle me."

---

I take her hand. She pushes. The arm that's beaten thirty-one garrison soldiers — men who outweigh her by sixty pounds — pushes with everything it has.

My arm doesn't move.

I don't push back. I hold her hand and look at her eyes. My thumb traces across her knuckles. I start talking. Casual. How long she's been a guard. What she knows about the area. Jobs that pay real money.

She keeps pushing for about twenty seconds. Then she stops pushing, looks at our hands, looks at my face, raises an eyebrow, and lets her grip soften — contest grip to just a grip. Holding on.

*"Oh,"* she says, half a smile. *"It's like that."*

I lower her arm slow while she tells me about the Ashenveil and the missing caravan and the wolves and the ruin. Her hand touches the table gentle. The softest pin. She leaves her hand in mine after.

*"Thirty-one and five,"* she says quiet. Grinning now. *"The other four losses made me angry. This one did not."*

---

### The Blacksmith

---

I tell her I want to buy her a polearm. The best one available.

"We've known each other for THREE HOURS—"

Holsk. Dwarven blacksmith. Garrison road. Four-foot-eight. Shoulders wider than the doorframe. He smells the abyssal shard nodachi from across the room.

"That's not standard steel in there. Crystalline lattice. Resonant alloy. Abyssal adjacent. Deep-forged."

He opens the door wider. Looks at Taryn. Reads her like metal — the calluses, the stance, the burn scar pulling the wrist, the half-second on the return stroke.

Custom commission. Mythril alloy. Rune-tempered. Three enchantment slots built into the crystal structure during forging. The weapon grows with the wielder. Forty gold. A week to forge.

I put the gold on the table.

Taryn stares at the lockbox where forty gold disappeared.

"Three hours. Three *hours*."

---

### The Inn

---

She takes the mask off herself. One hand. Quick. Looks at my face for two seconds.

*"Yeah. I'm keeping this. Just so we're clear."*

Fast. Hard. The bed frame hits the wall. She doesn't make noise — she breathes through exertion. The way she breathes through a drill.

After — she stretches her hand out, flexes the wrist, rotates it. Looks at the old burn scar. Rotates again. A slow grin starts.

*"Huh. My wrist doesn't hurt. It's hurt for eleven years. Ronin. What."*

She says it happy, not alarmed. Turns the wrist a third time. Laughs once.

---

Morning. The bed is in four pieces. Three noise complaints. A note from the innkeeper. Room 4 asked if the room is available tomorrow night.

I take her again. Longer. At three-quarters. After, she traces a hand down her own arm — the burn scar is smooth skin. The eyebrow is whole. The old ankle fracture. The shoulder. Eleven years of garrison wear gone. Her skin tightens. Two years of age easing backward.

"All of them. All of them are gone."

She catches her reflection in the washbasin water.

"I look twenty."

---

She runs morning drill for fifty recruits. I watch from the rooftop, invisible. The halberd moves like nothing Hadley has ever seen from a gate guard. Twelve forms. No hesitation. No compensation.

Hadley writes something in her book.

"Taryn. Your eyebrow—"

"*Don't ask.*"

---

Evening. Grilled lamb. Rice. Curry. Ironveil whiskey. Private room.

She gives me the map. Four jobs worth real money. The Ashenmere caravan — 150 gold. The Ashenveil scouting — 200 gold from Hadley. The wolf pack — 100 gold in bounties. The pre-kingdom ruin — unknown, ancient, sealed by Hadley after two scouts went down and one transferred to the coast.

"The ruin is either nothing or everything. That's why Hadley sealed it."

---

Bath. Tub. Not luxury — a wooden basin behind a curtain. She heats the water herself. She bought contraceptive herbs from the garrison road apothecary because she's a soldier and she's practical about everything.

Three times. She takes the tea. The ember slips small on the third.

"There it is. The warm thing."

---

Morning. Ashmere 22. She laces her boots in the dark.

I give her twenty gold.

"If you die in that ruin I'm going to find your body and kill you again."

She leaves for east gate duty. Bootfalls on the stairs. Quick. Even. No ankle. No wrist. The soldier she always should have been.

The ruin is south. The noon patrol gap opens in four hours.
