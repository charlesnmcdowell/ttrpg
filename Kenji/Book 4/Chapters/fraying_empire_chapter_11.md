# Book 4 — Fraying Empire
## Chapter 11: The Voice from the Marsh

**Date:** Ashmere 27–28, 1247 AR (Crownday night → Stillday morning)
**Location:** South road, miles 10–12. Camp at mile 12. March north to mile 8.

**Kenji dialogue note:** All Kenji spoken lines in this chapter are **player-declared** at the table. Default rule remains no AI-generated Kenji dialogue.

---

### The Hub

---

South of mile ten the road died. Not corrupted — *abandoned.* The sentries were gone. The relay hum was gone. The frost sat undisturbed on a road no dead thing had walked in hours.

"It pulled everything back," Jostin said. He was writing without looking at the page. "Three nodes down, network dark, and nothing between us and whatever shut it off."

Brynn shifted closer to the ronin's shoulder. Not tactical distance. Bodyguard distance.

They walked two miles in silence.

---

At mile twelve, Living Ground found it. Underground. A structure larger than the three nodes combined — fitted stone, Pre-Kingdom architecture, but this one had *depth.* Multiple chambers. Something alive with necromantic energy at the center, pulsing like a heartbeat.

"That's not a relay," Jostin said. "That's the hub."

The entrance was a stone staircase cut into a hillside. Two wight sentinels flanked it — heavier than the road wights, armored in black plate that still held an edge. They tracked the ronin the moment he appeared.

He drew.

The first wight saw the blade arc and raised its longsword — too late. The iaido strike went through the guard and through the wight behind it. One hundred and twelve points of force. The armor crumpled like foil. Vaporized.

The second wight lunged. The on-kill chain caught it mid-step. Through the neck. Through the spine. It folded and crumbled.

Two kills. One breath.

"I'm going to stop being surprised," Jostin said. "Starting now."

---

### The Bone Architect

---

The hub was one large chamber thirty feet underground. Stone walls. No mortar. The relay stone at the center was three times the size of the node relays — a trunk line, the command node that fed the three capillaries they'd already destroyed.

And standing beside it: a construct.

Not undead. Not a skeleton, not a wight, not a revenant. A *construct* — bone-ceramic and Pre-Kingdom engineering. Humanoid shape, seven feet tall, hands articulated with dozens of joints. It was touching the trunk relay with both hands, fingers splayed across the channels.

It turned when they entered.

"Builders," it said. The voice was dry. Mechanical. Like stone grinding on stone. "You are not builders."

Jostin's bow came up. Brynn's shield snapped forward.

The ronin walked toward it.

"Who do you serve, skeleton guy?"

The construct tilted its head. The articulated fingers lifted from the relay stone.

"I maintain the network. The voice from the deep marsh gives instruction. I execute. Nineteen months. Three capillary nodes. One trunk line. The boundary was to extend north to the river by the solstice."

"The voice from the deep marsh," Jostin repeated. He was writing. "That's the Ashenveil. That's the Lych's territory."

The construct's head tilted the other direction. "I do not know names. I know the voice. The voice commands. I build."

"Take me to your leader."

"I cannot leave the hub. The hub is my function."

---

The ronin looked at Jostin. Jostin looked at the relay — the trunk line still pulsing, still feeding whatever remained of the network south.

"Destroy it," Jostin said. "The construct and the relay. Cut the head off."

The ronin drew.

The construct didn't defend itself. It stood with its articulated hands at its sides and watched the blade come. Two strikes — the first cracked it open, the second scattered the bone-ceramic across the chamber floor. It collapsed like a puppet with the strings cut. No backlash. No scream. Just silence and dust.

The trunk relay was different.

The first strike cracked the basalt. The channels screamed — louder than any node, a sound that vibrated in the teeth and the spine. The stored necromantic energy was *massive.* Nineteen months of accumulated command energy.

The backlash hit like a wall.

Twenty-five necrotic slammed through the ronin. He took it standing. Jostin caught thirty-three — hit the wall, slid down it, coughing blood. Brynn took twenty-one on the shield and her boots scraped back six inches on the stone.

The second strike unmade it. The basalt exploded. The bone-ceramic channels shattered to powder. The bruise-light died.

And Living Ground *bloomed.* Three hundred feet. Four hundred. The suppression that had strangled the ley network for nineteen months collapsed in a single breath. The soil remembered what it was. Root networks fired back to life. The dead zone was dying.

---

"Jostin." Brynn was already moving. She caught the scout before he hit the floor a second time. His face was grey. Blood at the corner of his mouth.

"I'm fine," he said. He wasn't fine. Twenty-eight out of seventy-eight hit points.

"The network's dead," the ronin said. "Permanently. That was the command source."

Jostin coughed. Wiped his mouth. Opened his journal with shaking hands and wrote: *Voice from the deep marsh. 19 months. The Lych.*

### Camp — Mile 12

---

They set up above the destroyed hub. Jostin built the fire — smokeless, hidden, garrison training. Brynn cooked. The ronin sat against an oak with the nodachi across his knees.

Long rest rules: can't benefit from more than one in twenty-four hours. Last rest ended around 5 AM Ashmere 27. Next available starting 9 PM.

"We camp twenty hours," Jostin said. "Rest at nine tonight. Move at dawn."

Twenty hours was a long time. But Jostin at twenty-eight HP and Brynn at fifty weren't going anywhere useful.

---

The afternoon passed slow. Jostin slept. Brynn kept watch — shield at her side, eyes on the tree line.

The ronin ate. Stared south. Thought about a voice from the deep marsh that had been building infrastructure for nineteen months without anyone noticing.

---

Evening. The fire burned low. Frost thickening on the grass. Jostin woke, ate, and crawled into his bedroll twenty feet from the fire.

"I'll take second watch if there is one," he said. Then he was gone. Twenty-eight HP will do that.

The ronin looked at Brynn. Brynn looked at the fire.

"If you're scared, I'll share tents with you."

**IP Check — DC 23. Direct flirtation. Eye contact. Firelight.**
**Brynn rolls: 2.**

The last wall fell.

"I'm not scared," she said. Her voice said otherwise.

---

### The Night

---

Kenji stripped down to his smallclothes and sat next to her. Hip to hip.

"It's warmer next to you. Hold me close and keep me warm?"

She opened the blanket without a word. Both arms around him. Full grip. Her chin on top of his head. Her heartbeat hammering against his cheek through thin linen.

"...warm enough?" Two words. Her voice cracked on the second one.

He turned to face her. Pressed his face into the warmth between her breasts.

"So soft... I can stay here forever."

Her arms tightened around his head. Her fingers slid into his dreads at the root. She pulled him closer.

"You have permission to ride me as much as you need tonight, soldier."

She didn't move for three seconds.

Then her hands moved.

---

She used him the way he told her to. Garrison discipline applied to endurance. She was not gentle. She was not quiet.

At one point Jostin coughed from his bedroll. Not a real cough. She didn't stop.

He used his full strength. STR 40. Every position. Every time she bruised he healed it — Diagnostic Touch reading the damage, creation energy erasing it before the marks could form. She felt herself getting stronger instead of weaker. Didn't understand why. Didn't care.

Around 1 AM she was on top again. Face to face. Forehead against his. Tears running down her cheeks.

"Don't stop. Don't ever stop."

He didn't.

Around 3 AM she passed out mid-kiss. Her body gave up what her discipline wouldn't — unconscious, still smiling, still holding him.

Jostin lay in his bedroll with his blanket over his head and contemplated his life choices. The sounds had ranged from alarming to genuinely concerning to quietly devastating. He thought about supply requisition forms. He thought about patrol routes.

He did not think about the fact that at 2 AM the sounds stopped for ten minutes and then started *again.*

*What IS he.*

### Morning — Ashmere 28

---

Grey light. Cold. Jostin had a fire going and broth in the pot. His eyes were bloodshot. His expression was aggressively neutral.

"Morning. Broth's ready. We should move by seven."

A pause. He glanced at the blanket situation. Looked away.

"...I'll be over here."

---

Brynn pulled on her armor and paused. The breastplate went up and over her head — one-handed. The buckles closed faster. The shield—

She picked it up. Stared at it. Forty pounds of riveted steel felt like a dinner plate.

"Brynn." Jostin was watching.

"What."

"Your eyes."

"What about them."

"They're gold."

She touched her face. "I'm fine." The tone said *do not pursue this.*

---

### The March North

---

"Alright guys, let's continue to the objective."

The road north. Heading back toward Thornkeep. The relay network was dead — the road felt *alive* for the first time in months. Grass pushing through frost. Lichen on barren stones. The corruption pulling back like a tide.

But there were stragglers. Masterless undead — skeletons and wights left on patrol when the network died. Wandering. Confused. Some standing in the road staring at nothing.

The ronin walked point but didn't draw. He nodded at Brynn and Jostin.

*Your kills.*

---

Mile 11. Four skeletons in a cluster. Jostin took three — clean, professional. Brynn hit the fourth with her shield.

The skeleton *exploded.* Bones scattered across fifteen feet.

"...what," Brynn said.

"Brynn, what the hell was that?"

"I don't *know.*"

---

Mile 10. Two wights at the destroyed Node 3 site. Brynn walked straight at them. Shield bash — the first wight flew ten feet and hit a tree. She was on it before it stood. Longsword through the ribcage, twist, pull. The second clawed her back — 4 damage. She barely felt it. Spun, shield-checked it to the ground, stamped on its skull.

Eight seconds.

Jostin looked at the ronin. The ronin was leaning against a tree. Arms crossed.

"I feel..." She rolled her sword wrist. The blade spun — a move she'd never had the wrist speed for. "I feel *good.*"

---

Mile 9. Six skeletons. Jostin took three. Brynn took three — and cleaved the last one shoulder to hip with one hand.

She looked at the two halves. She looked at her arm.

"Jostin."

"Yep."

"Don't tell the commander about this."

"About what."

"*Any* of this."

"Telling her about what, Brynn? I slept through everything. My eyes are bad. I don't know what color gold is."

She almost smiled. The first real expression all morning. It died fast — but it was there.

---

Mile 8. Mid-morning. The frost burning off. The treeline greening.

Brynn walked beside the ronin. Shield on her back. Her amber-gold eyes kept flicking to him — quick glances she thought he didn't catch.

He caught all of them.

Four miles to Thornkeep.
