# Book 4 — Fraying Empire
## Chapter 7: The Dead Road

**Date:** Ashmere 25-26, 1247 AR (Fieldday-Stillday)
**Location:** Thornkeep → South Road → The Gap → Off-road camp

---

### Thornkeep Waystation — Morning

---

I check on her.

The door opens halfway. She hasn't slept. Red-rimmed eyes, grey hair loose, the room behind her barely touched. The candle burned to a stub. She looks up and everything she spent seven hours building collapses — her shoulders drop, her hand tightens on the doorframe, her eyes find my neck and stay there.

She fights it. Jaw tight. Staring at the wall like it owes her something.

"Did you need something?"

Let's go. I'm taking her to the captain.

---

### Thornkeep Garrison

---

Commander Renna Bracken. Mid-forties. Dark brown hair threaded with grey, pulled back in a soldier's knot. A scar from ear to jaw. Practical steel under a coalition tabard, dented at the shoulder. Cold mug. Colder eyes. She reads the room in two seconds flat.

"Come in. Sit."

Elda sits. The portrait comes out. Halden. Twenty-four. Sandy hair. Kind face. South road from Ashenmere. Three days.

Bracken pulls a map off the wall. Her finger traces the route and stops on a twelve-mile stretch between two jurisdictions.

"There's a twelve-mile gap in the middle where nobody's watching."

She offers Jostin — her best scout, due back from patrol at midday. Fourteen soldiers. Thirty miles of border. No bodies to spare for a search party.

I tell her I fought four wights on the south road last night. That they were after my sexy grandma.

Bracken doesn't blink. She checks her logbook. Jostin logged two sightings this week. I killed four in one night. On foot. With a civilian.

"Where exactly on the south road?"

I let Elda handle the details. Any information that leads to her son is appreciated.

Bracken asks my name. I'm already moving.

---

### The South Road

---

Hop on. Max speed.

Elda's arms lock around my neck. Her face buries against my shoulder. Her pulse hammers through the fabric. Cloud-step, tree-kick, branch-launch. The road blurs.

Twenty minutes south. God Sight catches it first — four undead off the road, rusted mail, cold-fire signatures. They haven't seen me.

I set Elda down behind a fallen log.

"Gotta keep the roads safe. I'll be right back."

---

### Combat — South Road Patrol

**Enemies:** 2 Wights (45 HP, AC 14), 2 Skeleton Warriors (26 HP, AC 16)
**Advantage:** Player (stealth approach, enemies unaware)

The draw comes from nothing.

Wight A doesn't exist anymore. The Abyssal Shard catches it mid-step and the thing unravels — grey dust, rust flakes, a puff of cold air. Vaporized. One hundred and one damage. Gone.

The blade doesn't stop. On-kill momentum into Wight B — horizontal slash through the ribcage. Sixty-four damage. Dead before it registers.

The skeleton warriors turn. I swing on the nearest one — blade clips a raised shield edge. Miss.

Showman's Sheathe.

The nodachi twirls in a slow reverse arc. Tip into the scabbard. *Shhhhiiiing.* Left foot plants. Right hand on the guard. Freeze.

Skeleton Warrior A swings. A six against AC 19. Not close.

The pose breaks. Iaido draw-strike from the sheathe — guaranteed crit. Ninety-two damage. The bones scatter.

Skeleton Warrior B gets lucky — comes in low, catches me across the ribs. Seven damage. Stings.

My turn. One cut. The last skeleton comes apart at the spine.

Four undead. Two rounds.

**Results:**
- Wight A: VAPORIZED (iaido draw-strike, 101 crit)
- Wight B: DEAD (on-kill continuation, 64 damage)
- Skeleton Warrior A: DEAD (Showman's Sheathe counter, iaido draw-strike, 92 crit)
- Skeleton Warrior B: DEAD (Round 2 attack, 58 damage)
- Kenji: 7 damage taken (326/333 HP)
- EXP: +2,300

---

Elda's voice from behind the log: "Are you *hurt?*"

Naw. Just emotional damage of being separated from you. Hop back on.

---

### Finding Jostin

---

Jostin. Renna Bracken's best scout. Late twenties. Lean. Dark circles under his eyes. Scout's kit, short bow, a journal tucked into his belt.

I give him the details. Missing son. South road. Three days.

He checks his journal. Didn't see a solo traveler three days ago. The gap — twelve miles nobody patrols.

"The activity's getting worse. A month ago I'd see tracks once a week. Now it's every patrol. They're not wandering anymore. They're moving with purpose. Formations. Scouting patterns. I put it in my reports and Commander Bracken puts it in hers and it goes up the chain and nothing comes back down."

He'll search the gap today. If Halden's out there, he'll find sign.

I'm not waiting.

---

### The Gap — Soul Nexus Active

---

Living Ground. Amaris's gift. Plant tremorsense — 200 feet in every direction through the root network. Every footstep, every heartbeat, every body touching soil.

Trade Warmth. Delia's gift. Live and dead trade routes. The road tells you who walked it and when.

Both active. Both passive. Both invisible.

The road answers immediately.

Trade Warmth reads the stone and dirt beneath my boots and the answer comes back cold. Dead cold. No living feet have touched this stretch in three days or more. No merchants. No travelers. The road hasn't gone quiet — it's gone dark.

This is where Halden disappeared.

Then Living Ground pulses. East. 150 feet off the road. Something alive. Humanoid weight. Stationary. Weak signal. Not moving.

I head to the signal. Bring Elda.

---

### The Hollow

---

A lean-to of branches and bark between two fallen oaks. Amateur construction. Desperate.

Underneath it — a young man. Sandy hair. Thin face. Pale. Wide, terrified eyes. A broken branch held out like a weapon.

He looks exactly like the portrait.

Three days hiding in this hole. Lips cracked. Hands shaking. Clothes torn from thorns. He ran from grey things on the road and couldn't find his way back.

Elda sees him over my shoulder. The sound she makes isn't a word.

"*Halden —*"

"*Mom?*"

---

### The Long Watch

---

Emergency tent. Travel canvas, two branches, rope work. Hidden in the hollow. I hand Elda the food and take my post outside.

Twelve hours. Living Ground sweeps 200 feet in every direction. Trade Warmth reads the dead road fifty feet west. Hearthsense keeps the tent warm without a fire. No light. No smoke.

Nothing comes.

Inside, Elda feeds her son. Holds him. Talks to him in the voice mothers use when they're holding something together. He sleeps. Wakes. Cries. Sleeps again.

The gap is quiet.

---

### Night

---

I pull the tent flap back. Halden is asleep. Real sleep. Color in his face.

Elda is awake. She looks up and her whole body tenses. Her hands grip her knees. Her eyes go to my face, my neck, the strip of skin above my collar.

"You saved my son."

I walk up to her slowly. Hand on her waist, looking in her eyes. Slowly up — midsection, arms, shoulder. I pull her in.

She breaks. Arms around me, face against my chest, holding on like letting go will kill her. Crying. Breathing me in. She knows what she's doing and she can't stop.

"I don't know what's wrong with me."

I wrap the cloth around her eyes. Gently.

"It'll be alright. Time for you to get some rest. I'll be right behind you."

She lies on her side facing Halden. I lie behind her. The blindfold stays on. The aura has nowhere to go. Her breathing slows. Her hand stays on her son's shoulder. Her back stays against my chest.

She sleeps like she hasn't slept in years.

---

Around 4 AM, a fox noses around the hollow. Four small paws in the tremorsense. It sniffs the canvas. Leaves.

Hearthsense keeps the tent warm. No fire. No light. Three people breathing in a hidden hollow while the dead road sits empty fifty feet west.

---

**Ashmere 26. Stillday. Morning.**

Grey light through the canvas. Frost on the branches. Cold air.

Halden is sleeping. Color back. Breathing steady.

Elda hasn't moved all night.
