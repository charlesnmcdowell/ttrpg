She stopped walking for half a second — just long enough for the recognition to pass through her — then kept moving. Whatever questions she had, she held them behind her teeth. Orc discipline. Bench discipline. *Mursha* discipline.

Korrim took a seat at the parley ground — a stone bench the dwarves had dragged out for the occasion. Practical. No ceremony. He surveyed the field with the expression of a man who'd opened his front door and found every neighbor he'd ever had standing on his lawn.

Mursha sat beside him. Not behind. Beside. Korrim didn't correct her.

The parley ground was full.

Ankunyx looked around at everybody attending.

South side: Vess, dismounted, standing with her military escort and two scribes. Arms crossed. Jaw tight. The sealed diplomatic pouch still in hand.

East side: The Eldest, seated on nothing — the ground simply *rose* beneath her, moss and root forming a living chair. Faelindra standing at her right shoulder, hand on blade. Nimue at her left, barefoot, water-blue robes pooling on stone.

North edge: Mirenne under her parasol, Virelda at her elbow with the folio open. Two shadow-cloaked attendants behind.

Mountain trail side: Drazhara, standing, walking staff planted.

The ridges above: Ignis on one ridge, young red dragon, wings folded. Vorathiel on the opposite ridge — ancient red, enormous, her shadow falling across half the parley ground.

The valley below: Sixty thousand orcs. Silent. Watching.

Beside Ankunyx: Nyx, still and dark, green eyes reflecting every face. Skratch, perched on a rock, trying to look important.

At the dwarf bench: Korrim. Mursha. Side by side.

Every faction on the continent. One table.

"Hi everybody, thanks for showing up."

The silence that followed was extraordinary. A single human greeting dropped into a gathering of powers that could level mountains.

Vess's left eye twitched.

Nimue snorted — actually snorted — and covered her mouth with her hand. Faelindra elbowed her without looking.

Korrim's stone expression didn't change, but his eyes narrowed. He didn't know this man.

Mirenne's parasol tilted. Behind it, a thin smile. Virelda's quill paused over her folio.

Drazhara exhaled through her nose.

Mursha's jaw tightened. She knew that voice. The bond confirmed what her ears already told her. But she stayed still. Watching. Waiting to understand why she was sitting at a table with an elder dragon overhead and an elf queen across from her.

The Eldest regarded Ankunyx with an expression that might be amusement. Might be recognition. Ten thousand years — she'd seen kings and conquerors and gods open proceedings. None of them started with *hi*.

Vorathiel did not find it charming.

**"I did not come to listen to a human greet me."** Her voice shook the parley ground. Pebbles bounced. Vess's scribes flinched. **"I came for the black dragon. My daughter's mate. I smell it on you, little thing. I smell it** ***in*** **you."**

Her head lowered from the ridge. Closer. The heat from her breath reached the parley ground — hot wind that smelled like sulfur and old fire. Her amber eyes were the size of wagon wheels, fixed on Ankunyx.

**"Explain."**

Every head turned to Ankunyx.
