# Book 4 — Fraying Empire
## Chapter 10: The South Road

**Date:** Ashmere 27, 1247 AR (Crownday, morning)
**Location:** South road, miles 7.5–10

---

### Node 2 — Underground

---

The stairs went down twenty feet into fitted stone. Same Pre-Kingdom architecture — no mortar, ancient precision. The relay stone pulsed at the center of a single chamber. Bruise-colored necromantic light. Bone-ceramic channels carved in patterns that hurt to look at.

Jostin sketched the layout from the doorway. Brynn held the threshold — shield angled to cover the stairs behind them.

The ronin drew.

The first strike cracked the stone. The channels screamed. Stored necromantic energy backlashed outward — the cold wave hit all three of them. Jostin staggered. Brynn took it on the shield and held.

The second strike unmade it. The basalt split. The bone-ceramic shattered to powder. The bruise-light died.

Living Ground bloomed. The ley network started breathing again.

Circle-and-line symbol on the wall. Same hand. Same tool. Recent.

"That's two," Jostin said, writing. "Same marking at both. Same architecture. Same relay design. This is a network, not random outposts."

---

### The March — Miles 7.5 to 10

---

South. Deeper into the dead zone. The frost thickened as they moved. Temperature hadn't broken freezing. Wouldn't for another hour.

The ronin took point. Jostin behind. Brynn shield-right.

Mile eight. Empty road. The sentries that should have been here were already ash — cleared during the night march.

Mile eight and a half. Living Ground detected the dead zone boundary — the line where healthy soil stopped and drained earth began. The line had moved. South. Two hundred yards south of where it had been before Node 2 fell.

"The boundary's retreating," Jostin said, watching the ronin's head tilt. "As we destroy nodes, the dead zone contracts. The ley network reclaims the soil." He wrote it down. "If we destroy enough of them, the whole zone collapses."

Mile nine. The soil changed again. Deeper corruption. Living Ground degraded — two hundred feet to a hundred and fifty, to a hundred.

Mile nine and a half. Two skeleton sentries. Posted at the road edge. South-pointing. They turned when the ronin approached. Both skulls locked onto him.

He walked through them. Drew. Two cuts. Two kills. Didn't break stride.

"He didn't even slow down," Brynn said.

It was the longest sentence she'd spoken all morning.

---

### Node 3 — The Prepared Position

---

Mile ten. The tree line east of the road. They stopped.

Node 3 was different.

The structure was the same Pre-Kingdom architecture — fitted stone, no mortar, ancient precision. But the garrison around it was *double.* Twenty-one skeletons in three concentric rings of seven. The outer ring faced outward — sentries. The middle ring patrolled a circuit around the node entrance. The inner ring stood shoulder to shoulder at the threshold, shields up, swords drawn. They weren't wandering. They were **garrisoned.**

Five wights. One at each cardinal point of the outer ring, and one at the entrance behind the inner skeleton formation. The entrance wight was bigger than the others — heavier armor, a longsword that still had an edge. It hadn't moved in the twenty minutes they'd been watching.

Jostin closed his journal softly.

"It doubled the garrison. Node 1 had ten. Node 2 had fourteen. This one has twenty-six." He tapped the page. "That's not reinforcement — that's a *prepared position.* Something told them we were coming."

A pause. He glanced at the ronin sideways.

"You're going to circle counterclockwise. Start with the sentries. Work inward. I've seen it twice. The wights at the cardinal points are spaced forty yards apart — if you're fast enough they won't signal each other before you reach the next one."

He waited a beat.

"But you're going to do it anyway, even if I told you not to. So I wrote the fallback in case the inner ring collapses on Brynn and me while you're mid-sweep."

Brynn shifted her shield grip. Her eyes hadn't left the perimeter.

"There's food in the satchel if you want it before." A pause. "Sir."

---

### The Straight Line

---

Jostin opened his mouth.

The ronin was already gone.

No circle. No sweep. No counterclockwise anything. He walked *forward* — straight at the outer ring, nodachi across his back, left foot leading, thumb on the guard.

The first skeleton sentry turned. Rusted shortsword came up—

*Click.*

The draw. The cut. The skeleton bisected at the ribcage and the top half turned to ash before it hit the ground. Vaporized. The bottom half collapsed. The blade didn't stop.

The wight at the two o'clock cardinal saw the skeleton disintegrate and lunged — longsword high, corroded mail rattling, blue fire burning in empty sockets—

The on-kill chain caught it mid-stride. One hundred and five points of force through its midsection. The wight came apart like wet paper. Ash and rust and silence. Vaporized.

The chain didn't break.

---

Two more skeletons in the outer ring — draw, chain, kill. A second wight — chain, kill. Into the middle ring. Four skeletons collapsed inward from their patrol circuit and the blade took them one after another — chain, vaporize, chain, vaporize, chain, kill, chain, kill. The red-black steel hummed with each contact. Ash drifted sideways in the frozen air. The sound was rhythmic: the wet splitting of bone, the *hiss* of vaporization, and under it all, the soft mechanical *shhhing* of the blade continuing its arc from one target to the next without ever stopping.

Five. Ten. Fifteen.

The cardinal wights converged from both flanks — too late. By the time they reached the middle ring, the middle ring was ash. Chain-kill took them both. Seventy-five and a hundred and four through corroded plate. Both crumpled. Neither burned.

Outer ring remnants closed from behind. Four skeletons. They reached him the way moths reach a candle. Chain, vaporize. Chain, vaporize. Chain, kill. Chain, kill.

The middle ring survivors. Two. Both died without slowing the blade.

Twenty kills. Not one sheathe. Not one pause.

---

The inner ring.

Seven skeletons in a shield wall at the threshold. Shields up, swords drawn, formation tight. The tightest defensive formation the command source knew how to build. They watched twenty of their garrison die in fifteen seconds and they held position.

The chain ate through them like acid through linen.

First. Second. Third. Fourth. Fifth. Sixth. Seventh. Each one a single continuation strike — guaranteed crit through the shield, through the rusted mail, through the bone. The shield wall collapsed inward like dominos falling. Bone and rust and ash.

The entrance wight — the heavy one, the bigger armor, the longsword with an edge — brought its weapon up. The chain caught it in the shoulder. One hundred and seven points of force. It folded sideways and crumbled.

Twenty-six kills.

One round.

One breath.

---

Silence.

The frost settled on twenty-six piles of rust and ash and bone fragments. Some were already disappearing — the vaporized ones left nothing but dark smears on the frozen ground. Eight of them. Gone entirely. The others collapsed slowly, metal pieces separating from bone, the last animation leaving them like heat leaving a corpse.

The nodachi completed its arc. Slow. Deliberate. Across the right shoulder, tip skyward, rotating down behind the back.

*Shhhhiiiing.*

*Click.*

---

Jostin's pen was on the page. It hadn't moved in fourteen seconds. There was a line of ink pooling where the nib pressed too hard.

He looked at the empty field. He looked at the twenty-six kill zones. He looked at his prediction — *counterclockwise, start with sentries* — written in his journal, now completely wrong.

He crossed it out with a single line.

Wrote underneath: *Straight line. Through everything. 26. One round.*

He underlined *one round* twice.

---

Brynn's shield dropped two inches during the chain. She caught it. Barely. Her knuckles were white on the grip. Her eyes hadn't blinked. Her mouth was slightly open — the first expression she'd shown that wasn't garrison discipline.

She watched a man walk into a reinforced garrison of twenty-six undead and come out the other side without being touched. Not dodged. Not defended. *Untouched.* The enemies never got a turn. The blade never stopped moving. The ash was still settling.

She pulled the shield back up. Adjusted her grip. Didn't look at him.

Her hands were shaking.

---

### Node 3 — The Relay

---

The structure was identical to the first two — fitted stone, no mortar, Pre-Kingdom precision. The relay stone pulsed at the center. Bruise-colored necromantic light. Bone-ceramic channels.

He drew.

The first strike cracked it — eighty points through enchanted basalt. The channels *screamed*. The stored necromantic energy backlashed outward—

This one hit harder. Reinforced node, more stored energy. The cold wave slammed through the chamber.

Kenji took ten necrotic. Shrugged it off. Jostin staggered into the wall — seventeen necrotic. Coughed. Brynn took fourteen on the shield. Her boots slid back three inches on the stone.

The second strike unmade it. The basalt split into pieces. The bone-ceramic shattered to powder. The bruise-light died.

Living Ground *bloomed*. Two hundred feet in every direction. The ley network exhaled. The dead soil remembered what it was.

---

On the wall beside the doorway — the symbol. But this time it was different.

The circle-and-line was there. Same hand. Same scratch pattern. Recent. But next to it: a **diagonal slash** through the circle. A second marking. Something had been here *after* the first mark was made — and it added to the message.

Jostin saw it. His pen stopped again.

"That's new. The first two had the circle-and-line only. This one has an amendment." He sketched it. "Something came back to this node and *updated* it. Recently. The scratch depth is the same — same tool, same hand. But the slash is newer than the circle. Days, not weeks."

He looked at the ronin.

"Whatever's marking these nodes is revising its notes."

---

### The Blackout

---

And then everything went quiet.

Not the quiet of a dead relay. Not the quiet of a destroyed node. *Everything.*

Living Ground swept south — two hundred feet of restored range — and found **nothing.** No pulse. No hum. No distant necromantic rhythm bleeding through the soil. The entire relay network had gone **silent.** Every node south of here, however many there were, shut down simultaneously.

Total blackout.

Jostin felt it too — the ambient hum that had been under everything since mile five, gone. Like a room where every machine turns off at once.

"That's not a node dying," he said slowly. "That's the whole network going dark. On purpose." He closed the journal. Opened it again. Wrote one word:

*Thinking.*

Then crossed it out and wrote: *Decided.*

---

The south road stretched into grey morning light. Empty. Silent. The frost was thick enough to crack under every step. Three destroyed nodes behind them. An unknown number ahead — but nothing was talking anymore.

Whatever was running this network had just turned everything off at once.

Brynn shifted her shield. Closer than tactical. She hadn't corrected it.

"What now?" Jostin asked. His pen was ready. "The road goes south. The network's dark. Either we're walking into nothing — or we're walking into whatever decided twenty-six wasn't enough."
