*Book 4 · The Ronin arc · Kenji*

## Chapter Five: The Eastbound Run

### Ashmere 23-24, 1247 AR — Millhaven & Beyond

---

Ashmere 22 I'd left the ruin south of Millhaven unopened — Bracken's seal, the noon window, Taryn on the wall expecting me to care about old stone before anything else. I didn't go. The letter from the bandit camp and the circle where ten wagons stopped existing both pointed east; the border Seravane drew wasn't where Bracken thought it was. East first. One night to sleep, then the road.

---

### The Eastbound Run

---

Dawn. Ashmere 23. Fieldday. I leave the Broken Wheel before first light. Smoke bomb in the alley. The clone takes the east gate at walking pace — the ronin heading out on business. I follow invisible, 120 feet behind. The aura doesn't fire. Nobody sees the real me. The ghost walks the road and the road doesn't notice.

Four hours east. The trade road thins. The farmsteads go dark. Shuttered. Abandoned. Nobody maintains this stretch anymore. Road Sense confirms — the trade warmth is dead. Whatever killed this route did it thoroughly.

---

Hour two. A shallow valley. Four men in the trees — leather, shortswords, a crossbow. Road bandits. Classic ambush. They step out and surround the clone.

"Stop. Weapons on the ground."

The clone bolts. Runs east. They chase. Four desperate men running after a ghost that doesn't breathe.

I loot the camp while they're gone. Twelve silver. A jade hairpin. A sealed letter — Ashenmere merchant house sigil, tower and crescent. Pocketed. Thirty seconds. No trace.

---

### The Caravan

---

Off-road. South of the east road. Living Ground finds the tracks — six sets of wagon ruts cutting through a harvested field. Loaded wagons moving fast. Not on a path. Running.

The tracks stop. All six. Simultaneously. Like they hit a wall.

A sixty-foot circle of churned earth. Hooves. Boots. Something heavier. Signs of struggle. No bodies. No wagons. No horses. No cargo.

Ten people and six loaded wagons entered this circle three weeks ago and ceased to exist.

At the center — a post. Wooden. Sharpened. Black cloth at the base. A parchment nailed to it.

*The living pass through my borders carrying things that belong to the living. This is permitted. The living pass through my borders carrying weapons pointed at my domain. This is not.*

*Ten carried swords. Ten were collected. Their goods feed the March. Their horses pull the carts. Their bodies stand in the column.*

*— the Lady.*

The border isn't where Bracken thinks it is. It's here. Fifteen miles north. Seravane moved it.

---

### The Ashenveil

---

Three hours southeast. The road dies. The smell starts. The trees stunt. The mist rises. Seravane's border stakes — every thirty feet, black cloth, another note:

*Beyond this line the living serve or the living fall. There is no third option.*

I cross. Invisible. The clone leads.

Thirty minutes in — the root network reacts. Every dead root in a hundred feet twitches. A signal sent. She knows something entered. Not what. Not who. But something living stepped on a nerve.

The column. Hundreds of them. Standing in knee-deep water. Man-shaped. Armor. Not moving. Facing south. In formation. The dead of a hundred years waiting for orders. Among them — four skeletons in Millhaven garrison steel. Bracken's soldiers. The ones she sent to Thornfield. Walking patrol in the armor they died in.

I take what I can without touching the bodies. A shield — silver inlay, anti-turning ward. A ring — gold, blue stone, preservation enchantment. Pocketed.

---

A clearing. Higher ground. A fire in a stone ring. A death knight.

Sir Corwyn the Fallen. Black plate. Polished. A greatsword called Oathbreaker — Steel Sight reads it screaming. Two signatures in the metal. One holy. One necrotic. The sword is in pain.

He looks at the clone. Studies it.

"An image. Someone's sending projections into my lady's domain."

Behind the log — an iron chest. Warded. Pre-kingdom preservation enchantment. Something inside.

I move while he's talking to the ghost. Wind Step. Hands on the chest. STR 37 lifts it like air.

The handle creaks.

He hears it. Turns. The greatsword coming off the shoulder.

Tied initiative. I'm faster. Bag of Holding open. Chest goes in. Wind Step — seventy meters of swamp in one breath.

"THIEF."

The clone stays. Walks deeper into the marsh. Corwyn chases the ghost. The column reactivates. The root network fires. Everything in the Ashenveil chases a phantom south while I walk north.

Four hours of illusion duration. They can chase it until dusk.

I walk out. Invisible. Clean. The marsh behind me waking up and screaming at nothing.

### Holsk's Forge — Dawn, Ashmere 24

---

Stillday. The custom halberd is ready. The thrum wanted to be finished today — the metal did half of Holsk's work for him. Two hours for the final fold and the enchantment inscriptions.

Three runes. Featherfall. Truestrike. Bondseal. Thrum-alloy head on dark ash haft. The weapon hums when Taryn takes it.

She doesn't make a sound. She goes quiet. The kind of quiet that means something landed deeper than words.

Mythril-alloy half-plate. Refitted. Dark steel with blue-silver sheen. Holsk shaped the shoulders to her deltoids. The articulated joints move with her body.

She stands in Holsk's forge looking like something the war colleges draw in textbooks.

"Acceptable," Holsk says. From him, that's a love letter.

One hundred and five gold for the rush and the armor. Worth it.

---

### The Wolf Pack

---

I do the clone trick. The ronin spectating from a boulder with crossed arms. Invisible me watching from elsewhere.

The oak valley. Sixteen wolves. One warg matriarch. Three pups in a den.

Taryn takes the choke point. Plants the halberd. Whistles.

---

Round 1. Action Surge.

The wolves charge. The halberd sings. The thrum resonates on every stroke — a hum that vibrates the air after the blade passes through it.

The first wolf enters 15 feet. The pike point takes its skull. Reaper's Chain fires. The return stroke catches the next wolf mid-leap. Chain. The axe blade opens the third from shoulder to hip. Chain. The butt end cracks the fourth's ribs on the backstroke. Chain.

The chain doesn't stop. Every kill feeds the next swing. Every swing finds the next wolf. She's not fighting sixteen wolves — she's running a harvester through a corridor of fur and teeth and none of them can touch her. The choke compresses them and the 15-foot reach means they die before they reach her. Sixteen wolves. One round. Six seconds. The Reaper's Chain cascaded through every single one. The halberd sang sixteen times and every note was a death.

---

The Warg Matriarch. Alone. She howled but her pack was already dead.

Round 2. Polearm Defense. Free dodge. The Matriarch's bite found air. Taryn's halberd found flesh — three hits, two Grievous Wounds. The mythril edge opened the black fur and the cuts didn't close. Severe bleeding. Fifteen percent of max HP per turn. Per wound. Stacking.

Round 3. The Matriarch closed. Bit her. Clawed her. 36 damage. Taryn took it standing. Hit back. Two more Grievous Wounds. Four stacks now. Eighty damage per turn in blood loss.

The Matriarch bled out looking at the woman with the singing weapon and the brown eyes that said *I've been waiting for this my entire life*.

Victory Rush. Full heal.

Taryn standing in the valley. Sixteen dead wolves. A warg at her feet. 97 out of 97. Not a scratch.

"I'm not practicing."

### Bracken's Office

---

The clone sits next to Taryn during the briefing. Nods at the right moments.

Sixteen pelts on the floor. Warg skin unrolled like a black carpet. Warg teeth on the desk.

Bracken looks at the woman who was a gate guard four days ago.

"You did this today."

"Took about six minutes, ma'am."

Eighty gold for the wolves. Twenty for the warg. Then the letter. Bracken's seal. Taryn's name.

Letter of commission. Authority to present Ashenveil intelligence to the Coalition Council. Scouting bounty — four seventy-five now, five thousand pending. All in Taryn's name.

Taryn looks at the letter. Looks at the clone she thinks is me.

"You gave it to me. All of it. The credit. The gold. The career."

The clone nods.

---

### The Cold Hand

---

Main street. Walking to the Broken Wheel. Taryn reaches for the clone's hand.

Cold. No pulse. No warmth.

She stops.

"Your hand is always warm."

She squeezes. The fingers don't squeeze back.

"This isn't you."

She looks at the south gate. The empty road. The dark.

"You're already gone."

---

### The South Road

---

Over the wall. Wind Step. Silent. The Windstrider boots on the south road. Stars above. First frost on the grass.

The iron key hums in the storage ring. The ley tether pulls south-southwest. Something waiting. Something sealed. Something the rings were holding back.

Behind me — Millhaven's lanterns getting smaller. A forge glow. Warm windows. A woman in mythril half-plate standing in the street holding a letter of commission and a handful of cold air.

The Still Night is twenty-one days away. Wynn told me about it over stew. The holiday about family and warmth and saying the things you've been carrying.

I don't have family. I don't have a home. I have an iron key pulling south and a road I've never walked and a bag with a stolen chest inside it.

The key pulls. I walk.
