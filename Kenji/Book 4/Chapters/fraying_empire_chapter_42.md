### I. The Underhall

---

The Underhall Express — dwarven ore transit, industrial carts on iron rails, crystal lights pulsing in the tunnel walls. Six hours beneath the Kharnic range, carrying every power on the continent toward a hole in reality.

Ankunyx looked around the carts at his people.

**"Has vigor run out for you guys yet?"**

Nyx stretched, tapping her sternum. "Seven stacks. Expires around Day 304. Fourteen days left. I'm at god-tier. I'm *fine*."

Ignis flexed her fingers. "Day 288. The Kettle. Three days left."

Mursha said nothing. Twenty-four days expired. Orc pride.

Vess — quiet, white-knuckled. "Long expired." Slate blue-grey eyes. No teal.

Elara flushed. "Same."

Sera closed her journal. "Expired."

Mirenne's voice drifted from the rearmost car: "Day 281. Nine days cold."

Nyx moved through the carts. No announcement. No ceremony. A brush of the hand here. A vial pressed into a palm there. Crystal vials, warm, sealed with black wax and a root sigil. One for Vess. One for Elara. One for Sera. One for Mursha. One each for Mirenne and Virelda.

Ankunyx stared at her.

**"Oh. That's what you've been doing in the bathroom."**

Nyx didn't look up. "You produce a resource. I manage logistics."

Mursha uncorked hers, drank it in one pull, crushed the empty crystal in her fist. No hesitation. Orc efficiency. The cart groaned under renewed strength.

Korrim stared at the tunnel ceiling. "I am not going to ask. No one is going to explain this to me. Those are my terms."

His scribe was writing furiously.

"*Stop writing.*"

The scribe did not stop writing.

### II. The Seam

---

They surfaced on the Ashfeld Reach — high plateau, grey-green scrubland, wind-scoured. The seam was visible from two miles. A crack in the air, forty feet tall, twelve feet wide, shimmering with the absence of light. Around it: a perfect circle of death. Two hundred feet of grey soil, grey stone, everything uncolored — not burned, not withered, *unwritten*.

Nyx's roots stitched across the seam's face were failing. Arrhythmic. Some going grey at the tips.

"It's worse than I left it," Nyx said. Angry, not afraid.

The Eldest sang one note. The seam *screamed* in response.

"The ward-songs my people set here three thousand years ago are gone. Not weakened. *Consumed*. The Fathom ate them. It is learning to eat magic."

Vorathiel shifted to humanoid form for the approach. The air superheated, then snapped cold, and there she was — tall, bronze-brown skin, black hair with copper-red streaks and silver at the temple, ridged horns, clawed hands, sheer red robe over black lace, not bothering to look human. An ancient dragon tolerating a shape.

Ankunyx looked. Really looked. Through God Sight, her aura was a bonfire — red-gold, layered with centuries, the most power he'd ever read on a living creature. And the IP was firing. Stack 1. She had no idea.

Mirenne appointed to coalition council — voluntary vampire conversion law established, forced turning criminalized, cure research ordered with Nyx's assistance.

Mursha confirmed the dwarven runestone ring sixty feet below — sixty leystone anchors in a circle, channels cracked in four places but repairable.

**"Is this tear you guys are trying to weld a space anomaly or a magical anomaly? Also, is it a force of nature or sentient?"**

INT check: 83 vs DC 18. Crushing pass.

He answered his own question. Both. The seam was dimensional — reality's foundation cracked by the deep dig. The Fathom was a sentient entropy — a living process of unmaking that learned from everything it consumed. It ate magic but couldn't eat biology as fast. Dragonfire was biological, not arcane. The weld order: living roots as framework, wards sung into roots (not stone), dragonfire to cauterize.

**"I think I know a way to close this, guys. Anybody have a piece of paper and a quill?"**

### III. The Goat

---

INT check: 83 vs DC 25. Pass.

Ankunyx sat on a flat rock. Took Sera's quill and page. Wrote the goat poem — the stupid childhood verse he'd carried since before he could read. Eight lines. Simple words.

Threaded into the meter: a recursive mathematical cipher. Each line's syllable pattern encoded a prime factorization referencing the next line, folding back into the first, generating layers that required holding all previous layers while computing the next. Complexity scaling factorially. By the eighth line: a googolplex operations to decode.

Easy to write. Impossible to solve.

He walked to the seam.

**"Hey. Eat this."**

He dropped the paper into the crack.

The hum changed. Stuttered. The steady pressure that had been building for months went arrhythmic — something trying to swallow a bone that wouldn't go down. The Fathom, compelled by its nature to decode before consuming, had found something it couldn't decode and couldn't move past.

Nyx's roots pulsed stronger. "It's *distracted*. The pressure dropped."

"The unmaking has paused," the Eldest said. "It found something it cannot unmake because it cannot understand it."

**"I fed it a logic bomb disguised as a poem. It'll chew on that until the heat death of the universe."**

Korrim: "You just weaponized a nursery rhyme against a dimensional horror."

### IV. The Forge Weld

---

Six hours. The work proceeded in layers.

Underground: Korrim's twelve-dwarf engineering team repaired the four cracked channels. Nyx grew new roots through the sixty-runestone ring — biological framework the Fathom couldn't instantly consume. The Eldest sang wards into the living wood, binding magic to biology.

Above ground: Ignis and Vorathiel prepared the cauterization. Dragon form for both — Ignis in the young red body (Drazhara swapped to dragonborn form), Vorathiel expanding to her full ancient scale.

The weld: sustained biological dragonfire on the seam's edges while Nyx's roots held the framework and the Eldest's wards prevented regrowth. The Fathom, still chewing on the goat poem, couldn't push back at full strength.

Forty feet to thirty. Thirty to twenty. Twenty to ten.

The last two feet: the Fathom abandoned the poem for one desperate surge. Nyx screamed underground — roots snapping, grey decay racing. The Eldest's song cracked and reformed. Vorathiel roared — sixteen centuries of fire in a single blast that turned the air white.

The seam closed.

Silence. Not the absence of sound — the absence of the *hum*. The subsonic wrongness that had pressed against this plateau for centuries was gone. Where the seam had been: a scar of fused, blackened reality, cauterized shut, threaded with roots from below and sealed with wards.

**Threat 4 — Hollowing / Fathom: RESOLVED.**

### V. Great Work, Ma'am

---

Vorathiel shifted back to humanoid form. The IP suppression released — Stack 4, accumulated over six hours, slamming back at once. She staggered. Caught herself on a rock with a clawed hand. Gold eyes wide. Breathing fast. The sheer red robe clinging to bronze-brown skin.

Ankunyx walked over. Asked for a moment of her time.

**"Great work, ma'am."**

"Don't call me ma'am. I am your mother-in-law. There is a difference."

**"So you approve of me now?"** He reached out and poked her in the arm.

She went still. The gold eyes dropped to where his finger met her skin. Her pupils went round — fully human round, dragon suppression cracking.

"I moved my vote. I burned a seam shut. I stood in humanoid form and listened to you call Korrim an *elder asshole*. If that is not approval, I don't know what your generation requires."

She didn't move away from the poke.

**"Dragons don't do foreplay."** The grin.

Silence. Smoke from her nostrils. Gold eyes blazing.

"No. We don't."

The portal opened behind her. Green-gold light — the granite shelf near the Dragonspine peaks.

He stepped forward. She stepped back. Through.

The portal closed.

### VI. The Mountain

---

The granite shelf. High altitude. Stars in the eastern sky. The same ledge where Ignis mated with the black dragon two days before.

What followed broke the mountain.

Vigor transferred five times. Each stack cascading through Vorathiel's Level 38 draconic biology — stats that were already astronomical spiking past comprehension. Her eyes shifted: gold bleeding to deep violet. The green Vigor glow appeared at her abdomen, pulsing through ruined lace.

Soul Nexus Bond #25 activated. Dragon God buff restructured: 1/day → **at will**, no cooldown.

She took control. Vigor-enhanced strength drove Ankunyx's HP from 6,288 to fifty percent — through sheer physics. The granite cracked. The mountain shook. He triggered the black dragon form out of survival instinct — the first non-combat transformation.

She shifted with him. Ancient red — eighty feet, twice his size. They took flight. Dragons locked together, spiraling into thin air like eagles. She climaxed twice more in flight — each roar stronger than the last, the final one parting the clouds in a perfect circle, thunder heard in three countries.

Ankunyx in dragon form: thirty percent health. 1,886 of 6,288. An ancient red in five-stack Vigor frenzy had done what no enemy in four books had managed — brought him to his knees through desire.

They fell like eagles. Separated. Landed. Transformed.

She walked to him. Pushed him down. Slowly, gently — a control that made everything before it look like preamble. The last Vigor transferred on a shudder and a sigh. Sixteen hundred years of sigh.

She fell asleep on him. On the mountain. Off the beaten path. For six hours.

He didn't wake her. He didn't dare.
