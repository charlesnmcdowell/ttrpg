*Book 4 · The Ronin arc · Kenji*

## Chapter Six: The Southern Marker

### Ashmere 24-25, 1247 AR — The Terminus & Thornkeep

---

The key pulls. I walk.

Millhaven disappears behind me in the dark. Taryn's cold hand on the clone's shoulder is already a memory — the real me is invisible on the south road, Wind-Stepping through bare oaks at 25 miles an hour. The smoke clone takes the main road north, wandering, a decoy for anyone watching. Nobody's watching.

An hour south-southwest. The ley tether sings through the soil under my boots. Living Ground reads the line like a vein — warm, deep, old. Pre-kingdom containment engineers put this seal here two centuries ago and the lock has been waiting for the key I'm carrying ever since.

The terminus. Grid H-9. The edge of Seravane's thirty-two-mile perimeter. I poke the iron key into the dirt at the ley tether's endpoint. It sinks smoothly to the crossguard.

Bond-Form Sight reads a two-century lock answering.

Living Ground reads forty feet of blood-warm soil spreading from the key.

The death-binder's nearest perimeter node twitches. The system knows something happened. Not who. Not what.

Seal unlock: eight minutes.

---

### The Death Knight's Camp

---

A fire that gives no warmth. A stone ring. A log seat. Black plate armor polished to a mirror finish. The death knight sits with Oathbreaker across his knees. Green fire behind the visor. He doesn't blink because he can't.

A woman is here. Canvas sack. Waterskin. On foot, alone, afraid. She wandered in from the road — the perimeter herded her. The death knight's domain doesn't just ward. It redirects. Anyone walking near the edge gets nudged inward. She didn't find this camp by accident.

She doesn't know that.

"You are the thief," says the death knight.

I toss him the chest. It lands at his armored boots.

"I thought dead men told no tales, yet here we are."

He doesn't flinch. The green fire doesn't shift. Oathbreaker hums — low, metallic, the kind of sound you feel in your teeth.

"Why are you here, swordsman?"

I don't answer. The woman introduces herself — Elda. Looking for her son, Halden, twenty-four, was traveling south from Ashenmere to meet her at the crossroads three days ago. He never arrived. She's been searching since.

I tell the death knight his mother taught him no manners. There's an elderly woman on the road alone and afraid.

"You're elderly, over thirty, right ma'am?"

Elda gives me a look that could curdle milk. "I'm fifty-three, you horrible young man."

I look at the death knight. I look at her. I hold out my hand.

"Kenji."

I grab her hand and walk.

---

### The South Road

---

Behind us, the seal opens. Living Ground reads it — a two-century lock exhaling for the first time. The ground sighs. Warm pulse through the soil, spreading, fading. Whatever's down there is accessible now.

I left the key in the ground.

The death knight watched me leave. He has his chest. He has his questions. He has a seal that just opened at the edge of his lady's domain.

That's his problem.

---

The walk is dark. Cold. Half-moon overhead. Elda doesn't let go of my hand. Fifteen minutes in, she's walking closer. Thirty minutes, she loses the thread of her own sentences. An hour, she asks to stop. Just for a moment.

I don't stop.

She doesn't pull away.

---

Hour two. The oaks close in. Living Ground reads four cold spots — no heartbeat, no warmth, necrotic frost signatures. Arranged in a crescent ahead. Seravane's perimeter patrol.

Wights. Four of them. Already positioned.

They come from the tree line on both sides. Rusted mail. Grey flesh. Longswords trailing frost vapor. Two in front, two behind. Elda screams. Her hand rips from mine. She stumbles backward — toward the back pair.

They swing on her. She trips on a root and falls under both cuts. The blades gouge dirt where her head was. Both miss.

The front pair swings on me. Both miss.

I enter iaido stance.

The draw takes the one standing over Elda — crown to sternum. The Abyssal Shard leaves nothing. The wight unravels in grey dust and rust flakes. Vaporized.

On-kill. The blade carries through. Cuts through the second back wight. Dead. Chains into the third — the counter on the front wight's miss opens it from hip to shoulder. Dead. Chains again — catches the last wight across the torso.

Three hit points left. It staggers.

The nodachi comes down for the kill.

Nat one.

The blade slides wide. A finger's width off.

I don't correct. I don't swing again.

The miss becomes a spin. The nodachi arcs out in a slow deliberate circle — wrist rotating, blade catching moonlight — and I step back. One. Two. The blade floats up over my right shoulder, traces a silver line through the dark, and slides into the scabbard.

*Shhhhiiiing. Click.*

I freeze. Left hand on the scabbard mouth. Right hand open at my side. Weight on the back foot. Eyes on the wight.

Still.

The wight swings at a man who just put his sword away. The longsword passes six inches in front of my chin. Frost vapor brushes the mask.

Miss.

The counter is a draw. The nodachi leaves the scabbard faster than it entered — a rising cut, hip to shoulder. Iaido. Auto-crit.

The last wight comes apart.

I hold the follow-through for one breath. Chiburi flick. Slow sheathe.

*Click.*

Four wights. Two vaporized. Two cut down. The road is dust and rust and silence.

Elda is on the ground staring at me.

"You... what *are* you?"

---

### Wind Step to Thornkeep

---

I hold my hand out. She takes it.

"It's getting late. Dark. I need you to climb on my back. I can get us to the inn quicker."

She doesn't argue. Arms around my shoulders. Canvas sack between us. Her face near my neck. She's very warm for a woman who's been shaking all night.

The ground leaves.

She makes a sound between a scream and a prayer. We're running — not on the road, above it. Oak branches, air-hops, thirty meters between touchpoints. The forest blurs. The moon slides sideways. Twenty minutes. Eight miles. The trees thin. Torchlight ahead.

Thornkeep. Palisade wall. Gate with two lanterns. Garrison flag.

I drop to the road fifty yards out. Walking pace. She's still on my back. Her grip hasn't loosened.

"You can get down now."

A long pause.

"Right."

---

### The Waystation

---

The inn is stone and timber on the main street. Two rooms. A late meal. One gold, three silver. The innkeeper hands over two keys without comment.

The stew is hot. Root vegetables and something that might be mutton. Elda sits across from me. She eats. She doesn't talk. Her eyes keep drifting to my mask, my jaw, the strip of skin between fabric edges. She catches herself. Does it again.

When the bowls are empty, I stand.

"Your room's the second one. Door locks from inside."

She holds the key in both hands. Opens her mouth. Closes it.

"Thank you," she says. "For the road. For the... all of it."

She goes upstairs. The lock turns.

I go to my room. Bare walls. Bed. Window facing the street. Moonlight through the shutters. I set the nodachi against the wall, pull the mask down, and sit on the edge of the bed.

Quiet night. Garrison boots on cobblestones every half hour. The fire downstairs dies to coals.

The seal is open fifteen miles north. The key is in the ground. The death knight watched me walk away. A woman I don't know is on the other side of a wall unable to sleep for reasons she can't name. Her son is somewhere on the south road between Ashenmere and the crossroads. Three days lost.

I left threads behind tonight. The seal. The key. The death knight's questions. Whatever is under that terminus.

But the boy matters more than the lock.

I sleep.

*Ashmere 25. Fieldday. Thornkeep Waystation. Morning light through the shutters.*

*The inn smells like breakfast. The road south goes to the border. The garrison goes about its business. Somewhere on the south road, a young man is three days lost.*

*The seal can wait. The death knight can wait. The key is in the ground.*

*The ronin is in a garrison town, and a mother needs her son found.*
