# Fraying Empire — Chapter 14: *Oathbreaker*

**Session span:** Ashmere 28 late afternoon through Ashmere 29 dawn (Day 250–251).  
**Corridor:** South road / east verge — miles ~16.5–19.5, Pallid March mist.

---

### Cold signals

After the junction went quiet, the party **split the geometry on purpose** so nothing on the crown could treat them as one knot. **Jostin and Brynn** left first — not up the wheel ruts where a death knight’s axis would read clean, but **east onto the verge**, then north along the birch line toward the **~16.9–17** waypoint Kenji had chalked for them in camp margin code. Their job was **signal and shelter**: Brynn’s ring-fire when she had line of sight to the junction; Jostin’s smaller burns answering on a stagger so the mist read **living discipline**, not panic.

**Kenji** stayed **on and beside the crown** with **Kenji-two** forward — a second silhouette walking the packed center where Corwyn’s approach would register first. Split-brief fires stacked: Brynn’s waypoint, Kenji’s answer-flame at the root junction, a second south-column burn after the recon double pushed past **~17.5** with **milepost chalk** pressed into a fence post for Jostin’s hand if the mist tried to steal bearings.

When Brynn’s fire did not read clean from the south position, Kenji **burned the last Wind Step charges** to close north on her vector, lit the **Brynn-slot ring east of the crown**, and regrouped physically while the road still pretended it had not noticed.

### Ridge thread

Northwest of the crown, mist once lifted long enough to show a **low ridgeline** — not Mordecai’s full estate, but the same broken spine of hills, far off. Something **wraith-cold** threaded that gap for a handful of heartbeats. Kenji gave it **no line, no duel**; the double’s screen and the eastward fires were the message. The cold thread slid off axis and thinned — **not a kill**, not a spend worth the crown’s attention.

### The knight on the crown

At mile **~18** Kenji intercepted **Sir Corwyn the Fallen** — talk first, then violence on a **narrow objective**. Corwyn’s Lawful Good voice held until steel decided: duty named, oath named, the March’s patience named. Kenji did not need him dead; he needed **Oathbreaker out of the binding’s hand**. The exchange that failed was still an exchange — two men who recognized each other’s **wrong kind of honest** — before the iaido line closed the argument.

Kenji opened with **iaido through the sword arm**; Corwyn landed **two true strikes** before the arm failed; regeneration swallowed the receipt. **Oathbreaker** went into the **Bag of Holding** — holy and necrotic arguing in the dark of extradimensional space. Corwyn lived, **disarmed**, ruined at the shoulder, alone in the ash-mist on the wheel ruts.

### Birch copse

East verge camp **~mile 17**: Jostin’s smoke discipline, rotating watch, three people pretending the road was not listening. Brynn and Kenji shared a tent again — permission still standing; she took the lead until both were spent. **Long rest at dawn (Ashmere 29, Rootday):** charges, slots, and miles walked reset into whatever Chapter 15 asks for next.
