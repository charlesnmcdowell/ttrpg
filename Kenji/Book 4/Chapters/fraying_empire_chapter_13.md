# Book 4 — Fraying Empire
## Chapter 13: The Shield Wall

**Date:** Ashmere 28, 1247 AR (Stillday, early afternoon → late afternoon)
**Location:** South road, mile 15.5 → mile 16.5

**Kenji dialogue note:** All Kenji spoken lines in this chapter are **player-declared** at the table. Default rule remains no AI-generated Kenji dialogue.

---

### The Draw

---

Twenty yards.

The clone stopped walking. Jostin's pen stopped scratching. Brynn's shield came up another inch.

Forty-two skulls stared. Forty-two swords held steady. The revenants behind the line burned blue and patient.

From the dead oak sixty yards west, invisible, Kenji dropped.

Wind Step. The world compressed to a single line — oak to rear rank center — and then he was *there*, behind the wall, behind the revenants, standing in the space between the third rank and the command layer where no enemy expected a living thing to exist.

Invisibility shattered on arrival. Purple dreads caught the mist-light. The nodachi was still sheathed across his back.

One heartbeat of silence. Every skull in the rear rank rotated.

Iaido.

The draw came from the shoulder — a single upward arc that caught the first skeleton at the collarbone and split it crown to pelvis. Guaranteed critical. The nodachi's void edge carved through bone and binding both, and the skeleton didn't fall so much as *separate*, the two halves drifting apart like pages of a book dropped in wind. Purple-black mist where the marrow should have been.

The blade didn't stop.

On-Kill Continuation. The nodachi swept left into the next skeleton before the first one's shield hit the ground. Another clean split. Another kill. The chain had started.

---

### The Chain

---

Six rounds. That was all it took.

The rear rank died in the first ten seconds — Kenji's opening iaido draw chained through skeleton after skeleton, each kill earning the next swing, the nodachi a continuous arc of dark steel and void energy that turned the back line into a corridor of falling bones. The formation tried to adjust. Shields rotated inward. The middle rank compressed to fill the gap.

It didn't matter.

Kenji moved through them the way water moves through a dam crack — finding the seam, widening it, pouring through. Two base attacks per round. Every kill chained into a free swing. The nodachi's 6d12 was grotesque overkill against skeleton-grade HP, which meant almost every hit was a kill, and every kill was another attack.

Round two. The revenants turned. Black plate, blue fire, coordination that suggested memory rather than instinct. They were the real threat — CR 5 each, armored, multi-attack, necrotic riders on every swing.

Kenji prioritized them.

The first revenant took a critical to the shoulder that carved through the pauldron and into the ribcage beneath. It staggered but didn't drop — 150 hit points of binding-grade undead don't die to one swing. The second attack finished it. The blue fire in its eyes guttered, flared, and went out. It fell in two pieces. The void edge had hit something fundamental in the binding, and the revenant's armor *dissolved* — vaporized, reduced to black mist that the wind took south.

The skeletons kept pressing. Six could reach him per round — sword strikes that pinged off AC 19 like hail off a temple roof. The ones that missed earned counters. Two free reaction strikes per round, each one powerful enough to split a skeleton in half.

Round three. Round four. The second revenant went down — another vaporization on a critical hit, the 25% chance landing twice in one fight. The third revenant lasted longer, landing a necrotic-laced strike that Kenji's regeneration erased before the bruise finished forming. 333 HP per round. Full regeneration. The revenant could have hit him all day and never made progress.

It didn't get all day. It got half a round.

Round five. The skeleton count was in single digits. The formation had stopped being a formation somewhere around round three — just individual skeletons swinging at a target that moved too fast and hit too hard. Kenji cut through the last cluster near the road's east ditch, the nodachi splitting two with a single horizontal sweep.

Round six. The last skeleton fell.

Forty-five kills. Forty-five out of forty-five. Two vaporized. Zero damage sustained that outlasted his regeneration. Six rounds from first draw to final sheathe.

The road was clear. Bone fragments and rusted shields littered the packed dirt from ditch to ditch. The mist curled through the gaps where a formation had been.

---

### The Pursuit

---

From the road's north edge, the clone had already moved. While Kenji was dismantling the wall from behind, the robed figure on the west flank — the living signature, the observer — had broken and run south the moment the iaido draw came out. Smart enough to know what it was seeing. Smart enough not to wait for the skeletons to finish dying.

Kenji sheathed the nodachi. Full sheathe — the cord tied, the weapon seated across his back, iaido stance reset. He wiped bone dust off the back of his hand and looked south.

"Their leader is still up ahead. Let's see where it ran off to."

The clone took point with Jostin and Brynn, heading south at a jog. Nothing left to fight — every skeleton that had stood in that formation was bone fragments on the road behind them. The only thing still moving south was the observer.

Kenji walked. Unhurried. Hand resting on the nodachi's hilt at his shoulder. The mist was thicker past mile 16, chest-high, and the root network under the road had changed character — the thin sensory tendrils giving way to thick trunks, each one wider than a man's leg, converging south like the spokes of a wheel toward something Kenji couldn't see yet.

---

### Mile 16.5 — The Junction

---

The roots converged in a clearing.

Six trunks — massive, each thicker than the road itself — met at a central point where the packed dirt gave way to a lattice of exposed root material. Necrotic nodules dimpled the surfaces, pulsing faint green in sequence. South. Always south. The rhythm was slower than the relay network had been. Deeper. This wasn't communication. This was *circulation.*

The robed figure stood at the junction's center.

The clone arrived first, closing to thirty feet. The figure turned fully, and Living Ground read the details before conscious observation caught up: human, male, gaunt but not undead-gaunt — *starved*. Sunken eyes with burst capillaries. A leather collar sat tight against his throat, and from it, a single root tendril descended into the ground at his feet like a leash. His robes were Coalition-issue. A courier's tabard, mud-caked, the rank badge torn off but the thread-holes still visible.

He held the relay stone out at arm's length. Not as a weapon. As an offering. Or a report.

Living Ground confirmed what the pursuit had already implied: same human frame as the west-flank watcher at mile 15.5 — the runner had simply arrived here first, ahead of the party, to stand where the circulation trunks wanted a mouth.

"She'll want to know how a dead man's puppet fights like *that*."

His voice cracked. Dry. Days without real water. His eyes flicked to the clone's nodachi, then back to its face.

"You're not him. The real one's behind me. I can feel the roots *screaming* where he walked."

The collar pulsed once. His jaw tightened — involuntary, like a flinch from inside.

"I have... seven words left before she pulls me back."

He swallowed.

"The knight is coming. Don't be here."

The relay stone went dark. The root at his collar *yanked* — not metaphorically — his body jerked backward two feet, boots dragging furrows in the dirt, and then he was sliding south along the root channel like something reeled in on a line, disappearing into the mist between the dead trunks.

Silence. The junction pulsed once more — green, south — and went still.

---

### The Clearing After

---

Jostin and Brynn were forty yards back. Jostin had his shortbow half-drawn, arrow nocked but not aimed. Brynn had her shield up, watching the root lines on either side of the road.

Real Kenji walked up. Unhurried. The mist curled around him at knee height. The clone turned and looked back at him. Waiting.

The junction was empty. The root trunks still converged. The nodules had gone dark — no pulse, no green, no signal. Whatever the courier had been reporting to, it had received the message and gone quiet.

Kenji lifted two fingers — dismiss. The clone unraveled into gray smoke that thinned against Jostin's veil discipline without fight; no second charge spent, only the projection ended before anything else on the road could treat it as a second Kenji worth chasing.

Jostin lowered the bow. "Coalition courier tabard. Rank badge stripped. He's been out here... weeks, maybe. The collar—" He stopped. Wrote something in the journal margin. "The collar was a root leash. She's using live conscripts as field observers."

Brynn said nothing. She was staring south, where the courier had disappeared. Her amber-gold eyes caught the last green pulse before it died.

"'The knight is coming,'" Jostin repeated. He looked at Kenji. "Corwyn?"

The mist pressed in. The junction was quiet. The road south vanished into it after twenty yards.
