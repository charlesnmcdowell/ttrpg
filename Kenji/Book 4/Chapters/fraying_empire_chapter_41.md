# Book 4 — Fraying Empire
## Chapter 41: The Kingdom

---

### I. Explain

---

Every head turned to Ankunyx.

The parley ground held its breath. Vorathiel's amber eyes burned down from the ridge — each one the size of a wagon wheel, fixed on the unarmored man standing between delegations like he'd wandered in from a tavern.

Ankunyx looked around at everybody attending. Korrim, stone-faced, his honor guard a wall of steel behind him. Mursha, seated and tense, jaw tight, the bond humming under her ribs. Vess, composure locked in place. The Eldest, ancient and still. Mirenne, parasol angled against the grey sky. All of them watching. All of them waiting.

Korrim and Mursha settled into their seats.

**"Hi everybody, thanks for showing up."**

Dead silence. Then Skratch clicked his mandibles once from his perch on a broken column. The Eldest's mouth twitched — ten thousand years, and no king had ever opened with *hi*.

Vorathiel's growl shook pebbles loose from the cliff face.

Ankunyx didn't flinch.

**"Every one standing beside me, back up and give me space. It is time I answer the remainder's questions."**

Nyx stepped back. Skratch hopped to a wider perch. Ignis — in humanoid form, crimson robes, fire staff in hand — moved to the edge of the clearing. Drazhara, already in red dragon form on the opposite ridge, held position.

And then Ankunyx shifted.

Not slowly. Not dramatically. The air *cracked* — pressure wave, ozone, the smell of deep water and something older. His body elongated, darkened, expanded. Green eyes became vast luminous pools set in a skull the size of a longboat. Black scales — not reflective, not shining, *absorbing* — spread across a frame that filled the parley ground and then some. Wings unfurled like thunderheads.

The black dragon.

Coalition guards stumbled backward. Elara's hand went to her sword. Sera grabbed her arm. Korrim's honor guard locked shields by reflex — dwarven muscle memory older than thought.

The Eldest didn't move. She'd known.

Mirenne's parasol tilted slightly. Virelda's quill scratched a single word in her folio.

Mursha's breath caught. The bond didn't just hum now — it *roared*. She gripped the arms of her stone seat hard enough to crack them.

Vorathiel went silent. The growl died in her chest. For the first time since arriving, the ancient red regarded the parley ground not with contempt, but with *attention*.

---

### II. The Dragon's Court

---

The black dragon's voice rolled across the clearing like distant thunder — deep, resonant, felt in the sternum as much as heard.

**"Ok first thing first. To the human coalition — Vess, one of my mates..."** A pause. The dragon's luminous green eye turned toward the coalition delegation. **"Conflicts of interest aside. Your situation is undead on one side, another brand of undead on the other side, orcs. I am from the human coalition. It is obvious that I counteracted these threats by dominating them. Such is my nature. Such is the black dragon."**

Vess stood frozen. Her diplomatic training had prepared her for hostile negotiations, border disputes, trade sanctions. Not for the man she loved transforming into a sixty-foot apex predator and casually claiming dominion over three continental threats.

**"Vess, stop gawking and get behind me."**

She moved. Not because she was told to — because in that moment, standing in front of the black dragon felt like standing in front of an open furnace door. She stepped behind his foreclaw, composure cracking at the edges. Elara and Sera flanked her, both pale.

**"Yeah, one of my mother-in-laws has short patience, so I'll make this quick."**

Vorathiel snorted smoke.

**"All parties present — you are witnessing the founding of the Kingdom of Ankunyx."** The dragon's tail swept a slow arc across the stone. **"Dragon Emperor Ankunyx. Not a council. Not a coalition. A kingdom. I have united every power on this continent — coalition, undead, orcs, elves, dragons, dwarves — not through negotiation. Through domination. Through bonds. Through being the only thing on this continent that every faction is afraid of and in love with at the same time."**

No one laughed. It wasn't a joke.

**"Faction leaders become my advisors. Vess handles civil governance and serves as my check on power."** He looked down at her — a dragon looking at a human, but the green eyes were still his. Still warm.

**"Yes, Vess — she's my check on power."** A beat. A wink that shouldn't be possible on a reptilian face but somehow was. **"She checks my power every night."**

Elara made a sound like she'd swallowed her own tongue. Sera elbowed her.

Vess closed her eyes. Drew a breath. Opened them. "I accept the role," she said, voice steady, because someone had to be steady and it clearly wasn't going to be the dragon making innuendos at a continental summit.

---

### III. The Eldest's Champion

---

The dragon turned. Slowly. The ground vibrated with each degree of rotation.

**"Now next — the Eldest. I am the champion of the elves."**

The Eldest inclined her head. Faelindra and Nimue stood behind her, Faelindra's hand resting lightly on the hilt of a blade that would be approximately as useful against a black dragon as a butter knife.

**"Technically a few of your grand-daughters are my baby mamas, so — family discount."**

The Eldest's expression didn't change. After ten thousand years, very little could surprise her. But Faelindra's left eye twitched, and Nimue pressed her lips together hard enough to turn them white.

"The Deep Wood recognizes the Kingdom of Ankunyx," the Eldest said. Her voice carried without effort — old magic, woven into every syllable. "The elven alliance stands. As it has since you earned the Champion's mark." A pause so slight only someone who'd spent millennia parsing pauses would notice. "We will discuss the *baby mamas* comment at a later date."

---

### IV. The Dragon Question

---

The black dragon lifted his head toward the ridge where Vorathiel perched, vast and red and radiating heat that warped the air.

**"As to the dragons, Vorathiel. Nothing changes with the dragons except — I'm not asking your flights to kneel. I'm asking them to join a civilization. Not civilizations. One. Dragons above it, beside it, part of it. Not ruling through fear. Not descending to burn. Building something that lasts longer than any single flight's memory."**

Vorathiel said nothing. Her golden eyes were fixed on the black dragon — measuring. Calculating. The kind of silence that precedes either a treaty or a firestorm.

Ankunyx lowered his massive head. Slowly. The neck bent — and for a dragon, that gesture cost more than any spoken word.

Then the air cracked again. The black scales receded. The wings folded into nothing. Ankunyx stood on the parley ground in his human form — green eyes, tattoos, unarmored — and bowed.

**"I too feel this dragon urge to not bow. But —"** He held the bow. **"I respect you for giving us the chance to build a better world for your children."**

Silence on the ridge. Vorathiel's chest expanded with a slow breath. Smoke curled from her nostrils — not aggression. Something else. Something that might, in a creature old enough to remember when this continent was ocean floor, be the faintest edge of being *moved*.

She did not respond. Not yet. She was waiting to hear the rest.

---

### V. Elder Asshole

---

Ankunyx straightened from the bow and turned.

**"Nope! Korrim, elder asshole!"** A grin — bright, irreverent, aimed at the dwarf like a thrown stone.

Korrim's expression didn't change. The honor guard behind him shifted. His scribe's quill paused.

The old dwarf stepped forward. Unhurried. He looked up at Ankunyx — a full two feet of height difference — and spoke in a voice like gravel rolling downhill.

"I have held the gates of Kharn-Dural for longer than your bloodline has existed. I have maintained order when empires burned around us. I have kept the law when every other power on this continent abandoned it for convenience." He paused. "And I have watched you, boy. Since the day you stood on my bench as a ronin with no name and no nation and somehow convinced my courts that you were worth listening to."

He clasped his hands behind his back.

"The Kingdom of Ankunyx is recognized by the Kharnic Holds. Conditionally. The conditions being that you don't burn my mountain down and that Vess actually *does* check your power, because someone clearly needs to."

A beat.

"And don't call me elder asshole in front of my scribes."

His scribe was already writing it down.

**"Thanks for that riveting speech, elder asshole. For such a chill guy, I think that's the most I heard from you this entire time. Did your scribe write that down?"**

The scribe held up the page. Korrim didn't look.

The grin faded. Ankunyx stepped closer to the dwarf. Voice lower now. The crowd-play dropping away.

**"Korrim. You held an orc that could've slaughtered a large population of your guards. You kept her in a cell because process demanded it — even though you knew she wasn't the threat. That's your flaw, old man. Process over justice. The system tells you to hold the powerful in a cage that can't hold them, and you do it because the system says so, even when it puts your own people at risk."**

Korrim's jaw tightened. The honor guard bristled.

**"Easy, Korrim, man."** Softer now. **"I'm not attacking you. I'm telling you what I see — because that's what friends do, and whether you like it or not, we're friends now. Vess is my check on power. You're my check on process. I need someone who believes in the system enough to tell me when I'm breaking it. And you need someone who'll tell you when the system is breaking people."**

Korrim stared at him for a long moment.

"You're asking me to be your conscience."

**"I'm asking you to be yourself. Just louder."**

The old dwarf exhaled. Nodded once. Returned to his position.

---

### VI. Not a Democracy

---

**"Korrim, good job. And on that note — that brings me to the bad news."**

The clearing stilled.

**"This is not a democracy."**

Ankunyx's voice carried across the parley ground without magic, without amplification. Just volume and certainty.

**"I didn't unite this continent by asking permission. I didn't defeat three existential threats by forming a committee. The Iron Horde, the Pallid March, the Red Court — I ended them. Not negotiated. Ended."**

He looked across the delegations. Each one in turn.

**"You all have a voice. Vess, Korrim, the Eldest, Mirenne, Mursha, Drazhara — you're my advisors. You speak and I listen. But when I decide, that's the decision. If you disagree, you tell me. If I'm wrong, Vess pulls me back. If I'm dangerous, Korrim tells me I'm breaking the law. But nobody here gets to pretend this is a vote."**

He paused.

**"The bad news is what happens to anyone who decides this arrangement doesn't work for them."** His eyes hardened — green going dark, the dragon showing through the human face. **"You saw what I did to the Iron Horde. To Gorath. To the Pallid March. Those weren't enemies I destroyed — those were problems I *solved*. If anyone in this kingdom becomes a problem, I will solve them too. Stand down or stand up — those are the options."**

He turned to Vess. His voice dropped, and for a moment the tyrant vanished and the man showed through.

**"But I don't want that. That's why she's here."** He nodded toward Vess. **"Marius Vael sat in the Chancellor's seat at Varenholm for twelve years, siphoning the magical essence of three hundred and forty-seven graduates through corrupted Assignment Seals — the Hollow Crown — feeding it to an entropy entity to extend his own life. He operated entirely within the system. Every form signed, every procedure followed. Lawful. Evil. The system didn't stop him. A twenty-three-year-old with no titles and no army stopped him."**

He looked at Korrim.

**"Mordecai did the same thing in Book Two. Power operating within the rules, eating people alive while the institutions watched. I'm not going to pretend the system works when I've spent my entire career proving it doesn't. But I'm not going to pretend I don't need guardrails either. Vess is the guardrail. Korrim is the alarm. And if I ever become what Vael was — someone using power to feed on the people below me — I expect every person in this clearing to put me down."**

Silence.

Vess looked at him. Something in her expression shifted — not surprise, not admiration. *Resolve*. She'd taken the role. Now she understood what it meant.

---

### VII. The Mother's Question

---

Vorathiel's voice came from the ridge like a rockslide.

**"You speak of threats solved. Of power consolidated. Of guardrails and checks."** Her massive head lowered. **"But you have not addressed the only question that matters."**

The air temperature spiked. The coalition guards nearest the ridge edge fell back.

**"The Reckoning."**

The word landed like a physical blow. Even Korrim flinched — a micro-movement, suppressed instantly, but it happened.

**"I hold four of seven flights for descent. Kaeltharion holds three for treaty. In eighty-three days, the vote finalizes. If descent carries, seven flights descend on this continent and burn it back to stone. Every kingdom. Every city. Every field and forest and mountain hall. We start over."** Her golden eyes narrowed. **"That has been the dragon answer to failure for longer than this continent has had a name."**

Ankunyx met her gaze.

**"Nope. We're doing this now. Not in eighty-three days. Now. Because in eighty-three days I'll either have fixed the Hollowing or I'll be dead trying, and either way there won't be a vote to take."**

Vorathiel's chest rumbled.

**"I don't want to kill my brothers and sisters-in-law. If they burn down my precious elves and humans — I mean, it is what it is, I guess."** He put his hand on his chin. **"It will also suck that your grandkids won't get to meet you, considering Ignis and I are totally repopulating the dragons after."**

The ridge fell silent. Even the wind held its breath.

Vorathiel's massive form went perfectly, unnaturally still. Her golden eyes fixed on Ankunyx with an intensity that made the stone beneath his feet vibrate. Not from magic. From the low, subsonic rumble building in her chest.

Ten seconds. Twenty.

Her neck lowered. Not submission — *examination*. She brought her enormous head down until her snout was level with his chest, close enough that each exhale washed over him like a furnace door opening.

"You stand in front of me," Vorathiel said, and her voice had changed. The diplomatic thunder was gone. This was quiet. Intimate. The voice she used in her own lair. "A hatchling. Twenty-three summers. And you threaten me with *my own bloodline continuing without me.*"

Her eyes narrowed to molten slits.

"Ignis told me what you are. What you *actually* are, not the performance. She told me about the Crown. About what Vael did to you. About the entropy still anchored to your soul. She told me you carry a weight that would have broken any dragon twice your age, and that you carry it while making jokes."

She exhaled — a long, controlled breath that sent his hair streaming back.

"I have lived one thousand, six hundred and twelve years. I have buried mates. I have buried *children*. I watched the last great war turn three mountain ranges to glass. And in all that time, no one — *no one* — has had the audacity to tell me my grandchildren won't know my face, while standing close enough for me to swallow them whole."

A pause. The rumble shifted pitch.

"You are *infuriating*."

Another pause.

"You are also not wrong."

She lifted her head, turning to look out over the valley — sixty thousand orcs, the coalition camp, the elven pavilion, all spread below like a living map.

"Kaeltharion holds three flights because he believes the younger races can be *reasoned with*. I hold four because I have seen what happens when they *can't*. But Kaeltharion never produced evidence. He only produced hope."

Her tail swept the ridge, sending stones tumbling.

"You — infuriating, reckless, twenty-three-year-old *you* — produced a kingdom. In under a year. With a dead orc warchief, a vampire court, an elven champion title, a dwarven alliance, and my own daughter warming your bed."

Smoke curled from her nostrils.

"The vote was four-to-three for descent. Mine was the deciding voice. I am *moving* my voice. Three-to-three, with my vote held in reserve — *conditional*. You have the eighty-three days. If the Hollowing is sealed and the seam is closed before the Reckoning falls, I cast my vote with Kaeltharion. Treaty. Coexistence. Your kingdom gets to *exist*."

Her head lowered again — eye to eye.

"If it is *not* sealed. If the Fathom breaks through and the wards fail and the younger races prove they cannot protect even their own reality — then I cast for descent. And not you, not Ignis, not your *grandchildren* threat will stop seven flights from burning this continent back to bedrock and starting over."

She held his gaze.

"You have your chance, Ankunyx. *Don't waste it.*"

She rose to full height, wings half-spreading — a silhouette against the grey sky that blotted out the sun.

---

### VIII. Double Time

---

**"Whew, guys — I really thought we were going to be barbecue there for a moment."** Ankunyx looked worriedly over at Vess.

A nervous laugh rippled through the coalition escort — the kind from people who genuinely thought they were about to die.

Vess hadn't moved. White-knuckled, composure cracking now that the pressure had released. Her eyes were glassy, her jaw tight.

She met his gaze. Swallowed.

"You called a thousand-year-old dragon *toots*," she said, voice perfectly level. "And then threatened her with grandchildren."

Elara exhaled so hard it sounded like she'd been holding her breath for five minutes. Sera had gone the color of parchment.

Nyx shifted closer to Ankunyx, her tail brushing his leg — not affection, *grounding*. Her yellow eyes still tracked Vorathiel's retreating silhouette. "She moved her vote," Nyx said quietly. "That's not nothing. That's everything."

Skratch clicked his mandibles once. "Eighty-three days. Fathom. Seam." A pause. "Skratch thinks vacation was short."

Korrim stepped forward, expression somewhere between grudging respect and a father watching his son juggle knives over a baby's crib.

"The summit stands in conditional accord," he said, loud enough for the scribes. "The Kingdom of Ankunyx is *recognized* — pending resolution of the Hollowing threat within the stated window. All parties present have heard the terms." He looked at Ankunyx. "I trust you have a plan for the seam, lad. Because eighty-three days is not a long time."

Drazhara's voice carried from the opposite ridge — warm, amused despite everything: "He always has a plan. Whether it's a *good* plan is a separate conversation."

**"Alright, I need everybody to follow me to the seam, double time. Korrim, I want all the leaders here, VIP access — please tell me there's a faster way than your gates. If not, just open them all. Let's go, people."**

Korrim stroked his beard once. "There's the Underhall Express. Deep transit line. Runs beneath the Kharnic range from here to the eastern seaboard. Hasn't carried passengers in forty years — we use it for ore shipments." He glanced at the red dragon on the ridge. "It will *not* fit a dragon."

"Vorathiel and I will fly," Drazhara called, already unfurling her wings. "Point us to the seam. We'll meet you there."

Korrim grunted. "Fine. VIPs only. Everyone else stays at Kharn under garrison hospitality." He turned to his honor guard. "Open the Underhall. Full clearance. Clear the ore cars off the main line."

Vess fell into step beside Ankunyx. "The seam — the one in the Ashfeld Reach? The one your reports said was *pinned*, not closed?"

Nyx answered. "Pinned. Wards weakening. The Fathom is still pressing from the other side. We bought time, not a solution."

"How much time?"

"Enough. Until it isn't."

The Eldest moved through the crowd like water through reeds. She stopped near Ankunyx. "The deep elves have sung wards against the Hollowing since before Korrim's transit line was stone dust. If you are marching to the seam, my voice goes with you." A pause. "This is not a request."

Mirenne materialized from the shadow of a stone pillar, Virelda flanking her. "I assume the undead contingent is invited to the world-ending rift as well. How delightful. Virelda and I will manage the transit — sunlight is *unpleasant* at altitude."

Mursha pushed through last, towering over the coalition guards who parted out of self-preservation. She didn't ask permission. She fell in line and cracked her neck.

Down through Kharn-Dural — the great gates, the market halls where dwarven merchants stopped mid-transaction to stare at the strangest diplomatic party in recorded history. Down the spiral ramps cut into living rock, lit by phosphorescent crystal veins, until the air turned cool and smelled of iron and deep water.

The Underhall Express. A massive tunnel bored through the mountain's roots, wide enough for three ore carts abreast, iron rails in the stone floor. The transit cars were rough — industrial, open-topped, built for hauling rock, not dignitaries. Engineers scrambled to bolt bench planks across the cargo beds.

"Luxury accommodations," Korrim said. "Six hours to the eastern junction. Surface access to the Ashfeld Reach."

Skratch leaped into the nearest car and settled onto a bench. Clicked once. "Skratch calls window seat."

There were no windows.

Ignis climbed in after him, staff in hand, and extended a hand to help Nyx up. The two exchanged a look — brief, private, the kind of wordless communication that only people who'd shared a bathtub and a battlefield develop.

The delegation leaders filed in. The Eldest sat like she was settling onto a forest throne. Mirenne and Virelda claimed the rearmost car — deepest shadow. Mursha sat and the entire cart groaned.

The crystal lights along the tunnel walls pulsed in sequence — a departure warning older than most surface kingdoms.

Korrim stood at the platform edge. Waiting.
