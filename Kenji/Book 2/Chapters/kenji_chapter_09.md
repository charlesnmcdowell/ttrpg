# The Sundered Gate — Chapter Nine: The Basin

**Day Eighteen — Late Morning**

---

Wardbreakers through the Bleakmoor Ruins portal — Senna, Finch, Varn, Thessaly, me. I'm maxed on every buff the ley line will hand out.

Finch, ahead: "Trail's fat. Someone's drinking deep."

Thessaly: "Flow's accelerating. Convergence isn't sealed — it's a hose."

First relay node. Two Glass Sentinels. Senna doesn't break stride. The bracelet pulses — Hearts and Minds logs the kills.

Thessaly and I push east two miles. Basin opens — quarter mile of black glass floor, three spires, three sentries, and at center an abyssal gate with something seated under it like a hole wearing a person.

"That's not a scar," Thessaly says. "That's a door with a lock picked."

I drop. Horizon Arc scours the basin — fire, frost, entropy — sentinels stagger.

Three Cyclone throws; Giant's Throw makes each hit feel like artillery. Shards. Black glass snow.

The Gate Warden unfolds — eight and a half feet of wrong. Gravity yanks; my ward eats the worst.

Cyclone. Cyclone. Cyclone. Third one center mass. It comes apart like a mirror off a roof.

Thessaly, from the ridge: "Channels keep east. This isn't the end — it's plumbing. Spires match the convergence work. Gate's stable. Someone built it and left the lights on."

We break the spires. Starve the gate.

Senna's squad catches the rubble walk. She reads three dead sentinels, one dead warden, two of us — doesn't slow. Doesn't speak.

Finch: "You didn't save us any."

---

End of The Sundered Gate — Chapter Nine.
