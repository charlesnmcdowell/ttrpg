# The Sundered Gate — Chapter Nine: The Basin

**Day Eighteen — Late Morning**

---

Portal in the Bleakmoor Ruins. Five of us on the inbound side — Senna, Finch, Varn, Thessaly, me. The Wardbreakers, as of an hour ago when Finch wrote it on the back of a map and nobody disagreed.

"Channels east," Thessaly says before we step through. "The node we cleared was a branch. The trunk is still pouring. Finch's trail work this morning says the next pressure point is a basin about five miles in. I want eyes on it before the afternoon light drops the contrast."

"Formation?"

"Finch takes point. I read the ley. Varn anchors rear. Senna floats. You —"

"Opens doors."

"— opens doors."

Through the portal. The Bleakmoor on the other side is worse than yesterday. The clouds are still going the wrong direction. The ground is still breathing somewhere half a mile to the south. My ember sits maxed against the corrupted ley, everything the current will hold, because the current is flinching away from the ambient ley and all the room in it has turned into storage.

Finch, ahead in the scree: "Trail's fat. Someone's drinking deep."

Thessaly: "Flow's accelerating. Convergence isn't sealed — it's a hose."

---

First relay node. Half a mile along Finch's line. A broken tower spike stuck in black ground, two Glass Sentinels standing sentry at its base. Same architecture as the pillars at the triangle — same void-gap in God Sight, denser, taller, better-tuned.

Senna doesn't break stride. She steps in between the left sentinel's beam-cycle and its plating resets with a fist-print the size of her hand. The sentinel folds the way the triangle pillars folded — head shattered, beam dark, body inert.

Varn takes the right one with the shield. One step. One push. The sentinel hits the broken tower spike head-first and comes apart. Varn keeps walking.

The bracelet pulses at my wrist. Hearts and Minds — Elara's ward-mark — logs both kills without my input. Two void-signature constructs. Location stamped. I'll see the entry in her ledger tonight.

"Efficient," I say.

"We've been doing this without you for three years," Finch says. "Keep up."

---

Thessaly and I push east two miles while Varn and Senna sweep a second relay Finch spots off the ridgeline. The ground sloping, the wrongness deepening, the sky compressing like somebody is sitting on it.

Then the ridge ends and the basin opens under us.

Quarter mile of black glass floor — not rough, *polished*, the way a kitchen floor is polished, reflecting the sky in a color the sky has never been. Three spires at the compass points — tall, thin, same black glass as the gate architecture. Three sentinels patrolling perimeter on slow circuits. At the exact center, an abyssal gate — not a pair of pillars and a membrane this time, a single arch wide enough to drive a wagon through, red veins pulsing across its surface in a rhythm that is not the rhythm of the others.

Under the arch, cross-legged on the glass: something seated like a hole wearing a person. God Sight won't fix on it any better than it fixed on the triangle's node.

Thessaly drops prone on the ridge beside me. Spectacles on. Staff across her forearms. Lips moving, counting the veins.

"That's not a scar. That's a door with a lock picked."

"Who picked it."

"Whoever built the triangle. Same hand. Same school. This is more practiced."

"How long has it been open."

"Longer than yesterday."

---

I drop off the ridge.

Windrunner Boots catch air. Sixty feet down, thirty feet out. The sentinels' heads rotate toward me in unison before my feet touch.

Emberfrost locks.

**Horizon Arc.** Both blades sweeping out in the thirty-foot ring — fire on the right, frost on the left, entropy threading the seam. The ring hits the three sentinels before they finish their pivot. All three stagger. None drop; the void-signature is too dense for the first pass. But I bought two seconds.

I spend them on Cyclone throws.

Emberfang leaves my right hand spinning — fire-and-frost helix trailing. Giant's Throw stacks onto it; the steel goes through the air like artillery instead of a knife. The first sentinel catches it center-mass and comes apart in a spray of black glass shards.

Recall. Throw. Second sentinel. Shards.

Recall. Throw. Third. Black glass snow drifts across the basin floor.

Thessaly, from the ridge: *"Warden's standing up."*

I turn.

The figure under the arch unfolds. Eight and a half feet standing — too many joints, too little face, plating like the sentinels but *cleaner*, better-engineered, a proper next-generation piece. The Gate Warden. God Sight finally gets a shape on it now that it's moving.

Gravity yanks. Downward pull toward the arch — I feel the pressure in my sternum, like the gate is trying to breathe me in.

Ward Mastery engages on the ember's signal. The worst of the pull stops at the barrier. What leaks through still tilts my balance three degrees toward the gate.

Solveth: *Do not enter the arch. Even with the Ward. It is not a weapon. It is a mouth.*

"Working on it."

---

I don't close. Closing is what it wants. I back off thirty feet and throw.

Cyclone. Giant's Throw stack. Emberfang hits the warden's shoulder plate and the plate cracks but holds. It doesn't flinch — the pull hauls the spin out of my blade mid-flight and the recall takes a half-second longer than it should.

Second throw. Frost Fang this time. Different angle. The plate at the opposite shoulder cracks to match.

Third throw. Emberfang again, center mass, everything I have left in the arm.

The warden catches it with a hand I didn't know it had.

Stops the blade midair. Pinches it between two fingers.

"…well."

Solveth: *Now would be an excellent time for something new.*

I already had it coming. I pivot Frost Fang into my left hand and fire **Ember Lance** down its length — not at the warden. At the gate behind it.

The golden beam crosses the basin and hits the arch dead center. Creation doesn't heal the arch. Creation *burns* it. The red veins flash gold for a half-second. The arch's rhythm staggers. The gravity pull drops ten percent in the span of a heartbeat.

The warden's grip on Emberfang loosens by the same ten percent.

I recall the blade through its fingers. The steel comes loose and slaps back into my palm.

Then I close. Full Emberfrost. Both blades into the cracked shoulder plate and then into the next plate and then into the next. The warden's answers are slower than they were a minute ago because the arch behind it is losing feed pressure and the warden runs on the arch. I feel it the way you feel an opponent's shield arm dropping when he's tired.

Cyclone, final throw, at close range this time — point-blank, less than ten feet. Emberfang enters the warden's faceplate and exits through the back of its head.

It comes apart like a mirror off a roof.

---

The gate is still standing. Veins dimmer but not gone.

Thessaly is already moving — down the ridge, across the basin floor, not running but walking fast with the focus of a woman reading a failing engine. She stops twenty feet from each spire in turn. Reads the pulse. Calls numbers.

"East spire carries forty percent of the load. Break it first."

Ember Lance. Gold beam. The east spire folds.

"South. Twenty-five percent."

Lance. Fold.

"West. Everything that's left."

The west spire is the thickest. I give it two lances, back-to-back, because the ember is still running hot and I don't want to be here when the sun starts to set. The spire collapses inward like a poured sandcastle.

The arch, without feed, gutters out. Red veins fade to black. The arch itself stays — a dead frame — and the gravity pull stops. The glass floor underfoot stops feeling like a kitchen floor and starts feeling like rock again.

Thessaly stops counting.

"Channels keep east," she says. "This isn't the end — it's plumbing. Spires match the convergence work from the triangle. Gate's stable now that it's dead. Someone built this and left the lights on for years."

"For what."

"Whoever comes through. If anything was still coming. Which — " she looks at the dead arch " — which I don't think was going to be anything small."

---

Senna and the rest catch up at the rubble walk — Senna first, Varn behind her with his shield on his arm, Finch on a perimeter circle before he even reaches us.

Senna reads the basin the way she reads a patient. Three dead sentinels, one dead warden, two of us standing, one of us — me — running low enough on the ember that she can probably see it in my color.

She doesn't slow. Doesn't speak. Just puts a hand on my shoulder for three seconds, reads the bone and the blood and the heart rate, and moves on.

Finch, from the perimeter: "You didn't save us any."

"We saved you the rubble."

"Rude."

---

End of The Sundered Gate — Chapter Nine.
