# The Sundered Gate — Chapter Ten: The Pipeline

**Day Eighteen — Afternoon**

---

Deeper east.

Finch takes point again, trail reading tighter than it was an hour ago. The ground under us is no longer even trying to be ground — it's a sheet of the same polished black glass the basin floor was, running in a corridor about twenty feet wide, heading due east with the stubborn purpose of a river that's already chosen its mouth.

"Channels running together," Thessaly says, mostly to herself. "Three lines. Four. Five. They're pooling."

"Toward what."

"Toward *someone.*"

Another mile. The black glass corridor widens. The sky compresses further. The Bleakmoor has stopped pretending to be a landscape and started being a *facility*.

Then the ground opens its mouth.

---

Crater. Two hundred yards across. Not blasted — *carved.* Step-terraces on the walls down to a flat floor seventy feet below us. The sides cut at angles that hate curves, the way the triangle node's architecture hated curves.

On the crater floor: a compound. Stone and black glass. Low buildings on three sides, arranged around a central courtyard the way a keep is arranged around a killyard. Slit windows with a red glow behind them that pulses in the same slow rhythm as the arch-gates. Three gates in a triangle at the courtyard's center — taller than the basin arch, wider than the triangle pillars. All three humming.

We drop prone on the rim. The glass under my chest is cold enough that I can feel it through the armor.

Varn, quiet: "That's not a relay."

"That's a house," Finch says. "With neighbors."

Thessaly, already counting windows: "Someone lives down there. On purpose. The architecture — this is the place the others are pointing at. The triangle was a switchyard. The basin was a pumping station. This is the thing they were switching for."

"How many inside."

"God Sight won't read a roof that thick. Too much void-weave in the stone. Guess-count on the windows — eight, ten, something in that range. Plus whatever is seated on the gates."

Solveth, through the blade, for the first time since the warden fell: *Ronin. Do you remember the conversation I had with you two days ago about not picking the fight on its own ground.*

"I remember."

*This is its ground.*

"I know."

*Then we go in fast. We do not give the house a chance to wake up. Whatever is seated under those three gates is what the triangle was building toward when you pulled the plug on it yesterday. It is a generation older than the warden. If we give it five minutes it will not be one fight. It will be seven.*

"Copy."

---

Senna doesn't debate. She has been watching the compound since we hit the rim, and her hand is already moving.

Two fingers. Finch left — the halfling is already a shadow down the terrace, taking the long west approach. Varn right — shield up, moving at the pace of a man who intends to be a wall on the east side of the courtyard before anyone inside the house knows a wall has arrived. Thessaly pulls back five paces behind me, staff planted, the ley at the rim of the crater already bending toward her work.

Senna, to me, for the first time since the basin:

"Gates first. Don't let the house wake up. If it wakes, you hold the center and I'll go through the front door."

"Copy."

Gold-team clean. Close the gates. Don't give the house time to wake up.

I step off the rim.

---

End of The Sundered Gate — Chapter Ten.
