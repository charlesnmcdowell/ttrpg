## Chapter Four — *Three Doors Down*

## Day Fourteen — Dusk

---

I don't turn back.

God Sight resolves the layout. Three gates in a triangle — equilateral, precise, the angles too clean for accident. Dissolution zones overlapping in the spaces between, forming a kill floor of black glass. Root veins pulsing through the floor — one-two-three — converging at the center in a junction that looks like a circuit diagram. Not a lair. An installation. Somebody designed this the way Aldwin designs ward architecture — load-bearing structure, redundant systems, power flowing where it's told.

Where the thing is sitting. Layered plates of black glass over a frame that has no bones. A block head with three seams — one per gate, God Sight confirms. Two massive arms, palms on the floor, drawing power from the root veins. Not a creature. A construct. A hub node with a body.

Solveth: *The gates sustain it. Kill the gates, kill the node.*

I point Emberfang at the nearest gate. Ember Lance.

The golden beam hits Gate A. Creation light floods the black glass — the red veins flashing gold for a heartbeat, the architecture fighting the overwrite the way a dam fights a river. It loses. The gate folds. One head-seam goes dark.

Solveth: *It understood creation. Whoever built this studied the same source as the Delve architects. The overwrite works because the underlying language is COMPATIBLE.*

I'll think about that later. Right now the entity's fingers are closing.

The glass floor cracks in concentric rings. The block head rotates. Two blazing seams orient on me.

It opens its mouth. Not a beam — sound. A wall of concussive force that hits like jumping into a frozen lake. Twenty-one damage. Fifteen feet backward. Prone. Deaf. The dissolution floor bites into my back where I land — the glass eating at the leather, the Academy blue dissolving at the contact points.

---

Emberfrost locks. Duality Aspect. Creation mode. Straight up — sixty feet. Both remaining gates birth watchers as I climb. The network defending itself.

By the third round of circling, creation heal has erased the entity's hit. Full health.

The entity kneels. Drives both hands into the floor. A geyser of dissolution erupts from the center — sixty feet tall, area denial. Corruption tendrils climb from multiple points, building a ceiling. But it's splitting power across too many systems.

Gate C. Far side. Unguarded except for one watcher whose beam can't reach altitude. I dive. Ember Lance. Watcher C's beam catches me mid-lance — temp HP absorbs most of it. Gate C folds.

The entity's second seam dies. The geyser thins. Tendrils collapse. One gate left.

---

The entity commits everything to Gate B. Both hands on the membrane. Pulling something through — the gate brightening, the red veins thickening, the air between the pillars rippling like heat haze over a road. Whatever is on the other side wants in.

Watcher B plants itself between me and the gate. Overcharging — plating thickening, head-seam blazing. Building toward a shot that doubles the normal beam. The drone climbs until my fillings ache.

God Sight reads the architecture from altitude. The overcharge pools in the nexus at center mass. The root vein connecting it to Gate B runs through the floor. Exposed. Pressurized. All that stolen energy flowing through a single line like water through a pipe.

I don't need to hit the watcher. I need to hit the fuse.

Through the tendril canopy — the dissolution tendrils scraping against the creation aura, hissing, disintegrating on contact but slowing me down, buying the watcher time. Emberfrost into the glass floor where the root vein is thickest. Both blades. Everything I have left in the arms.

The disruption races back — twenty, thirty, forty feet. The overcharged nexus receives a surge from behind. The watcher's head-seam flares white. The plating bulges outward.

The watcher unravels. The detonation expands forty feet and hits Gate B broadside. The membrane shatters. The breach collapses inward. The world hiccups — a skip in reality, like a heartbeat dropping a beat. The air tastes like copper and ozone.

Silence.

---

The entity. Both hands extended toward nothing. Three seams dark. The layered plates sagging, the frame losing whatever held it upright. I have two lance charges left. I could finish it. I don't. It's already dead. It just hasn't fallen yet.

Solveth, quiet: *Whoever built this will build more.*

I know.

I fly west. Duality expires on the road — the aura collapsing, the flight dropping me six feet onto packed dirt. Stride floods back. My arms are shaking. L2 slots empty. L4 down to one. The Academy leather scarred in three places the blue will never come back to. Seven meals left in the satchel and the ember is already asking for the next one.

The crossroads. Campfire light. Tarin's silhouette at the road's edge. Staff across her shoulders. She doesn't flinch when I come out of the dark at Stride speed.

"He's back."
