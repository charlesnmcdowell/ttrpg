# The Sundered Gate — Chapter Twelve: The Workshop

**Day Eighteen — Afternoon**

---

Past the junction. Another forty feet of corridor. The hum under the floor deeper now, the red glow from the sconces brighter.

The corridor opens.

A workshop. Long. Well-lit in the wrong spectrum. Benches along the walls — tools I recognize, tools I don't, a row of void-resonant crystals humming at different pitches in a padded rack. Half-built constructs on three of the benches, faceplates open, the insides looking more anatomical than mechanical.

A map table in the center of the room.

The whole Bleakmoor laid out in red ink. Every gate we've closed — marked and crossed through. Every relay we haven't found yet — marked and circled. Siphon lines in hatched ink running from the relays to a single point northeast of here. Twelve miles. Marked in a color I don't have a name for.

A pinned note at the northwest corner of the map. Handwriting tight, disciplined, a left-hander's slant.

*Phase 3: Convergence unsealed by outside actor. Accelerate timeline. Route energy to primary basin. Open the Sundered Gate before the ArchMagus finds it.*

I check the date. Two days old.

I read it aloud.

Thessaly, beside me, jaw setting: "So we're not chasing an accident. We're chasing a schedule."

"A schedule that knows my job title."

"A schedule that's watching the Varenholm gazette."

"Good. Let's ruin the schedule."

---

Glass Stalker drops from the ceiling.

No warning. No tell. Eight limbs, wall-colored — glass perfectly tuned to the ambient red so it reads as *nothing* until it moves. Vector: straight at Thessaly.

God Sight catches it a half-beat late — the void signature blooming as it comes off the ceiling. I'm pivoting. Not fast enough.

Thessaly's staff is.

She catches a reflection that shouldn't exist — the stalker's chromatic camouflage doesn't mirror properly, and her spectacles are cut to *see* the mismatch. Her staff comes up with a half-turn of wrist and she snaps a Crystal Beam — a prismatic lance of refractive magic — into the stalker's center mass.

The glass cracks. The camouflage fails.

The stalker becomes *visible* — eight-limbed, faceted body, void-gap underneath the cracked crystal like the gap inside the triangle pillars.

I have two seconds before it closes again.

Cyclone. Emberfang leaves the right hand spinning. The blade hits the stalker in the body and rebounds — the faceted surface deflecting the impact along a glancing axis.

*Seam,* Solveth says. *Between the fourth and fifth limbs. Her beam opened it.*

I recall. Second throw. Different angle this time — underhand, spinning on a flatter axis. Emberfang hits the seam.

The stalker detonates.

Limbs in eight directions. Black glass shrapnel against the workshop walls. One leg lands on the map table and slides to a stop against the pinned note.

Silence.

Thessaly is still at the map table. Spectacles clean. She flicks a single shard off her shoulder.

"You're welcome."

"I was in the middle of drawing."

"You would not have been."

I concede the point.

---

She reads.

I pace the workshop — checking the benches, the crystals, the constructs. Senna's voice comes through the ring at my hip, quiet. *"East wall clear. Two weavers in the courtyard. Down. Varn is pushing the north side."*

"Copy. Workshop in the south wing. Thessaly's reading documents."

*"Anything?"*

"Names. I'll have them in a minute."

Names surface while I pace.

Mordecai Corven. The signature on every third document.

*Ex-Academy,* Thessaly reads. *Expelled forty-one years ago for unsanctioned ley architecture. Reinstated under appeal. Re-expelled for falsifying appeal. His advisor was —*

She stops. Looks up at me.

"Elara."

"…say that again."

"His advisor was Elara. When she was still teaching instead of running the place. The second expulsion is in her handwriting on the appeal denial."

I log that. I don't say anything. There's a conversation due with Elara tonight that is going to be shorter than either of us wants.

Thessaly keeps reading.

*Sundered Gate* — the point northeast of here — sealed before the Academy existed. Pre-Imperial. Every relay in the net routes to it. The basin we broke this morning was a *pressure valve.* The triangle was a *switch.* This compound is the *foreman's office.* The Sundered Gate is the job.

Money for all of this runs through a comm array two rooms away. Supplies, materials, constructs — outsourced through a merchant network that has no idea what it's supplying. Legitimate invoices. Legitimate receipts. A budget.

Thessaly sets down the last page.

"He didn't break the moor. He's *renting* it. And he's almost finished the lease."

---

End of The Sundered Gate — Chapter Twelve.
