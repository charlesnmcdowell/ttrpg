# KENJI — THE SUNDERED GATE

## Chapter One — *New Skin*

## Day Thirteen, Evening → Day Fourteen, Dawn

---

The ley alcove. The aftermath. Cracked stone still warm under my boots. Aldwin's staff in two pieces on the flagstones. The sky returning to grey. Rain resuming like nothing happened.

The Circuit Bracelet hums on my wrist. Emberfang and Frost Fang separated at my hips. Solveth quiet in the blade — the satisfied silence of a god remembering freedom.

Seven people around me. Sera sheathing weapons she never used. Elara standing exactly where she stood through everything. Edwyn rising from his knees. Pip leaning on Ryn behind the pillar. Callen and Ashdown on the ground, alive, the hairs on their arms standing up.

The Academy wards flicker overhead. Hairline fractures spreading from the alcove. Voices inside — faculty, students, people who saw a column of light punch through their ceiling.

Running footsteps in the colonnade. City guards. Faculty in disheveled robes.

Elara raises a hand without looking at me. *Go. I'll hold the hallway.*

I go.

---

Out the back of the colonnade, past the service garden, through the kitchen alley behind the Academy mess. Two guards jog past the far end of the alley looking the wrong direction. The Veil isn't up yet — God Sight alone is enough to read their paths and slide around them. Ten minutes of quiet walking through evening rain and I'm at the Silver Draft.

Garrett's pouch is on the bar exactly where he said it would be if I ever came back to collect. Fifty gold. Wyrm bounty. I pocket it, nod at the tender who didn't ask a question, and take the corner booth.

Two meals. The plate of whatever the kitchen had ready, then a second of the same because the first disappeared faster than felt polite. The ember is burning hot in a way I haven't felt before — not the siphon's thin hot, not the combat hot. The hot that comes after a circuit empties for the first time in weeks. A day that started in a silver mine and ended with a freed god sits on top of me like weather. I eat through it.

Up the back stairs to the room I keep on the Draft's second floor. Boots off. Swords leaned against the bed frame. Bracelet still warm. Door closed. Eyes close before my head finds the pillow.

---

Long rest. Eight hours. The bottleneck is gone. The ember expands into space the 339 threads occupied for weeks. The current widening. New architecture forming where the siphon colonized — channels opening in directions that were walled off since Day One.

Somewhere inside it, something clicks over.

Level 10.

Through the bond, Solveth, dry: *Welcome to the other side of the bottleneck.*

*How does it feel on your side.*

*Quieter. Cleaner. I can hear myself think without the drain. You will find you can too.*

The ember settles. The current holds.

---

Day 14. Dawn.

I walk the three blocks from the Draft to the Academy gate in the grey pre-dawn. The city is unusually awake — lights on in windows that wouldn't normally be lit at this hour, someone in a nightshirt shouting for his wife to *come look at this,* a baker carrying his own fire out into the street because he hasn't needed to do it himself in years and he's crying.

The gate guard waves me through without making me show anything. He's crying too. Quietly. Not asking why.

Elara's waiting in the corridor with a slate like I'm a student who forgot homework.

"Maren's gear — today. Guild cut of nothing until the mine hearing shakes out. Kael's still neck-deep in Delve salvage. Miners are back on the payroll; first ore's pending. Council's quiet — which means they're planning something loud. Vael's in three-oh-two. Alive. Unconfined. Don't make him cry before breakfast."

"How many have cried this morning."

She doesn't answer that. She turns and walks. I follow.

---

Her office. Two floors up and halfway down the east corridor. Gold threads on the wall map now — tracking restored graduates alongside the old red ones.

"339 reports overnight. Every single one. Faculty healers running since midnight. The city temple received forty walk-ins before dawn." She almost smiles. "Talia Venn set her curtains on fire."

She taps the desk. "Underclothes — dark, weightless, resonance-woven. Veil's moonstone-threaded silver, eyes and crown open, three layers of mental resistance. Both calibrated. Try not to scratch your face through the silk."

"The armor needs Aldwin's signature. He hasn't responded."

"Two out of three isn't losing. It's pending. Workshop. Go."

---

Down two flights. Across the inner courtyard, where someone has already pinned a hand-written list of restored names to the noticeboard and a small crowd is reading it in the dark. Through the artificer wing's heavy door. The smell of resin and metal shavings and the particular ozone of a man who has been working the same repair for six hours.

Aldwin at the workbench. The staff in two pieces in front of him. Wire and resin binding the crack.

"I felt you coming." He doesn't look up. "The CHA resonance on that mask is loud enough to hear from two floors away. Sit or stand, your choice. Just don't touch the joining."

I stand.

He finishes a loop of wire, ties it off, sets the staff down. Then he looks at me. The grey eyes do a one-pass read — swords, bracelet, mask. Not a catalog. A tradesman's check.

"The armor was mine. Twenty years ago. I wore it when I was the most dangerous man in this building."

"What stopped being true."

"A woman. A seal that went wrong. A council that asked me to choose between my work and my guilt and I picked the work." He shrugs, not apologizing. "The leather got shelved. I got married. The man who wore it became a teacher instead. You can ask Elara the rest of that story if you want it. She was there."

"I'll ask her."

"Good. Don't ask me." He reaches under the workbench. The authorization. Already signed. Ink dry.

"I signed it before you walked in." He holds it out. "Wear the blue. And Kenji — don't break this one. The last one took three decades to forgive me."

---

Back through the courtyard. The crowd at the noticeboard is bigger. The Instructor's Guard Leather settles over the underclothes in Elara's outer room — Academy blue and silver, crest on the chest, force resistance a low hum I stop noticing after three breaths. Veil on last. The world sharpens; God Sight drinks it in.

Solveth: *Vanity suits you. Try not to trip.*

*Noted.*

Up two flights of the faculty stair. Early sun catching the stained glass at the landings. A junior instructor passes me on the stairwell, does a full double-take at the blue, opens his mouth, closes it, keeps walking.

---

Faculty quarters. Third floor. Room 302. Plain door.

Vael opens it. The glamour gone. What's left is 140 years old and looks it. Papery skin. Thin hands. A robe hanging off shoulders that used to fill it. The sunken eyes find the bracelet where his Crown used to be and stay there a long second before moving.

"I was the best abjuration specialist this Academy produced in a century."

"I know."

"I can't hold a ward for thirty seconds this morning. I tried before you came. The architecture remembers. The body won't."

"Good. You're going to need the architecture. I'll handle the body."

I put my hand on his shoulder. Ember Lance — low, steady, creation energy flooding a body that's been dying since yesterday afternoon. The papery skin tightens under my palm. The trembling steadies. The spine straightens by degrees, as if someone is letting air back into a room. Still 140. But vital. A man who could walk to Thornwall and back.

He stares at his hands. Makes a fist. Opens it. A small ward-glyph blooms above his palm — solid, bright, real. He closes his fingers on it and it stays.

His eyes wet once, and he makes them stop.

"You're hired." I keep my voice level. "My battle mages. Your own squad. Pick your people. Train them. Abjuration — wards, barriers, seals. That's your identity. Sera gets her own squad too. You both name your units. You answer to me."

"You're giving me a command. After what I did."

"What you did is done. What happens next is what matters. You built the best seal architecture this Academy has seen. Use it right this time."

A long breath. The ward above his palm pulses once.

"What do I call the squad?"

"Whatever you want."

He thinks about it for four breaths. Looks at the glyph still holding in his palm.

"The Ironveil."

Solveth from the blade: *You are full of terrible decisions that somehow work.*

---

A runner takes Vael's paperwork to the registrar. *Ironveil* hits the ledger; the clerk's pen hesitates once — then keeps moving.

---

Out the east gate of the Academy, down the old artisan's lane. The city is fully awake now. A woman on her front step hands me a warm roll without asking who I am. She is smiling the way a person smiles when something she stopped believing in has happened to her. I thank her and eat it walking.

Maren's shop, the Gilt Lens, is four blocks past the fountain. Black lacquer door. A small bell I've heard twice before. Inside smells like oiled silk, old brass, and a thin edge of whatever she burns when a customer she actually likes is expected.

"You look different from the man who spent fifty-two gold in my shop two weeks ago." Maren leans on the counter. "Better dressed. More dangerous. Same empty pockets?"

Plain iron ring on my right hand — strength, plus one. Soft leather gloves, silver-stitched at the cuff — dexterity eighteen while worn. Both bought from her counter for fifty gold on an earlier visit, both earning a flat professional once-over from behind the counter now.

"Don't let any of this go to your head. I still have your exclusivity contract."

"Which means what, specifically."

"Which means that when a man in Silverquill or Thornhold wants to know who put a ring of strength on the new Academy blue in Varenholm, they come through my door and I decide whether they get to know. You're in my book now. Try to be worth the paperwork."

"I'll try."

"Go. Garrett's expecting you. He's been staring at a map for an hour and it's getting on my runner's nerves."

---

Out of the Gilt Lens, back past the fountain, three blocks to the Silver Draft. The sun is properly up. People in the street are still in the mood that follows a miracle — quieter than yesterday, slower, noticing each other. I walk through it without the Veil lit and no one looks twice.

Garrett's office is behind the bar, up a short flight. His door is open.

"You look expensive." He doesn't do the full up-and-down; he lands on the bracelet and stays. The room's half war-room now — manifests swapped for documents, an eastern map pinned and bleeding red ink toward the border. "Say something reassuring."

"That's not my brand."

"It was worth a try."

I nod at the map. "Red's moved since yesterday."

"Red's moved since *breakfast.* Four new villages stopped answering their relay wards between midnight and dawn. Border garrison at Thornwall is sitting on orders they won't show me. Three merchant houses pulled their caravans back inside the walls in the last six hours. Whatever was coming is arriving." He taps the map with two fingers. "And the 339 thing last night — I don't know what your version of it was, but half this city is standing in the street right now deciding they're not going east for work anymore. Which is convenient for whoever is *already* east and doesn't want to be looked at."

"You want me east."

"I want you *ready* for east. I've got contracts stacking. I've got a council meeting in four days that's going to pretend none of this is happening. And I've got a guild roster that just got handed a freshly-blued ronin the whole Academy is going to be staring at for a week. So sit down, drink the bad tea, and let me brief you on what I actually think is out there."

I sit. He pours the bad tea. The map bleeds.

---

End of The Sundered Gate — Chapter One.
