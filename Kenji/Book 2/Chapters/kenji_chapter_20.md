# The Sundered Gate — Chapter Twenty: The Assault

## Day 23 — Morning

---

Dawn. Ley line under the bedroll pulses warm. I sit on black glass, eyes closed, let the ember drink.

God Sight — Giant's Throw — Haste — Stride. Four minutes, camp ley, not as deep as the Academy alcove, but enough to make the world feel honest.

Senna's already at the fire. She watches the gold wash without blinking.

"Light show over?"

"For now."

"Good. Dren moved at oh-four-hundred — eastern approach. Thessaly's with him."

---

The Stride eats the distance between the camp and Dren's column. Black glass crunches under boots that barely touch it. The eastern approach is lower ground, a shallow ravine cutting through the basin floor where the glass soil gives way to exposed rock. Good cover. Bad sightlines.

Dren's column is pressed against the ravine wall. Eight fighters in two fire-teams, kitted in the enchanted gear that was crates in a Varenholm warehouse six hours ago. Hardened leather with ward-thread stitching. Short swords. Crossbows. Professional. Quiet. The kind of quiet that means someone told them to shut up and they listened.

Dren is crouched at the front with a charcoal stick and a flat stone, scratching lines. Thessaly is beside him — staff across her knees, spectacles catching the grey light. She's pointing at something on his improvised map and speaking in a voice too low to carry.

Senna and Varn are thirty yards further east, prone on a ridge with a clear view of the gate perimeter. Five sentinels visible from their position — tall glass bodies in a loose arc, facing outward. Motionless.

Dren hears the Stride before he sees it. His hand goes to his sword. Then he sees who it is and the hand drops.

"Thought you were taking the west column solo." His voice is flat. Not a question — an observation. The Lord of Ironholt doesn't waste words on surprise. "Something change?"

Thessaly looks up. The spectacles tilt. She says nothing, but her eyes move from me to the western approach — where nobody is now — and back.

The western approach. Five sentinels, open ground, no cover. That's the one.

---

The Stride devours the gap. Five hundred forty feet in three heartbeats — glass soil shattering in a wake of heat shimmer. The sentinels register the incoming mass at two hundred feet. Their heads rotate in unison. Too late.

Emberfrost leaves both hands combined. The double-blade spins — fire trailing one edge, frost the other — and arcs into the center of their line. Giant's Throw turns the weapon into a saw blade the size of a cart wheel.

The Cyclone hits three of them. Sentinel Four takes the worst of it — the frost edge catches its torso and the fire edge follows through. The glass body cracks down the center, entropy threads snapping like cut wire. It staggers. The ward-script across its chest flares white. All of it. Pooling, not flowing.

The detonation sprays glass shrapnel across the basin floor. Nothing close enough to catch it.

Sentinels Two and Three are reeling. Slowed. Frost crawling up their legs. One and Five are untouched — One closing from thirty feet, Five a hundred and fifty out.

Emberfrost comes back to the hand trailing frost vapor. The kiting begins.

Back up sixty feet. Stride. Clean withdrawal. The frozen ones crawl forward on legs that don't work right anymore. Sentinel One gains ten feet. Not enough.

Four throws. Weakest first. Sentinel Three pops like a glass bottle. Sentinel Two catches the return arc and shatters mid-stride. Sentinel One — the closest, the one that almost reached melee — takes Emberfrost through the chest. The entropy threads snap. It folds.

Three detonations ripple across the glass plain. White flashes. Shrapnel pattering against dead soil.

Sentinel Five drags itself forward on frozen legs. Emberfrost punches through its skull at a hundred forty feet. The glass body folds. The ward-script flares white and detonates. Harmless.

Five sentinels. Thirty seconds. Zero damage taken.

---

The western approach is open. The convergence is worse up close.

Three ley lines meet here — or they did. Two are severed stumps, the flow cut by Senna's team days ago. The third — the northern line — the constructs are sitting on. What's left is a column of energy pulling from ambient ley absorption only. Green-black. Twenty feet across. It rises from a crack in the glass soil and spirals upward into nothing. The air inside it shimmers like heat haze, but it's cold. Wrong-cold. The kind of cold that isn't temperature.

God Sight reads the structure. The gate is a wound. Not a door — a tear. The ritual carved it open and it's been widening on its own momentum. Seventy-eight percent. The edges are fraying. At a hundred, it opens and whatever's on the other side comes through.

There's a stone platform at the base. Ritual circles carved into black glass. Spent reagents. Mordecai's work — but Mordecai isn't here. The circles are self-sustaining. This thing doesn't need a caster anymore. It just needs time.

Then the God Sight catches something south.

The thermal bloom. It's moved. It's not in the basin anymore. Three hundred yards south of the convergence and closing. Slow. Deliberate. The heat distortion is visible to the naked eye now — a shimmer sixty feet across, and inside it, something tall. Taller than the sentinels. The ground under it is glowing dull orange.

It's walking toward the gate.

---

It's not a construct.

It's stone. Living stone. Fifteen feet tall — twice the height of a sentinel. A humanoid shape but wrong in the proportions: arms too long, shoulders too broad, head too small. The surface is cracked volcanic rock laced with veins of molten orange that pulse in slow rhythm like a heartbeat. Where it steps, the black glass soil softens and glows. The sixty-foot thermal bloom is just its body heat radiating outward.

God Sight reads deeper. The energy signature isn't entropy. It isn't arcane. It's ley. Raw. Unprocessed. This thing is made of the same stuff the convergence is bleeding. It's not attacking the gate. It's not defending it.

It's drawn to it. Like a white blood cell to a wound.

It stops. The small head turns. Two points of orange light where eyes should be find me across fifty yards.

It doesn't attack. It looks.

---

Stride back to Dren's column. The Colossus watches me go. It doesn't follow. It turns back toward the gate and resumes walking. Slow. Patient.

Dren stands when I arrive. His eyes read my face — no injuries, no urgency, but something.

"Western five are down. Your constructs hit the northern six about twenty minutes ago. Sounds stopped ten minutes back — either they're mopping up or they're done. Forty glass bodies against six sentinels isn't a fight. It's arithmetic."

He taps the charcoal map on the flat stone. "East is us. Five sentinels. Haven't moved. They heard the western detonations — their formation tightened but they didn't break. Still holding."

Thessaly speaks without looking up from her staff. "The convergence destabilized when the western five died. Minor fluctuation — less energy feeding the perimeter wards. The gate itself is unchanged. Self-sustaining, as I told Senna three days ago."

She pauses. The staff pulses once. Orange.

"Something walked past the southern ley line four minutes ago. Large. Hot. Not entropy-signature. Not anything in my taxonomy." The spectacles turn toward me. "You saw it."

---

Thessaly pushes her spectacles up. The staff's orange pulse has settled to a slow rhythm — matching something underground.

"The gate is self-sustaining. Killing sentinels doesn't slow it. Cutting ley tributaries slowed it — we bought you two percent per day by severing two of three feeder lines. The third line your constructs are sitting on. But even at reduced intake, ambient absorption keeps it growing. Four percent a day. Five days."

She points the staff south. The tip glows.

"You have two options. One: I study the ritual circles at the convergence and attempt a counter-inscription. That takes time I may not have, and if Mordecai built failsafes — which he did, because he's not stupid — it could accelerate instead of collapse."

The spectacles turn toward the southern shimmer.

"Two: that thing walking toward the gate is ley-aspected. Raw ley. It's not a construct — it's a response. The land is trying to heal itself. If it reaches the convergence, one of two things happens. It seals the wound. Or it feeds it. I don't know which."

Dren cuts in. "You're telling me the plan is *hope*."

"I'm telling you the plan is *information*." Her voice is flat. Academic. "I need to get close to it. Read its resonance. Ten minutes with my instruments and I can tell you whether it's a cure or an accelerant. But I need someone between me and it in case I'm wrong."

---

The bracelet pulses warm. Every portal, all at once. The command is simple: come.

Glass bodies step through portal frames across the continent. Duskfen, Broken Antler, Thornwall, the Mines, Crestfall — every garrison empties. The daily spawns have been compounding since Day 20 — four per portal per day, four days. What was forty-eight on Day 21 is now over a hundred. They walk out of their posts and into the Bleakmoor through the Wardbreaker Camp portal. The ground vibrates with the footfall.

Thessaly's staff flares. Dren turns and watches the northern ridge fill with glass bodies. Row after row. Faceless. Still. Waiting.

"That's all of them," Thessaly says quietly. "You just stripped every town in the network."

Dren doesn't say anything for a long time. Then: "If this doesn't work, there's nothing between those towns and whatever comes next."

I know. Duskfen. Broken Antler. The Mines. Towns with children and farmers and people who sleep behind glass walls because the ArchMagus said they'd be safe. I just took that away. Every one of them. For this.

A hundred and four glass soldiers on the Bleakmoor — the forty from the northern garrison plus every construct from every portal in the network. Four from Varenholm still guarding Elara at the Academy. Eight mercs. Senna's team. The gate at seventy-eight percent. And something made of living stone walking toward it from the south.

---

Senna doesn't waste a breath. "Kael. Varn. Front line. Break their formation — Kael, you know what that looks like." Kael nods once. Doesn't speak. Both shortswords come out. The X position. The man who taught me the cross-guard doesn't need instructions on how to use it.

"Brindle. Take your four around the southern ridge. Cut the retreat line. When Kael hits them head-on, anything that tries to pull back walks into you."

Brindle rests the bastard sword on her shoulder. The heavy crossbow is already slung. "How far south?"

"Two hundred yards. Wide arc. You'll hear Kael."

"Everyone hears Kael." Brindle's smile isn't nice. She moves.

"Finch. High ground. Call targets." Finch is already climbing.

Senna draws her blade. Looks at me. "Go do whatever you're doing at the gate. We'll clean this up."

They move out. Kael's squad in a tight wedge. Brindle's four splitting south at a jog. Senna and Varn straight up the middle. Twenty constructs flowing into formation around them. Professional. Fast. Five sentinels against two squad leaders, a Wardbreaker captain, and a scout with elevation.

---

The constructs split without hesitation. Twenty glass bodies peel east toward the sound of Kael's squad hitting the sentinel line. Eighty-four more flow south — a river of faceless soldiers following Thessaly's staff-light into the thermal shimmer.

The convergence hums behind me. Green-black light spiraling upward. The ritual circles carved into the platform glow faintly — self-sustaining, patient, counting toward a hundred.

I sit on the stone platform. Eat from the satchel. The ember banks low. The God Sight dims to a background pulse. Somewhere east, glass shatters — five times, then silence. Somewhere south, Thessaly is holding instruments up to something older than the Academy and hoping the readings make sense.

The Bleakmoor stretches in every direction. Dead glass. Grey sky. The smell of ozone and cold stone.

Haste is gone. The speed that made the western assault feel like target practice — gone. What's left is still more than enough. Stride. Frost Fang. Giant's Throw. God Sight burning steady behind my eyes for another forty hours.

The ember knows the number. Close. Close enough to taste. The next threshold is waiting on the other side of a long rest and when it arrives, it's the last one. Level twenty. The ceiling. Whatever that means for a man who started in a dormitory with a silver-white sword and a meal plan.

The fire in my chest settles. Not straining. Not pushing. Patient.

I close my eyes. The convergence hums. The bracelet is warm.

Somewhere south, a flare will go up. Green or red. Cure or kill.

I wait.
