# The Sundered Gate — Chapter Eleven: The Threshold

**Day Eighteen — Afternoon**

---

Thessaly and I descend sixty feet of carved glass. The terraces are steep enough that I'm running one hand along the wall the whole way down. She's not running hers. She doesn't need to.

Varn has taken the east wall already — I can see his shield against a ruined buttress across the courtyard, still as carved stone. Finch is a shape inside a shadow on the west flank, moving in the stop-start glide of a man who knows how to be invisible even in daylight.

The compound door stands open.

Not kicked. Not breached. *Invited.*

Thessaly stops three paces from the threshold and puts her staff across the door's inside edge. The staff's head clicks once — her ley-read picking up a working underneath.

"Ward line on the threshold. Alarm, not blade."

"They want a visitor log."

"They want witnesses," she says. "Or bait. Or both."

"Cross it."

"Cross it."

We cross together. The ward flickers as we pass — not an attack, a *notification.* Somewhere deeper in the compound, a bell I can't hear has just rung. If anyone inside was asleep, they aren't now.

---

Corridor beyond the door. Black glass walls, red glow from sconces set at shoulder height. Symbols etched into the glass in a geometry that isn't Academy — older school, same handwriting as the relays. Thessaly runs two fingers along one of them as we pass.

"Convergence notation. Same dialect as the triangle's vein work. This is the hand we've been chasing."

"One hand."

"Maybe two. Teacher and student. Or two masters working together, which is rarer."

The hum under the floor isn't gears. Not pumps. Closer to lungs through stone — the compound *breathing* the way the breathing ground half a mile back breathed. The whole structure is part of the same body.

Twenty paces in, the corridor opens into a junction — three passages, T-shaped, the central one the widest. Two figures waiting in the central passage about forty feet ahead.

Void Weavers. Abyssal casters — human-sized, robed, faces that aren't faces, hands already moving in the first arc of a pattern.

Thessaly's staff comes up.

"I have a plan," she says.

"How long."

"Eight seconds."

"Too long."

I step in front of her.

**Horizon Arc.** Both blades — fire on the right, frost on the left, entropy along the seam. The ring fills the corridor end to end, glass walls bending the light back at weird angles. Both weavers catch the ring before their pattern closes.

Three Cyclone throws — Giant's Throw stacked — back to back down the corridor. Emberfang, Frost Fang, Emberfang. The first throw drops the left weaver in a spray of void-dust that is not dust. The second throw catches the right one shoulder-high and takes it off the floor. The third, recall-and-throw in two heartbeats, finishes what the second started.

Fire. Frost. Dust.

They're dead before they finish deciding we're real.

The corridor rings with the silence afterward. Thessaly's staff lowers.

"I had a plan."

"You can use it on the next two."

"There will be more than two."

"Good. You'll get to use it twice."

She almost — almost — smiles.

---

End of The Sundered Gate — Chapter Eleven.
