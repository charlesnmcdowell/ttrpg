# The Sundered Gate — Chapter Fourteen: Tournament's Eve

**Day Eighteen — Night**

---

Thornwall at night.

The city has a different temperature than Varenholm. Varenholm is an Academy town that happens to have streets; Thornwall is a market-and-fighting-pits town that happens to have walls. Everything is louder. Everything smells like woodsmoke, horseflesh, and the coin-sweat of men who have been at a bargain for three hours without resolving it.

Iron Coliseum behind the registration office. Four-story stone, ringed in black iron grille, the torches bracketed to the walls at a height meant to silhouette a man standing on top of the ring. Built for violence at a scale that makes the Bleakmoor feel honest — the Bleakmoor at least doesn't charge admission.

I step out of the fifteen-foot entropy-lined portal I set up at the city's west gate an hour ago. Two guards on the registration office door watch me arrive. Neither draws. One mutters to the other: "Wrong door for a picnic."

"Next time I'll bring a basket."

The guard's mouth twitches. Not a smile. He's seen worse tonight.

---

Emberfrost on my back. Moor dust still caked to my boots. The ember hums in the current like it's already read the bracket and found its favorite matchups.

Inside, the registration hall is a long room with a high desk, a clerk with a pince-nez and the expression of a man who has filed six hundred forms today and has no intention of remembering any of them, and a wall of posted brackets with names chalked in rotating columns.

I sign the form. *Kenji. Varenholm.* Rank section left blank; the clerk fills it in without asking. *Platinum candidate, Vanguard proving circuit.*

He looks up.

"First time at Thornwall?"

"First time."

"Try not to die. The morticians are expensive and the Coliseum charges your guild for the floor polish after."

"Noted."

I push the form back. He stamps it. A token drops into a brass cup under the desk with a heavy clink — my number in the bracket.

---

Outside, I find an inn two streets from the Coliseum — the Leaning Horse, chosen because Garrett mentioned it once in passing and because the sign is painted by someone with enough humor to draw the horse actually leaning. A room above the stable. Narrow bed. Shuttered window that looks straight onto the Coliseum's lit torches.

I eat from the satchel — Pip's double portion, which was always Pip's real gift, not the cooking. I lay Emberfang and Frost Fang against the bed frame where I can reach both.

Solveth, through the blade, for the first time since the compound:

*The contractor is watching the gazette. He will see your name on the bracket tomorrow morning.*

"That's the idea."

*He will assume distraction.*

"That's also the idea."

*Be careful not to become distracted in the course of convincing him you are. It is an easy mistake.*

"Noted."

---

Tomorrow: forms. Day after: blood.

I close my eyes.

---

End of The Sundered Gate — Chapter Fourteen.
