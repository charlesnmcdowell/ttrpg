# The Sundered Gate — Chapter Eighteen: Champion

**Day 21, Late Afternoon → Evening**

---

The championship bout is fourteen seconds long and will be argued about in Thornwall taverns for twenty years.

Sera walks into the ring in a cleaned version of the Academy blue, rapier sheathed at her hip, Breach Shard holstered at her belt. She has just beaten Dren Valdric in the other semifinal — rapier against greatsword, patience against adaptive armor, two Breach Bolts at point-blank and Dren on the ground with the same grin he was wearing when he walked in. Three years of Lord of Ironholt's pride went back into its box with a polite bow.

I walk in against her. Bell.

She draws. I don't.

"Kenji."

"I yield."

My palm goes over her mouth before she can return the favor. Twenty seconds of her trying to argue through my fingers. Eight thousand witnesses. And the truth I say quietly against her ear, under the noise of a stadium trying to decide what it just watched: *smitten since Day One.*

When I let her go, she breathes once.

"You could have said that without the gag."

"You would have argued."

"I would have."

"I know."

She lifts the cup like it might bite. Iron Coliseum Champion. A thousand gold in prizes. The crowd still doesn't know which anthem to scream.

---

The semifinal still replays in my skull while the announcer is reading Sera's title into the ring.

Me and Senna. An hour earlier. Same ring, same dirt. Round two.

Charm lands dirty — I spend the last reaction I have on a compulsion and it catches her guard slipping at the worst heartbeat. She says something honest about my hands, hates herself mid-sentence, sets her stance anyway.

"Fight me."

I sit. Cross-legged. Hands behind my head. Stare at her until she walks over and flicks my forehead.

"Yield."

She yields for me. The walk back to the alcove is the wrong grin — not competition, not recovery. The kind of grin that means the math she had on me this morning isn't the math she has on me this afternoon.

Elara will filet me for compulsion on an ally in front of a stadium. Good. I earned the lecture and I'd like to get it out of the way tonight.

*Spar-resistances faded — three days did it. Frost Fang would have hit full if I'd swung steel; I didn't need to. Charm did the blade's job for thirty seconds. She's burned both exposures on me; next one rolls off. Next conversation with Senna happens without shortcuts.*

The list Elara keeps of every documented incident has my name on it now. I put it there myself.

---

Sera's final started with a nod across the ring — the nod she gave me meant *don't pretend you don't know this is a script.* Two Breach Bolts I ate on borrowed nerve because the Ward didn't catch them clean. Neither of us drawing steel when pride would have let us.

Twenty seconds in, I tickled her once under the ribs, to break the pretense she was maintaining that this was a fight. She laughed — real, not performance.

"Still think I'm cute?"

She nodded.

Yield.

---

Sera finds me in the corridor behind the ring. Trophy in one hand. The other touching my arm — not grabbing, just resting there.

"You could have won."

"I did win."

"You know what I mean."

I do. The corridor is empty. The crowd noise muffled through stone. For a moment I'm not the ArchMagus. Not the Wizard King. Not the man with the plan and the quip and the exit strategy.

"I didn't want the trophy," I say. And my voice sounds different when it isn't performing. Quieter. "I wanted you to have it."

She looks at me for a long time. Then her hand slides from my arm to my hand and she squeezes once.

"I know."

She walks back toward the arena. The trophy catches light through the corridor arch. She doesn't look back. She doesn't need to.

---

The next threshold is waiting on the other side of a long rest. The ember can feel it — not straining, not pushing. Patient. Like it knows what's coming and wants me to be ready for it.

The constructs number forty-seven across six portals. Fear everywhere. By tomorrow morning seventy-one. The day after, ninety-five. The Wizard King's army grows whether he sleeps or not.

Four hundred fifty-three gold. The investments haven't compounded yet today. Tomorrow the tally jumps again.

Dren is waiting in the medical area. The Lord of Ironholt wants to talk. The grin he left the ring with was a recruitment grin. Ironholt needs allies. So do I.

The gate is at fifty-six percent. Five days.

---

"There he is." Dren's grin appears over a bucket of warm water in the medical area. Left arm submerged — the frost from my throws left the skin mottled blue-white from elbow to fingertip. "The man who fights better naked."

"Your armour was the problem."

"My armour is ALWAYS the problem. Sit down."

I sit. The medical area is mostly empty — the other fighters already cleared out or went to the celebration. Dren waves the attendant away. Rolls the frozen arm. Winces once. Then doesn't wince again.

"I want to talk business."

"I figured."

"Ironholt needs contracts. Real ones. Not the bounty-board scraps the Hall throws at us. The merc guild has capacity — forty fighters, enchanted kit, experienced field leadership. What we don't have is infrastructure." He looks at the bracelet on my wrist. "You've got portals that reach five cities. Constructs that hold territory. An Academy paying you a stipend. An operational mine. And the Vanguard Hall is offering you platinum tier."

"You've done your homework."

"I do everyone's homework. That's why I'm Lord of Ironholt." He pulls his arm out of the bucket. Flexes the fingers. They move. He's satisfied. "Here's what I want. A logistics contract. My guild handles field operations — security, escort, creature clearing, whatever needs doing. Your portal network gives us reach. We split the bounties — you take a cut for infrastructure, I take a cut for personnel. We both file with the Hall. Both get credit."

"And Bleakmoor?"

The grin drops. Just for a second. Then it's back, but different. Sharper.

"Bleakmoor is why I'm asking now and not next week. You've got a gate at fifty-six percent and five days. You need fighters. Not constructs — fighters. People who think, adapt, make calls. I've got eight ready for deployment. Two fire-teams. Enchanted kit. They can be at your staging point in six hours."

"What do you want in return?"

"The contract. Long-term. And when the gate is handled, I want Ironholt on the portal network. A gate in my city. My guild operating on your infrastructure. Partners, not employees."

He extends the unfrozen hand.

"You owe me a pauldron anyway."

I shake it. "I'll have Garrett draft the terms."

"Tell Garrett I don't negotiate by letter. I negotiate over food." The grin again. "And I'm buying."

---

"Close the door."

Elara doesn't look up from the parchment. The Chancellor's office in Varenholm. Late afternoon light through the windows. Ink-stained fingers. The quill moving without pause.

I close the door.

"Thessaly sent me a report. Two pages. Clinically worded. 'Incident of compulsion magic deployed on allied personnel during tournament semifinal. Caster: ArchMagus Kenji. Target: Captain Senna Dawnmere. No prior consent obtained.'" She sets the quill down. Looks at me. The angular face isn't angry. It's calculating. "Charm Person on an allied squad captain. In front of eight thousand people."

"It worked."

"That's not the point and you know it." She stands. The Chancellor's cloak hangs on the back of her chair — she's in shirtsleeves, ink up to her elbows. "You are the most visible person in this institution. Every Academy graduate on that continent watched you fight. Half of them will hear about the charm before the week is out. The other half will hear a version that's worse."

"Senna isn't filing a complaint."

"Senna is an Ashen Fist with too much pride to admit a spell made her say something she wasn't ready to say. That doesn't mean it was appropriate. That doesn't mean the Council won't add it to their file." She picks the quill back up. Points it at me. "I'm not your mother. I'm your Chancellor. And I'm telling you — the next time you use compulsion magic on an ally, I will know about it before you finish the incantation. And we will have a very different conversation."

She sits back down. The quill resumes.

"The crater portal. Thornwall Watch Commander dispatched an investigation team two days ago. They reached the crater, found constructs, and filed a report. The report is now on a desk in Crestfall. You have perhaps three days before it reaches the Council."

"Garrett knows?"

"Garrett knows. He's factoring it into the audit defence." She doesn't look up. "Go. You have a war to run."

---

End of The Sundered Gate — Chapter Eighteen.
