# KENJI — THE SUNDERED GATE

## Chapter Three — *Dead Ground*

## Day Fourteen — Afternoon to Dusk

---

At Stride the world becomes a blur — farmland flattening into rolling hills, the road narrowing from cobblestone to packed dirt to cart track. Trees whipping past. Fence posts counting off like heartbeats. The wind tearing at the Academy blue.

I pass a merchant caravan heading west — four wagons, six guards, oxen plodding. They see a streak of blue and silver and then I'm gone.

The Ashward crossroads in just over two hours. I pull the Stride back — legs braking, boots gripping dirt, gloves tightening as my body fights the momentum. The stop is clean. Ten feet from the stone marker.

At a hundred and fifty feet per round, stopping is a negotiation between what the Stride wants and what the ground will tolerate. The gloves help. The boots help. A bad surface at the wrong moment and I'm stopping where physics decides, not where I choose.

I eat. Dried meat and hard cheese from the satchel, standing at the crossroads. The ember drinks it in fifteen minutes.

The ring warms.

"Sera."

"Crossroads?"

"Ashward. I'm entering the attack zone. I'll call with what I find."

"Copy. Road behind you is clear. One merchant caravan heading west — they looked rattled. Tarin spoke to the rear guard. They said the eastern road is wrong."

"Wrong how?"

"They couldn't say. The guard crossed himself and wouldn't talk about it after." Hoofbeats behind her voice. "Be careful."

"Four minutes."

"I'm timing."

---

Garrett's envelope. The map I can read — three red X's along a twelve-mile stretch. Distances. Terrain. The curve of the highway through a river valley.

The three pages of witness statements beneath the map, I cannot. The ink is clean. The handwriting is careful. I can't read a single word. The most powerful man on this road and the field intelligence is useless because nobody taught the baker's kid his letters.

The map is enough. First X six miles ahead. I fold the pages away and start walking. Not Stride — walking. God Sight at full range. Eyes on the road.

---

Mile three. The grass turns grey.

Both sides of the road, thirty feet wide. Still standing, still shaped like grass, but wrong. I crouch and touch a blade. It crumbles between my fingers like ash. The soil beneath it is black — the colour of something that had its guts pulled out.

The ember flinches. A sharp cold pulse at the base of my skull. Not the familiar entropy chill from Duskfen. Something I don't have a name for.

The grey strip widens as I walk east.

---

Mile six. First X.

A wagon overturned in the ditch. The canvas dissolved — black edges crumbling to dust. One wheel missing entirely, the axle ending in a smooth curve as if the wood simply stopped existing past a certain point. Cargo scattered. A child's shoe. A cooking pot. A leather satchel with the strap eaten through at the buckle.

No bodies. No blood. No drag marks.

Twenty feet off the road — a circle. Eight feet across. The soil fused to black glass, veins of dull red threaded through it. I crouch at the edge and the ember tightens. I know this. The geometric precision. The same engineering as the Delve, the same bones as Vael's seals. I don't have the words for it — I'm not Elara — but I've stood in those places and touched those things. The craft is the same. Someone found the old blueprints.

The glass is cold. Weeks old. Whatever came through here came and went.

---

Mile eight. The grey strip eighty feet wide. Trees leaning inward, bark splitting. The road cracking beneath my boots.

God Sight catches movement. Three shapes — flat, disc-shaped, the size of small tables — sliding through the deadfall on both sides of the road. No legs. No visible locomotion. They glide across the dead ground in jerky stops and starts, like stones skipping in slow motion. The air around them goes flat and dead — a pressure change, ambient sound cutting to nothing wherever they pass.

They're pacing me. Flanking. Getting closer with each parallel pass.

I step off the road. The pattern breaks. They charge.

---

Emberfrost. Horizon Arc. The double-blade spins and the ring of fire and frost hits all three mid-charge. Stone fragments scattering, black glass cracking, caustic undersides sizzling. The sound they make isn't a scream — it's silence getting louder.

The healing is immediate. Three targets. Half the total damage flowing back as temp HP. The math against groups is obscene.

The nearest catches the Cyclone throw through its center and comes apart — fragments losing cohesion, dissolving into the dead ground. Two left.

I split the blades. Cross-guard. The first disc slides under my leading sword and Frost Fang catches it on the exit. The second lunges from behind and meets Emberfang. Both creatures coming apart within seconds. Three pools of caustic acid sinking into dead earth.

Silence. The real kind. I sheathe both blades.

Whatever those things were, they wanted me moving toward the second X.

---

Mile nine. The dead zone a hundred feet across. No grass. No standing trees. Black dust and cracked earth.

The gate. Twelve feet of black glass, red veins pulsing. Not a scar — alive. Each pulse pushing a wave through the air. The ground within twenty feet dissolving upward against gravity.

And the watchers.

Two pillars of black glass. Seven feet tall. Narrow. Angular. Roughly human the way a knife is roughly a finger. No arms. Heads tapering to smooth points. Surfaces reflecting nothing — light goes in and doesn't come back. They hover an inch above the dead ground, red veins pulsing in sync with the gate.

A drone. Not heard — felt. In my teeth, my skull. Getting heavier with each step closer.

They're staggered. The near one screening, the far one guarding the breach. The far one's plating thickening with each gate pulse. The gate is feeding it.

The near one's head orients toward me. A seam of red light opens along the point.

It fires.

---

The beam hits my chest. Not force — dissolution. A fist-sized circle of blue dye eaten from the leather, the surface crumbling. Thirteen damage from something Ward Mastery could have softened if I'd thought to cast it before the fight.

I cast it now. The purple-gold shimmer settles into the armor. Acid resistance online. One action spent on defense instead of offense. The clock noticed.

God Sight reads the watchers' structure as I close — red veins converging at a nexus point, center mass. But the beam comes from the head. The seam at the tip. Break the emitter, the beam dies.

Frost Fang into the near one's tapered point. Critical hit — the frost colonizes. Thunderous Strike detonates through the contact and launches the monolith ten feet backward. Prone. Cracked. The drone stuttering.

I turn my back on the downed one and close thirty feet to the second. Called shot — disadvantage — the tip shatters under Frost Fang. Black glass fragmenting. Beam emitter destroyed. Thunderous Strike drops it prone.

The near one hauls itself upright and fires from behind me. Ward Mastery catches most of the acid. Manageable. The far one pulses from the ground — a sphere of necrotic-acid energy. I twist sideways and the Ward eats the acid component clean.

I close back to the near one. Three strikes. Frost Fang, Emberfang, Frost Fang. The third hit sends ice through every vein simultaneously. The monolith crystallizes — one second of frozen monument — then topples and shatters. Caustic vapor. The Ward absorbs all of it.

The far one. Beamless. Prone. The chain from the first kill carries into a free Frost Fang strike on the nexus. Then both blades. The natural twenty sends frost through what's left. Fire follows into the frozen architecture. It doesn't shatter — it melts. The black glass losing rigidity, folding inward, the drone dying mid-frequency.

Silence.

---

The gate. Still pulsing. Brighter.

Emberfang. Ember Lance. Golden-white light from the tip — grey dust igniting into motes of gold along its path, the dead road cracking from something trying to grow beneath it.

The beam hits the gate. Black glass screams in frequency. Red veins flare white where creation touches them. Gold light racing through the corruption — the same principle as the cascade, the same truth. The gate fights back — the membrane bulging, something on the other side pressing through. I hold the beam. The golden light widens. The red veins die. The membrane flattens. Whatever was pushing from the other side flinches from the light.

The gate folds inward. Contracting to a point no bigger than a coin.

Then nothing. Scorched brown earth where the breach used to be. Dead but natural.

---

I turn west and run.

The colour returns mile by mile. At eighteen miles east of Varenholm the ring warms — from the other end. I stop clean.

"Sera."

"Where have you been? I've been pulsing the ring for twenty minutes."

"Deep in the attack zone. Where are you?"

"Eighteen miles east of Varenholm. Ryn spotted the grey strip starting two miles back. He's filling a notebook." Hoofbeats. Tarin breathing steady. "Status."

"Three of the small ones. Flat things — looked like chunks of dead ground that learned to slide around. They tried to box me toward the second site. I killed all three."

"And the second site?"

"Two bigger ones. Tall. Black glass. No arms. Floated off the ground. They shot beams from their heads — acid and something worse. Like having a piece of yourself erased for a second."

"How big?"

"Seven feet. Narrow. Like someone carved a person out of dark stone and forgot the arms. They made a drone — gets in your teeth the closer you get."

"You killed both?"

"Frost Fang through the head. Break the tip, the beam stops. They're tough but slow once you freeze them."

"And the gate?"

"Ember Lance. The gold light ate the red light. It folded up." I think about how to say the next part. "The gates are built the same way as the stuff in the Delve. Same bones. Someone found the old blueprints and is using them to open doors to something bad."

"That's significant."

"Elara conversation, not a road conversation. There's a third site six miles further east. Still open. I turned back to find you."

Silence.

"You turned back."

"You're on a road with this stuff and I couldn't reach you."

"The road behind you is clear because you cleared it. We're fine, Kenji."

"I know. I needed to hear it."

The warmth in her voice that she'll deny later: "Heard. Now turn around and deal with the third gate. We'll sweep and secure the dead sites. Camp at the crossroads."

"Copy."

"And Kenji — four minutes. It's been two hours."

"I was forty miles away."

"Excuses."

---

East again. The colour draining. Two more meals from the satchel.

Mile twelve. Dusk. The sky the colour of a bruise.

It's not a strip anymore. Both sides of the road, treeline to treeline, as far as God Sight reaches. No grass. No trees. Just black dust and cracked earth and silence.

And ahead — half a mile down the road — red light. Pulsing. Brighter than the second gate.

Not one gate. Three. Clustered. Overlapping dissolution zones creating a dead circle two hundred feet across.

Something standing in the center. Not a watcher. Bigger. Much bigger.

God Sight strains. 120 feet isn't enough. The shape — tall, broad, wrong — but not the detail.

The drone reaches me from half a mile away. In my teeth already.

I stand on dead ground with the dusk settling and both blades at my hips. The road behind me is clear. The road ahead is worse than anything I've seen today. Sera is thirty miles west with two people I put in her care. Thornwall is further east. The tournament is six days away and I'm standing at the edge of something that might not care about tournaments.

The ember doesn't want to go back.

The ember doesn't get to vote. Not tonight.
