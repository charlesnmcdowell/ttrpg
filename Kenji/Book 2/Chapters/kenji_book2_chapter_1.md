# KENJI — BOOK TWO: *The Descent*

---

*Thirteen days ago, a man woke face-down in mud at the edge of a forest that shouldn't exist, in a world he doesn't remember arriving in. No name. No memory. No possessions. Just the clothes on his back and a warmth at the base of his skull that hadn't decided whether to catch.*

*His name is Kenji. The warmth is creation — one half of the cosmic engine that built the world.*

*In thirteen days he has: killed his first monster with a borrowed axe. Earned a sword from a blacksmith. Tamed a creature of entropy with his bare hands. Stabilized a dying god beneath a farmer's cellar. Built a mercenary guild from a dismantled bandit operation. Discovered that the Academy's Chancellor had been siphoning the magical essence of 347 graduates for twelve years. Seized the Hollow Crown — the instrument of that theft — and overwrote its anchor with his own soul. Forced a public confession from a 140-year-old man afraid to die. Became the youngest Academy Champion in institutional history. Built a portal network. Cleared the Academy's combat gauntlet at a level nobody has reached in thirty-eight years. And looked his most loyal contractor in the eyes and said: "I'm the new asshole in charge."*

*339 threads are still feeding. The clock is ticking. The expedition into ancient ruins beneath Duskfen launches today. And the City Council of Varenholm is about to learn what new management looks like.*

---

## Chapter One — *What Lives in the Dark*

## Day Thirteen — Morning

---

Dawn. The Silver Draft. Both swords on my hips — amber warmth right, cold entropy left. The ember sits deeper than it did yesterday. Wider. The magic knows what it is now.

Pip is cross-legged on the bed. Dimensional folding. Past chapter ten.

I run the inventory. Nine gold, nine silver, eight copper. Twenty-six meals in the satchel. Three honey cakes on my body. Six and a half days of personal food at the rate the ember burns through it.

"How much would enchanted underclothes cost? Self-repair, self-cleaning, charisma resonance weave. And a mask — shows eyes and hair, charisma boost, layered mental resistance."

The textbook closes. One finger holding the page.

She runs it like a ledger. Self-cleaning is cheap — a few silver. Self-repair is sustained enchantment — a few gold. Resonance weaving is precision work, calibrated to the wearer. The mask is worse — layered abjuration, rare base materials to hold five or six active weaves without degrading.

"Fifty gold for both. You have nine."

---

"Wow, Pip — you are so smart and knowledgeable about enchantments and trade."

The faintest flush. Then the practical woman.

"I run an inn. I know what things cost. That's not smart — that's survival."

She's smiling when I gather my gear.

---

Garrett's room. The table covered in supply manifests and expedition logistics. He reads my face before I speak.

"That's your 'I need something unreasonable' face."

"What kind of work does the Broken Antler have that's risky enough to pay sixty to a hundred gold and leave me time to join the expedition?"

He lays it out. The guild is a week old. The highway contract is forty gold a month for the entire operation. Merchant escorts pay one to three gold per run.

Nothing on the board pays sixty gold for a morning's work.

"That said."

A crumpled notice from under the manifests. Council bounty board. The Ashward Mines — fourteen miles northwest. Something moved in three weeks ago, killed six workers. The garrison won't go in.

"This came through two days ago. I didn't bring it to you because the timing was wrong — you had the gauntlet, the Council session, the siphon work. The expedition is the priority." He smooths the parchment. "But since you're asking for sixty-gold morning work, this is what exists."

Fifty gold. Proof of kill. A silver mine that generates two hundred gold a year sitting idle because something with claws the size of chisels moved in and the military decided posting a bounty was easier than sending soldiers.

---

I tell him the plan. Not the bounty. The mine itself. Claim it. Portal it. New management. Two hundred gold offer to the Council. One hour deadline. Triple wages for miners. Guild and Academy benefits.

Garrett doesn't move for six seconds.

He explains — carefully, precisely — that the mine is a registered municipal asset. That seizing it burns every alliance I've built in two weeks. Elara's standing. The guild contract. The Academy residency. The expedition funding.

I adjust. Inform the Council the previous company is done. Offer still stands. New management. Not theft — a more efficient company claiming an idle resource.

Garrett puts both hands flat on the table.

"I'm asking you — as the man who left Voss because he saw where 'take what you want' leads — to think about who you want to be when this is over."

---

I look him in the eyes.

"Garrett. I didn't come this far because I'm a nice guy. I came this far because no one has killed me. Yet."

The room is quiet.

"I'll do what I can for the little guy. But I could care less about those rich noble assholes in their academies, councils, and whatever else."

I raise my hand. The air drops ten degrees. Frost Fang materializes — blue-black steel, green smoke, the jagged crystal crossguard. Pulled from across the Academy. From a priest's table. From a god's resting place.

Somewhere in the Council chamber, Edwyn's hand closes on empty air.

I look the blade over. Sheathe it at my left hip.

"I'm the new asshole in charge. And whoever disagrees can come have a chat."

---

Garrett delivers the ultimatum. I go collect Ryn.

Pip first. My room. She's still on the bed with the textbook.

I tell them both about the mine. A creature. Fourteen miles. A training run.

"If you know a basic spell to defend yourself with, come and use that. If not, head to Aldwin's class. I'll excuse the absence — we can call this special training from the ArchMagus himself." I look at them. "But I understand if either of you don't want to come."

Pip closes the book. The brown eyes steady.

"I don't have spells, Kenji. I've been reading theory for two days. I can't fold space. I can't make a shield. I can't cast anything."

She sets her hand on the textbook.

"I'd be a body you have to protect, not an ally who helps you fight. And you just told me you promise we'll be safe — which means you're already thinking about keeping me alive instead of thinking about the creature."

She unfolds her legs.

"I want to come. I'm telling you I shouldn't. Not yet."

The innkeeper who runs a business making a business decision. Not afraid. Strategic.

---

Ryn is in his doorway. Three books on the floor. Satchel packed.

"I don't know any spells either." Flat. Factual. "But I can throw a rock accurately and I can read what a creature does before it does it." He looks at the swords on my hips. "I'd be more useful watching you fight than sitting in Aldwin's classroom learning from a book. Aldwin teaches theory."

He picks up the satchel.

"You are the practice."

---

One honest no. One quiet yes. I give them both horses. Pip rides toward the Academy to cover for Ryn with Aldwin. Ryn rides northwest.

I cast God Sight. Stride L4. The world sharpens. The speed fills my legs. I'm through the western gate before the guards finish their shift change.

---

The Ashward Mines. Forty minutes at 150 feet per second.

A broad clearing cut into the hillside. Timber-framed main shaft sloping down. Abandoned work buildings. An overturned wheelbarrow. A pickaxe mid-swing in a log. A single boot in the gravel.

Two sets of scratches on the timber frame. Newer ones going in — sharp, deep. Older ones coming from inside — wider, worn smooth from repeated use. Two creatures. Ryn will figure this out in fifteen seconds when he arrives.

I wait. Eat from the satchel. Check the outbuildings. Take sixty feet of heavy rope and two torches from the tool shed.

Forty-three minutes. Hooves on the northwest road. Ryn dismounts. Badly. The horse sighs.

He looks at the mine entrance for fifteen seconds.

"Two creatures?"

---

I tie the rope from my waist to the ore cart's coupling. Tell Ryn to get in.

"You're going to pull me."

"At speed. Stay low."

Buffs. Ward Mastery L4 — lightning resistance. The ozone told me what I needed to know.

I run. Stride L4. The mine shaft swallows us. The cart screams on the rails behind me. God Sight opens the darkness like floodlights — 120 feet of perfect vision in absolute black.

The shaft descends. Silver veins thickening. Drag marks down the center — something serpentine. A junction — main tunnel continuing straight, side tunnel branching left. A broken ore cart. Claw marks.

The side tunnel has webs. Not spider silk — translucent sheets layered floor to ceiling. Bones caught in them. A mining helmet. A boot. The match to the one outside.

I cast Radiant Edge on Emberfang. Golden-white light floods the mine. Thirty feet of visibility for Ryn. His first sight of the tunnels — the webs, the bones, the two paths.

"Not spider," he says. "Wrong structure. Sheets, not radial patterns."

"Stay in the cart. Don't move. If anything comes from the main tunnel — yell."

---

The Prowler. Side tunnel. A natural chamber fifteen feet across. Webs everywhere. And pressed against the far wall — eight legs, six feet of tarnished silver chitin, four eyes, two blind, two glowing blue. Ley-sensitive. Hiding from whatever lives deeper in the mine.

The radiant light hits the web curtains. The chamber blazes gold. The blue eyes lock on Emberfang's signature.

It tries to run. Tries to climb. Spinnerets firing defensive barriers.

Three hits. Frost Fang through the flank — ice and force detonating between chitin plates. Emberfang trailing — creation fire in the cracked seam, the creature igniting. Emberfang again — overhead, two-handed, the golden blade splitting the triangular head.

God Sight crit on the third swing. Natural 18.

The webs catch fire. The chamber burns behind me as I Stride back to the junction.

Ryn is where I left him. He heard the acceleration. The shriek. Three impacts. The silence.

"It's dead?"

"It's dead."

---

The Wyrm. Main tunnel. Full Stride with the cart screaming behind me on the rails.

God Sight finds it at 120 feet. A cavern — sixty feet across, thirty high. Natural. The miners broke through into it. A ley tributary surfaces at the center, and coiled around the breach point — thirty-five feet of gunmetal serpent crusted with silver mineral deposits. Eyeless. Pit-organ heat sensors. A ridge of crystallized silver along the spine crackling with blue-white discharge.

A biological lightning cannon.

Wounded. A long gash on the left flank — the Prowler's work, weeks old. The thing came here to feed and recover. The ley energy keeping it alive.

Through the bond, Solveth. Brief: Old. Wounded. It did not choose to be here.

The Stride stop doesn't hold. I overshoot ten feet into the cavern. The cart slams into the tunnel-cavern transition and stops dead. Ryn jolts forward.

I dust myself off.

"Ryn... you didn't see that when retelling this story to Pip."

From the cart, quiet: "See what?"

Good man.

The spine ridge flares. The jaws open — not teeth. A silver-lined cavity crackling with concentrated lightning. Aimed at me.

The bolt crosses sixty feet. Ward Mastery catches seven points. The rest punches through. My arms buzz. The Stride stutters and reasserts.

Three rounds until it fires again. Enough.

---

"Ryn. Stay there. Keep watch. I'm going to show you a new lesson."

God Sight finds the weakness. The spine ridge — a biological capacitor. It channels ley energy toward the jaw cavity. But two-thirds down the body, where the Prowler's old wound scarred the flank, the crystalline structure is fractured. Damage that section and the charging cycle breaks.

Stride. Seventy feet in a heartbeat. Both swords drawn. The scarred section right in front of me.

Frost Fang leads. The blue-black blade finds the fracture. Natural 19 — God Sight turns it into a critical hit. The cold detonates inside the capacitor channel. The ridge shatters along a two-foot section. The charging pulse hits the gap and dies.

Emberfang trails. Golden-white radiance flooding the crater. The wound ignites — amber fire burning along the ridge line in both directions. The Wyrm's body arches. The coils release the ley breach for the first time.

Frost Fang. Stride extra action. Natural 20. The blade buries to the crossguard in the shattered ridge. Ice crystallizes along the spine in both directions — head and tail. The entropy edge unmaking the nervous system section by section.

The head rises one last time. The jaws open — a reflex, the capacitor destroyed, nothing to fire. They close. The head drops. Thirty-five feet of serpent hitting the cavern floor in a sequence of impacts that echo for three seconds.

---

Ryn watched all of it. From the tunnel mouth in the wrecked cart.

"Three things," he says when I walk back. "You identified the weakness before you engaged. You used the Prowler's old wound — finished what it started. And you stayed close instead of retreating because I was behind you."

He pauses.

"That last one isn't a combat lesson. That's a different kind."

---

Portal Gateway. Ten minutes. The fourth golden archway rises from the cavern wall beside the ley breach. Varenholm. Duskfen. Broken Antler. Ashward Mine. Four doors.

We step through to the Academy. Ley line refill. Buffs renewed.

---

Garrett's room. The parchment on his table is different. A single document. Council seal.

"The mine?"

"Cleared. Both creatures. Portal built."

He processes the timing. Two hours round trip including two kills and a permanent magical gateway.

"The Council declined the ultimatum. The mine is a municipal asset. They don't negotiate ownership under time pressure."

"But."

A counter-proposal. The bounty stands — fifty gold. Plus a management contract. Twelve percent of monthly ore output to the guild.

I don't blink.

"No deal. Give me the fifty gold. Take them the proof — that's payment for services rendered. The mine is mine. They missed the deadline. Tell them they can work out a contract with the Broken Antler on purchasing ore. Bidding starts at three times the old price. Open to any buyer — including neighboring territories and kingdoms. We pay standard taxes. We're not criminals. We're entrepreneurs."

Garrett looks at me. He's done pushing back. The contractor takes the job.

"Fifty gold. What proof?"

I hand him the Wyrm's jaw-crystal. Silver-crusted. Unmistakable.

He wraps it. Heads for the door. Pauses.

"The Council will respond to this, Kenji. Not today. Not this week. But they will respond."

He leaves.
