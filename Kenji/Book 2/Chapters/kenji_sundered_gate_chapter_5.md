# KENJI — THE SUNDERED GATE

## Chapter Five — *The List*

## Day Fourteen Night — Day Fifteen Dawn

---

The campfire. The crossroads. Stars over a road that runs east into dead ground and west toward everything we left behind this morning.

Sera is sitting next to me. Not across. Next to. Close enough that her shoulder touches the acid-burned leather on my left side. The professional mask has slipped and whatever is underneath is warm and quiet and looking at the fire.

The extra travel was worth it to hear your voice and know you were ok.

The mask drops entirely. Two seconds of something unguarded in her expression. The flush at her cheekbones. The twitch — the real one, the one that reaches her eyes.

She pulls my hand. Toward the tent. Not gently.

---

Besides... I know it's been awhile since the horse had to travel so far and so fast with so much extra weight. I'm glad it's still with us in the land of the living. I hope.

The shoulder against mine goes rigid.

"Did you just call me heavy."

Ryn's charcoal stops. Tarin turns her head for the first time since I arrived.

"The *horse.* You're concerned about the *horse.*"

I stand. Walk to the bay mare. Run my hand along her neck — warm, steady, well-fed. She nudges my palm looking for food.

"Very impressive."

Sera's voice, quiet and precise: "The list is getting longer."

---

Juuuust kidding. I smack her on the back and join Ryn to call it a night.

Ryn doesn't move for three seconds. His mouth opens. Closes. Opens.

"I —" He swallows. "Yes. Night. Good."

Tarin's voice from the perimeter. Flat. Without turning.

"Dead man."

The last thing I hear before sleep is Sera's tent flap closing. It closes the way a sword enters a sheath. Precisely. Once. With finality.

---

Eight hours. The ember expands in the dark — filling space that yesterday's fights carved open. A fifth register unfolds. Deeper. The spells I already know stretch into it like water finding a new channel.

Level 11.

The portal network widens in my mind. Four gateways was a house. Eight is a web. The expanded gates will be different — larger, heavier, the bracelet's dual nature stamped into the architecture. Creation and entropy outlined together. Towering things. Not subtle. Not meant to be.

---

Dawn. Grey light. Frost on the stone marker.

I eat from the satchel before anything else — the ember reminding me before my stomach does. Dried meat and bread. The timer resets.

Frost Fang. The old summon expired overnight. I push an L2 slot through the bracelet and the blue-black blade reforms at my left hip. The gold line down the center catching the dawn. Solveth settling back into his home without comment.

Stride. L5 this time. The new register. 180 feet per round. The current flooding my legs differently than L4 — not faster, *smoother.* The road already calculating beneath me.

Sera is at the far side of camp. The dark hair pulled tight for travel. She doesn't look at me. She looks at the road.

"Orders stand?"

"Orders stand."

Ryn — satchel packed, notebooks secured — nods once without looking up. He understands the assignment.

Tarin is already at the road's edge. She doesn't say goodbye because she doesn't say things that aren't operationally necessary.

"Four minutes," Sera says to the horizon.

"I'll make it three."

The faintest twitch. Aimed at something I can't see.

I head west.
