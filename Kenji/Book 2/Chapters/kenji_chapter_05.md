## Chapter Five — *The List*

## Day Fourteen Night — Day Fifteen Dawn

---

The campfire. The crossroads. Stars over a road that runs east into dead ground and west toward everything we left behind this morning.

Ryn is sketching by firelight. The scavengers. Three different views — top, side, the sliding motion captured in quick strokes. He holds up the page.

"Flat. Disc-shaped. No visible sensory organs. But they flanked you — that's coordinated behavior. Something was directing them."

"Or they're programmed." Tarin, from the perimeter. Flat as always. "Constructs don't need eyes. They need instructions."

"The question is who's writing the instructions," Ryn says. He turns back to the page.

Sera next to me. Shoulder touching the acid-burned leather. Her fingers brush the dissolved circle on the chest piece — the watcher's beam mark. She doesn't say anything. She doesn't need to.

I turn my head. The firelight catches the flush at her cheekbones. The mask she wears for the squad — captain, professional, controlled — is thin right now. Mine is too.

"Tent," she says. Quiet.

---

Not the performance version. Not the theater in the courtyard or the quip with the ring. Just her hands finding the buckles of the ruined armor. My forehead against hers. The campfire through canvas making everything amber.

"You got hit," she says against my mouth.

"Barely."

"Liar." Her fingers press the bruise under the acid burn. I flinch. She doesn't apologize.

The list of things I should be thinking about — the third gate, the node, whoever built the network — goes quiet. For an hour it's just the canvas and the warmth and the sound of her breathing slowing down.

---

I step out into the cold. The fire is low. Ryn doesn't move for three seconds. "I — yes. Night. Good."

Tarin's voice from the perimeter. Flat. "Dead man."

---

Eight hours. Level 11 — fifth register opens like a ceiling lifting instead of a door.

Solveth: *Road east got heavier overnight. You sealed a branch. Trunk's still feeding.*

"Good pep talk."

Dawn. Frost on the marker. I eat; Frost Fang returns to my hand like a dog that heard breakfast. Stride hums smoother — stops don't jar, turns don't pull.

Sera is up. Dark hair pulled tight. Arms folded — the default. She doesn't look at me. One hand resting on the Whisperstone ring, turning it once.

"Orders stand?"

"Orders stand."

"Four minutes," she says to the horizon.

"I'll make it three."

I head west.

---

End of The Sundered Gate — Chapter Five.
