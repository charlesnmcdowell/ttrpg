## Chapter Four — *Three Doors Down*

## Day Fourteen — Night → Day Fifteen — Dawn

---

The crossroads camp is three fires and four horses picketed in a half-ring. Sera's set a ward circle wide enough to sleep four squads inside. Ryn's at the center fire with his notebook open, sketching what he saw of the grey strip from the road. Tarin's at the far verge with her staff across her knees, watching east.

I come out of the dark at walking pace. Sera looks up from the wardline and reads the armor before she reads my face — the circle gone from the chest plate, the scorch on the back.

"Triangle," I say. "Not one gate. Three, arranged. Something in the center the eye can't settle on. I didn't cross the line."

"Good."

"It wasn't bravery. The ember pulled back without asking."

"Even better."

I eat while I talk. Pip's satchel makes a fourth meal appear when a third had already vanished. Ryn draws while I describe — three pillars, twelve feet each, pulsing in sync, a node in the middle that refuses to be looked at. His hand keeps drawing until the scene is on paper.

"Your plan," Sera says.

"Go back at first light. Drop the gates one at a time before I touch the node. You hold a ward line a mile west. If the node comes off its anchor I need a wall to run back to that isn't you in its path."

"A mile is a long walk if it's fast."

"Stride closes it."

"And if Stride doesn't."

"Then you hold the wall and write the letter to Elara."

Tarin, without turning: "I like this man."

Sera looks at the fire. "One sleep. Dawn."

One sleep. I take it in forty-five minutes because the ember won't let me have the whole night. Dawn comes grey.

---

First light. Darkblades a mile west at a ward ring Ryn spent the last hour seeding. I run the last mile alone.

The clearing is where I left it. The triangle still pulses. The node in the middle still won't let God Sight focus on it cleanly.

God Sight paints what it can — triangle, clean angles, kill floor where the dissolution fields overlap. Veins run from each gate to a center junction like Aldwin sketched it on purpose.

Solveth: *Engineered. Not stumbled into.*

"You're sure you're not just impressed?"

*I'm terrified. That means I'm sure.*

Center mass resolves as my eyes adjust: plates, no bones, block head, three seams — one per gate. Arms on the floor, drinking the veins. A hub. Not a monster — infrastructure with a pulse.

Solveth: *Gates sustain it. Kill the gates, kill the node.*

"Music to my ears."

I point Emberfang at the nearest gate. Ember Lance.

The golden beam hits Gate A. Creation light floods the black glass — the red veins flashing gold for a heartbeat, the architecture fighting the overwrite the way a dam fights a river. It loses. The gate folds. One head-seam goes dark.

Solveth: *Same language as the Delve. Compatible overwrite.*

"Save the lecture."

*Your funeral.*

The entity's fingers close.

The glass floor cracks in concentric rings. The block head rotates. Two blazing seams orient on me.

It opens its mouth. Not a beam — sound. A wall of concussive force that hits like jumping into a frozen lake. Fifteen feet backward. Down. Deaf. The dissolution floor bites into my back where I land — the glass eating at the leather, the Academy blue dissolving at the contact points.

---

Emberfrost locks — twin blades crossed at the guard, fire on the right, frost on the left, creation running the seam.

Windrunner Boots catch air. Straight up — sixty feet in two beats. Both remaining gates birth watchers as I climb, black glass pillars rising from the ground like they were always there and nobody told me. The network defending itself.

*Breathe through it,* Solveth says. *Creation aura stitches while you climb. Don't stop climbing.*

By the third arc over the clearing, creation heal has erased the back-dissolution, the small burns, the fight on the floor. Full strength.

The entity kneels. Drives both hands into the floor. A geyser of dissolution erupts from the center — sixty feet tall, area denial. Corruption tendrils climb from multiple points, building a ceiling. But it's splitting power across too many systems.

Gate C. Far side. Unguarded except for one watcher whose beam can't reach altitude.

I dive. The wind pulls tears across my face. Emberfang forward, tip first, Ember Lance already building in the palm. Gold light pouring down the blade.

Watcher C's beam catches me mid-lance — the ward barrier absorbs most of it, the rest removing another handful of Academy blue from the shoulder. I don't care about the leather anymore. Aldwin will understand.

The lance hits Gate C dead center. Gold eats red. The gate folds.

The entity's second seam dies. The geyser thins. Tendrils collapse. One gate left.

---

The entity commits everything to Gate B. Both hands on the membrane. Pulling something through — the gate brightening, the red veins thickening, the air between the pillars rippling like heat haze over a road. Whatever is on the other side wants in.

Watcher B plants itself between me and the gate. Overcharging — plating thickening, head-seam blazing. Building toward a shot that doubles the normal beam. The drone climbs until my fillings ache.

God Sight reads the architecture from altitude. The overcharge pools in the nexus at the watcher's center mass. The root vein connecting it to Gate B runs through the floor. Exposed. Pressurized. All that stolen energy flowing through a single line like water through a pipe.

I don't need to hit the watcher. I need to hit the fuse.

Solveth: *Back-pressure will flash. Be outside the blast radius before it lands.*

"Working on it."

Through the tendril canopy — the dissolution tendrils scraping against the creation aura, hissing, disintegrating on contact but slowing me down, buying the watcher time. Emberfrost into the glass floor where the root vein is thickest. Both blades. Everything I have left in the arms.

The disruption races back — twenty, thirty, forty feet. The overcharged nexus receives a surge from behind. The watcher's head-seam flares white. The plating bulges outward.

The watcher unravels. The detonation expands forty feet and hits Gate B broadside. The membrane shatters. The breach collapses inward. The world hiccups — a skip in reality, like a heartbeat dropping a beat. The air tastes like copper and ozone.

Silence.

---

The entity. Hands empty. Seams dark. Plating cracking along the joins like a statue that forgot why it was standing.

I spend two Ember Lances anyway. Gold down the blade into center mass. First lance opens the plating. Second lance empties what the plating was hiding. The node comes apart in layers — outer shell, then inner lattice, then a final bright moment of something *looking* at me before the looking ends.

Silence. Wind, again, moving into the space three gates and a node used to occupy. The world filling a hole for the second time today.

Solveth: *Whoever built this will build more.*

"I know."

*They will know, by now, that you closed it.*

"I know that too."

---

West.

Emberfrost sheathed. Boots down. Stride opens. The dead zone falls behind in a handful of heartbeats — then the grey strip — then the first green mile — then the mile where a bird calls again.

Two miles out from the crossroads Stride runs dry. The ember is at the bottom of the well for the first time since the Delve. I walk the last mile. The armor creaks. The leather at my back has a hole the size of a fist and the leather at my shoulder is the color of raw hide where the blue used to be. Aldwin's going to look at it and not say anything, which will be worse than saying something.

Seven meals left in the satchel and my stomach doesn't care about the number.

---

Crossroads. Firelight through trees. Ward ring still up — Ryn's work holding, the glyphs steady. Sera's bay notices me first and doesn't spook. The horses know my smell now.

Tarin at the verge, staff across her shoulders. She doesn't flinch when I come out of the dark.

"He's back."

Sera's up from the fire before Tarin finishes the sentence. She doesn't run. She walks the six paces to the wardline, drops the seam for me, and walks me through. Her hand on the small of my back where there isn't a hole.

Ryn at the fire: "How many."

"Three gates. One node. All of it down."

"Cost."

"Armor. Reserves. A meal I owe Pip."

Ryn writes it in the book. Sera sits me down at the fire and pulls the satchel off my shoulder like she's been waiting to do it for an hour. Tarin crosses to the far fire and puts water on without being asked.

Sera, quiet, only to me: "Four minutes."

"It was closer to two hours."

"Close enough."

---

End of The Sundered Gate — Chapter Four.
