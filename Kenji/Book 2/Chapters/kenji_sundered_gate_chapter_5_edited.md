## Chapter Five — *The List*

## Day Fourteen Night — Day Fifteen Dawn

---

The campfire. The crossroads. Stars over a road that runs east into dead ground and west toward everything we left behind this morning.

Ryn is sketching by firelight. The scavengers. Three different views — top, side, the sliding motion captured in quick strokes. He holds up the page.

"Flat. Disc-shaped. No visible sensory organs. But they flanked you — that's coordinated behavior. Something was directing them."

"Or they're programmed." Tarin, from the perimeter. Flat as always. "Constructs don't need eyes. They need instructions."

"The question is who's writing the instructions," Ryn says. He turns back to the page.

Sera next to me. Shoulder touching the acid-burned leather. Her fingers brush the dissolved circle on the chest piece — the watcher's beam mark.

The extra travel was worth it to hear your voice and know you were ok.

The mask slips. The flush at her cheekbones. She pulls my hand toward the tent.

---

Besides... I know it's been awhile since the horse had to travel so far and so fast with so much extra weight. I'm glad it's still with us in the land of the living. I hope.

"Did you just call me heavy."

"The *horse.* You're concerned about the *horse.*"

I walk to the bay mare. Pat her neck. "Very impressive."

"The list is getting longer."

---

Juuuust kidding. I smack her on the back and join Ryn.

Ryn doesn't move for three seconds. "I — yes. Night. Good."

Tarin's voice from the perimeter. Flat. "Dead man."

The last sound before sleep is Sera's tent flap closing. Precisely. Once.

---

Eight hours. Level 11. The fifth register unfolds — not a door opening but a ceiling lifting. The ember expands into space that wasn't there yesterday. Wider channels. Deeper current. The portal network widens in my chest like a map redrawing itself — four gateways was a house. Eight is a web. I can feel where the new ones want to go before I build them.

Solveth, through the dawn quiet: *The road east grows heavier. What you sealed yesterday was a branch. The trunk is still feeding.*

Dawn. Frost on the stone marker. I eat from the satchel. Frost Fang resummoned — the blade arriving in my hand like a dog returning to heel. Stride L5 — 180 feet. The new register handles the speed differently. Smoother. The stops don't jar. The turns don't pull.

Sera is up. Dark hair pulled tight. Arms folded — the default. She doesn't look at me. One hand resting on the Whisperstone ring, turning it once.

"Orders stand?"

"Orders stand."

"Four minutes," she says to the horizon.

"I'll make it three."

I head west.
