# KENJI — THE SUNDERED GATE

## Chapter Four — *Three Doors Down*

## Day Fourteen — Dusk

---

The ember doesn't get to vote. Except tonight it does.

I don't turn back.

God Sight resolves the layout as I close. Three twelve-foot gates arranged in a triangle, dissolution zones overlapping in the center where the ground has fused to a single dark mirror. Root veins pulse through the glass floor in a staggered rhythm — one-two-three — converging at the center.

Where the thing is sitting.

Layered plate over plate like geological strata compressed into a body. A block head with three seams pulsing in sync with the three gates. Two massive arms ending in flat spade hands pressed to the glass floor, drawing from the network. A watcher flanks each of the two far gates.

I point Emberfang at the nearest gate. Sixty feet.

Ember Lance.

---

The golden beam hits Gate A and the overwrite cascades. The gate folds. One of three head-seams on the entity goes dark.

The entity's fingers close. The glass floor cracks. Both hands lift. The block head rotates with the slowness of something that hasn't been interrupted in a very long time. Two blazing seams orient on me.

It opens its mouth. Not a beam — sound. The drone weaponized into a wall of concussive force. It hits everywhere at once — chest, ribs, skull. Twenty-one damage. Fifteen feet backward. Prone. Deaf.

---

Emberfrost locks. Duality Aspect. Stride dies. Golden light blooms. Creation mode. I go straight up — sixty feet vertical, the dead circle shrinking below.

Both remaining gates birth watchers as I climb. The network defending itself.

The deafened whine fades by the second round. Sound returning in layers — the drone, the gate pulses, the crack of glass. By the third round of circling, creation heal has erased the entity's hit entirely. Full health. Temp HP stacking.

The entity kneels. Drives both hands into the floor. The root network weaponizes — a geyser of dissolution erupts from the center, sixty feet tall, thirty feet wide. Area denial. Corruption tendrils climb from multiple points, rising ten feet per round, building a ceiling between me and the gates.

But it's splitting power across three gates, two watchers, a geyser, and tendrils. Too many systems on too little fuel.

Gate C. Far side of the triangle. Unguarded except for Watcher C. The entity shifted to cover B.

---

I dive. 120 feet of flight makes the math generous. Through the tendril layer — Ward Mastery eats the acid, the necrotic grazes me — and Emberfang tips downward.

Ember Lance. Gate C screams and folds.

Watcher C's beam catches me mid-lance. Dissolution burn across my left side. Temp HP absorbs most of it.

I'm climbing before the watcher recharges. The entity's second seam dies. The geyser thins. Tendrils on Gate C's side collapse.

One gate left.

---

The entity commits everything to Gate B. Both hands on the membrane, pulling something through from the other side. Not attacking. Fully invested in the summon.

Watcher B plants itself between me and the gate. But it's doing something new — pressing its base into the root network, the plating thickening, the head-seam blazing hotter. Overcharging. Building toward something bigger.

God Sight reads the architecture from 110 feet. The overcharge pools in the nexus at center mass — a swollen knot holding energy like a dam. If the dam cracks before the seam fires, the energy goes everywhere.

The watcher is forty feet from Gate B.

The root vein connecting them runs through the glass floor. Exposed. Pressurized. A six-inch conduit extending twenty feet past the watcher on my side — outside beam range, inside Cyclone range.

I don't need to hit the watcher. I need to hit the fuse.

---

Through the tendril canopy. Ward eats the acid. Emberfrost into the glass floor where the root vein is thickest. Fire and frost split the pressurized conduit open. The disruption races back along the vein — twenty feet, thirty, forty — and hits the overcharged nexus from behind.

The dam receives a surge from its foundations. Wrong direction. Wrong flow.

The watcher unravels. Plates separating. Stored energy venting in a sphere that expands forty feet and hits Gate B broadside. The membrane — stretched thin by pulling from both sides — takes the shockwave and shatters. Not a fold. A detonation inward. The breach collapsing, sucking fragments into a point that compresses until the world hiccups.

Silence.

---

The entity. Both hands extended toward nothing. Three seams dark. Root network dead. No gates. No feed.

It turns toward me. The block head rotating upward. Reflecting nothing.

It stands there. And it does not attack.

I have two lance charges left. I could put the light through the block head. One shot. Clean.

I don't. It's already dead. It just hasn't fallen yet.

---

Three gates sealed. The dead circle beginning to remember what alive feels like where the golden aura touches it. Something twitching in the soil. Not growth. A memory of growth.

I fly west. Duality running. The entity standing alone in the dark behind me — a monument to whatever someone built here and whatever I just took apart.

Fifty minutes later the golden light fades. Creation mode expires. My boots hit the road and Stride floods back into my legs — the familiar current, the ground calculating distance beneath me. I eat from the satchel without stopping. The ember drinks it in minutes.

The crossroads. Campfire light ahead. Sera's bay mare tied to the stone marker. A grey gelding beside it. A figure standing watch at the road's edge — tall, broad-shouldered, staff across her back.

Tarin sees me first. The scarred hands don't move. The dark brown eyes track my approach and she calls over her shoulder without turning.

"He's back."
