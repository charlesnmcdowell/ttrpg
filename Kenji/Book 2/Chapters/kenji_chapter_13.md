# The Sundered Gate — Chapter Thirteen: Orders

**Day Eighteen — Dusk**

---

Senna closes three gates in the courtyard while Thessaly and I are still climbing out of the workshop. By the time we reach the crater floor she's standing in the middle of the triangle with her knuckles bloody and her braid half-down, looking at the three dead frames the way a carpenter looks at work she's satisfied with.

"Clean?" I ask.

"Clean."

We stack the paper Thessaly pulled onto a courtyard bench — journals, ledgers, the pinned note, a stack of invoices in five different merchant hands. Varn walks back in from his sweep of the north wing with a satchel. Finch from the west with a rolled parchment. Everything goes on the bench.

Then we climb. Sixty feet back up the terraces. The Wardbreakers on the crater rim — dust, sweat, nobody smiling like it's a party. The sun past its last quarter, Bleakmoor light gone flat and rust-colored.

"Compound clear?" Senna, even though she watched most of it.

"Looted for paper," Thessaly says. "Mordecai Corven. Ex-Academy. Twice-expelled. Sundered Gate twelve miles northeast — pre-Imperial seal, every relay in the net feeds it. He's not a storm. He's a contractor. And he's on a schedule."

"How far on."

"Two days old on the last memo. Call it four days of buffer before he detonates."

Finch: "Great. I hate landlords."

Varn, around a mouthful of something he's been eating for the last hour: "We leaving breadcrumbs?"

"No breadcrumbs. We split the intel and cover it three ways."

I lay it out.

"Elara gets the journals — she advised him forty years ago, she'll read them faster than anyone else and she's the only one who can tell me what she didn't catch the first time. Garrett gets the short version and the ledger pages — he's the one who can trace the merchant network through the invoices and shut it down at the money side. Thornwall and Crestfall get nothing yet; their garrisons will move the day they know and I don't want to show Corven the wall until we're ready to put him against it."

Senna's eyes flick to me. "You?"

"Tournament registration closes in Thornwall tomorrow. Coliseum. Platinum proving-ground circuit runs in parallel with my Vanguard contract. If I'm going to bleed in public to satisfy the Hall, I want a number in the ledger. And Corven reads the gazette."

"You want him to know you're in town."

"I want him to know I'm in town *and distracted.* Every hour he spends watching a tournament is an hour he's not finishing the lease."

Finch, appreciative: "Bait."

"Bait."

Senna nods once.

Orders settle.

*Thessaly — Varenholm. Journals to Elara tonight.
Senna — Varenholm, then the Silver Draft. Ledgers to Garrett.
Varn — Crestfall rotation, quiet hold at Thorne's gate. Don't engage.
Finch — Bleakmoor trail-work. Map every relay we missed. Don't close any of them yet.
Me — Thornwall. Paperwork and a queue.*

---

The Bleakmoor Ruins portal — the first one, the one we came through this morning — guttered out during the workshop fight. Corven's hand reaching across his own network and pinching what he could reach. I rebuild it at the crater lip instead. Fifteen feet, gold-green entropy thread in the frame, the seam clean enough that Thessaly's spectacles catch the reflection and she nods once without comment.

Portal six is back. The net stops screaming at the bracelet.

Thessaly through first. Journals in her satchel, staff over her shoulder, the Bleakmoor already forgetting her the moment she crosses.

Varn next, with a grunt that is all the goodbye he will ever give.

Finch stays — mock-salutes me from the rim, points west, and vanishes into scree.

Senna goes through last.

She stops at the threshold. Looks back at me.

Not warmth. Not threat. Just *confirmed* — a captain signing off on a day's work and a man she's now decided is worth the paperwork.

Then she's through. The gate holds. The net is quiet.

---

End of The Sundered Gate — Chapter Thirteen.
