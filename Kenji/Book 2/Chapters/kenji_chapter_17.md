# The Sundered Gate — Chapter Seventeen: The Iron Coliseum, Part II

**Day 21, Afternoon**

---

The half-hour break between rounds is shorter than it feels. Senna uses twenty minutes of it on a bench in the alcove with her eyes closed, not because she needs rest but because she is running the math on Varn and she likes to do her math with the lids down. Varn spends the same twenty minutes on the opposite bench with six glass vials laid out in a row on a folded cloth in front of him. He doesn't look at her. She doesn't look at him. Nobody interrupts either of them.

Sera is re-wrapping her forearm. Dren Valdric is across the alcove drinking water and watching me the way a man watches a problem he's looking forward to solving. The grin hasn't faded in an hour.

Solveth, quiet in the sword at my hip: *They have all watched you now. The second round is a different tournament.*

"I know."

*Do not be clever twice with the same trick.*

"I won't be."

*You will be.*

"I might be."

The bell calls us.

---

"ROUND TWO. BOUT ONE. SENNA, THE ASHEN FIST. VERSUS VARN."

The wall versus the healer. Eight thousand people and every one of them doing the same math I'm doing. Senna regenerates everything. Varn absorbs everything. Something has to give.

Varn walks in with six vials strapped to his belt. I can see the labels from the alcove — Nullroot. Regen suppression. The man brewed counter-chemistry for this fight and didn't tell anyone.

Round 1. Senna opens with Ki Intrusion. The fist punches through Varn's guard and the force ripples into his ribs. Deep damage. Varn doesn't flinch. Shield Bash. Senna hits the dirt. Gets up grinning.

Round 2. Varn uncorks a vial and throws it at her feet. The Nullroot mist hits her face. Her regen stutters — the ash light flickers, dims, goes dark. One round of suppression. He closes. Warhammer. She takes the hit without healing. It stays.

She takes another. That one stays too.

Round 3. The Nullroot fades. Ash light crawls back. Everything Varn did in that window — gone. Full health. She punches him in the jaw and the ki shockwave rattles the greatshield.

Varn pops another vial. Nullroot again. Senna's regen dies again. He walks in. Bash. Down. Hammer.

Round 4. Regen comes back. Full health. Varn is bleeding from a dozen places. The vials are working — but only for one round each, and she only needs one round to heal it all.

Round 5. Varn is at half. Senna at full. She doesn't rush it. She breaks him down the way she breaks everyone down — patient, precise, the math impossible. His fifth vial is empty. The sixth is cracked on the ground. He's out of tricks.

The warhammer swings one more time. She catches it. The green eyes look at him.

"Good fight."

Ki Intrusion. Full power. Varn's feet leave the ground. He hits the stone and doesn't get up.

Senna crouches next to him. The ash light wraps his ribs. Heals the deep damage. The bruises. The cracked jaw. Varn opens his eyes and sees the Ashen Fist kneeling over him with one hand on his chest.

"The Nullroot was smart."

Varn: "Not smart enough."

---

"BOUT TWO. KENJI, ARCHMAGE OF VARENHOLM. VERSUS DREN VALDRIC, LORD OF IRONHOLT."

Dren walks into the ring in full enchanted kit. The armour reads like a library to God Sight — ten layers of ward-work, damage absorption, elemental resistance, an adaptive counter-system that learns from every hit. Greatsword. No shield. The system IS his shield.

The grin hasn't left his face since Round 1.

"Nice trick with the constructs."

"Nice trick with the axe."

"It's a sword."

The bell rings.

Round 1. He's fast. Faster than Greave, faster than he looks. Three precision strikes — calculated, engineered, every angle chosen. The first bounces off my guard. The second finds a gap. The third — the system strike — hits something that shouldn't be hittable and my left arm goes numb for half a second.

God Sight flares. The enchantments light up like a schematic. I can see every layer. Every shortcut. One pass too few on the left pauldron. The ward-thread on his right gauntlet is fraying from the inside.

I throw Emberfrost. Giant's Throw multiplier. He *parries a thrown double-blade*. The impact drives him back three feet.

Round 2. His Adaptive Counter starts learning. My second throw — same angle — he reads it, shifts, and it barely nicks his shoulder. Third throw — adjusted arc — clean. The frost edge catches his left pauldron where the ward-work is thin. Ice crawls. The enchantment layer cracks.

Dren looks at his shoulder. Then at me.

"You read the weak spot."

"Two seconds ago."

"That armour took me ten years."

"It's good armour."

"You're going to make me take it off, aren't you."

I sit down. Cross-legged. In the dirt.

"Your move."

Round 3. He charges. Full speed, full commitment. The sword comes overhead — everything he has. I dodge. Haste-speed, five feet to the left. The sword bites dirt.

God Sight reads the enchantments again. The adaptive system is learning my movement — every dodge logged, predicted. In two more rounds he'll read me perfectly. The armour is the problem. The armour is always the problem.

I strip. Instructor's Guard off. Ring of Strength off. Chancellor's Veil off. Threadwalker Gloves off. Standing in the ring in my underclothes with a double-blade and a bracelet.

Eight thousand people go very, very quiet.

Dren stares. Then the grin becomes a laugh. A real one.

"You absolute madman."

No armour. No enchantments to read. No ward-work to adapt to. His system has nothing to learn because there's nothing to learn FROM. It's just a man with a blade.

Round 4. He can't read me. The adaptive system cycles blank — no data, no pattern, nothing but raw speed and a weapon he's never seen thrown like this. Emberfrost comes back from angles the system can't predict because there's no enchantment signature to track.

The frost builds on his right side. His shoulder. His arm. His grip changes. The sword slows.

Round 5. I throw for the face. He blocks — barely. The frost from the last four throws has built to critical mass. His left arm is frozen to the elbow. His best trick — the system that learns, the armour that adapts — is buried under three inches of supernatural frost.

Emberfrost comes back to my hand one last time. Dren looks at his frozen arm. Looks at me. Standing in the dirt in my underwear.

"I yield."

He drops the sword. Steps back. Raises one hand — the unfrozen one.

"Best fight I've had in ten years. You owe me a drink."

"I owe you a new pauldron."

---

"BOUT THREE. SERA. VERSUS VAEL."

Sera walks into the ring. Rapier at her hip. The Breach Shard stays in the alcove. No magic. She told me on the bench: "He counters everything I cast. So I won't cast anything."

Vael adjusts his spectacles. Force Shard manifests in his left hand.

Round 1. Sera closes. The Cage — her stance, her invention. Rapier angled. Vael fires a Force Shard. She doesn't dodge. She weaves through it. The Cage predicts angles and Vael's angles are academic. Predictable.

The rapier catches him across the forearm. First blood.

Vael: "You're not casting."

Sera: "No."

Force Shard. She sidesteps. Closes. Rapier finds the same forearm. The paralysis triggers — Vael's hand locks. One second. Enough. She slashes twice more before he breaks free.

Round 2. Vael tries Counterspell reflexively. There's nothing to counter. He wasted the reaction. Sera's rapier hits his ribs.

"Stop trying to counter what isn't there."

Force Shard. Sera takes it. Doesn't flinch. Closes. The rapier is a needle and Vael is a pincushion.

Round 3. Vael is bleeding from six places. His spectacles are crooked. The Force Shards are landing but they're not enough — she walks through them, absorbs the damage, and gives back worse.

Round 4. He goes defensive. Ward shell. Sera walks around it. Waits for it to drop. It does. The rapier finds his throat — flat of the blade, not the edge. A tap. A reminder.

"Yield."

Round 5. Vael doesn't yield. He fires three Shards in one burst — the most aggressive thing he's done all tournament. Two hit. Sera bleeds.

Then the Cage closes. The rapier moves like it's alive. Three strikes. The paralysis procs on the second. Vael freezes. The third strike opens his guard completely.

Sera stands over him with the rapier at his chest. Blood on both of them.

"Yield, Vael. You can't counter steel."

He adjusts his spectacles with a shaking hand. Looks at the rapier.

"...Yield."

The crowd erupts. The woman with no magic just beat the Counterspell specialist with a blade and patience.

---

Five rounds in my underwear. Dren bleeding, frosted, grinning like I did him a favor. The crowd doesn't know whether to cheer or file a complaint, so it does both.

Solveth, in the blade: *You read his armor like a menu.*

"Ten years of wards. Two seconds to bill him."

*He will go home and build a version of that armor with fewer signatures. You will fight him again in three years and it will be harder.*

"Something to look forward to."

I dress in the alcove. Sera is already bandaged. Varn is already on his feet, ribs wrapped, watching Dren with a fighter's quiet respect. Senna is eating. Finch is nowhere — he's on the Bleakmoor, where he's supposed to be, Whisperstone silent which means *still no news.*

The bracket resets for the semifinals. Me against Senna. Sera against Dren.

Gate sits at fifty-six percent, per Thessaly's last ring-pulse. Five days left on Corven's clock. Meanwhile, the Coliseum prepares for the semifinal by selling more ale.

---

End of The Sundered Gate — Chapter Seventeen.
