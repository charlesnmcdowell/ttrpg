Cookie is alone on the stage for ten more seconds after the last applause finishes its third wave, and the auditorium ushers — for the first time in their careers — let her have it instead of moving her along.

The cedar floorboards under her bare feet are warm. The Anklet's filigree has gone silver again. The gold in the air is thinning back into ordinary lamp-light. Two hundred and forty humanoids are turning to each other to ask the same question in slightly different shapes: *what just happened. What did we just see.*

She walks off the stage left wing without bowing twice — old performer's instinct again, the kind that knows the second bow can only ever be smaller than the first.

The hallway behind the dressing rooms is what victories look like in real life. Compressed, loud, lit too bright after the auditorium dim. Fern crashes into Cookie before Cookie's left foot has cleared the wing — a full-body halfling-and-human collision, eighteen years of best-friendship in one hug, both Millward stable boys politely studying the ceiling. Senna nods once from across the corridor in a way that means *we'll talk in the morning, get this sorted first.* Varn is impassive but his shoulders have dropped a half-inch, which on Varn is the equivalent of a broad smile. Finch is a polite three steps back from the mob, watching the door, working — but his bow is unstrung and the working voice is gone. He just looks tired and proud.

Torren is sobbing in a doorway in a way that is going to be embarrassing for him later and important to remember now. Mrs. Millward has appeared from somewhere and is patting his arm. Hazel is on the far side of the corridor with three women Cookie does not yet know by name — already the next layer of business is forming, contracts and bookings and a tour the noble-women circuit was apparently *waiting* to write. Vess is not here. She does not need to be; her invitation for tomorrow is the work of being here.

Silka is in the same place she was when Cookie left for the stage. Stagedoor wing. Three feet from where Cookie has to walk to get out of the building. Gold visibly above the collar now in the corridor light. Hands folded. Eyes wet but not crying. Waiting. She has not moved during the entire backstage scrum because she is not part of any of those orbits — she is part of *this* one, the orbit that has not yet been named between her and Cookie, and she is going to wait for as long as Cookie needs her to wait.

Ashworth catches Cookie's eye one last time from the conductor's desk where she's putting her ledger away. She doesn't say anything. She just dips her chin once toward Silka, in a gesture that isn't permission but might be advice, and goes back to her work.

Outside the Civic Auditorium the Varenholm night is full. The lamplighters have lit the Crown Quarter perimeter. Two streets over, somewhere up near the V.E.A. registry, someone is already singing a half-remembered fragment of *From Kettlebrook* in a tavern doorway, faltering on the bridge because the bridge needs Cookie's voice and no other.

The world is open in three different directions and none of them are wrong.
