# Cookie's Campaign — Chapter Four: *The Bat Dance*

*Starter Campaign · The Bard arc · Cookie*

---

## Chapter Four: The Bat Dance

### Windday, 4 Bright Turn 1247 AR — Varenholm & The Burnt Amphitheatre

---

### The Academy

---

Fifteen students in a half-circle. Stone floor. Morning light through high windows. Professor Ashworth standing at the center with her hands behind her back and the expression of a woman who has spent fourteen years watching teenagers fumble through cantrips.

"Today you perform. One at a time. Any discipline. I want to see what you think you know."

Cookie takes the floor barefoot. The Firebird outfit — deep crimson wrap, gold threading, open shoulders — draws exactly the reaction it's designed to draw. Three boys forget to breathe. Two girls roll their eyes. Silka, in the back row, watches without expression.

Dragon's Fire. The dance Tomas never saw because Cookie only practiced it on the rooftop of The Wren's Rest at midnight when her mother was asleep. Feet strike stone. The spin builds. The ember in Cookie's chest — the warm thing she doesn't have a name for yet — catches.

Performance: 22 versus DC 14.

The air shimmers gold. Not illusion — resonance. Sound bends around Cookie's movement like heat off summer stone. Nine of fifteen students lean forward in their seats without knowing they've moved. Heartstring, passive, doing what it always does. WIS DC 12. Nine failures. Six hold, including Silka, who grips the edge of her bench and doesn't blink.

Ashworth's pen stops moving.

"Again. Slower. Watch where the light starts — it's not in your hands, it's in your spine."

Cookie does it again. Slower. The gold traces along the scroll-marks on her shoulders and Ashworth writes something in her notes that she underlines twice.

Silka sings after. An elven lullaby, pitch-perfect, voice like cold water over river stones. The room goes still. Cookie thinks: *I want to be her friend.* The thought arrives fully formed and doesn't leave.

---

### Fieldwork

---

Three bounty sheets pinned to the board outside Ashworth's office. Combat quests — wolves, a bandit cell, a troll sighting near the northern road. One Academy assignment — survey and clear the Burnt Amphitheatre, south of Varenholm. Spiders reported. Students required.

Cookie picks the Academy job. Not because it's safer. Because it means a team. Because teams mean an audience.

Team Two: Silka, quiet and careful. Ivor, lanky and nervous, three cantrips and a lute. Fenella, stocky and freckled, shortbow and field medicine. The kind of girl who tracks deer for fun and considers "ambiance" a waste of daylight.

They walk south. Cookie talks. Ivor talks back. Fenella maps. Silka listens, and when she thinks nobody is watching, she watches Cookie the way a person watches a fire they're trying to understand.

---

### The Amphitheatre

---

Stone bowl in the hillside. Old. Pre-war. The seats are cracked and moss-covered. The stage is a flat disc of pale stone that still holds a faint acoustic signature — speak at the center and the back row hears you whisper.

Something lives underneath. Cookie can feel it the way she feels a wrong note — a tension in the air that shouldn't be there.

"I have an idea. Don't laugh."

Echolocation. Tap-dance on the stone stage. Let the sound map what's below. Performance check with the dance inspiration die.

Performance: 38. Natural high roll on the d12. The taps cascade across the stone and come back carrying geometry — vaulted underchambers, three large signatures moving in patterns that aren't random. Phase-shifting between the material and ethereal plane. Spiders. Three of them. Big ones.

Ivor throws Minor Illusion and the sound-map renders visible — blue wireframe architecture hanging in the air above the stage like a blueprint made of music. Fenella sketches it. Silka stares at Cookie with her mouth slightly open.

"Three phase spiders. Underchamber access through the east stairwell. They're cycling — material for six seconds, ethereal for six seconds."

Fenella: "We should get the garrison."

Cookie: "We should get paid."

---

### The Fight

---

Protector's Surge on Fenella before they descend. Not the standard version — the Ember-enhanced version. The warm thing in Cookie's chest flares and Fenella's eyes go wide as +40 floods every ability score she has. Her shortbow becomes a siege weapon held by a demigod in a freckled seventeen-year-old's body.

"What — what did you just—"

"You're welcome. Don't miss."

Round one. Cookie begins the Healing Dance. The performance roll lands. Feet on stone in the torchlit underchamber, spinning, the gold light building. Allies gain free attacks. Fenella draws.

Phase Spider One materializes. Fenella's arrow hits like a battering ram. Enhanced DEX, enhanced STR, enhanced everything. The spider doesn't just die — it detonates. Chitin fragments spray across the chamber wall. FATALITY. Ivor ducks. Silka doesn't flinch.

"Silka, Ivor, take the survivors and flee."

They don't argue. Ivor grabs Silka's arm. They run. Good.

Round two. The dance continues. Two spiders phase in. Fenella pivots, draws, fires. Phase Spider Two catches the arrow through the cephalothorax at thirty feet. The impact pins it to the wall. FATALITY. The sound it makes dying echoes through the underchamber and comes back harmonized by Cookie's dance.

Round three. The last spider. It phases in already lunging. Fenella meets it with an arrow drawn to her ear, enhanced reflexes calculating the intercept before her conscious mind catches up. The shot goes through the spider's open mandibles and exits through the dorsal carapace. Phase Spider Three drops. FATALITY.

Three rounds. Three spiders. Three kills. All Fenella. All Cookie's magic making it possible.

The Healing Dance completes its third round. The golden light erupts — 100% max HP heal for both of them, 10% regen per round for the next hour. Every scratch, every bruise, every old ache from the walk south: gone.

Fenella lowers the bow. Stares at her own hands.

"That wasn't me. That was you."

"That was both of us."

---

### The Market

---

Spider parts. Venom sacs, chitin plates, phase-silk glands. The kind of material alchemists and armorers pay real money for.

Cookie haggles. Performance and Persuasion working in tandem — the smile, the lean, the way she holds up a venom sac like it's a bottle of expensive wine and waits for the buyer to talk himself into paying more.

Fifty gold for the lot. The buyer thinks he got a deal. He didn't.

Quest turn-in at the Academy: forty-nine gold bonus. The Burnt Amphitheatre is clear. Ashworth's assessment arrives by evening courier — *exceptional teamwork, unconventional approach, recommend continued fieldwork assignments.*

Ninety-nine gold total. More money than Cookie has ever held at once.

---

### The Enchanter

---

A shop on the second terrace. Clean. Quiet. The kind of quiet that means wards on the walls and money on the shelves.

Two commissions.

The Healer's Ring. Simple silver band, enchantment woven into the metal during setting. When Cookie heals someone, their maximum HP flows back to her as temporary hit points until combat ends. The cost: whatever was hurting the person she healed now wants to hurt her instead. Aggro redirect, one turn. The price a healer pays for being good at it.

The Anklet of Unarmed Combat. Fine chain, mithril links, a single rune-cut bead that sits against the ankle bone and hums when she moves. +4 DEX. Kicks count as weapons now — 6d8 plus the new modifier. The tai chi she invented in a bandit camp on Day 2 just became a real martial art.

Forty gold for both. Haggle success. The enchanter thinks he overcharged. He didn't.

Cookie walks out with DEX 7, AC 22, and a kick that hits like a warhorse. The anklet hums against her skin. The ring sits warm on her finger.

Sixty-two gold remaining. Two attunement slots filled. One left.

---

### Evening

---

The inn. Fern is there, already eating, talking about Torren's shop and a customer who tried to return a cream that "didn't make her look younger" after one application.

"Cookie. Cookie. Your eyes are doing the thing."

"What thing."

"The golden glowy thing they do when you're excited. Sit down. Tell me everything."

Cookie tells her about the spiders. About the dance that mapped the underchamber. About Fenella shooting three monsters into walls. About the enchanter and the ring and the anklet. She leaves out the part where Silka sang a lullaby that made the room forget to breathe. She doesn't know why she leaves it out.

Fern notices. Fern always notices.

"Who's Silka."

"Nobody. A girl from class. She sings."

"Uh-huh."

---

### Long Rest

---

The bed at Millward Chandlery. Clean sheets. Fern snoring across the room with one arm hanging off the mattress. The city sounds of Varenholm settling into night — carts, distant music, a dog barking at nothing.

Cookie runs through tomorrow. The earrings from Isolde in the morning. The Starling show in the evening. The performance that either starts her career or proves Kettlebrook was right to call her a dreamer.

The warm thing in her chest settles. Not gone — resting. The scroll-marks on her shoulders fade to skin-tone in the dark.

Spell slots full. HP full. Mind quiet for once.

Day 5 is the one that matters.
