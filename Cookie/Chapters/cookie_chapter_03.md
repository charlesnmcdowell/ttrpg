# Cookie's Campaign — Chapter Three: *Golden Returns*

*Starter Campaign · The Bard arc · Cookie*

---

## Chapter Three: Golden Returns

### Day 3 — Varenholm & Surrounding Farmland

---

### The Quest Board

---

Morning. The Adventurer's Guild. Cookie's party — Dorith, Dalla, Silas, Marta, Garron — is waiting by the bounty board, looking like professionals for the first time. Yesterday they raided a bandit camp. Today they're a team.

The posting: Missing Survey Stones. Three boundary markers removed from the northeast perimeter. Alderman Edric Tosse wants them found and returned. Standard civic work — the kind of job that pays modest and builds reputation.

Cookie takes it. The party moves out.

---

### The Trail

---

Marta earns her keep inside the first hour. The ranger crouches at the first empty marker site, reads the ground the way Cookie reads a room, and finds what nobody else would see — cart ruts. Fresh. Heavy enough to mean stone. Leading northeast toward the farmland.

They follow the tracks through field roads and hedgerows. The ruts lead to a turnip farm. Small. Modest. The kind of place where a man works twelve-hour days and still can't make the tax payment.

Castor's farm.

---

### Castor

---

The party arrives and Castor is in his field. He sees six armed adventurers walking toward his property and the blood leaves his face. He knows why they're here. The three survey stones are in his barn — he didn't even try to hide them well.

He's afraid. Shaking. The kind of fear that comes from a man who did something stupid and knows exactly how stupid it was.

Cookie tells the party to wait. She walks up to Castor alone.

They talk. Just the two of them, standing between turnip rows. He tells her why he took the stones. The reason isn't greed — it's desperation. The kind of desperation that makes a farmer steal government property because the alternative is worse. Boundary markers shifted mean boundary lines shifted mean his field gets six more feet of planting row and maybe — *maybe* — he makes enough to survive the winter.

Cookie listens. Doesn't judge. Doesn't threaten.

"I'll advocate for you."

---

### The Alderman

---

Alderman Edric Tosse. Lawful Neutral. The kind of official who follows procedure because procedure is the only thing standing between civilization and chaos. His office is clean. His desk is organized. He has the face of a man who has heard every excuse and believes none of them.

The party returns the three stones. Quest complete.

Edric looks at Cookie. Pretty Privilege fires — the passive perk that turns modest payments into generous ones when the employer is male. The posted reward was 6 silver pieces. Edric reaches for his coin purse and counts out six gold.

Ten times the posted rate. He doesn't even blink. He just hands it over like that's what it always cost.

"Now." Cookie leans forward. "About Castor. He's a farmer, not a criminal. He took the stones because he's struggling. If you could show some leniency—"

Persuasion check. Cookie gives it everything — the eyes, the voice, the lean. Heartstring pulling at Edric's resolve.

Failed.

Edric shakes his head. Not cruel — firm. The law is the law and survey stones are government property. Castor gets fined two gold pieces and thirty days of supervised labor. The sentence is lenient by the book and harsh by the farm. Castor will survive it, but the winter just got harder.

Cookie tried. The system didn't bend. She takes the six gold and walks out knowing that compassion doesn't always win and the world isn't always fair and sometimes being charming isn't enough.

It's the first time Pretty Privilege felt like it wasn't a victory.

---

### The Pale Lantern

---

Evening. The Pale Lantern. Isolde's shop.

The shop is quiet the way expensive places are quiet — not empty, just controlled. Wards on the walls. Enchanted items behind glass. The light comes from sources that don't flicker because flickering is for candles and this is not a candle shop.

Isolde. Female. Enchantress. Apprenticed under Maren Holt of the Gilt Lens. She looks at Cookie the way a jeweler looks at an uncut stone — professional interest, not attraction. Heartstring hits her and she registers it the way a sommelier registers a wine — *interesting vintage, not buying*.

She's immune to Pretty Privilege. She's aware of the aura and unswayed by it. Cookie is, for the first time in Varenholm, dealing with someone who sees through the performance.

Cookie likes her immediately.

"I want earrings. Custom. Enchanted."

Isolde raises an eyebrow. "Custom enchanted earrings start at eighty-five gold."

Cookie has six gold and change.

"What if I wore them at my show? The Starling. Fifty upper-class patrons, maybe more. Torren's handling invitations. Every wealthy woman in that audience sees your earrings on the performer and asks where she got them."

Isolde considers. This is not charm. This is not Heartstring. This is business — a halfling dancer offering advertising reach that Isolde can't buy for eighty-five gold.

"+2 Charisma. Advantage on Persuasion and Performance. Custom design — your aesthetic, your brand. Four gold and you wear them at the Starling. Visibly. Every time someone asks, you tell them The Pale Lantern."

"Deal."

Cookie pays four gold. The earrings will be ready Day 5, morning. Just in time for the show.

Isolde watches Cookie leave. Makes a note in her ledger. The note says more than the transaction.

---

### The Room

---

Night. Millward Chandlery. Fern is reorganizing the beauty supplies she brought home from Torren's shop, sorting pigments by color and talking about a customer who wanted "the Cookie look" after seeing Fern's makeover.

Cookie sits on the bed and counts what's left. Six gold from Edric, minus four to Isolde, plus whatever she had before. The math is tighter than yesterday. The math is always tight.

But the earrings are coming. The show is coming. Torren is sending invitations to fifty people who have money and opinions and the power to make a barefoot halfling from Kettlebrook into something the city remembers.

She thinks about Castor. About the turnip field and the fear in his eyes and the thirty days of labor that start tomorrow. About how six gold appeared in her hand because a man looked at her face and his brain said *give her more* and she couldn't use any of that to help a farmer who stole three rocks to feed himself.

Pretty Privilege. The name she'll never say out loud because saying it means knowing what it is and knowing what it is means knowing it isn't earned.

She pulls the blanket up. Tomorrow is the Academy. Tomorrow is the beginning of everything that matters.

Six thousand experience points. Level 4 to Level 5. Three days in Varenholm and Cookie is already someone the city is starting to notice.
