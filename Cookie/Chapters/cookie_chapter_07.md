## Chapter Seven: Daddy's Girl

### Rootday, 7 Bright Turn 1247 AR — Varenholm

---

### Afternoon — Halbert's Funeral (14:00)

---

The canal district smells like tallow and old water. Cookie walks through it in the grey mourning dress — long sleeves, high neck, bare feet on wet cobblestones. No earrings today. No color. No Firebird look. The golden scroll-marks on her shoulders are hidden under fabric for the first time since she arrived in Varenholm.

She looks like a mourner. That's the point.

The procession gathers at the canal district chapel — a low stone building with a green copper roof. Maret stands at the front in black, one child on each hand. The boy's jaw is tight. Lena is holding a river-green ribbon.

There are maybe forty people. Dockworkers. Neighbors. A foreman with his hat in his hands. No officials. No guild banners. Just people who knew a man who worked the cranes and sang badly while he cooked.

Ashworth stands at the back. She's not here as faculty. She's here to evaluate. Cookie ignores her.

The procession walks from the chapel to the burial grounds — a quarter mile along the canal. Cookie walks behind the family, barefoot on the stone path that runs along the water.

She doesn't dance. She sings.

---

### Performance Check — DC 14 (lowered from 18 by prep visit to Maret)

**Roll: 14 + 9 = 23 vs DC 14. Success by 9.**

---

No Performance check with dancing. No d12. No Firebird flash. Just voice.

Cookie sings a river song — one of the dock ballads Maret said Halbert loved. She changes the words. Instead of the sailor going out to sea, the sailor comes home. Instead of the storm, there's a kitchen. Instead of the maiden on the shore, there are two kids at the table and a man who checks on them before he takes his coat off.

Heartstring projects at Enhancement — not grief, not sorrow, but the warm ache of remembering someone exactly as they were. The dockworkers slow down. Some of them are mouthing the melody — they know the original. They hear what Cookie changed and they understand why.

Maret doesn't cry. She holds Lena's hand tighter and listens to a song about a man who came home every night, and the kids hear their father in it, and that's enough.

The procession reaches the burial grounds. Cookie finishes the last verse as they lower the casket. The canal catches the late afternoon light — river green, the way Halbert liked it.

Ashworth writes nothing in her ledger. She just nods, once, and leaves.

### Walk to the Adventurer's Guild (15:30)

---

Cookie changes back into color on the walk over. Today's outfit was the mourning dress all day — she didn't randomize because the funeral was the day's only real appointment. But after the burial grounds she ducks behind a market stall and swaps to her First Flame crimson wrap. The grey goes into the satchel.

She's a performer. Grief was the gig. Now the gig is done.

The Guild is a twenty-minute walk from the canal district — up through the market quarter, past the Academy's east gate, and into the Vanguard district where the serious money and the serious violence keep offices next door to each other.

---

### Guild Registration Update (16:00)

---

Gruff is at the counter. He looks up. Looks down at his ledger. Looks up again.

"Level 8," he says. Not a question.

Cookie updates her registration. Class: Dancer. Roles: support, healer, buffer, tank. Level 8. She lists herself available for party hire.

"Rate?"

Cookie thinks about it. Two gold seemed right when she was Level 5 and nobody knew her name. She performed at the Starling four days ago. She's a local sensation. Two gold is what a competent cleric charges for dungeon work.

"Five gold per quest."

Gruff writes it down. He does not comment. Gruff never comments.

---

### The Wardbreakers

---

Cookie is still at the counter when the guild's front door opens and a different kind of gravity walks in.

Four people. The kind of four people who make the room change temperature.

A woman leads — lean, auburn braid over one shoulder, green eyes that scan the room the way a fighter scans a room, which is to say: exits first, threats second, furniture third. She looks mid-twenties. She moves like someone who's been doing this for decades anyway. There's no grey in her hair, no scars on her arms, no evidence that time has touched her at all. The uncanny youth on a woman carrying that much combat experience is deeply unsettling to anyone who knows what they're looking at.

Senna Dawnmere. Diamond-tier Vanguard Hall. Former Wardbreakers squad leader. Azarinth Healer — the regeneration halts cellular decay entirely. She's chronologically in her late forties. She looks like she could be Cookie's classmate.

Behind her: Finch, a halfling rogue barely taller than Cookie, sandy-brown hair, patched leather vest bristling with throwing knives. He's grinning at something only he finds funny. Varn — six and a half feet of half-orc fighter, shaved head, greatshield strapped to his back, the kind of quiet that comes from being the biggest thing in every room for thirty years. And Thessaly — pale, severe, black hair in a tight knot, crystal focus mounted on a silver chain at her wrist. She's already looking at Cookie.

The crystal is glowing.

They don't browse the bounty board. Diamond-tier teams don't walk into branch offices to pick up fetch quests. They're seated at a corner table reviewing reports before Cookie finishes her paperwork.

Then Cookie turns from the counter.

Heartstring pulses. Passive. Always active. Thirty-foot radius. WIS save DC 12.

Senna's head comes up like she's been slapped. Her eyes find Cookie — the gold-flecked irises, the scroll-marks on her shoulders where the crimson wrap leaves skin bare, the warmth in the room that wasn't there before she walked in. Twenty-five years of sparring with the Dragon Emperor every morning he was around. Twenty-five years of feeling this exact warmth — deeper, stronger, male, but the same fundamental frequency.

Senna knows what an Ember feels like. She spent half her adult life standing next to the strongest one alive.

She leans over to Gruff, who's passing their table with a ledger. "Who's the halfling?"

---

### Cookie Ignores Senna

---

Cookie doesn't register the Wardbreakers as anything other than background noise. She's Level 8. They're sitting at a table. She has places to be.

She walks past Senna without a second look. Heartstring hits the whole table as she passes — Finch's grin softens into something confused, Varn's shoulders shift toward her like a compass needle finding north (Combat Magnetism, male, close range), and Thessaly's crystal focus flares bright enough to cast shadows.

Thessaly's hand closes around the focus. Her eyes narrow. She's academically aware of what she's sensing — arcane Ember signature, resonance subtype, absurdly strong for a Level 8 support class. The math doesn't add up.

Cookie sits down next to Finch. He's the only other halfling in the room and he's got a nice smile.

---

### Cookie Talks to Finch

---

"Hey, handsome."

Finch blinks. He looks at Senna. Senna's expression says *don't look at me, handle your own halfling problems.*

Finch looks back at Cookie. Pretty Privilege is doing its thing — the 10x male humanoid response is mostly economic, but at close range, with Heartstring radiating happiness and flirtation and the Ember scroll-marks catching lamplight on her shoulders, the social version isn't nothing either.

"Uh," Finch says. This is a man who has talked his way out of dragon hoards. "Hey yourself."

Senna is watching Cookie the way a hawk watches a field mouse, except the field mouse is glowing gold and smells like the Dragon Emperor and is currently flirting with her rogue.

Varn has not moved. His knuckles are white on the table's edge. Combat Magnetism at this range is a full-body pull — the instinct isn't violence, it's capture. Protect. Keep. He's aware of it. He's fought beside Kenji for years. He knows what Ember proximity does to male humanoids. He's riding it out like a professional.

Thessaly is writing something in the margin of her report. The crystal hasn't stopped glowing.

---

### The Line

---

Senna can't take it anymore. She leans forward. Her voice is controlled, measured, the voice of someone with INT 18 and the patience of a woman who taught at a war college for a decade.

"Little one. I don't know who you are, but that warmth you're carrying — I've felt it before. A long time ago. You should know that what you have isn't just—"

Cookie looks at Senna for the first time. Really looks at her. The ageless face. The fighter's eyes. The auburn braid. The Diamond-tier Vanguard Hall insignia on her vest. A woman who's been around long enough to recognize Ember and isn't flinching.

Cookie tilts her head.

"Hmmmm. You one of the ladies that bang my dad?"

---

The table goes silent.

Finch's mouth opens. Closes. Opens again.

Varn's grip on the table edge tightens so hard the wood cracks.

Thessaly's quill stops moving.

Senna Dawnmere — who fought in the Iron Crown War, who earned Diamond tier alongside the Dragon Emperor, who has an INT of 18 and a WIS so low she just got hit by a DC 12 Heartstring aura from across the room — stares at Cookie with an expression that contains approximately fourteen emotions, none of which she was prepared for.

---

## Chapter Seven — End

---

*Cookie drops the biggest cliffhanger of her campaign and walks away from the detonation like she doesn't know what she just said. She probably doesn't. WIS 7.*

*Senna Dawnmere just met a halfling Ankuspawn who claims the Dragon Emperor as her father — casually, rudely, with zero understanding of the geopolitical implications. The woman who sparred with Kenji every morning for twenty-five years is now sitting in a branch-office guild chair processing the fact that the most powerful man on the continent apparently has a barefoot dancer daughter who flirts with rogues and charges five gold per quest.*

*The conversation has not been answered. Chapter 8 opens on a loaded silence.*
