## Chapter Six: Gold in the Morning

### Stillday, 6 Bright Turn 1247 AR — Varenholm

---

### Morning — Outfit of the Day

---

Cookie wakes up a local sensation. The Starling show did what the Starling show was always going to do — the whole district knows her name now.

Outfit: saffron yellow-gold Firebird halter wrap, cropped, bare midriff. Matching fingerless gloves running to mid-forearm. Thigh-high amber leather boots with gold buckles. Thin black choker. Isolde's earrings. Gold ribbon in twin tails — left high, right low, asymmetric. The scroll-marks on her shoulders glow faintly in the morning light.

Fern is already gone to work. She left a note on the table: a man from the Alderman's office is downstairs. He wants to book Cookie for a private event. He's been waiting two hours.

Cookie eats honeyed oats with dried apricots and a cup of warm cider. Then she goes downstairs, looks at the Alderman's man, and tells him to route all bookings through Torren at the Gilt Lily.

---

### Morning — Bridge Payout

---

Cookie swings by the Adventurer's Guild. The bridge quest payout is ready: 8 CP base.

**Pretty Privilege: 10x gold from male humanoid employers.**

Gruff processes the payout. 8 CP becomes 8 SP. He does not comment on this. He never comments on this.

---

### The Walk

---

The walk to the Academy is different today.

A flower stall woman recognizes her from the show. Academy students stop and stare — one of them walks directly into a lamppost. An older merchant tips his hat as she passes.

This isn't being noticed for beauty. Cookie has been noticed for beauty since she was fourteen. This is fame. People know who she is because of what she did, not what she looks like. It's a different kind of warmth.

---

### Academy — Ashworth's Class (08:00)

---

Cookie arrives two minutes late. She was stopped three times in the hallway. She signed a pamphlet for a student.

The classroom holds fifteen. Ivor and Fenella are in their usual seats. Silka is in the back row — silver chain, muted green tunic, watching Cookie walk in. Then looking away.

Ashworth opens with silence. She notes that Cookie was at the Starling last night. She notes that Cookie is no longer Level 5. Then she calls Cookie first for the exercise: perform a piece demonstrating emotional range.

---

### Performance Check — DC 16 (advantage from earrings, +d12 Dance Inspiration)

**Roll: 18 (adv) + 9 + 5 (d12) = 32 vs DC 16. Success by 16.**

---

Cookie performs a Kettlebrook lullaby. "The River Carries You Home" — her mother Daisy's song. The one Daisy hummed while washing dishes, while braiding Cookie's hair, while standing at the kitchen window watching the river pass.

The dance is slow. Barefoot. Arms extended, palms up, eyes closed. Cookie doesn't perform the song — she remembers it. Heartstring projects deep, genuine homesickness at tenfold Enhancement, and the room fills with the ache of a place you can't go back to.

Ivor cries. Of course Ivor cries.

Fenella turns away. Her jaw is tight.

Silka grips the edge of her bench. The silver chain at her throat warms. Her chest tightens — a feeling of home with no home attached, resonant and familiar and wrong in a way she can't name. A faint pulse of Ember sibling resonance moves between them, once, like a heartbeat. Neither of them recognizes it.

Ashworth watches the entire performance with white knuckles and a controlled expression. When Cookie finishes, Ashworth writes three lines in her ledger. Underlines them twice. There is recognition in her eyes — not of fame. Something older.

"The children of fire," she murmurs. Then says nothing else about it.

> **Performance DC 16: 1,500 XP + 5,000 domain bonus (25% of 20,000 L7→L8 gap) = 6,500 XP**
> **Running: 73,298 XP (Level 7)**

---

### Post-Class — Graduation Requirements

---

Cookie stays after class. She has questions.

"What are the requirements to graduate?"

Ashworth explains the structure:

- **Three-term program**, 40 days each.
- **Four assessment areas**: technical proficiency, emotional range, audience engagement, original composition.
- **Three methods**: class exercises, fieldwork, and a term-end recital judged by a panel — Ashworth, a guest instructor, and a working professional.
- **Term 1: INTERPRETATION** — perform existing work at professional level.
- **Term 2: ADAPTATION** — constraints. Unfamiliar style, audience, or venue.
- **Term 3: CREATION** — original composition.

Recitals are scheduled individually by Ashworth when the student is ready. The Dean can override in exceptional circumstances — this has happened twice in thirty years.

Cookie asks Ashworth about risk-taking. Ashworth reveals she left a tenured position at Ashenmere Conservatory thirty years ago to build Varenholm's program from nothing.

Cookie files that away.

---

### The Pitch — Accelerate Graduation

---

Cookie frames a deal.

Term 1 is already complete by Ashworth's own criteria. Class attended. Fieldwork done. The Starling performance serves as the recital — two hundred people, a standing ovation, and a panel that included the venue's own management and three patron inquiries afterward. Cookie wants Term 2 fieldwork assigned today and the Term 2 recital before next class.

She offers Ashworth credit for a revolutionary new accelerated program. Then adds the stick: if refused, Cookie will trash the curriculum publicly once she's famous. And she will be famous.

---

### Persuasion Check — DC 20 (advantage from earrings)

**Roll: 19 (adv, both dice 19) + 9 = 28 vs DC 20. Success by 8.**

---

Ashworth concedes acceleration in principle but counter-offers: the Starling doesn't count as a formal recital. She wants a Term 1 recital in the Academy's recital hall tomorrow with a proper panel. Term 2 fieldwork assigned today. Term 2 recital as early as Day 10.

> **Persuasion DC 20: 2,500 XP**
> **Running: 75,798 XP — LEVEL UP → Level 8! New HP: 50/50.**

---

### Cookie Counters — Starling Must Count as Term 1

---

Cookie pushes back. The Starling performance was professional-level interpretation in front of a paying audience. A recital hall is a step down, not a step up.

---

### Persuasion Check — DC 18 (advantage from earrings)

**Roll: 8 (adv from 8/2) + 9 = 17 vs DC 18. FAIL by 1.**

---

Ashworth holds firm. "A soldier who wins a bar fight hasn't passed a combat evaluation." Performance is not examination. She maintains her counter-offer.

> **Persuasion DC 18 (FAIL): 2,500 XP**
> **Running: 78,298 XP (Level 8)**

---

### The Ultimatum

---

Cookie walks to the door. Turns around. Hand on the frame.

"Take the deal or no deal at all. You'll get credit for being revolutionary or for standing in my way."

---

### Persuasion Check — DC 22 (advantage from earrings)

**Roll: NAT 20 (adv from 20/8) + 9 = 29 vs DC 22. Success by 7. Natural 20 = double XP.**

---

Ashworth is silent for a long time.

She didn't leave the Conservatory because she played it safe. Cookie's interpretation skills aren't Term 1 level — they're professional circuit quality. The recital hall formality was about control, not measurement. Ashworth knows this. She knew it when she wrote those three underlined lines in her ledger.

"The Starling counts as Term One."

> **Persuasion DC 22 (NAT 20): 5,000 x 2 = 10,000 XP**
> **Running: 88,298 XP (Level 8)**

---

### Term 2 Fieldwork Assignment

---

Ashworth assigns the fieldwork immediately.

Tomorrow — Day 7, Forgeday, 14:00. A funeral.

A dockworker named Halbert fell from a cargo crane six days ago. His widow, Maret, requested music for the procession from the canal district chapel to the burial grounds. No one at the Academy has taken the volunteer request.

Ashworth's rules: serve the grief. Don't redirect it. Don't amplify it for effect. Don't make it about Cookie.

Serve it.

Prep: visit the widow today. Brewer's Row, east side of the canal, above the chandler's shop.

---

### Visit to Maret

---

Cookie walks to Brewer's Row. Fifteen minutes through the canal district. The buildings are older here — brick and timber, laundry lines between windows, the smell of tallow from the chandler's shop below.

There's a mourning wreath on the door.

Maret opens it. Mid-thirties, tired, red-rimmed eyes, apron still on. Behind her, a boy around eight and a girl around five or six — Lena. The apartment is small. Two rooms and a kitchen. Clean but worn.

There's a coat on the fourth chair. Halbert's coat. Maret moves it to a hook on the wall before sitting down.

Cookie asks about Halbert.

He was thirty-eight. Crane operator since he was sixteen. Quiet at work — the kind of man who did his job and didn't talk about it. At home he talked to his kids for hours. Made up bedtime stories. He was bad at it. The kids didn't care.

His favorite color was river green — the color the canal gets in late afternoon when the light hits it right.

He wanted his kids to have more than he did. Worked doubles so Maret could stay home. Never complained. When he came home at midnight he checked on both kids before he took his coat off.

He sang while he cooked. Badly. River songs, dock ballads. "The Millhand's Daughter" with the words wrong on purpose because it made the kids laugh.

Maret's hands are steady when she talks about the fall. They shake when she talks about the singing.

"I want them to remember him happy," she says. "Not the fall. Can you make it feel like that?"

Cookie nods. Hugs her. Leaves.

### Shopping

---

Cookie stops at a canal district tailor on the way back. Buys a basic mourning dress — dark grey, long sleeves, high neck, simple. Nothing flashy. Nothing that says "look at me."

Cost: 5 CP.
