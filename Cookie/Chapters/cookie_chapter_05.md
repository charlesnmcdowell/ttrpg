# Cookie's Campaign — Chapter Five: *The Four Seasons of Love*

*Starter Campaign · The Bard arc · Cookie*

---

## Chapter Five: The Four Seasons of Love

### Fireday, 5 Bright Turn 1247 AR — Varenholm

---

### Morning — Outfit of the Day

---

Cookie comes downstairs in a cobalt halter wrap over a sheer black mesh undershirt, gold chains looped at the hip, bare midriff showing the faint scroll-marks on her torso. Twin tails freshly tied with thin gold ribbon. Barefoot. Isolde's enchanted earrings catching the morning light from the inn window — the gold-and-sapphire drops that cost her 4 GP in negotiation and 0 GP in actual money thanks to the advertising deal.

Fern looks up from her porridge. "You look like you're going to war."

"I'm going to breakfast."

She grabs a bowl of honeyed oats with dried berries and a cup of warm cider from the inn's kitchen. Quick, functional. She has a bridge troll to deal with before tonight's show.

---

### Morning — The Adventurer's Guild

---

Cookie walks into the Adventurer's Guild and finds Gruff behind his desk, sorting paperwork with the energy of a man who's been doing exactly this since before she was born.

"I need to update my file."

Gruff doesn't look up. "Name."

"Cookie. I was in here two days ago. I recruited thirty people in your lobby."

He looks up. Reaches for her registration card. Cookie rattles off her current stats, abilities, and gear — the full inventory. Updated completed quests: the survey stones job (farmer responsible for theft, turned in, already paid) and the Academy fieldwork assessment (three phase spiders killed, support role both times).

Gruff writes it all down. Stamps the card. Updates her designation: **Dancer — Support (tank/healer/buffer)**. Custom class.

"Anything else?"

"Any tough jobs? I need to be back by afternoon."

---

### The Bridge Troll

---

The quest is a shakedown job. An ogre has claimed the stone bridge on the northern trade road — three miles outside Varenholm — and is charging a toll. Merchants can't get through. The guild wants it resolved, preferably without casualties. The posting says "combat recommended, party of 4."

Cookie's regular adventure party (Dorith, Dalla, Silas, Marta, Garron) is on a different job. She pulls from the available board: **Davan** (greatsword fighter, human, built like a barn door), **Aveline** (archer/scout, human, quick and quiet), and **Jessamine** (druid, human, talks to plants more than people). Three strangers and a halfling in a halter top. Classic adventuring party.

They walk north. Cookie decides to let Jessamine do the talking.

"What are we supposed to be doing again?" Cookie asks, walking between Davan and Aveline like she's on a stroll.

Jessamine explains: the ogre isn't violent, just greedy. He showed up a week ago and started charging 5 CP per crossing. The merchants can afford it — barely — but the principle of the thing is the problem. The guild wants him moved. Peacefully if possible.

They reach the bridge. The ogre is exactly what you'd expect — nine feet tall, grey-skinned, sitting on a boulder next to the road with a crude toll sign propped against a tree. He's eating a raw fish.

Cookie has an idea.

---

### Performance Check — DC 18 (with advantage from earrings, +d12 Dance Inspiration)

**Roll: NAT 20 (advantage) + 9 + 11 (d12) = 40 vs DC 18. Success by 22.**

---

Cookie tells Aveline to run back and grab a stack of advertisement pamphlets for tonight's Starling show. Tells the rest of the party to step back. Then she walks up to the ogre alone.

She performs one of Ignis's songs — *A Dragon's Love of Shiny Things* — emitting the emotion of greed and excitement through Heartstring. The dance is all gyration and belly rolls, flashing her jewelry, the enchanted earrings throwing sparks of light. She lets the Ember Enhancement amplify the emotional bleed tenfold. The scroll-marks on her shoulders glow gold.

The ogre stops eating his fish.

His pupils dilate. His jaw goes slack. He watches Cookie dance with the focused intensity of a creature who has never in his entire life seen anything he wanted more than what is happening right now. The earrings. The gold chains. The movement. The *feeling* pouring off this tiny woman in waves of raw, supernatural want.

Heartstring passive: WIS DC 12. The ogre's WIS modifier is not good. He fails by a lot.

He stands up. Nine feet of grey muscle and bad decisions, and he is *smitten*.

---

### Persuasion Check — DC 14 (with advantage from earrings)

**Roll: success.**

---

Cookie asks the ogre — carefully, sweetly, with her hips still moving — to let people cross the bridge. And also to hand out pamphlets for her show tonight. To everyone who crosses.

The ogre agrees to both things immediately because Cookie asked him to and Cookie is the most important thing that has ever happened to him.

Aveline returns with the pamphlets. The ogre takes the stack with both hands, delicately, like they're made of glass. He doesn't understand what pamphlets are but he understands that the pretty lady wants him to give them to the small people who cross his bridge and he will do this until he dies.

Jessamine stares at Cookie from thirty feet away with an expression that is difficult to classify.

"Did you just... solve the quest... by making the troll fall in love with you?"

"He's an ogre," Cookie corrects. "And yes."

### Afternoon — Quest Turn-In

---

Back at the guild. Gruff processes the paperwork. The bridge is open, the ogre is employed as the world's worst pamphlet distributor, and nobody died.

Quest payout: shared among the party. Gruff handles distribution. Cookie's cut, filtered through a male employer...

**Pretty Privilege: 10× gold from male humanoid employers.**

Cookie smiles. Gruff does not smile back but he does pay her what the ledger says she's owed, which is significantly more than the other three party members received, and he does not question this because it has always been this way and it will always be this way and he has stopped thinking about why.

---

### Evening — The Starling: The Four Seasons of Love

---

The Starling is packed. Two hundred seats and standing room. Torren's promotion worked — she can see merchant families in the front rows, Academy students clustered near the stage, adventurers from the guild in the back nursing ales. Isolde is in the rear corner, watching from shadow, the enchantress who bet 81 GP on a halfling she'd met once.

Cookie has four outfits. Four songs. Ignis's famous *Four Seasons of Love* cycle, enhanced by her own dance routines, amplified by Ember Enhancement's tenfold emotional bleed.

The Starling's stage manager dims the candles. The room goes quiet. Cookie walks to center stage barefoot.

---

### Song One: BLACK — *The Season of Grief*

*Lost love. The death of what was.*

---

Cookie steps into the light wearing black — a mourning dress, high-necked, long-sleeved, the fabric eating the candlelight. She looks like a ghost of herself. The earrings are the only bright thing on her body.

The song begins low. A dirge. Cookie emits grief through Heartstring — deep, genuine sadness, the kind that sits in the chest like a stone. The dance is ballet. Classical. Controlled. Twirls that make the black dress spiral outward, elegance that masks the ache underneath.

**Performance DC 16 (advantage from earrings, +d12 Dance Inspiration):**
**Roll: 4 (adv kept higher) + 9 + 3 (d12) = 16 vs DC 16. Success by 0.**

The audience doesn't applaud when it ends. They can't. Half the room is crying. The other half is trying not to. The dock foreman in the third row has his face in his hands. A woman near the aisle is gripping her husband's arm so hard her knuckles are white.

Cookie breathes. One down.

> **Song 1: 1,500 XP (DC 16) + 3,750 domain bonus (25% of 15,000 L6→L7 gap) = 5,250 XP**
> **Running: 47,798 XP (Level 6)**

---

### Song Two: BLUE — *The Season of Longing*

*The ache of wanting. Vulnerability. The door you're afraid to open.*

---

Quick change backstage — thirty seconds — and Cookie returns in blue. Deep indigo. The dress flows like water, cut low, arms bare, the fabric moving with every breath. She looks softer. Younger. Open.

The emotion shifts. Loneliness. The wanting of something you don't have yet. Heartstring projects vulnerability and the ache of being alone, and at 10× Enhancement the entire room feels it in their ribs.

The dance is a jig with swoops — lighter than the first, playful in its sadness. Jazz hands and style, feet quick on the stage, a dancer who's performing for an audience of one and hoping they're watching from somewhere she can't see.

**Performance DC 16 (advantage, +d12):**
**Roll: 7 (adv) + 9 + 6 (d12) = 22 vs DC 16. Success by 6.**

The room exhales. Someone in the back actually says "oh" out loud, involuntarily, the way people do when they see the first star of evening. The Academy students are leaning forward. Ivor, if he's here, is probably crying. He cries at everything.

> **Song 2: 1,500 XP (DC 16) + 3,750 domain bonus = 5,250 XP**
> **Running: 53,048 XP (Level 6)**

---

### Song Three: WHITE — *The Season of Passion*

*Lust. Selfishness. The pleasure of love and the fear of commitment.*

---

White. Sheer. The outfit is barely an outfit — lingerie-as-performance, pure Firebird aesthetic, the kind of thing Ignis wore onstage that made a generation of young women think "I could do that." Cookie absolutely can do that. The scroll-marks on her shoulders and torso glow faintly gold against dark skin.

The emotion is lust. Selfishness. The wanting-without-giving. Heartstring at full Enhancement projects desire so thick the air feels warm.

The dance is *filthy*. Pole work — Cookie found the stage's support column and made it hers. Legs splayed wide in the air. Bending backward until her twin tails brush the floor. Twerking with precision that suggests years of practice Daisy never knew about. Tongue out. Eyes half-lidded. The ahegao face of a woman who knows exactly what she's doing and is enjoying the fact that you know she knows.

**Performance DC 18 (advantage, +d12):**
**Roll: NAT 20 (adv) + 9 + 11 (d12) = 40 vs DC 18. Success by 22.**

The room *breaks*. Not applause — something more primal. The dock foreman stands up. Three women in the merchant section fan themselves. A teenage boy in the Academy cluster goes so red his friend has to check if he's breathing. Isolde, in the back corner, sets down her wine glass very carefully and writes something in a small notebook.

The WIS DC 12 Heartstring save hits 200 people at 10× Enhancement. The failure rate is catastrophic.

> **Song 3: 2,500 XP (DC 18) + 3,750 domain bonus = 6,250 XP**
> **Running: 59,298 XP — LEVEL UP → Level 7!**
> **New HP: 45/45. New level gap: 20,000 (L7→L8). New domain bonus: 5,000/check.**

---

### Song Four: RED — *The Season of True Love*

*Finding it. Keeping it. The joy that doesn't end.*

---

Red. Cookie's favorite color. The Firebird color. Crimson wrap, gold threading, open shoulders — this is the outfit she wears when she wants the world to see her as she actually is. Not the seductress. Not the mourner. The girl from Kettlebrook who believes in love stories because her mother raised her on them, even if Daisy would never admit it.

The emotion is happiness. Contentment. Loyalty. The finding of the thing you spent three songs looking for. Heartstring projects pure, uncut joy at tenfold Enhancement and the room fills with warmth like sunlight through stained glass.

The dance is *everything*. High energy. Sliding across the stage on her knees. Breakdancing — Cookie drops to her back and spins, legs in the air, then kicks up to standing in one fluid motion. Aerial acrobatics off the stage's edge. The kind of movement that shouldn't be possible for someone who was doing pole work thirty seconds ago but Cookie's DEX is 24 and her body does what she tells it to do.

**Performance DC 18 (advantage, +d12):**
**Roll: NAT 20 (adv) + 9 + 1 (d12) = 30 vs DC 18. Success by 12.**

Two natural twenties in a row. The dice are not subtle about how this night is going.

The room stands. All of it. Every seat. The ovation starts before the last note fades and it doesn't stop. Cookie stands center stage in red, drenched in sweat, gold-flecked eyes bright, the scroll-marks on her shoulders pulsing with fading golden light, and she thinks: *this is what Ignis feels. This is what it's like to be the fire.*

> **Song 4: 2,500 XP (DC 18) + 5,000 domain bonus (25% of 20,000 L7→L8 gap) = 7,500 XP**
> **Running: 66,798 XP (Level 7)**

---

### After the Show

---

Honey mead. Fern paid. Roasted vegetables and Ashmere red on the house — the Starling's manager comped everything and offered three more show slots. Torren worked the room and locked down three patron inquiries. Isolde watched the whole thing from the back corner with an expression that said "I am a genius for investing in this girl."

Cookie sat in a corner booth with her feet up and ate roasted carrots with her fingers because nobody was going to tell her not to.

The walk home was quiet. Varenholm at night — lanterns on the canal, the smell of river water and baked bread from the overnight bakeries. Fern talked. Cookie listened. She was too tired to talk and too happy to mind.

Long rest.
