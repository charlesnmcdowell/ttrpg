# Cookie's Campaign — Chapter Two: *The Indigo Hustle*

*Starter Campaign · The Bard arc · Cookie*

---

## Chapter Two: The Indigo Hustle

### Day 2 — Varenholm

---

### The Makeover

---

Cookie wakes up before Fern. This is intentional.

"Get up. I need you."

"It's not even—"

"Get. Up."

Pillows. Fruit. Makeup. Twenty minutes of Fern standing in her smallclothes saying "this is weird" while Cookie stuffs her bottom with cushioning and arranges two pieces of fruit to give her bust a shelf it doesn't naturally possess.

Then the makeup. Cookie's hands move fast — powder, color, line, blend. The same skills she taught herself at The Wren's Rest with whatever she could borrow from the general store. Foundation to even the skin. Liner to sharpen the eyes. Color on the lips. Shadow to sculpt the cheekbones.

Fern looks in the mirror. Stops talking, which is how you know it worked.

"Cookie, I look—"

"You look like a model. Don't move too fast or the fruit shifts."

---

### Torren's Shop

---

They meet Torren at the shop. Maeven is there — the tailor, pins in her teeth, measuring tape around her neck, the permanent expression of a woman who can tell your measurements from across the room.

Torren sees Cookie and lights up. Then he sees Fern and his brain stalls.

"You know, Cookie — you could wear my dresses. Walk around the city. Let people know where you got them. Good for business."

"Or," Cookie says, "Fern here could be a paid model for your line."

Torren looks at Fern. Maeven looks at Fern. Fern stands there looking like she costs fifty gold a day.

Then Cookie touches her own face. Wipes. Shows them her hand — makeup on her fingers. Bare skin underneath.

"I did this in twenty minutes. Pillows and fruit for the rest."

Maeven puts down the pins. Torren's mouth opens and doesn't close for three seconds.

"Pay me one gold a week. Not two silver — one gold. I'll run a makeup and beauty line in your shop. Women will pay for this. Fern works the counter. You expand the business."

Torren considers. Maeven considers. The math is easy. One gold a week for a service nobody else in Varenholm offers, run by a girl who just turned a Kettlebrook barmaid into a runway model with kitchen supplies.

Deal.

Maeven pulls Fern aside to fit her for a free dress — payment and proof of concept in one garment. Fern is officially employed.

---

### The Dressing Room

---

While Maeven's measuring Fern, Cookie reaches up and touches Torren on the back. Halfway up — she's four-foot-ten, she can only reach so high. Her hand traces slow down his side, along his hip, down his leg.

She walks toward the dressing room. Looks over her shoulder. The look says *follow me.*

He follows. Of course he follows.

"Which color? The copper or the indigo?"

"Indigo."

He brings the dress. She points at the chair. He sits.

Cookie undresses in front of him. Slowly. Layer by layer until she's standing in her lingerie — the Firebird-style underthings, lace and string and skin that glows dark chocolate under the dressing room lamplight. The scroll-marks on her shoulders catch the light. She turns around.

Bends over.

Grinds back against him in a slow rolling dance. Her hips press into his lap, moving in the rhythm she uses for the third act of Dragon's Fire — the part that's all breath and weight and gravity. She feels him get hard through his pants, his manhood pressing against her through the thin fabric. His breath catches. His hands grip the armrests like he's trying to hold onto something that left five seconds ago.

She straightens up. Takes the indigo dress off the hook. Puts it on — slowly, one arm at a time, smoothing the fabric down over her body like she's wrapping a gift. Adjusts the neckline. Checks the mirror.

Walks out.

Maeven looks up from Fern's hemline as Cookie approaches.

"That fits you well."

Torren takes a few minutes to appear. When he does, he's flushed. Shirt untucked on one side. Not making eye contact with anyone.

"I was only in there for thirty seconds," Cookie says. Innocent as sunrise. "Surely not long enough for anything inappropriate."

Maeven and Fern look at Torren's state. Look at each other. The kind of look women share when a man has been completely dismantled and doesn't even know it yet.

"Torren." Cookie clasps her hands in front of her. "I need you to arrange for the top patrons — fifty, maybe more, upper class — to attend my show in a few days. The Starling. Can you do that?"

He nods. Still recovering. "And what — what do I get out of it?"

"You get to see me in the copper dress next time."

His face goes crimson. He holds out his hand to shake — professional, formal, the merchant's reflex.

Cookie doesn't take the hand. She holds both arms up.

He picks her up. She kisses him on the cheek. Soft. Deliberate. Close enough to his mouth to make a promise she hasn't made yet.

Maeven and Fern laugh. The sound follows Torren out the door as he leaves to gather himself and start writing invitations. He walks into the doorframe on the way out. Nobody mentions it.

Cookie leaves after. The indigo dress is hers now. Advantage on social checks. Cost: nothing but thirty seconds in a dressing room and whatever's left of Torren's composure.

### The Adventurer's Guild

---

The guild hall smells like sweat and leather and ambition. Cookie walks in wearing the indigo dress — barefoot, blue-haired, four-foot-ten — and every head in the room turns.

Registration. The clerk takes her information. Name. Class. Specialty.

"Dancer. Buffer. Support."

The clerk writes it down without looking up. He's heard stranger.

Cookie scans the bounty board. One gold for escort work. Two gold for a retrieval. And at the bottom — bandit camp, northeast road. One gold *per head.*

Per head.

Cookie reads it again. Per *head.* Not per party. Not per adventurer. Per bandit killed.

She looks around the guild hall. Thirty adventurers, give or take. Most of them drinking. Most of them between jobs. Most of them bored enough to say yes to a halfling in an indigo dress who makes eye contact like she's casting a spell.

"Who wants to make money?"

---

### The Rally

---

Thirty adventurers. Cookie stands on a table in the guild hall — the only way to see over the crowd — and lays it out. Bandit camp. One gold per head. The more people, the faster the fight, the more heads, the more gold. Everybody eats.

A dwarf at the front asks if she's serious. She tells him she's the most serious person in the room. He laughs. Signs up first.

Twenty-nine more follow. Some because the money's good. Some because they were bored. Some because a barefoot halfling just organized a raid in fifteen minutes and they want to see what happens next.

Dorith — healer, warm face, steady hands. She joins because someone's going to get hurt and she'd rather be there when it happens.

Dalla — fighter, broad shoulders, practical. Cookie stays near her because Dalla looks like the kind of woman who doesn't let people die.

Silas — rogue, sharp eyes, says less than he thinks. He joins because thirty people hitting a bandit camp is easy money and he's not stupid.

Marta — ranger, quiet, reads tracks the way other people read books. She'll matter more in Chapter 3 than she knows.

Garron — crossbowman, reliable, doesn't talk much. Shoots straight.

Thirty adventurers march out the northeast gate. Cookie at the front. Barefoot. Indigo dress. No weapon.

---

### The Bandit Camp

---

The camp is in a clearing off the northeast road. Fifteen, maybe twenty bandits. Tents. A fire. Stolen goods stacked under tarps. They're not expecting thirty armed adventurers led by a halfling who's already doing warmup stretches.

The fight starts fast. Thirty on twenty isn't a battle — it's a flood. The adventurers spread through the camp in pairs and trios, swords and arrows and the particular violence of professionals doing a job they're good at.

Cookie doesn't have a weapon.

Cookie doesn't need a weapon.

The first bandit comes at her and her body does what it knows. Dance. The spin from Dragon's Fire, the weight shift from the third act, the hip rotation that powers every performance she's ever given — but this time the foot keeps going. Her kick catches the bandit in the ribs and he folds.

She doesn't stop moving. The dance continues — she's performing in the middle of a raid, bare feet on dirt, the indigo dress flaring with every turn. Performance roll. The ember in her chest flares — the warm thing she doesn't understand yet — and the adventurers near her hit harder, move faster, fight like the music only she can hear is telling them when to strike.

Six Performance rolls over the course of the fight. Six times the battlefield bends around a barefoot dancer who's making up a martial art as she goes.

She kicks a bandit who's twice her height. He goes down. She spins out of a sword swing that would have taken her arm off, drops low, sweeps a leg. Another one falls. The kicks look like dance moves because they *are* dance moves — extended lines, perfect form, the body of a girl who's been drilling choreography since she could walk repurposed into something that breaks bones.

Tai Chi is born. Not named yet. Not understood yet. But born.

The camp falls in minutes. Twenty bandits down. Thirty adventurers standing. Cookie breathing hard in the middle of the clearing, barefoot in a dirty indigo dress, sweat on her collarbone, the warm thing in her chest settling back to its resting hum.

---

### Payday

---

One gold per head. Twenty heads. The guild pays out and everybody gets their share. The adventurers who fought beside Cookie — the five who stayed close, who saw what she did, who felt the Performance rolls sharpen their instincts — find her at the guild hall afterward.

Dorith. Dalla. Silas. Marta. Garron.

"We want in. Whatever you're doing next."

Cookie has a party.

---

### The Room

---

Evening. Millward Chandlery. Fern is sitting on the bed sorting through fabric samples from Torren's shop — already taking the job seriously, already planning displays.

Cookie drops onto her bed. The indigo dress has dirt on the hem and a small tear near the knee. She doesn't care. She made more gold today than she made in a year at The Wren's Rest. She has a party. She has a job at Torren's shop. She has Torren.

Fern looks at her.

"You have dirt on your face."

"I raided a bandit camp."

"You WHAT—"

Cookie pulls the blanket up. Her feet ache. Good ache. The ache of a body that discovered something about itself today. Her legs can do more than dance. Her kicks can do more than perform.

Tomorrow she'll figure out what to call it. Tonight she sleeps.

Twenty thousand experience points. Level 2 to Level 4 in one day. Kettlebrook feels very far away.
