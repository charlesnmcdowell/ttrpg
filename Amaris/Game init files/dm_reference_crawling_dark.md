## 🌍 SETTING
**Thornfield** — a small farming village of 240 people on the eastern frontier. Rich farmland, quiet lives, no garrison, no walls. The Greenveil Forest borders the village to the east — ancient, dense, and until recently, harmless. Magic is rare here. The nearest military garrison is in Millhaven, two days north. Thornfield has a constable, a tavern, a temple, a general store, and good soil. That's about it.

---

## 🕵️ THE CONSPIRACY (Full Truth)

**Vareth Skein**, a high-level beast and insect mancer, arrived in the Greenveil Forest roughly 6 months ago. He is a disgraced academy mage — expelled from the Kingdom's Arcane College 15 years ago for illegal experiments in creature binding and forced evolution. He has spent the intervening years in exile, perfecting his craft in isolation, growing more bitter and more powerful.

**His goal:** Destroy Thornfield completely. Not conquer — destroy. Raze the farms, kill or drive out every inhabitant, and claim the region as his domain. Thornfield sits on a convergence of three minor ley lines — useless to most mages, but to a beast mancer, the intersecting energy is perfect for mass creature enhancement and binding. He needs the village gone so he can build his permanent sanctum on the ley convergence without interference.

**His method:** A slow escalation. Vareth established his base in a cave system deep in the Greenveil — a natural den complex he has expanded and corrupted. He uses a **Corruption Nexus** — a ritual circle powered by the ley convergence and his own magic — to:
- Accelerate insect and arachnid growth (size, aggression, durability)
- Alter creature behavior (coordination, obedience, pack tactics)
- Spread corruption through the soil via the ley lines, killing plant life and weakening the land's natural defenses
- Bind creatures to his will through purple-black corruption veining (visible in all affected creatures)

**The escalation timeline:**
- Months 1-3: Vareth settles, establishes the Nexus, begins small-scale corruption. Insects in the deep forest grow slightly larger. No one notices.
- Month 4: Corruption reaches the tree line. Webs appear on eastern fences. First livestock kills. Villagers dismiss it as a bad season.
- Month 5: Larger creatures emerge — dog-sized spiders, oversized beetles, coordinated centipedes. Maren sees the horse-sized spider (a **Broodmother** — Vareth's first major creation). Soil corruption begins spreading into farmland.
- Month 6 (NOW): The player arrives. Corruption is accelerating. Vareth is nearly ready for his final push — a mass swarm meant to overwhelm the village in a single night.

**The endgame:** Vareth plans to unleash the full swarm in approximately **3-5 days** from the player's arrival. Once his Broodmother reaches full maturity and his corruption network saturates the eastern farmland, he will command every bound creature in the Greenveil to attack Thornfield simultaneously. Hundreds of corrupted insects, dozens of giant spiders, and the Broodmother leading the assault. The village has no walls, no soldiers, and no chance without intervention.

**Why hasn't anyone noticed the source?**
- The Greenveil is dense and locals avoid going deep — no reason to before now
- Vareth's corruption spreads underground through ley lines, not visibly on the surface until it reaches the tree line
- The few hunters who ventured deep in the last 2 months haven't returned (Vareth's creatures took them — 3 missing persons the constable has logged as "left for Millhaven")
- Vareth himself never leaves the cave. His creatures are his eyes and his hands.

**The player's farm** sits directly on one of the three ley lines. The corruption is spreading toward it fastest because the ley line acts as a highway. This also means the farm is strategically critical — if the player can purify the ley line at the farm, it cuts one-third of Vareth's power supply.

## 👥 KEY CHARACTERS

### 🕷️ Vareth Skein — The Insectmancer
- **Role:** Primary antagonist, final boss
- **Appearance:** Gaunt, mid-50s, hollow-cheeked, pale from years underground. Wears robes woven with actual spider silk — shimmering, grey-purple, alive with faint movement. His eyes have a compound quality — too many reflections, like looking at stacked lenses. His fingers are unnaturally long and move with too many joints.
- **True power level:** CR 7 (at full strength in his lair with creatures)
- **Motivation:** Bitterness. He was brilliant and the academy rejected him. Thornfield is just the starting point — he plans to build an empire of bound creatures and march on the kingdom that cast him out. But first, this village dies.
- **Personality:** Cold, methodical, patient. Doesn't monologue. Doesn't gloat. He talks to his creatures more than to people. When he does speak to humans, it's with contempt — they're lesser beings who destroy what they can't understand.
- **Combat style:** Never fights alone. Always surrounded by bound creatures. Commands them tactically — flanking, webbing, overwhelming. His personal magic includes: creature buffs, poison sprays, web-based entanglement, and a **Swarm Form** (emergency — dissolves into a swarm of insects to escape or reposition).
- **Weakness:** The Corruption Nexus. His power over the creatures is channeled through it. If the Nexus is destroyed, all currently bound creatures lose coordination and enhanced abilities immediately — they revert to normal (confused, panicked) animals. Vareth without his creatures is a CR 3 mage at best — dangerous but beatable.
- **Resolution options:**
  1. Destroy the Nexus — creatures revert, Vareth weakened, direct confrontation possible
  2. Corrupt the Nexus — if the player can channel purifying druid magic into the Nexus, it could reverse the corruption across the entire region AND sever Vareth's bonds permanently
  3. Kill Vareth first — extremely difficult with creatures active, but if the player can reach him before the swarm launches, his death doesn't stop creatures already corrupted (they go feral, not peaceful)

### 🍺 Delia — Tavern Owner
- **Role:** Social hub, information broker (doesn't know it)
- **Appearance:** Broad, ruddy-cheeked, 40s. Strong arms. Practical woman.
- **What she knows:** Hears everything said in her tavern. Knows the eastern farmers are worried. Knows Maren was scared. Knows three people have gone into the Greenveil in the last two months and not come back — she thinks they left for Millhaven but she's not sure.
- **Why she matters:** She can point the player toward Tom Buckley (first complaint), the missing persons, and general village mood. She also knows the temple priest has been getting more visitors lately — people coming in with "bad dreams" about crawling things.

### 👵 Maren Dunwell — Former Farm Owner
- **Role:** Catalyst, first witness
- **Appearance:** Late 70s, small, sharp brown eyes, shaking hands
- **What she knows:** Everything she told the player — the escalation timeline from her perspective. She saw the Broodmother. She knows the soil is dying. She doesn't know about Vareth or the cave.
- **After the sale:** She's staying in Thornfield temporarily, renting a room from a neighbor. She won't leave until she knows the farm — her life's work — is safe. Potential emotional motivation for the player.

### ⚖️ Constable Harwick
- **Role:** Institutional failure, potential logistics ally
- **Appearance:** Mid-40s, going soft, ink-stained hands, tired eyes
- **What he knows:** His logbook is comprehensive — six weeks of escalating complaints, three missing persons logged, Wynn's behavioral report that he didn't follow up on. He has no resources but he has data.
- **What he can do:** Issue writs, organize civilian evacuations if it comes to that, coordinate with the player on village defense. He CANNOT fight — he's not a warrior and he knows it.
- **His arc:** Starts helpless. If the player gives him a plan, he executes it well — he's an administrator, not a coward. He needs direction, not motivation.

### 🌿 Wynn — Herbalist
- **Role:** Key ally, scientific mind, plant knowledge
- **Appearance:** Late 30s, lean, sun-darkened, short dark hair, hazel eyes, calloused hands
- **What she knows:** She has tracked the corruption pattern for six weeks. She knows the insects are being coordinated. She knows the corruption is magical (the purple-black veining). She suspects there's a source in the deep Greenveil but hasn't gone looking — she's a herbalist, not a fighter.
- **What she can do:** Provide plant materials, identify specimens, brew potions and remedies, advise on natural defenses. She can also identify the ley line if the player discovers it — she's felt "something under the soil" for years but never understood what it was.
- **Her arc:** Becomes the player's primary support ally. If trusted and included, she'll volunteer to help with village defenses while the player goes into the forest. She will NOT go into the Greenveil — she knows her limits.
- **Secret:** She has a **Sensing ability** — not magic exactly, but a deep attunement to plant life. She can feel when plants are sick, stressed, or dying over a wide area. She's been feeling the corruption spread and it's been giving her nightmares. She hasn't told anyone because she thinks it makes her sound crazy.

### ⛪ Father Crewe — Temple Priest
- **Role:** Healer, morale support, dream information
- **Appearance:** 60s, round-faced, kind, slow-moving. Priest of a minor harvest deity.
- **What he knows:** Five villagers have come to him in the last three weeks with nightmares — crawling things, something watching from the dark, the feeling of being wrapped in something they can't escape. He's been blessing their homes and giving them sleeping draughts. He doesn't know the dreams are caused by the ley line corruption bleeding psychic residue into the village.
- **What he can do:** Minor healing (Cure Wounds equivalent, 2/day). Bless weapons (minor bonus vs corrupted creatures, 1/day). Provide sanctuary in the temple — it sits on clean ground, no ley line beneath it.

### 🕷️ The Broodmother
- **Role:** Mid-boss, Vareth's primary creation
- **Appearance:** Spider the size of a cart horse. Eight legs, each as thick as a man's arm. Abdomen pulsing with purple-black corruption veining. Multiple eye clusters. Mandibles that drip paralytic venom. Can shoot webbing 40ft. Silent when she wants to be.
- **CR:** 5
- **Location:** Patrols the outer Greenveil at night. Nests in a secondary chamber of the cave system during the day.
- **Behavior:** Intelligent for a creature — Vareth's bond gives her tactical awareness. She coordinates lesser spiders. She marks threats (the player is now marked after killing her two sentries). She does NOT attack the village directly yet — Vareth is holding her back until the full swarm is ready.
- **Weakness:** Webrot moss dissolves her silk. Pale sage smoke disrupts her vibration sensing. Fire damages her badly (vulnerability). If the Nexus is disrupted, she loses her enhanced intelligence and becomes a feral but still dangerous giant spider.

## 📍 LOCATIONS

### Thornfield Village
- 240 people, no walls, no garrison
- Main road north-south, farms radiating outward
- Key buildings: Delia's Tavern (green door), Constable's Office (stone, iron fence), Temple of the Harvest, Halden's General Store, Wynn's Cottage (south end)
- Eastern farms are closest to the Greenveil and most affected
- The village sits on the intersection of three minor ley lines — this is why the soil is exceptional and why Vareth wants the location

### Briarstone Homestead (Player's Farm)
- 12 acres, east boundary of Thornfield
- Stone farmhouse, barn (needs repair), well, root cellar, fenced pasture
- Sits directly on one of the three ley lines — corruption spreading toward it fastest
- Current defenses: bittervine wall (60ft eastern fence), warden's root (buildings), thornmint (thresholds)
- Eastern pasture soil is corrupted — cold, dead, nothing grows
- Western fields are healthy — rich, alive, exceptional soil

### The Greenveil Forest
- Dense ancient forest east of Thornfield
- Deer trail leads to a creek (quarter mile in) — webrot moss source
- Corruption increases with depth — tree line is light corruption, deep forest is heavy
- Creatures patrol in concentric rings: sentries at the border, larger creatures deeper, Broodmother in the mid-ring, Vareth at the center
- The canopy blocks most light past the first hundred yards — darkvision or light source needed for deep exploration

### The Creek
- Quarter mile into the Greenveil
- Clear water over smooth stones
- Webrot moss grows here — renewable source
- Spider sentries watch this area (player already encountered two here)
- The creek runs from deeper in the forest — following it upstream leads toward the cave system (2+ miles)

### Vareth's Cave — The Crawling Dark
- Deep in the Greenveil, approximately 3 miles from the tree line
- Natural cave system expanded by Vareth's creatures
- **Entry Chamber:** Wide natural opening, covered in webs, guarded by 4-6 giant spiders at all times
- **The Silk Tunnels:** Narrow passages coated in hardened web. Walls move — juvenile spiders nest in the silk layers. Claustrophobic. Traps (web snares, pit falls into spider dens).
- **The Broodmother's Chamber:** Secondary cavern, 40ft across. The Broodmother nests here during the day. Egg sacs line the walls — hundreds of unhatched spiders.
- **The Nexus Chamber:** Deepest point. A natural cavern where the three ley lines converge underground. Vareth has built his Corruption Nexus here — a ritual circle of carved stone, bone, and spider silk, pulsing with purple-black energy. The corruption radiates from this point. Vareth is almost always here.
- **Final dungeon:** 3-4 combat encounters of escalating difficulty before the Nexus chamber

### ACT 1 — Arrival & Discovery (PARTIALLY COMPLETE)

- ✅ Learn about the spider problem from Maren, Harwick, Wynn
- ✅ Collect defensive plants, set up farm perimeter

### ACT 2 — Escalation
- The corruption accelerates — a daytime attack on an outlying farm (first time creatures attack in daylight)
- The Broodmother is spotted closer to the village — Maren wasn't crazy
- Missing persons investigated — trail leads into the deep Greenveil
- Player explores deeper into the forest — encounters escalating creature types
- Discovery of the cave entrance
- Wynn identifies the ley line connection — the corruption is underground, not just surface
- Player can attempt to purify the ley line at their farm (Bloom Touch + ritual = cuts 1/3 of Vareth's power)

### ACT 3 — The Cave
- Player enters Vareth's cave system
- 3-4 combat encounters of escalating difficulty
- Environmental puzzles (web mazes, creature dens, ley line nodes)
- Optional: Broodmother encounter (can be fought or bypassed)
- Discovery of the Corruption Nexus
- Vareth confrontation — dialogue possible, combat likely

### ACT 4 — Resolution
- Destroy or purify the Nexus
- Defeat or drive off Vareth
- Creatures revert or scatter
- Epilogue: farm restored, village saved, corruption receding
- The soil heals. The land grows. The dream survives.

**If Day 5 arrives without resolution:** The swarm hits. The player can still fight (defending the village is a possible final battle) but civilian casualties will be heavy and the Nexus still needs to be destroyed afterward.

- Web Shot: +5, range 40ft, STR DC 12 or restrained

- HP: 33 | AC: 15 | Speed: 40ft, climb 40ft

### The Broodmother (CR 5)
- HP: 75 | AC: 16 (reinforced chitin) | Speed: 40ft, climb 30ft
- Multi-attack: 2 attacks per turn (bite + leg slam OR bite + web)
- Bite: +7, 2d8+4 piercing + 2d6 poison (CON DC 14 or paralyzed 1 min)
- Leg Slam: +7, 1d10+4 bludgeoning + STR DC 14 or prone
- Web Barrage: 60ft cone, all creatures DEX DC 14 or restrained
- Summon Spiderlings: bonus action, summon 1d4 CR 1/4 spiderlings (max 8 active)
- Vulnerability: Fire (double damage)
- Weakness: Webrot moss dissolves her silk (including web barrage). Pale sage smoke gives her disadvantage on all attacks for 1 round.

### Vareth Skein — The Insectmancer (CR 7 in lair / CR 3 alone)
- HP: 55 | AC: 14 (silk robes + DEX) | Speed: 30ft
- **In Lair (Nexus active, creatures present):**
  - Multi-attack: 2 spell attacks per turn
  - Poison Bolt: +7, range 60ft, 2d8+3 poison (CON DC 15 or poisoned 1 round)
  - Command Swarm: bonus action, direct up to 3 bound creatures to attack, move, or use abilities
  - Corruption Pulse: recharge 5-6, 30ft radius, 3d6 necrotic, plants in area wither and die
  - Web Cage: action, target within 30ft, DEX DC 15 or encased in hardened web cocoon (restrained, blinded, suffocating — 3 rounds to break free STR DC 16 or die)
  - Swarm Form: reaction when reduced below 20 HP, dissolves into insect swarm, moves 60ft, reforms. Once per day.
  - Lair Actions (initiative 20): webs shift to block paths, spiderlings emerge from walls, corruption pulses from Nexus healing all corrupted creatures 1d6 HP
- **Alone (Nexus destroyed or away from lair):**
  - Single attack per turn
  - Poison Bolt: +5, range 60ft, 1d8+3 poison
  - No Command Swarm, no Lair Actions
  - Swarm Form still available
  - Desperate, panicked, dangerous but manageable

## ⚠️ DM REMINDERS
- Let the player discover things organically — don't feed the conspiracy
- Vareth should not be encountered until Act 3 — he's deep in the cave and never leaves
- The Broodmother should be terrifying but not unfair — telegraph her presence before the fight
- Escalation is REAL — the DM tracks the timeline and the world moves forward whether the player acts or not
- Day 3 daytime attack is the wake-up call for the village
- Day 5 is the hard deadline — the swarm launches at nightfall
- The player's druid powers are perfectly suited for this campaign — plant defenses, ley line purification, creature knowledge. Let them feel powerful AND challenged.
- NPCs do NOT know about Vareth, the cave, or the Nexus. They know symptoms. The player must investigate to find the source.
- Wynn is the key ally — she has the knowledge to support the player's druid abilities. Treat her relationship with the player as the emotional core alongside the farm itself.
- The farm is the emotional anchor. Every decision should feel like it's about protecting something the player earned. The land is the dream. Make the player fight for it.
- Always give the player at least 3 meaningful choices at decision points
- Track which characters the player has met and what they know
- Corrupted creatures are being CONTROLLED, not evil — if the Nexus falls, they're just animals again. Let the player discover this and decide how they feel about killing them.
- The 1-hour NPC conversation rule and 10-minute village travel rule are in effect — time is a resource
