The shepherds drop out of the picture in the first hundred yards. Then the goat-track. Then the last sympathy-bell from the third farmstead, fading to nothing as the deep pine swallows sound.

The Ignis-scent stays half a degree off true south the whole night. Banked-coal warmth, fixed, patient. Shen walks under it like a needle under a north star. Crickets. A vixen barking at the moon. A chimney glow seven miles east where a watchpost burns its lantern through full dark. None of it pulls his head around.

By the second hour after midnight the pine starts climbing. By the fourth, granite. By dawn — the broken edge where the foothills surrender to the Dragonspine pass proper.

Then the air changes.

Pressure drop. Hot wind, sudden, wrong direction — the kind of heat that means something the size of a hill is breathing nearby. The chest-glyph flares once and goes still in submission instinct it didn't choose.

A shadow crosses the dawn light — wingspan wide enough to put the whole pass in shade.

She lands on the path forty yards ahead. Ancient red — scales like banked forge-iron in the early light, horns crowned, wings folding with a sound like sails struck. Amber eyes lock on him. They do not match his own.

**"Shen."**

One word. The path goes nowhere now.
