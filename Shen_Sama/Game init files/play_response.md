The road holds its silence the way the marsh did three days ago — like something has decided not to speak yet.

The sergeant has not moved his horse forward. The blade is still angled across his saddle. The courier has stopped weeping; he is, instead, holding the slate flat against his chest with both hands like it is the last warm thing on the road. There is a faint tremor in his hands that has not stopped. There will not be a quote on the slate. There will be a sketch — the shape of what stood here. Slates do not survive courier-relay; the sketch will be transcribed at the tower into proper ink.

Marshal A is no longer on the road. Marshal A is, in pieces and aerosol, *across* the road for about twenty feet of arc. The horse — riderless, terrified, blood-flecked — has stopped about forty feet south, pressed against a pine, breathing hard. It will not be ridden today.

Marshal B is gone. The pines closed behind him three minutes ago and the hoofbeats have receded toward Highford's tower along the back-track he and the patrol came from. He will reach the lord by sundown. The lord will read what Marshal B says about a single claw strike at flag-of-truce distance and will sit very still in his audience chair for some time. The petition for a Mage-Hunter writ will be written before he sleeps tonight.

Shen has continued walking. The dragon-tattoo on his chest has dimmed back to its inactive black, the green-fire pulled inward, only the faintest residual glow at the collarbone. The road south stretches in front of him. The blood is already cooling on the granite culverts. By morning the rain will have moved most of it and the rest will be a stain that won't quite leave for a season.

The sergeant watches him go. He does not pursue. He will, when he has stopped shaking, dismount and stand over what is left of his man and try to gather enough of him for last rites. He will fail. He will write something to that effect on the second slate, the one he keeps in his saddle-bag, the personal one. He will sign it with his name. The first slate goes to the tower; the second slate goes nowhere.

The Cult of Anku does not know yet, but a courier-pigeon out of Highford's tower at dusk Day 3 will carry a one-line description that intersects the Reader's pipeline within four days. By Day 8 a pale thin man Shen has never seen will be walking south from Stormhaven with an Ember-Shade ward in a leather pouch and a calm, patient expression.

The pines smell of resin and iron. Shen walks. The road south of Highford waits. None of his options for what comes next are good ones.
