"...*Mother of —*" The sergeant has finally turned. The sword is still in his hand and that hand is now limp at his side, a man who has stopped pretending to be soldiering. "*Calden — eyes down — EYES DOWN, BOY —*" A scream from the courier, choked off, and then the sergeant's voice again, very calm now, the calmness of someone reciting a will: "I'm watching it. I'll write it. Don't look. Don't look. *Don't —*"

Black scale unspools across Shen's shoulders the way a sail unfurls and finds its wind. The dragon-tattoo doesn't migrate; it *is* him, the chest-glyph and the arm-runes pulling out into a cathedral of carbon and cartilage, neck lengthening, jaw lengthening, wings cracking open with a sound the foothills will remember as thunder for a generation. Forty feet of clearance is enough. The road's pine canopy parts under his shoulder rise; one tree splits at the trunk, the other goes over. The redtail hawk that had been hunting voles abandons the sky entirely. From the chimney a mile and a quarter ahead — a goat-bell, then a dog, then a woman shouting one word in coastal-dialect, then bells, *real* bells, plural, alarm pattern. Half the southern valley has just become an audience.

The first downstroke crosses the patrol like a passing storm — the courier on the ground covering his head, the sergeant on his knees not from fear but from training (you do not stand under a dragon's wingspan if you want to keep your spine), the riderless horse going over sideways and screaming. The second downstroke clears the first switchback. The third clears the second. Marshal B is half a mile farther than Shen estimated — the horse has terror in it now, *real* terror, not soldier-flight, the kind that comes when the prey-mind smells what's overhead — and the rider is half-turned in the saddle, sword drawn for nothing, and his face is the face of a man counting his last breaths in fractions. The bite resolves at speed. *(Bite-pluck on Marshal B — d20+126 vs AC 16: 11+126=137 → HIT. 3d10+124 = (5+8+9)+124 = 146 piercing → FATALITY, Marshal B HP ~22 → -124, body destroyed beyond resurrection.)* The horse runs three more strides riderless and collapses against a pine. There is nothing of Marshal B large enough to bury. The sword falls into the ferns sixty feet below the road. The bells from the farmstead are still ringing.

The wind smells of pine and copper and — for the first time today — of a thing the Highford lantern-courier system does not have a word for. The sergeant, when Shen banks back over the road heading south, has his slate out. He is writing. He has not stopped writing since the transformation began.
