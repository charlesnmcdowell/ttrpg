# Ankuspawn — Playable Race

**Origin:** Children of AnkuNyx the Dragon Emperor, sired through humanoid or dragon partners across the Kingdom of Ankunyx and beyond. The combination of the Bane of Eve perk (drawing female humanoids) and the God Emperor perk (drawing female dragons) means Kenji fathered many children across the continent over the 25 years since the kingdom's founding.

**Inspiration:** Bhaalspawn (Baldur's Gate / D&D). Children of a godlike figure scattered across the world, each carrying a fragment of their father's nature without necessarily knowing their full heritage.

---

## Age Rules (STRICT)

- **Minimum age: 18.** No Ankuspawn younger than 18 exists in play. This is a real-life legal storytelling constraint — no exceptions.
- **Maximum age: 25.** The Kingdom of Ankunyx did not exist more than 25 years ago. No Ankuspawn can be older than 25.
- **Aging override:** Regardless of the mother's race (elf, dragon, dwarf, orc, human), Ankuspawn age like normal humans. An Ankuspawn with an elven mother does not age on elven timescales — they mature at human speed.
- **Appearance cap:** Ankuspawn never look older than their mid-20s. Even at 25, they appear youthful — a subtle mark of their father's supernatural vitality.

## Racial Traits

### Core Traits (all Ankuspawn share these)

- **Human aging rate.** Mature at the same speed as humans regardless of maternal race.
- **Youthful appearance.** Never appear older than mid-20s physically.
- **Individual Ember spark.** Every Ankuspawn carries their **own unique spark of Ember** — not a faint echo, but a genuine fragment of the sentient flame that manifests differently per individual. One might command minor fire magic, another might heal by touch, a third might see through illusions. The domains are unpredictable and span the full range of magical and physical abilities. These powers are **potent** — stronger than what a normal mortal of equivalent training could achieve — but **far weaker than Kenji's current state**. Think of it as inheriting a candle from a bonfire.
- **Soul echo.** Ankuspawn occasionally sense strong emotions from nearby bonded creatures (echoes of the Soul Nexus). This is unreliable and involuntary — more unsettling than useful. A faint awareness that something connects them to a larger web they don't understand.
- **Supernatural resilience.** Ankuspawn are physically and magically **stronger than normal mortals** across the board. They heal faster, resist disease more effectively, and have deeper reserves of stamina and willpower. Not invulnerable — just noticeably tougher than their peers.

### Female Ankuspawn — Special Traits

Female Ankuspawn inherit a concentrated version of their father's supernatural magnetism. These traits are **in addition to** core and variable traits:

- **Otherworldly beauty.** Female Ankuspawn possess an unnatural, arresting beauty that goes beyond genetics. It is not subtle — people notice, people stare, people remember.
- **Male-attracting aura.** They exude a **strong scent and presence that drives men wild** — an inherited echo of their father's Bane of Eve magnetism, but reversed in polarity. This is involuntary and always active. It affects most males within proximity regardless of race. It can be suppressed with effort or magic but never fully eliminated.
- **High magical aptitude.** Female Ankuspawn have an exceptionally high ceiling for gaining special abilities. They can learn and master strong magic across **all domains** — not limited to a single school or element. Combined with their individual Ember spark, this makes them extraordinarily versatile casters and combatants.
- **Valuable biological resources.** Female Ankuspawn blood, essence, and Ember fragments are alchemically potent — more so than male Ankuspawn. This makes them **targets** for anyone who knows what they carry. (See: Cult of Anku, Ashenveil Academy.)

### Variable Traits (depend on maternal heritage)

| Mother's Race | Inherited Traits |
|---------------|-----------------|
| **Human** | No additional racial traits beyond core. The "baseline" Ankuspawn. Most common type. |
| **Half-Orc** | +1 Strength or Constitution (choose one). Slightly more muscular build. May inherit high fertility trait (1/5 chance per encounter vs human 1/20). Grow at human rate, NOT half-orc double rate. |
| **Elf** | +1 Charisma or Wisdom (choose one). Slightly pointed ears (subtle). May inherit limited darkvision (30 ft vs full elven 60 ft). Age at human rate, NOT elven rate. |
| **Dwarf** | +1 Constitution. Stockier build but human height. May inherit poison resistance (advantage on saves vs poison). Age at human rate, NOT dwarven rate. |
| **Dragon** | +1 to any ability score. Faint scale-like markings somewhere on body (subtle, can be hidden). Resistance to one damage type matching mother's color (fire for red/gold, lightning for blue/bronze, acid for black/copper, cold for white/silver, poison for green). These are the rarest and most visually distinctive Ankuspawn. |

## The Mother's Curse — Post-Ankuspawn Infertility

**Any woman who bears Kenji's child becomes unable to conceive with other men afterward.** This is not a choice — it is a biological or magical side effect of carrying an Ember-touched child. The pregnancy itself changes something fundamental in the mother's body. Whether it's Ember residue, Soul Nexus interference, or something about the Ankuspawn's spark rewriting maternal biology during gestation, the result is the same: one pregnancy with Kenji, and then no more children with anyone else. Ever.

**The pattern across the kingdom:** Kenji's ex-lovers found husbands — good men, successful men, men who wanted families. But the children never came. Pip settled down with a respectable man who helps run the inn. No children with him. Amaris married a farmer near Thornfield. No children with him. The story repeats across dozens of women in every corner of the kingdom. They don't all know each other, and most don't realize their infertility shares a common cause. They just know it didn't work out.

**This is why Vess needs Nyx's elixir.** Vess bore Ankuspawn with Kenji years ago. Now she wants to marry Korrim and carry his lineage. Her body won't allow it. She's heard rumors of a powerful fertility elixir from the Ashenveil Academy — she doesn't know what it's made from, but she's desperate enough to pursue it. The irony: the elixir is brewed from harvested Ankuspawn, children who exist because of the same curse that now sterilizes their mothers.

**DM note:** This curse explains the population distribution of Ankuspawn. Each mother produces exactly one batch (single child for humanoids, one clutch for dragons) and then stops. The total Ankuspawn population is equal to the total number of women Kenji has been with — no repeats, no second litters. It also creates a universal quiet tragedy among his ex-lovers that a perceptive new PC could eventually piece together.

## Distribution

Ankuspawn are scattered across the Kingdom of Ankunyx and potentially beyond its borders. They are not organized, do not know each other (usually), and many do not know their father's identity. Some were raised by their mothers with full knowledge; others were abandoned, adopted, or raised in ignorance.

**Population density:** Ankuspawn are uncommon but not rare. In any major city, there might be a handful. In frontier villages, finding one would be notable. The Bane of Eve and God Emperor perks ensured Kenji had many encounters, and his supernatural fertility (especially with orcs at 1/5 and dragons at 100%) produced a meaningful number of offspring.

**Dragon-born Ankuspawn** are the rarest. Dragon clutches produce 4-10 eggs, but only 2/10 typically survive the environment. Dragon Ankuspawn who survived to adulthood are exceptional individuals — they had to survive both the egg stage and the dangerous early years of dragonkind, all while aging at human speed (making them vulnerable much longer than normal dragon young).

## Social Standing

- **In the Kingdom:** Ankuspawn have no special legal status. They are not royalty, not heirs, not recognized by the throne unless AnkuNyx personally acknowledges them (which he almost never does). They are citizens like anyone else.
- **Public perception:** Most people don't know Ankuspawn exist as a category. Those who do might view them with curiosity, suspicion, or reverence depending on local culture.
- **Among themselves:** Ankuspawn who discover their heritage may seek out others — or may want nothing to do with it. There is no organized Ankuspawn faction, guild, or community. Yet.

## Bhaalspawn Mechanic (DM Tool)

Like Bhaalspawn in Baldur's Gate, Ankuspawn exist as a **narrative mechanic** the DM can deploy:

- **Recognition scenes:** An NPC recognizes the Ankuspawn's features — the eyes, the jawline, the way fire doesn't quite burn them. "You look like someone I knew once."
- **Sibling encounters:** Another Ankuspawn crosses the PC's path. Ally? Rival? Someone who wants answers?
- **Heritage revelation:** The PC discovers who their father is. This can be a slow burn across a campaign or a single dramatic moment.
- **Ember dreams:** Vivid, unsettling dreams that hint at the Soul Nexus — a web of connections they can feel but not understand.
- **Dragon attention:** Dragon-blooded Ankuspawn may draw attention from dragonflights — curiosity, hostility, or protection depending on the dragon's relationship with AnkuNyx.

The DM controls the pacing and intensity of these elements. An Ankuspawn PC can play an entire campaign without their heritage becoming central — or it can be the spine of their story.

### The Cult of Anku (ACTIVE THREAT to Ankuspawn)

The **Cult of Anku** is a secret organization founded by **Nyx** (the Lich Queen, wife of AnkuNyx). The cult recruits women who practice the dark arts and swear loyalty to Nyx. Their operations have two phases:

**Phase 1 — Harvesting from Kenji:** Cult members disguise themselves as natural Bane of Eve encounters, seduce Kenji, and after mating, collect his sperm in vials which are delivered to Nyx at the Ashenveil Academy. Kenji does not know they serve his wife. To him, they're just more lovers.

**Phase 2 — Ankuspawn capture (RECENT):** Nyx recently escalated to directing the cult to **locate, capture, and secretly imprison mature Ankuspawn** (18+) within the Academy's lower levels. There, they are harvested for blood, essence, and Ember fragments — resources Nyx uses to brew extremely potent elixirs and further her research into restoring life to the undead.

**What this means for Ankuspawn PCs:** An Ankuspawn character is a **potential target**. The cult may approach them as friendly contacts, romantic interests, or fellow dark arts practitioners before attempting capture. Rumors of missing Ankuspawn are beginning to circulate. An Ankuspawn PC who learns about the cult faces an impossible political question: the woman running it is the Dragon Emperor's wife, and the kingdom bears her name.

**CRITICAL — Ember nullification:** Nyx has spent years studying Ember and has **mastered the ability to nullify Ember-based abilities** — in Ankuspawn AND in Kenji himself. Her cult assassins and captors carry wards or enchantments that suppress Ember on contact or proximity. **An Ankuspawn PC captured or hunted by the cult CANNOT rely on their Ember abilities to escape.** They must use mundane skills, combat training, social manipulation, alliances, non-Ember magic, or raw cunning to survive.

**The key difference between Ankuspawn and their father:** Ankuspawn are young. They're still learning, still growing, still developing skills outside their Ember spark. They CAN adapt. Kenji cannot. After 25 years of godhood, Kenji is **100% Ember-dependent** — every perk, every ability, every stat boost comes from Ember. Take it away and he's a charming rogue with an INT of 9 and a sword he barely trained with. His time has passed. His children's time is beginning. The campaign is theirs to solve — not his.

## Character Creation Notes

1. **Choose maternal race** — this determines variable traits.
2. **Choose age** (18-25) — determines how long they've been alive and how much of the kingdom's history they've lived through.
3. **Choose knowledge level** — do they know who their father is? Do they know they're Ankuspawn? Do they know what that means?
4. **Choose location** — anywhere in or near the kingdom. Frontier, heartland, coast, mountains — Ankuspawn can be from anywhere.
5. **Backstory** — who raised them? What do they want? The "child of a god-emperor" hook is available but not mandatory. An Ankuspawn who just wants to run a tavern is as valid as one seeking their father.
